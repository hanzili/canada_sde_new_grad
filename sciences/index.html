Application guide

How to use this sciences jobs board

This daily-updated page is built for Canadian students and recent grads searching for sciences new grad, junior, and entry-level roles: biology, biotech, lab, chemistry, environmental science, geoscience, clinical research, and science operations roles. Hanzilla gives science students a dedicated path for lab, research, environmental, biotech, quality, and field roles that are usually buried across university boards and employer career pages.

1.Use this page for Canadian science internships, lab assistant roles, research technician openings, biotech jobs, environmental field roles, geoscience roles, chemistry positions, and quality-assurance jobs.
2.Browse student and new-grad opportunities across universities, hospitals, labs, biotech companies, environmental consultancies, mining/geoscience employers, and public agencies.
3.For each role, note whether the employer needs wet-lab techniques, field sampling, GMP/quality systems, clinical research exposure, GIS, environmental monitoring, or mine-site travel before tailoring your resume.
4.International students should check work authorization, background checks, vaccination or safety training, and whether the role is on-site because many science placements are tied to labs, hospitals, field sites, or regulated facilities.

Frequently Asked Questions

What science jobs can Canadian students find on Hanzilla?

Hanzilla includes biology, biotech, chemistry, environmental science, geoscience, clinical research, lab operations, quality assurance, and research assistant roles that fit students, co-op candidates, and recent grads.

Are science internships only in universities?

No. Science students can find roles in universities and hospitals, but also biotech startups, environmental consultancies, mining and geoscience companies, food and pharma labs, public agencies, and quality teams.

What should science students check before applying to lab or field roles?

Check the required lab techniques, field certifications, safety training, vaccination or background-check requirements, site location, travel expectations, and whether the employer is looking for co-op status, recent-graduate availability, or full-time work authorization.

How can science grads search beyond academic research jobs?

Look for adjacent titles such as quality assurance associate, regulatory affairs assistant, clinical research coordinator, environmental technician, geoscience technician, lab operations associate, and product or manufacturing roles at biotech, pharma, mining, food, and clean-tech employers.
