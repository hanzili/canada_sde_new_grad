Big Five Banks New Grad Programs in Canada 2026: Complete Application Guide

February 08, 2026 14 min read

Canada's Big Five banks run some of the largest and most structured new graduate programs in the country. This guide covers every program at each bank ‚Äî from capital markets to CPA rotations ‚Äî with real salary data and application deadlines.

RBC ‚Äî Royal Bank of Canada

RBC has the broadest range of named new grad programs among the Big Five, and the highest overall bonus pool ($9.98B variable compensation in 2025, up 13% YoY).

Leadership Development Program (LDP)

  • Duration: 2 years, four 6-month rotations
  • Structure: Associates collaborate with senior leaders on complex assignments. Formal training and executive exposure throughout.
  • Requirements: Master's-level graduate, 3-7 years of professional experience
  • Location: Toronto
  • Application Timeline: Posted September 2025; deadline September 27, 2025
  • Source: RBC Careers, Versafi

Capital Markets Program (Global IB / Global Markets)

  • Duration: Full-time analyst role (preceded by 10-week summer internship)
  • Locations: Toronto, Montreal, Calgary, Vancouver, New York, London
  • Salary: IB Analyst base CAD $95K-$105K; total comp $160K-$200K. Global Markets base ~$90K with IB bonus 90-110%
  • Application Timeline: Canadian Big 6 IB postings typically open mid-March for Toronto, 1-3 weeks earlier for Calgary/Vancouver
  • Source: RBC Capital Markets Careers, PrepLounge

Quantitative Technology Services Rotational Program

  • Duration: 16 months, multiple rotations in programming, design, and technical architecture
  • Location: Montreal (1 Place Ville Marie)
  • Requirements: Python, Java, C#, C/C++, Git, Linux/Unix, middleware
  • Application Timeline: Posted January 7, 2026; deadline March 1, 2026
  • Source: RBC Careers, Simplify

General Analyst Program (Management & Data Analytics)

  • Streams: General Management and Data Analytics
  • Location: Toronto
  • Requirements: Recent graduate, effective communication, growth mindset. Data Analytics stream prefers SQL, statistical analysis, or data tools
  • Compensation: Competitive salary + bonuses, commissions, stock options, flexible benefits
  • Source: RBC Careers

Other RBC Programs

  • CPA Pre-approved Program: 30-month rotational; complete CPA experience and CFE while mentored by senior leaders
  • Indigenous Peoples Development Program: For recent graduates who self-identify as First Nations, Inuit, or Metis
  • Borealis Fellowship: Scholarship + mentorship for diverse students
  • Pathways Program: $10,000 financial award + Summer Analyst placement for diverse students (deadline: February 9)

TD Bank Group

TD is strongest in technology-focused rotational programs and has expanded its US presence significantly through the TD Cowen acquisition.

TD Securities FinTech Software Engineer Rotational Program

  • Duration: 16 months (4 x 4-month rotations)
  • Structure: Full-time employees rotating through global FinTech areas with guaranteed employment upon completion
  • Includes: Onboarding curriculum, mentoring, rotational training, leadership development
  • Requirements: 2026 graduate
  • Source: TD Careers

TD Securities Analyst/Associate Programs (Capital Markets)

  • Structure: Full-time analyst classes start July, primarily from past summer interns
  • Departments: Investment Banking, Corporate Banking, CIB Credit, Global Transaction Banking, M&A, Equities, Fixed Income, Municipals, Operations, Technology
  • Locations: Toronto (primarily), Montreal, Calgary
  • Salary: IB Analyst base CAD $100K-$105K; total comp $155K-$190K
  • Application Timeline: Full-time offers based on summer internship completion; additional seats recruited throughout the year
  • Source: TD Securities Careers, PrepLounge

TD Securities Graduate Programme (Finance)

  • Structure: Full-time analyst on the Finance Team within TD Securities
  • Requirements: Graduating 2026 or recent 2025 graduate from any academic background
  • Source: The Muse

TD Securities Quantitative Rotational Program

  • Requirements: Master's or PhD graduating Dec 2025-Dec 2026 in Financial Engineering, Statistics, Applied Math, Computer Science, Economics, Physics, Quantitative Finance, Operations Research, Data Science, or Engineering
  • Skills: Python, R, SAS, SQL, Spark, Java, C++
  • Source: TD Careers, Prosple

US Graduate Leadership Program

  • Requirements: Recent MBA with technically focused undergrad (STEM) OR recent Master's with business undergrad; 3-5 years experience
  • Focus: AI, automation, advanced analytics, digital intimacy
  • Source: TD Careers

BMO ‚Äî Bank of Montreal

BMO Capital Markets runs well-regarded rotational programs with strong IB and Global Markets tracks.

Global Markets Analyst/Associate Rotational Program

  • Duration: 12-16 months rotating through various sales and trading desks
  • Tracks: Global Fixed Income (FICC), Global Equity Products (GEP), Global Structured Products (GSP)
  • Hiring: Generalists and Quants
  • Locations: Toronto, Montreal, New York
  • Salary: IB Analyst base CAD $90K-$100K; total comp $148K-$185K. Corporate Banking analyst base $90K (employer-confirmed).
  • Source: BMO Capital Markets Careers, Glassdoor, PrepLounge

Investment & Corporate Banking Program

  • Structure: Full-time analyst and associate classes start July, primarily from past summer interns
  • Work: Financial modeling, valuations, industry research, pitch books, M&A analysis, client presentations
  • Application Timeline (Canada): Summer Analyst/Associate recruitment February-June; full-time recruitment (if seats available) August-October
  • Source: BMO Capital Markets Careers

Campus Recruitment (Non-Capital-Markets)

  • Events for 2026: Career Fairs (Feb), CM Campus Recruitment Networking (Feb), Toronto Corporate Tour (Apr), "Launch Your Career in Canada" sessions
  • Key fact: BMO is now posting Summer 2027 IB analyst internships as early as January 2026 ‚Äî over 1.5 years in advance
  • Source: BMO Careers

Scotiabank (Bank of Nova Scotia)

  • Commercial Banking Rotation: $80K-$85K (Glassdoor)

  • GCM Corporate Banking (NYC): USD $95,000/year

CIBC ‚Äî Canadian Imperial Bank of Commerce

CIBC focuses heavily on MBA/Master's programs and has a unique pipeline from co-op to full-time.

Graduate Leadership Development Program (GLDP)

  • Duration: 2 years, four 6-month rotations across revenue-generating, functional, and strategic units
  • Includes: Comprehensive orientations, training, exclusive senior leader events, executive and alumni mentorship
  • Requirements: MBA or Master's graduate (within last 2 years or upcoming), 3+ years work experience
  • Application Timeline: August-September
  • Salary: ~CAD $74,774/year (Indeed estimate)
  • Location: Toronto
  • Source: CIBC Careers, Indeed

Technology Leadership Advancement Program (TLAP)

  • Duration: 2 years, three 8-month rotations across technology groups
  • Rotations: Payments Technology, Cards Technology, Wealth Management Technology, Capital Markets Technology, Personal Banking Technology
  • Requirements: MBA or Master's graduate
  • Application Timeline: August-September
  • Source: CIBC Careers

Technology Graduate Rotational Program (TGRP)

  • Duration: 2-year full-time rotational within technology
  • Unique requirement: Must have completed at least one 4-month CIBC co-op or internship
  • Start Dates: January, May, or September
  • Source: CIBC Careers

Private Wealth Rotational Program (PWRP)

  • Duration: 12 months ‚Äî Private Banking (3 months), Head Office (3 months), Wood Gundy (6 months)
  • Focus: Cash management, credit/lending, investments, wealth planning, estate planning/insurance
  • Requirements: Recent undergrad or MBA in business, economics, or finance
  • Application Timeline: August-October
  • Start Date: June 2026
  • Locations: London ON, Regina, Edmonton, Vancouver
  • Source: CIBC Careers

Quantitative Associate Rotational Program (QRP)

  • Duration: 2 years, four 6-month rotations (Capital Markets, Risk Management, Internal Audit, Model Quantification, Model Validation)
  • Requirements: Master's or PhD in Financial Engineering, Mathematics, Statistics, Physics, or related (2026 graduates). Knowledge of generative AI, LLMs, NLP is an asset.
  • Application Timeline: November
  • Location: Toronto
  • Source: CIBC Careers, Prosple

Capital Markets Salary

  • IB Analyst base CAD $85K-$95K + bonus $25K-$76K = total comp ~$145K
  • CIBC World Markets average (all levels, WSO): $80K base, $35K bonus = $115K total
  • Source: PrepLounge, Wall Street Oasis

National Bank of Canada

Montreal-headquartered National Bank has the highest reported IB analyst total compensation among the Big Six domestics (~CAD $171K).

Corporate and Investment Banking Development Program

  • Structure: Analyst position in CIB. Starts with multi-week technical training (financial modelling, communication, CIB ecosystem familiarization). Ongoing mentorship and career exploration.
  • Location: Montreal (primary), Toronto, Calgary, Vancouver
  • Hiring: Primarily from former interns who have recently graduated
  • Salary: IB Analyst base CAD $95K-$100K + bonus $55K-$94K = total comp ~$171K
  • Source: National Bank Capital Markets, PrepLounge

Capital Markets Sales & Trading Rotation Program

  • Structure: Multi-month program rotating among desks (Equity Capital Markets, Debt Capital Markets, Equity Research, Fixed Income, Institutional Equities, Foreign Exchange, Derivatives) with guidance from a mentor
  • Eligibility: Must have completed a National Bank S&T internship the previous summer + graduated
  • Upon Completion: Transition to permanent desk placement
  • Source: National Bank Capital Markets

Scholarship & Diversity Programs

  • $10,000 scholarship + paid internship + mentorship from senior leader for students from diversity-identified groups
  • Source: National Bank Capital Markets

FAQ

Which Big Five bank has the best new grad program?

RBC has the broadest range (6+ named programs across capital markets, CPA, technology, general management, and diversity initiatives). Scotiabank has the most specialized programs through TILT (9+ programs covering nearly every business line). For capital markets specifically, RBC and TD pay the highest. For pure program breadth and structure, Scotiabank's TILT ecosystem is the most comprehensive. Source: Bank career pages, PrepLounge.

When should I apply for Big Five bank new grad programs?

Most Capital Markets full-time programs fill through intern conversions, so you need to secure a summer internship the year before. IB postings typically open February-March for the following summer. General co-op applications follow the semester cycle: January for summer, May for fall, September for winter. Some programs like CIBC's GLDP open August-September. Source: TD Careers, BMO Capital Markets, CIBC Careers.

What is the salary for a new grad at a Canadian bank?

It depends heavily on division. Capital Markets IB analysts earn $140,000-$200,000 CAD total compensation. General new grad roles (technology, operations, retail) pay $55,000-$90,000 CAD. National Bank reports the highest IB analyst total comp at ~$171,000 CAD. RBC Glassdoor shows ~$64,500 for general new grad roles. Source: PrepLounge, Glassdoor, bank career pages.

Do I need an MBA for Big Five bank new grad programs?

No ‚Äî most analyst-level programs are for undergraduate graduates. However, CIBC's GLDP, TLAP, and QRP specifically target MBA/Master's graduates. RBC's LDP also requires a Master's degree with 3-7 years experience. The standard path is: undergraduate summer internship ‚Üí full-time analyst offer. MBA recruiting exists but is "much less developed" than in the US, per Mergers & Inquisitions. Source: CIBC Careers, RBC Careers, Mergers & Inquisitions.

Which Big Five bank pays the most for investment banking?

RBC Capital Markets pays the highest total compensation among the Big Five at $160,000-$200,000 CAD for first-year analysts. National Bank surprisingly leads among all Big Six domestics at ~$171,000 average total comp. TD Securities is a close second to RBC with $155,000-$190,000, and TD Cowen has been paying "outsized bonuses" post-acquisition. Source: PrepLounge, Wall Street Oasis, eFinancialCareers.

Can I get a full-time offer without doing an internship?

It's possible but much harder. All Big 5 banks state that full-time analyst classes are "primarily comprised of past summer interns." BMO and TD only recruit for remaining seats August-October if available. Your best bet without an internship is to network heavily, attend bank events, and apply during the narrow August-October window. Some banks like CIBC's TGRP explicitly require prior CIBC co-op experience. Source: BMO Capital Markets Careers, TD Careers, CIBC Careers.

Final Advice

  1. Start early ‚Äî IB recruiting now begins 1.5+ years in advance. BMO posted Summer 2027 analyst internships in January 2026
  2. Internship is the path ‚Äî Full-time offers come primarily through intern conversions. Focus on landing the internship first
  3. Division > Bank ‚Äî An IB role at CIBC pays more than a general new grad role at RBC. Choose your division wisely
  4. Network 30-40 times ‚Äî The consensus on Wall Street Oasis is 30-40 coffee chats before applying
  5. Consider TILT ‚Äî Scotiabank's TILT programs offer the most structured rotational experience with executive exposure
  6. Don't overlook National Bank ‚Äî Montreal-based, highest IB total comp among Big Six domestics, smaller class = more deal exposure
