Finance Internship Guide Canada 2026: Salaries, Timelines, and How to Get Hired

February 08, 2026 13 min read

Finance internships in Canada range from $19/hour at a Big Four accounting firm to $50/hour at a top fintech. This guide breaks down every major category ‚Äî what they pay, when to apply, and how to get hired.

1. Investment Banking Summer Analyst Programs

The most competitive and highest-paying finance internships in Canada.

Big Five Banks IB Internship Pay

Bank Summer Analyst Salary (CAD, Annualized) Full-Time Analyst Total Comp Conversion Rate
RBC Capital Markets ~$95,000-$110,000 $160K-$200K High

National Bank ~$95,000-$100,000 ~$171K High

Source: BMO confirmed job posting (2027 IB analyst, $100K), Scotiabank via Prosple ($110K), PrepLounge

Program Details

  • Duration: 16 weeks in Canada (May-August); 10 weeks at US-based banks
  • Training: 1-week orientation + structured training program, then desk placement
  • Daily work: Financial modeling, company valuations (DCF, comps), pitch books, M&A analysis, client presentations
  • Conversion: Full-time analyst classes are "primarily comprised of past summer interns" ‚Äî BMO, TD, and RBC all confirm this on their career pages

Requirements

  • Penultimate-year undergraduate (3rd year of 4-year program) or 1st year MBA
  • GPA: 3.5+ strongly preferred
  • Technical skills: DCF valuation, 3 financial statements, comparable company analysis
  • Behavioral: STAR method answers
  • Superday interviews: 4-6 hours, 4-5 rounds (technical + behavioral + case)

Target Schools

Tier 2 McGill (Desautels), Waterloo (AFM), UofT (Rotman) Strong placement

Application Timeline

Critical insight: IB recruiting has moved dramatically earlier. Some banks now post summer analyst roles 1.5+ years in advance. Students need to start networking in Year 1 of university.

How to Get Hired

  1. Network 30-40 times before applying ‚Äî WSO consensus is 30-40 coffee chats
  2. Start early ‚Äî begin networking 6-12 months before applications open
  3. Come to Toronto in January/February for in-person meetings
  4. Submit within first 2 weeks of the posting opening to signal preparedness
  5. Prep 30+ hours ‚Äî technical (DCF, LBO, comps) + behavioral (STAR method)

2. Commercial Banking Co-op Programs

A more accessible path into banking with less competition than IB.

Salary

  • BMO Commercial Banking: ~$85K annualized (WSO)
  • Scotiabank Commercial Banking: ~$80K annualized (WSO)
  • General Big 5 co-op (non-IB): $20-$30/hour ($42K-$62K annualized)
  • CIBC average intern: ~$52K/year (Indeed); up to $122K for certain CM roles

Available Departments (TD, representative)

Asset Management, Commercial Banking, Corporate Banking, CPA Pre-Approved Audit, CPA Pre-Approved Finance & Accounting, Data & Analytics, Digital Strategy, Financial Planning, Private Banking, Wealth, Risk, Compliance/AML

Tip: RBC offers full-time internships of 4, 8, or 12-16 months. TD and BMO Capital Markets feed into analyst programs through their co-op pipeline.

Source: TD Careers, RBC Careers, Wall Street Oasis

CPA vs. CFA: Which Certification Path?

If you're deciding between accounting and investment management, this comparison matters:

Feature CPA CFA
Focus Auditing, taxation, financial reporting Investment analysis, portfolio management
Cost (CAD) ~$14,000 ~$4,813
Study Time ~400-600 hours total ~900 hours total
Pass Rate ~50% per section ~22-56% per level
Entry Salary (CAD) ~$55K-$65K ~$60K-$70K
Senior Salary (CAD) Higher at senior levels Higher at entry, plateaus
Best Path To Auditor, CFO, Controller, Corporate Finance Portfolio Manager, IB, Research Analyst
Required? Often mandatory for accounting roles Encouraged, not compulsory

Key Canadian insight: CPAs earn more at senior levels. CFAs start ~$5,000/year higher at entry level but the gap reverses over time. Some professionals pursue both.

Bank CPA pathways: TD and CIBC offer "CPA Pre-Approved Audit" and "CPA Pre-Approved Finance & Accounting" co-op streams ‚Äî you earn CPA-qualifying experience during your bank internship.

Source: upGrad, CFA Institute, CPA Canada, Princeton Review

FAQ

What is the highest-paying finance internship in Canada?

Investment banking summer analyst programs at elite boutiques like Evercore pay the most ‚Äî up to $208,000 CAD annualized. Among Canadian banks, Scotiabank GBM pays $110,000 annualized (confirmed via Prosple). Wealthsimple's software engineering internship pays up to $50/hour ($104,000 annualized). The lowest are Big Four accounting internships at $19-$35/hour. Source: PrepLounge, Prosple, Levels.fyi.

When should I start applying for finance internships?

IB recruiting has moved dramatically earlier ‚Äî some banks post summer analyst roles 1.5+ years in advance. BMO posted Summer 2027 internships in January 2026. For general bank co-ops, apply in January for summer, May for fall, September for winter. Big Four accounting recruits mainly in fall (October). Start networking 6-12 months before applications open. Source: BMO Careers, TD Careers.

Do I need to go to a target school for finance internships?

For investment banking, Western (Ivey) and Queen's (Smith) dominate placement. However, Waterloo AFM is rising fast and has mandatory co-op that builds a strong pipeline. For non-IB finance roles (commercial banking, Big Four, insurance), school matters less ‚Äî strong GPA and networking can overcome a non-target background. Source: Mergers & Inquisitions, Wall Street Oasis.

How much do Big Four accounting interns make in Canada?

PwC Toronto pays $32-$35/hour (~$67K annualized). Deloitte Canada pays $25-$32/hour. KPMG Toronto tech interns earn $19-$20/hour. The Canada-wide average accounting intern rate is $18.74/hour (PayScale). Advisory/consulting intern roles pay more than audit/tax. KPMG has a 90% intern-to-full-time conversion rate. Source: Reddit r/Big4, Levels.fyi, PayScale, Becker.

Should I pursue CPA or CFA in Canada?

If you want accounting, audit, CFO track ‚Üí CPA (costs ~$14K, ~50% pass rate). If you want investment management, equity research, IB ‚Üí CFA (costs ~$5K, ~22-56% pass rate). CFAs earn ~$5,000 more at entry level, but CPAs earn more at senior levels. You can do both. TD and CIBC offer CPA Pre-Approved co-op streams so you can earn CPA credit during bank internships. Source: upGrad, CFA Institute, CPA Canada.

Are Canadian pension fund internships worth it?

Yes ‚Äî Canadian pension funds (CPPIB, OTPP, OMERS) are among the most sophisticated institutional investors globally. CPPIB manages $570B+ and competes with Blackstone and KKR for deals. Intern pay ($20-$35/hour) is lower than IB, but full-time Associate compensation ranges from $150K-$350K CAD with better work-life balance than IB. OMERS also offers a dedicated PE internship in NYC. Source: OMERS Careers, Mergers & Inquisitions.

Final Advice

  1. IB pays the most but isn't the only path ‚Äî Fintech and pension funds offer strong compensation with better hours
  2. Start networking in Year 1 ‚Äî IB recruiting is now 1.5+ years ahead; other finance roles are 6-12 months ahead
  3. Internship = full-time offer ‚Äî All major employers convert interns to full-time hires; focus on getting the internship first
  4. Consider certification early ‚Äî Each actuarial exam passed increases salary; CPA co-op streams let you earn credit during internships
  5. Don't sleep on pension funds ‚Äî CPPIB, OTPP, and OMERS offer world-class investing experience unique to Canada
