Canadian Student Job Search Keyword Cheat Sheet

April 26, 2026 4 min read

Canadian student job search is fragmented. The same early-career opportunity can be called an internship, co-op, summer student role, analyst role, associate role, junior role, coordinator role, EIT role, rotational program, or campus hire depending on the employer and field.

Use this cheat sheet to search broader than one title. You can also start from live Hanzilla pages for Canadian internships and co-ops, new grad and entry-level roles, finance roles, engineering roles, business roles, science roles, and tech/data roles.

1. Stage keywords: use more than "internship"

What you mean Keywords to try
Student roles student, summer student, campus, early talent, early careers
Co-op roles co-op, coop, work term, placement, university program
Internship roles intern, internship, summer intern, trainee
New grad roles new grad, graduate program, recent graduate, rotational program, campus hire
Entry-level roles junior, entry level, associate, coordinator, analyst, assistant
Engineering early career EIT, engineer-in-training, field engineer, project coordinator, technical trainee
Science/lab early career lab assistant, research assistant, research technician, quality technician, environmental technician

A lot of Canadian postings that are realistic for students never say "internship" in the title. If you only search one word, you miss banks, insurers, engineering firms, hospitals, labs, utilities, government organizations, startups, and local employers that use different hiring language.

3. Location modifiers that matter in Canada

For students, commute and relocation constraints matter. Pair stage + field + location:

  • Toronto summer student finance analyst
  • Mississauga business analyst intern
  • Waterloo software developer co-op
  • Vancouver engineering intern
  • Burnaby data analyst co-op
  • Montreal lab assistant student
  • Ottawa policy analyst student
  • Calgary junior project coordinator

4. Employer-page searches that work better than generic job boards

Many early-career roles are easiest to find on employer career pages. Try search strings like:

  • site:careers.* "summer student" "Canada" "analyst"
  • site:jobs.* "co-op" "Toronto" "engineering"
  • site:greenhouse.io "Canada" "new grad"
  • site:lever.co "Canada" "intern"
  • "early careers" "Canada" "rotational program"
  • "campus careers" "Canada" "new grad"

Then save companies that repeatedly hire your field. Check them weekly during peak hiring windows.

5. A practical weekly search routine

  1. Check broad live pages: internships/co-ops and new grad/entry-level roles.
  2. Check one field page: finance, engineering, business, sciences, or tech/data.
  3. Check one city page if location matters.
  4. Search 5-10 keyword variations from this guide.
  5. Track saved/applied/interview status in a spreadsheet or the free Hanzilla application tracker.

The goal is not to search everywhere every day. The goal is to avoid missing roles because an employer used "summer student," "analyst," "co-op," "EIT," or "coordinator" instead of your exact target title.
