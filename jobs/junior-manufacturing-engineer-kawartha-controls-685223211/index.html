About the Role

Support CAD/CNC design and process improvements

About Kawartha Controls

Early-career friendly manufacturing company with automation focus

Full Description

Kawartha Controls is an advanced manufacturing company specializing in precision hydraulic manifolds, CNC machining, automation, and AI-assisted manufacturing systems. We are seeking a motivated Junior Manufacturing Engineer to join our engineering and manufacturing team.

This position is supported through a government-funded employment initiative focused on innovation, technology, and early-career technical development.

Position Overview

The successful candidate will support a variety of advanced manufacturing and process improvement initiatives, including:

  • CNC programming validation and process optimization
  • Engineering design and CAD modeling
  • Manufacturing costing and estimating
  • AI-assisted manufacturing and workflow improvements
  • Quality control and production documentation
  • Automation and manufacturing data analysis
  • Development and testing of new manufacturing processes and technologies

The role provides hands-on exposure to advanced manufacturing, automation systems, AI-assisted workflow development, and engineering operations within a high-technology manufacturing environment.

Qualifications

Candidates should possess:

  • A diploma or degree from a recognized post-secondary institution in engineering, engineering technology, manufacturing, automation, robotics, physics, computer science, skilled trades, business, or a related field
  • Strong problem-solving and communication skills
  • Interest in manufacturing, automation, robotics, AI, or process improvement
  • Experience with CAD software, programming, CNC machining, or manufacturing systems is considered an asset

Experience with any of the following would be beneficial:

  • SolidWorks
  • CAM software
  • Python
  • Manufacturing automation
  • CNC machining
  • Data analysis
  • AI-assisted workflow tools

Employment Details

  • Full-time position
  • 40 hours per week
  • Location: Omemee, Ontario
  • Compensation will be based on qualifications and experience

Eligibility Notice

This position is intended to provide early-career experience to recent post-secondary graduates through a Government of Canada employment funding program focused on innovation and technology-related work experience.

Applicants may be required to complete program eligibility documentation as part of the hiring process. Final program eligibility is determined by the program administrator.

Belair Mechatronics Inc. is committed to an inclusive and equitable workplace and welcomes applications from qualified candidates of diverse backgrounds and experiences.

Please submit your resume and a brief cover letter outlining your interest in the role.
