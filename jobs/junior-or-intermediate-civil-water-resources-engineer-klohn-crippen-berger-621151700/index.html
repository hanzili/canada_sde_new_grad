About the Role

Design and analyze water resources civil projects

About Klohn Crippen Berger

Global multidisciplinary civil/water engineering firm

Full Description

As a Junior or Intermediate Civil/Water Resources Engineer , you will have the opportunity to work on small to large scale water resources civil engineering projects from concept through construction and commissioning. You will expand your academic training and professional practice by working along-side senior engineers & senior environmental scientists on water resources and civil engineering projects in western Canada.

KCB is an established, award-winning company with a global influence whose success is based on delivering solutions to the toughest technical challenges facing our clients. You will be part of an award-winning group in Alberta and advance your career in our One Team culture that builds strong, collaborative and focused teams who enjoy themselves throughout project delivery. We value a winning attitude, a hunger for learning and innovation, adaptability and flexibility, respectful engagement with our team and our client representatives, and a sense of fun. We also support career development and diversification.

This role will give you the opportunity to:

  • Work on a variety of projects such as surface water hydrology; numerical flow modeling; river engineering; dams and hydraulic structures; mine water and storm water management systems; geohazard assessment and repair; ditch and culvert design; erosion protection design; flood protection works; and dam-safety related assignments.
  • Broaden your professional practice by working in other engineering disciplines while maintaining your focus on hydrotechnical engineering.
  • Develop your project management skills by working with experienced and skilled senior project managers on sustainable projects.
  • Be involved in all stages of a project, from concept to completion, including: undertaking the design; preparing design reports, drawings, and specifications.
  • Work throughout western Canada with potential opportunities to travel internationally.
  • Work in a dynamic company culture with a diverse, hands-on, small, and hard-working office team that supports career development and diversification.

If you are registered or are eligible to register as an Engineer-In-Training or a Professional Engineer in Canada, have a Bachelor's or Master’s degree in Civil or Water Resources Engineering and a minimum of 2 years work experience in the civil and water resources market sectors, we want to hear from you!

We value your:

  • Work experience on water resources engineering and multi-disciplinary projects;
  • Strong initiative, innovation and commitment to technical excellence as well as your enthusiasm and drive toward long-term success.
  • Strong verbal and written communication skills including experience in contributing to proposals, technical reports and general project correspondence.
  • Exceptional team spirit, interpersonal skills and your ability to build long-term, successful relationships with our clients and project teams.
  • Excellent time management and organizational skills.
  • Attention to detail and analytical skills.

About Us:

Klohn Crippen Berger Ltd. (KCB) is a multi-disciplinary consulting firm with five major business units providing engineering and environmental services in the mining, hydropower, transportation, ports, infrastructure, geotechnical, oil & gas, environmental, and water resources sectors. We offer a full range of services from our offices in North America, Australia, South America, and Europe.

From our offices in Calgary, Edmonton, Saskatoon, York (UK), Naas (Ireland), Sweden, and Dominican Republic our EMIE Group works on challenging projects in the Water, Power and Transportation, Oil Sands, Mining, and Oil and Gas market sectors.

KCB is a platinum member of Canada’s Best Managed companies. We are committed to employment equity and encourage applications from all qualified career seekers. We offer professional career development tracks and salaries commensurate with experience and an attractive benefits package.
