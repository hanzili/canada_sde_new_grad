Full Description

Job Title: Junior Mechanical Engineer (Mechanical Engineering, Automation & Materials Science)

Location

Hand In Hand Technologies

Employment Type

Full-Time | Entry-Level

About Us

HIH is an innovative materials and component repair / engineering organization focused on designing, developing, and optimizing mechanical systems for advanced manufacturing and automation environments. We integrate mechanical engineering principles, smart automation technologies, and cutting-edge materials science to deliver high-performance, reliable solutions across multiple industries.

Position Overview

We are seeking a motivated and detail-oriented Junior Mechanical Engineer to join our engineering team. This entry-level role is ideal for recent graduates or early-career professionals passionate about mechanical component design, automation technologies, and materials engineering.

The successful candidate will support the design, development, testing, and optimization of mechanical components and automated systems while applying material selection and performance analysis principles.

Key Responsibilities

Mechanical Engineering

  • Assist in the design and development of mechanical components and assemblies using CAD software (e.g., SolidWorks, Inventor, CATIA).
  • Create 2D drawings and 3D models in compliance with engineering standards.
  • Perform basic engineering calculations (stress, strain, load, thermal analysis, air flow calculations, brazing calculations, etc.).
  • Work with our ‘Bearing’ design team during development, troubleshooting or failure analysis.
  • Support prototype development, testing, and validation activities.
  • Collaborate with cross-functional teams to ensure design feasibility and manufacturability.

Automation & Mechatronics

  • Support integration of mechanical systems with automation and control systems.
  • Assist in the design and implementation of automated production equipment.
  • Participate in continuous improvement initiatives to enhance system efficiency and reliability.

Materials Science

  • Assist in material selection based on mechanical, thermal, and environmental requirements.
  • Support testing and evaluation of materials (Tungsten Carbide alloys, Silicon Carbide materials, metals, polymers, composites).
  • Analyze material performance, durability, and failure modes.
  • Document findings and contribute to material qualification reports.

Qualifications

Education

  • Bachelor’s degree in Mechanical Engineering or a related field (Materials or Mechanical Engineering Technologist, Mechatronics Engineering).

Technical Skills

  • Basic understanding of mechanical design principles.
  • Familiarity with CAD software.
  • Knowledge of manufacturing processes (machining, welding, forming, additive manufacturing).
  • Understanding of materials properties and material selection criteria.
  • Exposure to automation systems, PLCs, or robotics is a plus.

Soft Skills

  • Strong analytical and problem-solving abilities.
  • Effective communication and teamwork skills.
  • High attention to detail and documentation accuracy.
  • Willingness to learn and adapt in a fast-paced engineering environment.

Preferred Qualifications (Nice to Have)

  • Internship or co-op experience in mechanical design or automation.
  • Experience working in a manufacturing or R&D environment.
  • Familiarity with industry standards and safety regulations.
  • Exposure to lean manufacturing or Six Sigma principles.

What We Offer

  • Mentorship from experienced engineers.
  • Hands-on project experience in automation and materials engineering.
  • Professional development opportunities.
  • Competitive salary and benefits package.
  • A collaborative and innovation-driven work environment.

If you are passionate about mechanical design, automation technologies, and materials innovation, we encourage you to apply and grow your engineering career with us.
