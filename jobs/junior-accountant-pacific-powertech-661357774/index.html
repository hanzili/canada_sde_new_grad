Full Description

About Pacific Powertech

Pacific Powertech Inc. (PPI), established in 1994, is a primary independent electrical testing/commissioning service provider in BC. We offer NETA certified Field Service and Engineering resources at the highest technical level to the power industry.

Our key values are:

  • People - Create a respectful, challenging and rewarding work environment where team members operate with integrity and achieve success in their careers. Inspire our team to believe in themselves and others by approaching challenges with a positive attitude.
  • Safety -Ensure that safety is at the forefront of everything we do by continuously updating our training and developing safe work procedures for every aspect of the work we perform
  • Services - Aspire to be the leading provider of specialized electrical testing and engineering services while remaining trustworthy and dependable to all stakeholders. Respond promptly and efficiently to the challenges and demands of our customers.
  • Community - Be a responsible corporate citizen that gives back to the community and makes a difference by helping support those less fortunate

Our team is rapidly growing and we have an open employment opportunity for accounting support.

We are seeking a detail-oriented and proactive Junior Accountant to join our team. This role involves managing daily accounting tasks. The ideal candidate is a self-starter who thrives in a collaborative environment.

Qualifications

The ideal candidate must have:

  • Minimum 1–2 years of relevant accounting experience
  • Diploma or degree in Accounting, Finance, or related field (or equivalent work experience)
  • Proficiency in accounting software (e.g., SAP Business One, Sage, QuickBooks)
  • Proficient in MS Office package, including Excel, Word, and Outlook
  • Familiarity with payroll is an asset
  • Exceptional attention to detail and organizational skills
  • Excellent interpersonal, oral, and written communication skills
  • Organized self starter, able to adapt and problem solve
  • Strong sense of ownership and accountability with a humble work ethic
  • Ability to prioritize multiple tasks and work independently or collaboratively within a team environment
  • Open to learning new things

Job Description

The duties associated with this role include, but are not limited to:

Bookkeeping

  • Record AP invoices and maintain the accounts payable ledger
  • Record credit card statements and perform credit card reconciliations and resolving identified discrepancies as necessary
  • Communicate with vendors to resolve discrepancies and assist onboard new vendors
  • Periodic review of the Accounts Receivable ledger and collections
  • Review jobs with project managers and invoice jobs: produce AR invoices and supporting customer summaries as needed

Payroll

  • Assist with payroll preparation

Banking

  • Assist with bank deposits and accounts payable transfers

Compliance & Reporting

  • Assist with month-end reconciliations and reports as needed

This role reports to the Controller.

This role requires full-time in-office presence.
