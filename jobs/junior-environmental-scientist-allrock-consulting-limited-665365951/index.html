Full Description

About Us

AllRock Consulting Ltd. is a fast growing and innovative geotechnical, materials and Environmental engineering company. AllRock’s geotechnical engineers, and materials specialists are focused on solving design, construction, and rehabilitation issues impacting the communities in which we work and live. By pairing cutting-edge site investigation technology with tried-and-true experience, we bring unparalleled expertise to the entire geotechnical spectrum—from planning and investigation, to execution and quality control.

AllRock works with clients in the pre-construction phases of a project to discover and examine what lies beneath their construction project. Our team collaborates with clients during the design phases to ensure they have all the geotechnical information needed to complete a successful project. This information allows projects to be completed on time and on budget, by working through problems upfront rather than during construction.

AllRock is proud to say that we are a 100% owned and operated Mi'kmaq First Nations company. The Qalipu Mi'kmaq First Nations & Millbrook Mi'kmaq First Nations are proudly represented through our owners and senior management. We are proud to be registered as a Certified Aboriginal Business with the Canadian Council for Aboriginal Business. Indigenous candidates are strongly encouraged to apply.

The Position - Junior Environmental Scientist

An exciting and unique opportunity to begin your Environmental Career. AllRock is a fast growing Geotechnical/Materials/Environmental Engineering firm that is looking for a Junior Environmental Technologist/EIT to join our multidisciplinary team of professionals. This is a unique opportunity to deliver environmental services on a wide variety of exciting projects across Ontario. We are looking for a candidate to be in a permanent full-time position in our Toronto office.

Job Duties and Responsibilities

  • Conduct Phase I Environmental Site Assessments and Assessments of Past Uses. Completing site visits, interviews, historical and regulatory reviews and recommendations.
  • Conduct field activities for Phase II Environmental Site Assessments and the Excess Soil Regulation. Conducting soil and groundwater sampling, surface water and sediment sampling, and soil tracking.
  • Conduct field activities relating to remedial excavations.
  • Analyze field data, produce tables, figures, and prepare technical reports.
  • Oversee abatement and remediation processes.
  • Comply with applicable Health and Safety procedures and guidelines.
  • Co-ordinate, liaison and build business relationships with laboratories, Clients, sub-contractors and third-party stakeholders.
  • Prepare proposals and cost estimates for projects.
  • Schedule and manage projects, managing scopes of work, budgets and Client expectations.
  • Collaborate with a diverse team in both field work and office environments to achieve project goals.
  • Travel to project sites, which may require overnight trips and flexibility to work outside of regular business hours as needed.
  • Other related duties as required.

Qualifications

  • Bachelor’s Degree or Diploma in Earth or Environmental Science, Civil, Environmental, or Geological Engineering, Hydrogeology or Geology, or equivalent related disciplines. Eligibility for P.Geo. or P.Eng. certification would be considered an asset.
  • A valid driver’s license, insurance and a personal vehicle is required.
  • 0-2 years plus of relevant work experience.
  • Eligible for federal security clearance.
  • Flexibility to travel periodically.

Preferred Skills

  • Experience in environmental monitoring, including species at risk surveys, discharge monitoring, and fish surveys.
  • A solid understanding of geology and the principles of physical and chemical hydrogeology, particularly in relation to contaminated site assessments, delineation, and remediation.
  • Relevant experience in monitoring and sampling surface water and groundwater, borehole drilling, installation of monitoring wells, logging field samples, and preparing borehole logs.
  • Excellent interpersonal, oral and written communication skills.
  • Strong attention to detail and accuracy.
  • Exceptional organizational skills.
  • Data compilation and technical report writing.
  • Work in both a self-lead and team environment, in office and field settings.
  • Knowledge of Ontario Regulation 153/04 and 406/19.
  • Consulting experience with O. Reg. 406/19
  • Proficiency with Microsoft Suite, including Word, Excel and Outlook.
  • Experience with Phase I ESAs would be considered an asset.
  • Experience with invoicing, budgeting, and other areas of project related management would be considered an asset.
  • Auto-CAD or related experience would be considered an asset.
  • gINT or related experience would be considered an asset.
