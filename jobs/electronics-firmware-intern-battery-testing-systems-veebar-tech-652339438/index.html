Role Description

We’re looking for an Electronics & Firmware Intern to help develop a custom battery testing system used to evaluate performance under real-world conditions.

This is a hands-on role focused on building a battery testing platform (not a BMS) — combining electronics, embedded systems, and data logging.

You’ll be working on a system that can:

  • Apply controlled discharge (current/power)
  • Test high discharge rates
  • Log performance data (voltage, current, temperature)
  • Enable repeatable and accurate testing

This position is on-site / hybrid.

What You’ll Work On

  • Designing and building a battery testing jig
  • Implementing controlled load systems (constant current, variable discharge)
  • Working with high-current circuits and power components (MOSFETs, loads)
  • Writing firmware for microcontrollers (STM32, Arduino, ESP32, etc.)
  • Integrating sensors (current, voltage, temperature)
  • Developing data logging systems (SD card, serial, etc.)
  • Debugging both hardware and firmware

Qualifications

  • 3rd or 4th year student in Electrical, Mechatronics, or similar
  • Strong fundamentals in electronics and embedded systems
  • Experience with C/C++ (embedded)
  • Comfortable working with real hardware (not just simulations)

Bonus if you have:

  • Experience with power electronics or high-current systems
  • PCB design experience (KiCad, Altium, etc.)
  • Hands-on projects involving hardware + firmware integration
  • Experience with data logging or testing setups
