The Opportunity

Your Impact Starts Here - As a Junior Vegetation & Wetlands Ecologist, you’ll be part of a collaborative team shaping resilient landscapes across the Prairies and North. Based in Edmonton, you’ll contribute to meaningful environmental work that supports responsible development, ecological stewardship, and future‑focused decision‑making. This is your chance to build technical depth, gain field experience, and help deliver solutions that make a tangible impact on communities and ecosystems. Join a forward‑thinking, high‑performing team now.

Your Impact

  • Identify environmental constraints for proposed developments, including wetlands, vegetation communities, listed species, and invasive/non‑native species.
  • Conduct vegetation and wetland field assessments across diverse sites in accordance with industry standards and regulatory requirements.
  • Support and participate in training of junior field staff, ensuring high‑quality data collection and safety in all field conditions.
  • Plan, scope, and oversee vegetation surveys to ensure robust ecological datasets for complex, multidisciplinary projects.
  • Analyze and interpret ecological data to support project decision‑making and environmental impact assessment processes.
  • Prepare, review, and refine technical deliverables, ensuring accuracy, clarity, and compliance with client and regulatory expectations.
  • Engage with clients, regulators, and internal project teams to align objectives and foster collaborative outcomes.
  • Travel throughout western Canada for field programs, including remote and varied terrain, particularly during summer field seasons.
  • Contribute to project proposals, budgets, and scopes, supporting business development and technical excellence.
  • Champion WSP’s and client HSE programs to ensure safe, responsible execution of all field activities.

The Skills That Set You Apart

  • B.Sc. or M.Sc. in Botany, Biology, Ecology, Environmental Science, or a related field with demonstrated wetlands focus.
  • More than 2 years of plant identification experience across prairie, boreal, and wetland ecosystems.
  • Experience in ecological consulting or equivalent environmental experience.
  • Proven capability in wetland mapping, delineation, and classification (AB/SK wetland systems, aerial imagery interpretation).
  • Familiarity with GIS tools for spatial review, desktop analysis, and ecological mapping.
  • Understanding of environmental regulatory frameworks and guidelines relevant to western Canada.
  • Experience collaborating within multidisciplinary environmental teams.
  • Eligibility or registration with a professional association (e.g., P.Biol., P.Ag., R.T.Biol.) in Alberta or Saskatchewan.
  • A valid Class 5 driver’s licence (non‑GDL).
