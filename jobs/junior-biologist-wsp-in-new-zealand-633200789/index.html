We are WSP. THE OPPORTUNITY Shape the World Around You – Join our Environment team in Fort St. John , where your biological expertise directly supports responsible development across northern BC. Your work will help protect ecosystems, inform major infrastructure planning, and support communities and project partners across the region.

YOUR IMPACT

  • Conduct field programs supporting wildlife, vegetation, wetlands, soils, and ecological assessments.
  • Apply biological expertise to deliver high‑quality technical reports that inform regulatory submissions and client decisions.
  • Lead components of environmental impact assessments aligned with provincial and federal legislation.
  • Collaborate with interdisciplinary teams to produce integrated environmental solutions.
  • Contribute to permitting processes and mitigation planning for resource, infrastructure, and energy projects.
  • Engage with Indigenous communities, land users, and stakeholders to support environmental stewardship.
  • Analyze biological data to assess project effects and provide evidence‑based recommendations.
  • Champion safe work practices and support junior staff through mentorship and guidance.

The Skills That Set You Apart

  • Approximately five years of applied experience in biology, ecology, or environmental science within BC’s regulatory framework.
  • Experience conducting field biology programs in northern or remote environments.
  • Strong working knowledge of provincial and federal environmental legislation and permitting.
  • Proficiency with ecological field methods and tools (habitat assessment, wildlife surveys, wetland classification, GPS/GIS‑enabled data collection).
  • Ability to work safely in rugged outdoor conditions and meet all fieldwork requirements.
  • A mindset rooted in curiosity, environmental stewardship, and meaningful project impact.
