Boardwalk is seeking an enthusiastic and motivated Marketing Assistant (Summer Student) to support our Marketing and Operations teams during the busy summer leasing season. These roles provide hands-on experience in event planning, community engagement, marketing, and brand execution across multiple residential communities. This position will be in-office May through August.

The successful candidate will play a key role in coordinating Resident events, supporting local marketing initiatives, and conducting on-site marketing audits. These positions offer exposure to real-world marketing operations, collaboration with cross-functional teams, and the opportunity to build professional relationships across the organization.

This is an ideal opportunity for a student pursuing a degree or diploma in Marketing, Business, Communications, or a related field.

Responsibilities Event Planning & Execution (Primary Focus)

  • Assist in the planning, coordination, and execution of Resident engagement events and community activations
  • Attend and support on-site events including set-up, tear-down, vendor coordination, and guest interaction
  • Represent Boardwalk at community and sponsorship events
  • Help coordinate Kids Club activities and summer programming
  • Track event attendance and assist with post-event reporting and feedback collection

Site Audits & Brand Compliance (Secondary Focus)

  • Conduct on-site audits at residential communities to review signage, marketing materials, and brand presentation
  • Document findings through photos and reports
  • Identify opportunities for improvement and communicate recommendations to the Marketing team
  • Assist with coordination of signage updates and installations

Collaboration & Relationship Building

  • Work closely with Operations teams (Leasing and Site staff) to support marketing initiatives
  • Build positive working relationships across communities
  • Act as a liaison between Marketing and site teams when coordinating events and materials

Marketing Support

  • Assist with social media content creation and scheduling
  • Support advertising and promotional campaigns as needed
  • Help manage digital assets such as photos and marketing collateral
  • Provide support for regional sponsorship initiatives
  • Assist with administrative tasks such as tracking invoices and organizing marketing materials

Experience & Education Requirements

  • Currently enrolled in a diploma or degree program in Marketing, Business, Communications, or related field
  • Previous customer service, retail, hospitality, or event experience considered an asset
  • Interest in marketing, events, or community engagement

Technical Requirements

  • Proficiency with Microsoft Office (Word, Excel, PowerPoint)
  • Familiarity with Canva preferred
  • Familiarity with social media platforms (Instagram, Facebook, TikTok) preferred
  • Basic content creation skills are an asset

Non-Technical Requirements

  • Strong communication and interpersonal skills
  • Comfortable interacting with Residents and representing Boardwalk at events
  • Highly organized with strong attention to detail
  • Positive and professional attitude
  • Ability to work both independently and collaboratively
  • Ability to manage multiple tasks in a fast-paced environment
  • Willingness to learn and take initiative

Additional Details

  • Full-time hours from May through August
  • Some evening and weekend work required (events)
  • Valid driver’s license required
  • Access to a vehicle required (travel between communities)
  • In-office and on-site work required

What You’ll Gain This role is designed to provide practical marketing experience beyond classroom learning. By the end of the summer, you will have:

  • Hands-on experience planning and executing real events and community activations
  • Exposure to how marketing supports leasing, Resident retention, and occupancy
  • Opportunities to create social media content
  • Experience collaborating with cross-functional teams
  • Insight into branding, property marketing, and local advertising strategy
  • Networking opportunities across multiple communities and departments
  • Mentorship and feedback from an experienced marketing team
