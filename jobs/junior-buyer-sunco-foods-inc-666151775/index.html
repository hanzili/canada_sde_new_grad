About the Role

We are seeking a motivated and detail‑oriented Junior Buyer to join our team. This role is ideal for someone early in their supply chain career who is eager to learn, grow, and contribute to efficient purchasing operations. You will support sourcing activities, manage purchase orders, coordinate with suppliers, and help maintain optimal inventory levels.

This position offers excellent exposure to procurement processes and opportunities for advancement into Buyer and Category Management roles.

Key Responsibilities

  • Prepare, issue, and manage purchase orders, ensuring accuracy and compliance with company policies.
  • Track and expedite orders to ensure timely delivery of goods and services.
  • Maintain accurate purchasing records, supplier data, and delivery schedules.
  • Monitor inventory levels and recommend replenishment strategies.
  • Collaborate with internal teams (operations, finance, engineering, sales) to understand procurement needs.
  • Support supplier relationship management, including performance evaluations and issue resolution.
  • Assist in developing category strategies by gathering spend data and identifying cost‑saving opportunities.
  • Monitor supplier lead times, capacity constraints, and potential supply risks; escalate issues when needed.
  • Coordinate with logistics teams to track shipments and resolve delivery discrepancies.
  • Review invoices and resolve pricing or quantity discrepancies with suppliers and finance.
  • Maintain procurement dashboards, KPIs, and reporting tools.
  • Support implementation of procurement policies, compliance requirements, and process improvements.
  • Assist with new product introductions by sourcing components and obtaining quotes.
  • Maintain item master data, including pricing, product specs, and supplier codes.
  • Track contract expiration dates and assist with renewals or renegotiations.
  • Collaborate with quality teams to address non‑conformances and corrective actions.
  • Provide administrative support such as documentation, filing, and record‑keeping.

Qualifications

  • Bachelor’s degree in Supply Chain Management, Business Administration, Procurement, or related field.
  • 6 months–2 years of experience in purchasing, procurement, or supply chain.
  • Strong analytical, communication, and negotiation skills.
  • Proficiency in Microsoft Office, especially Excel (pivot tables, VLOOKUP/XLOOKUP, data analysis tools).
  • Experience with ERP or procurement software is an asset.
  • High attention to detail and strong organizational skills.
  • Ability to multitask and work in a fast‑paced environment.

Preferred Skills

  • Ability to collaborate effectively with cross‑functional teams.
  • Familiarity with inventory management and supply chain processes.

Pay: From $45,000.00 per year

  • Extended health care
  • Paid time off

Work Location: In person
