Intern | Project Coordinator (12-16 months)

at Cortex Design Inc

📍 Location Toronto, ON M5V 2G5 Remote Hybrid
📋 Details Internship · 12–16 months · $27/hour · Hybrid (Toronto)
📅 Posted Mar 27, 2026

About the Role

Coordinate projects, track milestones, maintain project docs

About Cortex Design Inc

Medtech design firm; ISO 13485 certified

Full Description

Project Coordinator Intern

The Opportunity

Located in Toronto’s design district near King & Spadina, Cortex Design is a full-service product development firm specializing in the design and development of technically complex medtech products.

We’re hiring a Project Coordinator Intern for a 12–16 month internship to help drive structured execution across multidisciplinary medical device programs, where strong coordination improves delivery quality, increases team velocity, and builds trust across functions. This role sits within our Project Management function and helps keep projects organized, visible, and moving. You’ll work closely with Project Managers and cross-functional teams to maintain documentation discipline, support workflow clarity, track milestones, and improve operational visibility across active programs.

This is a strong fit for someone who likes bringing order to complex work, communicates clearly, and notices gaps before they become problems. You won’t own the highest-risk decisions yet, but you will own important parts of execution that help teams stay aligned and help projects stay on track.

This is a hybrid role based in Toronto, with an expectation of approximately 70% in office. This is also a student internship role: we are only considering candidates who are currently enrolled in a post-secondary program, and applicants must be currently enrolled in at least the 3rd year of a Bachelor’s degree in Business, Engineering, Science, or a related discipline.

Compensation: starting at $27/hour, aligned with the Toronto living wage.

What are WE Like?

We’re not your average medtech consultancy.

Cortex is an ISO 13485-certified, design-led firm that lives at the intersection of human-centred design and engineering excellence. We exist to create beautiful, life-changing experiences that substantially improve human quality of life.

We’ve helped clients develop award-winning, emotionally resonant products that span healthcare and consumer markets—and we’re just getting started. Our team is multidisciplinary, collaborative, fast-moving, and deeply invested in client success.

We’re also the only Canadian team ever to win an XPRIZE, and we take pride in blending artistry with technical rigour.

Clients come to us because they want products that are intuitive, beautiful, and engineered with discipline. Our downtown studio houses everything from early research and prototyping to precision assembly, giving us the ability to move fast and deliver with confidence.

If you want an inside look at how we think and work, check out our website, follow us on LinkedIn, or explore our blog.

What You’ll Do

  • Keep project information accurate, current, and usable by maintaining documentation within Cortex’s quality and design control systems.
  • Support project planning and execution by updating task status, milestone tracking, and resource visibility inside our project management tools.
  • Prepare for meetings with discipline, coordinate agendas, and validate AI-generated meeting minutes so teams leave with clear records and next steps.
  • Help build operational visibility by entering, organizing, and synthesizing data across dashboards and internal systems.
  • Track milestones, identify schedule or documentation gaps early, and surface issues to Project Managers before they create downstream risk.
  • Support forecasting and utilization tracking activities that help the team make better resourcing decisions.
  • Contribute to low-complexity internal and client-facing projects under supervision, with increasing responsibility as you build trust.
  • Improve the way work gets done by identifying opportunities to reduce manual reporting, strengthen workflows, and improve coordination across teams.
  • Help Design, Engineering, Sales, and Operations stay aligned by maintaining clear communication and reliable project records.
  • Create the conditions for technical teams to stay focused by owning scheduling, tracking, and documentation logistics with consistency.

What We’re Looking For

Foundational Requirements

We’re looking for people who are curious, motivated, and ready to take initiative when the path is not fully defined. You do not need to have done everything before, but you should show sound judgment, follow-through, and a willingness to step up when the work calls for it.

  • You are currently enrolled in at least the 3rd year of a Bachelor’s degree in Business, Engineering, Science, or a related discipline.
  • You can commit to a 12–16 month internship.
  • You are currently enrolled in a post-secondary program. We are not considering candidates who are not actively enrolled.
  • You write and speak clearly, and you can tailor communication to stakeholders with different levels of technical depth and seniority.
  • You can manage multiple priorities without losing track of details.
  • You know how to break complex workflows into structured, trackable pieces.
  • You are comfortable working with data and spotting process inefficiencies.
  • You can operate effectively in a fast-moving environment where not everything is defined upfront.
  • You take ownership of your work, ask good questions, and follow through.

Stretch Competencies

These are stretch competencies rather than hard requirements. You may have gained exposure to them through previous internships, university extracurriculars, student teams, or coursework.

  • Familiarity with medical device development, design controls, or regulated documentation environments such as ISO 13485.
  • Experience with project or task management platforms such as Wrike, Jira, Asana, or Notion.
  • Exposure to dashboard reporting, workflow automation, or AI-enabled productivity tools.
  • Basic familiarity with automation logic, integrations, or scripting.
  • Exposure to multidisciplinary product development environments spanning hardware, software, prototyping, or systems work.
  • Previous internship, startup, or team-based leadership experience in structured environments such as Formula SAE, robotics teams, or engineering competitions.

How We Work

You’ll do well at Cortex if you can understand what different stakeholders need, work through ambiguity, and improve systems while operating inside constraints. We value people who notice friction, propose better ways of working, and act on them. Strong performers create clarity for others, keep momentum up when priorities shift, and reliably close loops across teams. We also expect strong coordination across disciplines. That means listening carefully, keeping others informed, and making sure important details do not get lost between teams.

This is a coordination role with real ownership, and it requires initiative and follow-through. In a small, high-performing team, strong coordination changes outcomes. The best candidates will treat accuracy, follow-through, and operational clarity as part of how great products get built.

Show Us What You’ve Got!

We care more about proof of execution than broad claims. We especially value applications that include a clear, visual portfolio showing how you organize information, communicate process, and bring structure to complex work. Show us projects, internships, student teams, coursework, or systems you helped run where you improved organization, built clarity, tracked moving parts, supported a team more effectively, solved coordination problems, or made a process measurably better.
