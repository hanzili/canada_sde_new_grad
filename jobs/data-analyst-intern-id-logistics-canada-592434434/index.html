About the Role

About ID Logistics Canada

Global logistics leader with international operations

Full Description

ID Logistics is a leading international contract logistics company with operations in more than 19 countries worldwide providing innovative supply chain solutions for some of the world’s largest retailers and manufacturers. As the first site in Canada, our focus is on operational excellence, safety, and customer satisfaction, with a strong commitment to teamwork, innovation, and continuous improvement.

ID Logistics Canada – XYY1 in Ajax, ON is looking for an ICQA Data Analyst to join our growing team for three (3) months. Reporting to the Stock Manager the ICQA Data Analyst will be responsible for the following:

Responsibilities:

· Collect, clean, and analyze large datasets related to inventory accuracy, cycle counts, quality trends, and operational performance.· Develop daily, weekly, and monthly ICQA reports, dashboards, and KPIs for leadership review.· Identify discrepancies, patterns, and root causes impacting inventory accuracy and quality assurance.· Support cycle count processes by validating results and tracking variances.
· Conduct deep-dive investigations into inventory issues, shrink, and process gaps.
· Partner with Operations Supervisors, Inventory Control, and Quality teams to implement corrective actions.· Monitor key ICQA performance metrics such as accuracy %, defect rate, count compliance, and audit results.· Maintain data integrity through validation, reconciliation, and error-checking processes.
· Present insights and findings to management to support data-driven decision making.
· Support ongoing and ad-hoc ICQA projects during the contract term.

The above responsibilities describe the general nature and level of work being performed. They are not intended to be an exhaustive list of all responsibility, duties and skills required for this position. Additional duties may be assigned as needed.

Qualifications:

· Post‑secondary education in Data Analytics, Data Science, or a related field.

· Strong hands‑on expertise in SQL, with the ability to design and run complex queries for large datasets, optimize performance, and extract data from multiple WMS and inventory management systems.

· Expert‑level proficiency in Excel, including Advanced formulas and functions, Pivot tables and Power Query (mandatory), Development of custom macros to automate reporting and streamline data extraction workflows

· Proven experience with data visualization tools (Power BI mandatory), capable of building interactive dashboards and translating raw data into actionable insights.

· Knowledge of warehouse management systems (WMS) or inventory software, with practical experience in integrating and extracting operational data.

· Strong analytical thinking, attention to detail, and problem‑solving abilities, with a focus on identifying trends and improving process efficiency.

· Excellent communication skills to present complex data insights clearly and concisely to both technical and non‑technical stakeholders.

· Ability to work independently, prioritize tasks, and meet deadlines in a fast‑paced, high‑volume environment.
