Intern, Engineering Systems (Master Data) This is a full-time summer internship lasting from May 11, 2026, until August 28, 2026.

Job Summary The summer intern supporting the Engineering Master Data Team will gain hands-on experience in railway engineering while contributing to meaningful business outcomes. This role focuses on enhancing the accuracy of Engineering Master Data, which drives value through technology and process improvements. The intern will play a key part in identifying, implementing, and supporting both business processes and technological advancements within the Engineering function.

Major Responsibilities

  • Update CN Enterprise Plant Inventory, Systems, Applications and Products (SAP), Geographic Information System (GIS) database, and Enterprise Geographic Information System (EGIS) to reflect changes to assets for CN’s track network
  • Integrate spatial and non-spatial data formats as well as apply quality control procedures to meet the rigorous accuracy and network topology standards required for safe and efficient rail operation
  • Collate and analyze complex data from multiple sources to develop actionable insights
  • Communicate between different engineering disciplines and business teams to acquire spatial data, promote GIS initiatives, and coordinate updates
  • Cooperate and communicate with peers, field personnel, and various departments

Requirements

  • Knowledge of Microsoft Office (Word, Excel, and PowerPoint)
  • Collaborates with others and shares information
  • Applies critical thinking
  • Identifies potential safety and security risks
  • Demonstrates active listening
  • Knowledge of AutoCAD Map 3D and GIS topologies
  • Knowledge of Environmental Systems Research Institute (ESRI), ArcGIS Desktop, ArcMap Spatial Analyst, 3D Analyst, Network Analyst extensions, high precision Global Positioning System (GPS) technologies and post-processing, as well as Commercial Relational Database Management System (RDBMSs) such as Oracle*

Education

  • Working towards a bachelor’s degree in Geography, Civil Engineering, Computer Science, or related field with geospatial emphasis
  • Any experience/education/skills/knowledge for these above would be considered as an asset

About CN CN is a premium railroad that sustainably generates value for our customers, shareholders, employees, and stakeholders with an unwavering commitment to safety and service. Essential to the economy, to the customers, and to the communities it serves, CN safely transports more than 300 million tons of natural resources, manufactured products, and finished goods throughout North America every year. CN's network connects Canada's Eastern and Western coasts with the U.S. South through a 20,000-mile rail network. CN and its affiliates have been contributing to community prosperity and sustainable trade since 1919. CN powers the North American economy and is committed to programs supporting social responsibility and environmental stewardship.

At CN, we are dedicated to building North America's safest , most inclusive and sustainable railroad, which includes reflecting the communities in which we operate.
