Co-op Student, Geology

at Nutrien

📍 Location Saskatoon, SK
📋 Details Co-op · unknown · unknown · unknown
📅 Posted Apr 17, 2026

About the Role

Assist geoscience team with exploration and reporting

About Nutrien

Global potash mining leader with geoscience focus

Full Description

Nutrien is a leading provider of crop inputs and services, and our business results make a positive impact on the world. Our purpose, Feeding the Future, is the reason we come to work each day. We’re guided by our culture of care and our core values: We put safety first. We act with integrity. We are stronger together. We deliver with excellence.

Through the collective expertise of our nearly 26,000 employees, we operate a world-class network of production, distribution, and ag retail facilities. We efficiently serve growers' needs and strive to provide a more profitable, sustainable, and secure future for all stakeholders. Help us raise the expectation of what an agriculture company can be and grow your career with Nutrien.

This position will be posted from April 17, 2026 - April 21, 2026.

What You'll Do:

  • Assist the Nutrien Subsurface Geoscience team with a variety of geological tasks that directly support Nutrien’s potash mining operations.
  • Duties include, but are not limited to:
  • Provide geological support at both the mine and regional scales.
  • Assist with the execution of surface-based potash exploration drill programs.
  • Sample, test, analyze, and report.
  • Wireline log analysis and interpretation (picking geologic markers, etc.).
  • Preparation of technical reports and presentations.
  • Installation and management of surface and underground geotechnical equipment.
  • Assist with geophysical survey acquisition such as ground penetrating radar (GPR), electromagnetics (EM), and seismic.
  • Occasionally work in Nutrien’s underground potash mines.
  • Some travel may be required.
  • Some longer than normal workdays may be required.

What You'll Bring:

  • Completed second or third year of a degree in Geology or Geological Engineering, from an accredited university.
  • Superior interpersonal, organizational, communication, writing, and computer skills will be required.
  • Past industry experience in potash, mining, or oil and gas would be considered an asset but is not necessary.
  • NOTE: Enrollment in a Co-op program is not required.
