About the Role

Develop and support instrumentation and controls at sites

About Imperial Oil

Leading Canadian energy company with strong EIT programs

Full Description

At Imperial, we work every day to responsibly develop Canada’s energy resources – applying technology and innovation to supply affordable, accessible and reliable energy while reducing emissions in support of a net-zero future. Imperial recognizes the important role we can play by advancing climate solutions within our operations and by providing lower life-cycle emission products to our customers. This effort is powered by a unique and diverse workforce fueled by a pride in what we do and what we stand for.

The success of our upstream, downstream and chemical, and corporate divisions is the result of the talent, curiosity and drive of our people. They bring solutions every day to optimize our strategy in energy, chemicals, lubricants and lower-emissions technologies.

Imperial honours diverse backgrounds so we can be a place where people can bring their full selves to work, no matter their identity, race, gender, sexual orientation, age, or ability.

At Imperial, we want you to come for the position and stay for the career. As an integrated company, we are uniquely positioned to offer you a wide range of career prospects and growth opportunities through the course of your career with us. We invite you to bring your ideas and help create sustainable solutions that improve quality of life and meet society’s evolving needs.

Come be part of the Product Solutions Department at Imperial which has more than 140 year history in Canada and a workforce of 5,500+ total employees. Our offices are located in Toronto, ON.

What Role You Will Play In Our Team Imperial is seeking an engineer-in-training (EIT) to provide technical Instrumentation & Controls support to it's Midstream Business Canada Fuels Operations (CFO) at its distribution and pipeline facilities across Canada.

What You Will Do

  • Scope of responsibility

  • Guide development of projects at pipeline and terminal facilities.

  • Review field device specifications for accuracy & applicability
  • Guide development of process control network to ensure compliance with Industrial Control System Requirements
  • Review drawings for compliance with standards and industry best practices
  • Provide I&C support to field operations sites and aid in troubleshooting operational issues (onsite or remotely)

  • Reports to Field Engineering Lead

  • Key accountabilities

  • PLC Programming and maintenance

  • Maintain technical drawings (Network architecture for process control networks)
  • Develop procedures for installing, configuring and calibrating field equipment
  • Obtain & restore back-ups for field devices
  • Review and documented changes to network connections and support the review/approval process

This posting is for an existing vacancy About You Skills and Qualifications

  • Engineering degree from an accredited university with the ability to register as an EIT in Canada.

Preferred Qualifications/ Experience

  • Demonstrated interest in Instrumentation and Controls engineering
  • Basic IT security and controls knowledge
  • Clear communications, interpersonal and issues resolution skills in a diverse team environment
  • Efficient planning, organizational and decision-making skills while influencing various stakeholders
  • Adaptable to changing priorities (may include availability outside of regular working hours/weekends/holidays to solve urgent operational issues)
  • Ability and willingness to travel across Canada for field activities

Start date: May 2026

Location: Toronto, ON
