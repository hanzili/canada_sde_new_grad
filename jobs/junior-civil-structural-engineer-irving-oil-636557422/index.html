About Irving Oil

Full Description

As a Junior Civil/Structural Engineer (Member-In-Training/P.Eng.), you will begin your engineering career in an entry-level, developmental role with a focus on learning to apply theoretical knowledge you gained through school and/or previous experience. Your goal will be to engage with Irving Oil’s community of engineers, designers, purchasers, operations, maintenance and construction professionals, in a collaborative project environment. Your success will be demonstrated in your support of capital improvement initiatives at our operating facilities.

What We Offer

  • Work Environment – Work at our Saint John Home Office location including onsite visits to our operating facilities as required
  • Competitive Compensation – Including an annual bonus plan and pension plan
  • Flexible Benefits Plan – In effect from day one and offers three levels of coverage to select from to meet your unique, personal needs
  • Paid Vacation – Plus an annual option to purchase additional vacation
  • Wellness Support – With an annual wellness allowance, paid personal care days, a 24/7 Employee & Family Assistance Program, and access to our fitness facility
  • Opportunity to give back to some amazing causes in our community – Choose when and where to make an impact with a paid volunteer day, company volunteer opportunities, and a donation matching program

Your Responsibilities

  • Collaborate with multidisciplinary teams to ensure compliance with safety regulations, codes, industry standards and specifications
  • Assist in the design, analysis and review of civil & structural components for capital and expense projects
  • Assist more experienced engineers with complex tasks such as preparing technical drawings, calculations and reports
  • Support the development and continuous improvement of Engineering Services work processes and management system
  • Safely perform field activities at our operating facilities and construction sites as necessary to support the development of assigned tasks

Your Skills

  • Good communication skills, both oral and written
  • Show the ability to work collaboratively and learn from others
  • Must be flexible and able to multi-task effectively
  • Analytical and problem-solving skills
  • Excellent computer skills; should be familiar with common engineering software programs used as well as typical word & database software programs such as Microsoft Word and Excel

Your Experience

  • Bachelor's Degree in Civil Engineering
  • Solid knowledge of core engineering concepts, practices, and procedures, with minimum 1-4 years’ experience
  • Proficiency with structural analysis software is an asset
  • Must be registered as a member-in-training or Professional Engineer with APEGNB, or other Canadian Provincial Engineering Association
