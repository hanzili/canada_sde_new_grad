About New View Society

Non-profit; community-focused; offers growth opportunities

Full Description

The Organization:

New View Society is a community mental health non-profit based in Port Coquitlam, BC. We pride ourselves on a positive workplace culture and a valued reputation in our community. We are known for our great care towards clients, employees, and community alike, through our core values: integrity, respect, inclusiveness, caring and trust.

We believe in creating an environment for growth and recovery for adults challenged by mental illness through:

  • Supporting an individual’s choice to live, work, learn, and be part of their community.
  • Promoting recovery in a friendly, safe, and supportive environment for individuals affected by mental illness in the Tri-Cities.
  • Being a flexible and caring Society dedicated to mental wellness.
  • Providing housing, employment, social, and personal growth opportunities.

Why Should You Apply?

We offer:

  • Flexible working hours.
  • Opportunities for professional development (we are committed to staff growth).
  • Positive workplace culture.

The Position: We’re looking for a F/T Temporary Jr. Bookkeeper to join our Admin team through the Canada Summer Jobs Program. It would be 35 hours a week for 8 weeks. The position is for a detail-oriented and motivated junior bookkeeper to support our accounting department and the Accounting Assistant. You will be maintaining accurate financial records and day-to-day bookkeeping tasks. The wage starts at $19.25 per hour.

Job Summary:

Reports to Executive Director and Accounting Assistant:

  • Must be proficient in QuickBooks Online
  • Duties may include word processing, data entry, creating spreadsheets and presentations, and scanning and filing.
  • Extensive software skills, experience with the Microsoft suite (specifically Excel), QuickBooks desktop, internet research abilities, and strong communication skills are required.
  • The junior bookkeeper will oversee recording day-to-day financial records, such as accounts payable, receivable, payroll, and bank reconciliations, maintain and update the general ledger, process invoices, and receipts.
  • You will be supporting the Accounting Assistant in monthly, quarterly, and annual financial reports. Additionally, you will be supporting the Accounting Assistant in reconciling bank reports and ensuring compliance with financial policies and procedures.
  • All administrative tasks require high sensitivity to the vulnerable populations that we serve, and the confidentiality of data moving within our organization.

Requirements:

  • Vulnerable population criminal record clearance
  • Ability to make administrative/procedural decisions and judgments.
  • Skilled in time management and the ability to multitask and prioritize work.
  • Attention to detail and problem-solving skills.
  • Must have excellent written, verbal, and interpersonal communication skills.
  • Must have the ability to work with co-workers and be team-oriented.
  • Must be comfortable working in an environment with individuals with mental illness.
  • Must be a Canadian citizen, permanent resident, or person who has been granted refugee status in Canada
  • The Canada summer jobs program states that you must be a student between the ages of 15 and 30, and be a student or recent graduate.

Wage: $19.25 per hour; on June 1st, it will become $19.65.

Job Types: Full-time, Fixed term contract
Contract length: 8 weeks

Expected hours: 35 per week

Benefits:

  • Casual dress
  • Flexible schedule
  • On-site parking
