About the Role

Lead site investigations and remediation field work

About Stantec

Global environmental consulting leader with strong mentorship

Full Description

Grounded in safety, quality, and ethics, our experts lead their fields with dedication, a creative spirit, and a vision for growth. We draw from more than 20 technical specialties worldwide and are devoted to fostering a community of diverse talents, backgrounds, and expertise. Here, you can apply your passion and collaborate with top environmental professionals on work that’s vital to our clients and the communities they serve.

Join a team that’s naturally committed to the environment

Your Opportunity

Stantec is currently seeking a Junior Environmental Scientist or EIT to join our Site Investigation, Remediation & Response (SIR) team in British Columbia. This is an exceptional opportunity for an early career professional who wants hands on field experience, exposure to real world contaminated sites projects, and the chance to grow alongside experienced practitioners in a highly respected consulting environment.

In this role, you’ll be directly involved in site investigations and remediation programs across a broad range of sectors, including provincial and federal agencies, ports and harbours, transportation infrastructure, and industrial and commercial developments.

You’ll work closely with intermediate and senior staff in both the field and office, gaining practical experience while building the technical foundation needed for a successful career in contaminated sites practice. From day one, you’ll be trusted with meaningful responsibility and supported through mentorship, collaboration, and on‑the‑job learning.

This Role Is Well Suited To Someone Who

  • Enjoys working outdoors in dynamic environments
  • Is eager to learn and take initiative
  • Values teamwork and safety
  • Wants to build long‑term technical depth in environmental consulting

Your Key Responsibilities

As part of the SIR team, your work will include a diverse mix of field and office responsibilities, such as:

  • Supporting and implementing Phase I, II, and III ESAs, Stage 1 PSI, Stage 2 PSI/DSI, and remediation programs
  • Conducting soil, soil vapour, sediment, groundwater, and surface water sampling
  • Developing site‑specific health and safety plans and helping lead field programs with safety as the top priority
  • Producing clear, accurate field notes and documentation that meet data quality objectives
  • Supervising and coordinating with subcontractors (e.g., drillers, hydrovac operators, utility locators, heavy equipment operators)
  • Assisting with BC Contaminated Sites Regulation applications, including Approvals in Principle and Certificates of Compliance
  • Managing and organizing field data using specialized equipment and software
  • Supporting project managers with scopes, budgets, schedules, and field logistics
  • Collaborating with multidisciplinary teams across Stantec Canada, including geotechnical, water resources, atmospheric sciences, and indoor environments groups
  • Actively contributing to—and strengthening—a strong health and safety culture

You’ll be exposed to a wide range of site conditions, regulatory frameworks, and technical challenges—accelerating your learning curve and professional development.

Your Capabilities and Credentials

You’re an enthusiastic early‑career environmental professional who’s ready to learn, contribute, and grow. You bring curiosity, professionalism, and a strong work ethic to everything you do.

You’ll Thrive In This Role If You Have

  • A minimum of 2 years of relevant experience, ideally within British Columbia
  • Previous experience in contaminated sites investigation (a strong asset)
  • A collaborative mindset and comfort working with senior and intermediate staff
  • Strong written and verbal communication skills
  • A genuine interest in field work, with flexibility to travel for assignments
  • The ability to work independently and confidently with subcontractors
  • An understanding of provincial and federal environmental regulations and guidelines
  • Eligibility for, or progress toward, a professional designation (e.g., P.Eng., P.Geo., P.Ag.) is considered an asset

Education and Experience

  • A diploma or degree from a technical college or university in Environmental Science, Geology, Hydrogeology, or Engineering
  • Previous experience in contaminated sites investigation is a strong asset
  • Possession of, or eligibility for, a professional designation (e.g., P.Eng., P.Geo., P.Ag.) is considered an asset

About Stantec Stantec is a global leader in sustainable engineering, architecture, and environmental consulting. The diverse perspectives of our partners and interested parties drive us to think beyond what’s previously been done on critical issues like climate change, digital transformation, and future-proofing our cities and infrastructure. We innovate at the intersection of community, creativity, and client relationships to advance communities everywhere, so that together we can redefine what’s possible.
