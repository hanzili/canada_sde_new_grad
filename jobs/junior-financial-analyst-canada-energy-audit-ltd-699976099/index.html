Full Description

About CEA

Canada Energy Audit Ltd. (CEA) supports energy-efficiency initiatives and provides energy services for residential and commercial clients. We operate in a fast-paced environment where accuracy, structure, and timely reporting are essential.

Role Summary

We are hiring a Junior Financial Analyst to support day-to-day finance operations and reporting. This entry-level role is ideal for someone with strong Excel skills, solid accounting fundamentals, and high attention to detail. You’ll support transaction review, reconciliations, month-end close tasks, and recurring reporting, including exposure to multi-currency (CAD/USD) activity.

Responsibilities

1) Reporting & Analysis (Junior level)

  • Prepare and maintain recurring weekly/monthly reports and KPI trackers in Excel using accounting exports and internal schedules
  • Support basic variance tracking and investigate data inconsistencies for review
  • Maintain trackers for revenue, vendor spend, purchasing/sales summaries, and margin support schedules (as assigned)

2) Month-End Close Support

  • Assist with month-end close using established templates and checklists
  • Prepare supporting schedules (e.g., accrual support, prepaid/expense analysis, clearing account support)
  • Perform basic balance sheet reconciliations and follow up on outstanding items

3) Transaction Processing & Documentation

  • Organize and validate supporting documents for key transactions (invoices, receipts, approvals, purchasing records)
  • Support consistent coding/classification to maintain clean financial records
  • Coordinate with internal teams to obtain missing documentation and clarify transaction details

4) Multi-Currency Support (CAD/USD)

  • Support tracking and documentation of multi-currency transactions (CAD/USD) and related schedules
  • Assist with basic reconciliation support for multi-currency payments/receipts and ensure documentation is complete
  • Maintain FX support schedules (e.g., rate trackers) as assigned

5) Process Improvement & Data Quality

  • Help improve reporting templates and standardize recurring trackers
  • Identify process gaps and propose practical improvements (checklists, standardized naming, automation opportunities)
  • Assist with documenting finance procedures and maintaining organized records

Qualifications

  • 0–2 years of finance/accounting experience (co-op/internship experience counts)
  • Strong Excel skills: pivot tables, XLOOKUP/VLOOKUP, SUMIFS; comfortable working with structured templates
  • Basic accounting knowledge (GL, reconciliations, accrual concepts, balance sheet vs P&L)
  • Strong attention to detail and ability to manage time-sensitive, recurring tasks
  • Clear communication skills and ability to follow structured processes

Assets (Nice-to-Have)

  • Experience with QuickBooks Online (QBO) or similar accounting software (asset; training available for strong candidates)
  • Exposure to multi-currency transactions and/or purchasing/sales workflows
  • Interest in pursuing CPA (or related professional development)

What We Offer

  • Stable full-time role with hands-on training and mentorship
  • Exposure to real-world finance operations, reporting, and multi-currency workflows
  • Growth opportunities within a collaborative team environment

Pay: $40,000.00-$45,000.00 per year

Benefits:

  • Casual dress
  • On-site gym
  • On-site parking
  • Paid time off
  • Wellness program

Work Location: In person
