Student Project Coordinator (Kakabeka Falls)

Where Greatness Grows

The greatest achievements in history are borne from the greatness within people – where human potential meets vision, and passion fuels evolution. Unlocking this potential is the most important thing we do at Bird.

As a leader in Canadian construction for over 100 years, the impact of our team is etched deeply within the core of our legacy. Beyond Bird, this impact is felt in the fundamental aspects of our everyday lives. From the critical infrastructure we depend on, to the energy and resources that keep us moving - we are powering our communities and shaping Canada’s skylines coast-to-coast. Entrenched in the foundation of a culture built more than a century ago is an enduring quest to reimagine what is possible. Our impact is greater than ever, and we are looking for those who seek to redefine their story.

We are a dynamic team of over 5,000 with a range of professions, backgrounds, and areas of expertise. This breadth of diversity in people and opportunities is one of greatest aspects of building your career with Bird. Every trajectory looks different. At Bird, you hold the pen, and you write your story. As you unlock your potential, you are surrounded by a team that supports you every step of the way.

Be a part of our team, where we pride ourselves on the quality of our work and the way we treat each other and our partners. You will build a career and long-lasting relationships based on respect, collaboration, and a solution-focused mindset. Bird is a place where you will unlock your potential and achieve your goals.

Our internships and Co-ops are designed to provide you with a business relevant project that will allow you to independently manage your own area of responsibility, while receiving support from an assigned Supervisor and Technical Mentor. We’ll help you discover your strengths and development areas in a challenging, fun, and supportive work environment.

Projects are scoped in alignment with your term length and typically focus on a current construction project.

We are seeking a student to join us for a 4 month co-op position starting in May 2026. This position will be based out of Thunder Bay.

What You’ll Be Working On

Project Coordination:

  • Coordinate drawings and work specific scope/trades.
  • Review trade quotes, verify material quantities, hours, and rates.
  • Participate in project team meetings.
  • Conduct quality walks and update deficiency logs.
  • Assist with LEED submittals and reviews
  • Issue warranties.
  • Take and coordinate progress photos.
  • Update field drawings and track the production of as-built drawings.

Field Coordination:

  • Perform quantity takeoffs.
  • Coordinate sub-trade work
  • Assist with Site Logistics.
  • Verify site conditions and coordinate with drawings.
  • Set up and maintain record keeping.

Scheduling:

  • Update project schedules.
  • Create manual schedules and update schedules
  • Obtain scheduling information from sub-trades.
  • Create and maintain graphical scheduling
  • Monitor manpower productivity.

This job description outlines the general nature and level of work performed by the Student Project Coordinator. It is not an exhaustive list of all responsibilities and duties required for the role, and it may vary depending on assignment. Bird may modify or add to the job duties and qualifications as needed.
