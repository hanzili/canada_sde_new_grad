Full Description

Position: NOHFC Contract – Human Resources Internship

Location: 8 Herrick Street, Phase I Supportive Treatment Program

Duration: 52-Weeks Hours: 35-Hours per Week Compensation: $18.50 - $22.50 per hour

NOHFC Candidate Criteria (Mandatory):

  • Candidate must be a new entrant into the workforce, are transitioning to a new career, or the unemployed or underemployed who are entering a new field.
  • Candidate must have not previously participated in an NOHFC funded internship in the 2020 Mandate.
  • Candidate must be 18 years of age or older.
  • Candidate must be legally eligible to work in Canada.
  • Once hired, the candidate must reside in the Northern Ontario community in which they are employed.

About Us: The Ken Brown Recovery Home is an 18-bed supportive residential treatment program for men who are looking to recover from substance use disorder. We are a 90-day program that implements the philosophy of 12-Step Recovery to help clients see their futures with a sense of hope. Our services/programs include life skills, group therapy, anger awareness, relapse prevention, one-on-one counselling, and case management. We are currently seeking a detail-oriented and experienced Contract Human Resources Specialist to help us update, revise, and create human resources and operational policies that align with our values and legal requirements. This is an exciting partnership opportunity made possible by the support of the Northern Ontario Heritage Fund Corporation (NOHFC). Your role as an NOHFC Intern will have a meaningful impact on a renowned communal organization while supporting the staff, Board of Directors, and stakeholders of the Ken Brown Recovery Home.

Position Overview:

The role requires strong knowledge of HR best practices, Federal/Provincial/Municipal labor laws, and non-profit sector nuances, as well as the ability to create clear, effective, and accessible policies for a diverse workforce.
Over the duration/term of contract, the HR Specialist will be expected to work closely with the Executive Director and the Board of Directors to develop, revise, and update all human resources and operational policies of the organization for the purposes of ensuring that the Ken Brown Recovery Home is relevant as well as compliant with all federal, provincial, and municipal laws and/or policies and/or ethical standards with a particular focus on areas related to Equity, Diversity, and Inclusivity.

Key Responsibilities:

Policy Development & Updates:

  • Review and update existing HR and operational policies including but not limited to employee handbooks, compensation, benefits, leave, equity, diversity, inclusivity, harassment, abuse, and violence in the workplace, health and safety, performance management and workplace conduct.
  • Research and develop new policies as needed to ensure compliance with applicable laws and human services best practices.
  • Collaborate with organizational leaders to ensure policies reflect the organization’s mission, values, and culture.

Documentation & Communication:

  • Ensure policies are clearly written, accessible, and understandable to all staff.
  • Develop templates and guidelines for consistent policy implementation, enforcement, and revision.
  • Provide guidance and support to the Executive Director and Board of Directors on the practical application of organizational policies.

Compliance & Best Practices:

  • Stay up to date with federal, provincial, and municipal labor laws, regulations, policies, and standards of practice that impact the organization.
  • Ensure all policies are legally compliant and reflect current best practices in HR, human services, and nonprofit management.
  • Advise on potential risk areas and provide solutions to mitigate organizational liabilities.

Qualifications:

  • Proven experience in Human Resources, with a focus on policy development and legal compliance (preferably in a non-profit organization).
  • Strong knowledge of HR best practices, employment law, and organizational development.
  • Experience writing clear, concise, and legally sound policies.
  • Ability to work independently and manage multiple projects simultaneously.
  • Excellent communication and interpersonal skills.
  • Strong attention to detail and organizational skills.
  • A commitment to equity, diversity, and inclusion, with an understanding of how to apply these principles in HR practices

Preferred Qualifications:

  • University Degree or College Diploma in Human Resources; working towards CHRP
  • Experience in the non-profit sector or working with organizations that serve diverse populations.
  • Familiarity with HR software and systems, particularly in small or medium-sized organizations.

To Apply: Please submit your resume, a cover letter detailing your relevant experience, and any relevant work samples or portfolio (e.g., policy writing samples) to executivedirector@kenbrownrecoveryhome.org. Applications will be reviewed on a rolling basis, and early applications are encouraged.

The Ken Brown Recovery Home is an equal opportunity employer and is committed to diversity and inclusion. We will accommodate your needs under the Ontario Human Rights Code, and we will fairly hire the best candidates based on merit.
