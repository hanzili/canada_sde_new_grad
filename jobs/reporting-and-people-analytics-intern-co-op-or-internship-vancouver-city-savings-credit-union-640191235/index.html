About the Role

About Vancouver City Savings Credit Union

Community-focused credit union with strong social impact

Full Description

Our Story & Purpose:

Vancity is a member-owned credit union built on the principles of inclusion, social justice, and community impact. Since 1946, we’ve worked to challenge the status quo, break down barriers, and build a more equitable future. With bold commitments—including becoming net-zero by 2040 across all mortgages and loans—we are championing financial resilience, climate action, and belonging for our members and communities.

As the largest private sector Living Wage Employer in Canada and home to a team of 2,300 passionate employees, Vancity offers competitive rewards and benefits, diverse career pathways, and the opportunity to be part of something bigger.

The Opportunity:

We’re seeking an intern to join our HRIS & Analytics team for a four‑month opportunity, supporting internal People & Culture initiatives through employee data, reporting, and analytics. This team plays a key role in ensuring workforce reports and dashboards are accurate, relevant, and efficient, helping leaders make informed, data‑driven decisions.

This role will report to the Manager, HRIS and Analytics and offers hands‑on exposure to people data, reporting improvement work, and the foundations of people analytics within a values‑driven financial institution.

A typical day would involve:

  • Support the refresh and improvement of core People & Culture reports and dashboards—helping ensure they are accurate, relevant, efficient, and easy for stakeholders to use.
  • Help respond to ad‑hoc people data questions by preparing clean, well‑structured outputs and clear summaries (e.g., for audits, benchmarking requests, and others).
  • Assist with data quality checks and troubleshooting—spotting inconsistencies, coordinating fixes, and improving the integrity of employee data used for reporting.
  • Collaborate with partners across People & Culture to translate business questions into reporting requirements and help shape dashboards that tell a clear story.
  • Contribute to internal projects that strengthen our people data foundations—such as data definitions, metric/report inventory work, and data governance efforts.
  • Document processes and create lightweight guidance (job aids / quick references) to support consistent reporting, repeatable refresh cycles, and self‑serve use where appropriate.

You are:

  • Customer-Centric - You define and measure success through the eyes of your internal customers and anticipate, understand, and respond to their evolving needs. Demonstrate strong follow-through: Consistently keep commitments to customers and team members.
  • Results-Oriented - You have a demonstrated ability to engage stakeholders to elicit requirements gathering, analyze findings, generate appropriate solutions, and build deliverables all while securing team's commitment to these solutions.
  • A Critical Thinker - You seek to facilitate data-driven decision making, including "rolling up your sleeves" to elicit requirements, research, analyze data
  • A Problem Solver - Your skills to diagnose challenges and issues, develop innovative business solutions, and assess risks and opportunities that may affect the business, are showcased on a daily basis.
  • Curious & innovative - You always do your research and analyze it to assess and recommend a course of action considering industry best practices
  • A team player - you can engage with members of your team as well as cross-functional teams and are willing to collaborate across projects and initiatives.

You have:

  • Current enrollment in an undergraduate or postgraduate program within an applicable field and eligible for co-op/internship opportunities
  • An interest in data analysis, analytics and financial services
  • A willingness to work in a highly flexible environment with multiple competing priorities.
  • Excellent communication and interpersonal skills
  • A high level of attention to detail

Bonus points, if you have:

  • Experience (coursework or projects) using Power BI (visuals, data modeling)for analysis.
  • Experience with SQL, Python, and/or R (even at a beginner level).
  • Familiarity with Human Resources data sets
  • Prior coursework or experience in analytics, data science
  • Comfort handling sensitive information with discretion and following data governance/privacy expectations
