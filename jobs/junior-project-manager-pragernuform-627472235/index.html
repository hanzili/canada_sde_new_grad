About the Role

Coordinate projects, schedules, and production logistics

About Pragernuform

Legacy Canadian manufacturer of retail displays.

Full Description

Overview

At PragerNuform Inc., we transform ideas into reality. Come join our creative, diverse and collaborate team. Since 1938, we are the leading manufacturer and distributor of retail displays and store fixtures in Canada, known for serving thousands of retailers, our innovative custom solutions are in a store near you.

As part of our team, you will join a culture that values a healthy work environment that encourages the support of your success. Our focus on people and the environment is at the heart of what we do. Due to significant growth and opportunity, we are seeking a Junior Project Manager. This is a new position.

Position Summary

  • Review customer requests, drawings, and estimates for completeness and consistency before releasing projects.
  • Organize and maintain project information, schedules, and milestones.
  • Initiate projects by releasing work to production and issuing purchase orders to vendors.
  • Coordinate design changes and ensure updates are documented with the design department.
  • Support accuracy and quality by coordinating required sign-offs at key stages.
  • Communicate status updates with internal teams and external stakeholders.
  • Track labour, materials, vendor progress, and report any variances.
  • Escalate issues to operations or sales when risks or conflicts arise.
  • Coordinate logistics, deliveries, and customer communication during production.
  • Complete project close-out by confirming all materials, labor, and POs are captured.
  • Provide feedback and document lessons learned for continuous improvement.
  • Participate in weekly project status meetings and monthly project management meetings.
  • Engage in health and safety training to support a safe work environment.
  • Seek learning opportunities to gain exposure to all four divisions that make up the organization.

Requirements

  • Post-secondary education in a related field or equivalent experience.
  • 1‚Äì2 years of experience in project coordination, production coordination, or a similar role.
  • Ability to read and interpret basic technical drawings and shop drawings.
  • Understanding of manufacturing processes such as wood, metal, finishing, and assembly.
  • Familiarity with quotes, bill of materials (BOMs), and production specifications
  • Experience managing schedules, tasks, and project deliverables.
  • Proficiency with Microsoft Office, ERP, and MRP systems.
  • Proactive, detail-oriented, and comfortable taking direction.
