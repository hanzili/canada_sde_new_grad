Junior Biologist

About the Role

Conduct field surveys, collect data, support reporting

About R. Radloff & Associates Inc

Regulatory-focused environmental consulting practice

Full Description

Job Summary
R. Radloff & Associates Inc. is seeking a Junior Biologist or Biologist-in-Training (B.I.T.) to support environmental monitoring, field surveys, and regulatory compliance activities on infrastructure and community development projects across British Columbia.

Working under the guidance of senior environmental professionals and in collaboration with engineers and project managers, the successful candidate will assist with environmental fieldwork, data collection, technical reporting, and construction monitoring. This position provides an excellent opportunity for early-career professionals to gain experience in applied environmental consulting while contributing to projects that support communities and environmental stewardship.

Responsibilities

Fieldwork & Environmental Monitoring

  • Conduct field surveys and environmental monitoring activities, which may include habitat assessments, vegetation surveys, invasive species surveys, benthic sampling, and construction environmental monitoring.
  • Assist with implementation of environmental management plans, including Erosion and Sediment Control (ESC) measures and Construction Environmental Management Plans (CEMPs).
  • Fieldwork/data collection for Environmental Site Assessments (ESAs)
  • Collect field data, maintain accurate monitoring records, and document environmental conditions through notes, photographs, and sampling where required.
  • Participate in site visits and field programs across Northern British Columbia and other project locations as required.

Project Support & Technical Reporting

  • Compile field data and assist with data analysis and interpretation.
  • Support preparation of environmental reports, monitoring summaries, and regulatory documentation.
  • Assist with environmental overview assessments and other environmental deliverables under the supervision of senior staff.
  • Maintain organized project documentation and assist with quality control of environmental records.

Collaboration & Professional Development

  • Work collaboratively with project managers, engineers, environmental professionals, and field staff to support project delivery.
  • Participate in internal training and mentorship opportunities to support professional growth and development.
  • Contribute to a positive, team-oriented work environment that values learning, collaboration, and continuous improvement.

Education & Experience Requirements

Required

  • Bachelor’s degree in Biology, Environmental Science, or a related discipline.
  • Registered as a Biologist-in-Training (B.I.T.), or eligible for registration, with the BC College of Applied Biology.
  • Familiarity with environmental regulations and protocols in British Columbia or Canada.
  • Valid Class 5 driver’s license and reliable access to a vehicle.
  • Strong organizational and time-management skills.
  • Strong written and verbal communication skills in English, including: the ability to prepare clear technical reports; and, communicate environmental requirements effectively with project teams, clients, and regulators.
  • Ability to work independently and collaboratively in both office and field environments.Preferred / Assets
  • Previous experience conducting environmental field surveys or construction environmental monitoring.
  • Experience supporting environmental assessments or regulatory submissions.
  • Familiarity with infrastructure projects such as water, wastewater, transportation, or community development projects.
  • Experience working with Indigenous communities or supporting projects involving Indigenous consultation processes.

Working Conditions

  • This role includes outdoor fieldwork in varying weather conditions and may require travel to project sites across British Columbia.
  • Occasional overtime or extended field days may be required depending on project schedules.
