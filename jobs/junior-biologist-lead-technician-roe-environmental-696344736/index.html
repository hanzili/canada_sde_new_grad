About Roe Environmental

Environmental consulting firm focused on field services

Full Description

POSITION SUMMARY

We are seeking a motivated Junior Biologist or Lead Technician to deliver high-quality environmental consulting and biological services. This role will require field and office work, effective communication with team members and clients, and support for environmental assessments, permitting, and compliance monitoring.

Key responsibilities include:

  • Assist with the delivery of environmental consulting services in a professional and ethical manner.
  • Following safety plans, protocols, and procedures while concentrating on pre-task work analysis and the continuous safety of you, your coworkers, and any other people who could be nearby while we are providing our services.
  • C reate a culture of safety adhering to Roe's policies and plans, and the requirements of the worksites in which we work.
  • Conduct routine environmental monitoring, including erosion and sediment control and construction compliance monitoring (observation, field note recording, photo-documentation, and reporting).
  • Conduct wildlife and plant surveys and monitoring.
  • Conduct water quality and soil sampling and interpret results under the direction of a Managing Biologist.
  • Preparation of technical reports and permit applications. Note: mentoring to support permit application and development of reporting skills will be provided.
  • Various administrative duties.
  • Ability to be available on a rotational basis after-hours and weekend for participation in Roe's Environmental Response Call-out Program – Details to follow.
  • Be available to work outside of normal working hours and on weekends, during busy seasons.
  • Demonstrate and maintain a high level of physical fitness to support the physical demands of the role, including prolonged periods of standing, walking distances of up to 5 km over uneven, slippery and/or rough terrain without rest breaks, frequent bending, and the safe lifting and carrying of items weighing up to 20 kg.
  • In addition to the above listed primary duties and responsibilities, other duties may be assigned from time to time by Roe’s management team, and/or Board of Directors.

POSITION TERM

Full-time with 6-month onboarding period. Note that available hours may fluctuate with seasonal demands. It is expected work weeks will require 30 – 40 hours late fall through early spring (November – March) and 40 – 60 hours late spring through early fall (April – October). This position features a flexible, variable schedule with changing days and hours each week, including potential shifts on mornings, evenings, weekends, and holidays depending on operational needs.

POSITION LOCATION

  • Office location in North Vancouver, BC with projects primarily in Metro Vancouver, Fraser Valley and Sea-to-Sky Corridor, with additional opportunities throughout the South Coast region and Vancouver Island.
  • North Vancouver office will be the primary place of work.
  • We generally offer a flexible working environment – the need to be in the office or ability to work from a home office is highly dependent on a combination of personal preference, task and administrative requirements, and the preference of your respective project-specific supervisor. Functionality and efficiency are the mutual objectives that we will strive for. You will be provided laptop, with remote access to our server so that you can sometimes work at your preferred location.

REQUIREMENTS

  • B.IT or R.B.Tech designation or eligibility within one year required. Must maintain good standing with all professional or 'in-training' designations, meeting ongoing professional development requirements.
  • Ability to work remotely approximately 7 days per month and be available frequently for after-hours and weekend call-out for environmental response. Please indicate interest in longer stretches of remote or camp work in your application email.
  • Valid Class 5 Driver’s License and clean Driver’s Abstract. Transportation to the office is required to access equipment and fleet vehicles.
  • Ability to work independently and as part of a group in both remote and urban settings.
  • Electrofishing: Theory, Safety and Application Certification

PREFERRED QUALIFICATIONS

  • Erosion and Sediment Control Experience/Courses.
  • Level 1 First Aid.
  • Strong interpersonal skills for client and contractor communications.
  • Proficiency in environmental report writing, including CEMP and EIA documentation.
  • Experience with AutoCAD and/or GIS software applications.
  • One to three years experience.

COMPENSATION

Competitive wage based on experience and education and courses - $64,480 to - $74,880. Overtime per provincial standards.

BENEFITS

  • Extended health/dental (after 6-month onboarding period) and paid vacation.
  • Professional Dues: Compensation for select professional dues (e.g., CAB membership) will be covered by Roe to a certain extent – details to follow.
  • Professional Development: $1000/year self-directed professional development allotment, with eligibility after the six-month onboarding period – details to follow.

HOW TO APPLY

Email a cover letter and resume to hiring@roe-env.ca
