Full Description

We are looking for Software Quality Assurance Co-op students to support our QA team in ensuring the highest standards of software quality. The position is focused exclusively on Manual QA tasks, and does not involve coding responsibilities. As a co-op, you will be responsible for testing software applications to identify defects and ensure the functionality meets the requirements and expectations.

Key Responsibilities

  • Conduct manual testing of software applications to ensure functionality, performance, and usability meet the required standards.
  • Create, execute, and document test cases and test plans based on project specifications and requirements.
  • Identify, report, and track defects in the software using our defect tracking system.
  • Collaborate with developers, project managers, and other QA team members to ensure a smooth and efficient testing process.
  • Participate in team meetings and provide input on quality assurance processes and practices.
  • Assist in the preparation of test data and perform regression testing as needed.
  • Verify that fixes are correctly implemented by retesting issues and ensuring resolution.
  • Maintain thorough documentation for testing activities and results.

Requirements

  • Must be a Canadian Permanent Resident (PR) or Citizen to apply.
  • Enrollment in a recognized post-secondary program (e.g., Computer Science, Information Technology, Engineering).
  • Strong attention to detail and a passion for ensuring quality in software products.
  • Excellent written and verbal communication skills.
  • Ability to work well both independently and in a team-oriented environment.
  • Prior experience in manual software testing or quality assurance (preferred but not required).
  • Familiarity with software development lifecycle (SDLC) and testing methodologies is an asset.
  • Ability to learn quickly and adapt to new tools and technologies.
  • A proactive, problem-solving mindset with a keen interest in software quality.

Perks

  • Gain hands-on experience in software testing and quality assurance processes.
  • Work in a collaborative and innovative environment with experienced professionals.
  • Opportunity to contribute to impactful projects and improve software quality.
  • Hybrid work model (2 days a week in the office).

How We Work

You will be expected to work in a remote/hybrid environment reporting to our Kanata, Ontario location 2 or more times per week.
