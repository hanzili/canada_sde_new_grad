About Chu Cho Environmental (CCE)

Founded in 2013, Chu Cho Environmental (CCE) is a 100% Tsay Keh Dene Nation–owned company, established under the Nation’s Economic Development Corporation. As an Indigenous-owned business, CCE reflects the values of the Nation and delivers high-quality environmental services rooted in respect for community and territory.

As a for-profit social enterprise, CCE creates employment, builds local capacity, and supports economic growth for Tsay Keh Dene Nation. Our mission is to build a resilient, skilled, and multidisciplinary team that delivers professional environmental services across northern British Columbia.

CCE provides a wide range of environmental services, including baseline studies, regulatory support, environmental research, and long-term monitoring for resource development projects. Our multidisciplinary team brings expertise in fisheries, wildlife, botany, aquatics, atmospheric science, reclamation, and GIS.

You can learn about some of our projects here: https://www.youtube.com/channel/UCAlYv6ShGBnlHuSY0bMEonQ

Position Summary

Chu Cho Environmental is an Indigenous-owned environmental consulting company firm seeking Junior Biologists to support our northern British Columbia field teams. The role is designed for individuals early in their careers who are eager to learn, gain hands on experience, contribute meaningfully in the field, and support environmental work related to environmental monitoring, fisheries, wildlife, and applied science.

The Junior Biologists will work under the guidance of Intermediate and Senior Biologists, contributing to field programs through accurate field data collection and consistent application of safety protocols.

Key Responsibilities

Fieldwork Support

  • Support field programs related to wildlife, fisheries, water quality, vegetation, and habitat assessments
  • Collect field data following established protocols and safety procedures
  • Prepare , use, and maintain field equipment in accordance with project and safety requirements
  • Work in remote locations and variable weather conditions

Data & Reporting Support

  • Record, organize, and manage field data accurately
  • Assist with data entry, summaries, and basic analysis
  • Support preparation of field notes, daily field reports and technical documentation

Team & Community Engagement

  • Work respectfully with colleagues, clients, and community members
  • Conduct work in a manner that reflects the values and priorities of Tsay Keh Dene Nation
  • Support relationship building through respectful conduct and clear communication during field activities

Health & Safety

  • Follow CCE’s health and safety management systems
  • Participate in safety meetings, tailgate meetings, and training
  • Identify and report hazards or unsafe conditions

Qualifications

Education & Experience

  • Completion of, or current enrollment in, Bachelor’s degree program in biology, environmental science, natural resources, or a related field (required)
  • Field experience gained through school programs, summer work, internships, or volunteer roles (asset)
  • Willingness and ability to work outdoors in remote environments (required)

Skills & Attributes

  • Strong attention to detail and willingness to learn
  • Ability to work effectively as part of a team
  • Good communication and organizational skills
  • Physically fit and comfortable working outdoors in all weather conditions
  • Valid Class 5 driver’s license (preferred)

Assets (Not Required)

  • Exposure to wildlife habitat assessments and pre-disturbance wildlife surveys
  • Exposure to fisheries or aquatic field work
  • Familiarity with vegetation identification or soil classification
  • Certifications such as Swiftwater Rescue, electrofishing certification, or wilderness first aid
  • Familiarity with northern BC ecosystems

Working Conditions

  • Combination of field-based work and limited office duties
  • Extended field days and remote work may be required during peak seasons

Compensation & Benefits

  • Competitive entry-level wage of $30 – $34/hr, commensurate with experience
  • Training, mentorship, and skill development opportunities
  • Opportunity to build experience with an Indigenous-owned environmental consulting firm
  • Meaningful work contributing to environmental stewardship in northern British Columbia
