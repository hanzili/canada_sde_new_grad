About the Role

Perform laboratory testing on sugar products

About Redpath Sugar Ltd

Global sugar refining leader, part of ASR Group

Full Description

Redpath Sugar is part of the ASR Group, the world’s largest refiner and marketer of cane sugar, with an annual production capacity of more than 6 million tons of sugar.

The Redpath Belleville plant is a blending and packaging operation producing a variety of sugar containing products for customers in Canada and the U.S. The plant operates state of the art high speed mixing and packaging systems supported by high level safety, quality and logistics systems.

We are seeking individuals who enjoy working in a fast-paced manufacturing operation and have excellent communication skills, a drive for results and have the energy and vision to recognize and implement improvement opportunities both independently and as part of a team with members of all levels of an organization.

Overview The Quality Assurance Laboratory Technician Student is responsible for effectively planning and coordinating their time to ensure accurate analysis of all materials. Through monitoring and auditing of all production areas the lab technician assists production to maintain ASR-Redpath Sugar Ltd. Quality and Food Safety Standards.

Duties

  • Actively participates in Redpath’s safety goals and objectives
  • Interacts and participates in departmental safety meetings and activities
  • Performs timely analysis
  • Provides feedback on analyses
  • Provides testing support in process troubleshooting
  • Actively participates in maintaining HACCP plan and GMPs
  • Identifies areas requiring attention and suggests the appropriate follow-up action
  • Maintains healthy working relationships with co-workers
  • Complete laboratory testing in accordance with ISO 17025 standards

Responsibility

  • Various testing on granulated sugar containing products, raw materials, in-process, and finished product samples, other samples as assigned
  • Laboratory equipment verification
  • Sample collection
  • Documentation and analysis of results
  • Filing various paperwork
  • Maintains and troubleshoots testing equipment
  • Assist in troubleshooting of process, packaging and maintenance systems
  • Assists with HACCP and GMPs
  • Other tasks as identified

Relationships/Interdepartment Liaisons

  • Interacts with process and packaging supervision on paperwork issues, sampling issues.
  • Communicates to process and packaging supervision quality issues with finished products.
  • Interacts with QA team members to ensure the performance of various testing equipment
  • Provides feedback on trends to quality management
  • Interacts with process and packaging teams to maintain the integrity of HACCP plans and GMPs
  • Works closely with other laboratory staff in order to maintain efficient testing, clean working environment

Experience EDUCATION AND/OR EXPERIENCE REQUIRED :

  • To be considered for this position, you must be at least 18 years of age and MUST be returning to school in the fall. Returning students are required to complete a new application.
  • Demonstrates good laboratory skills and techniques.
  • HACCP training and experience in maintaining food safety programs beneficial
  • Must be capable of troubleshooting and resolving issues in fast-paced manufacturing environment. Knowledge and proficient computer skills in standard Microsoft applications (Word, Excel, Access, Visio)
  • Familiarity with SAP a definite bonus.

Education

  • Enrollment in a College or university program related to chemistry, food science (or equivalent) focus.

What We Offer

  • The wage for this position is $22.88 / hour.
  • This position has shift premiums and is overtime pay eligible
  • This is a vacant seasonal contract position

This is a seasonal position from approximately May 4, 2026 to August 28, 2026. All applicants must be enrolled and/or returning to a post-secondary institution in September 2026. This seasonal position is also available for students completing co-op terms.

Job applicants must already be legally eligible to work for Redpath Sugar Ltd. in a full-time permanent role. The company does not provide immigration sponsorship or support for this role.

For more than 100 years, our employees have taken pride in “Making Life a Little Sweeter” by providing our customers and consumers with quality service and sugar products. Our commitment to that principle drives us in all that we do and encourages us to uphold our company’s high ethical standards of business conduct to ensure our continued success.
