Full Description

Embedded Systems Intern (4-Month)

The Opportunity

Located in Toronto’s design district near King & Spadina, Cortex Design is a full-service product development firm specializing in the design and engineering of technically complex medtech products.

We’re hiring an Embedded Systems Intern for a 4-month paid internship to help strengthen the foundation of how we design hardware. To be considered, you must be currently enrolled in a post-secondary program for the full duration of the internship. This role sits within our Embedded Systems team and focuses on further enhancing and scaling our component library in Altium Designer, aligning with IPC best practices to support efficient design workflows, minimize review iterations, and strengthen the quality and traceability of our electronics development across projects.

This is not a passive support role. You’ll take ownership of a system the team will rely on long after your internship ends: schematic symbols, PCB footprints, metadata structure, reusable design blocks, and the documentation that makes the library maintainable. You’ll work closely with experienced engineers, but the value of this role comes from your judgment, precision, and ability to turn messy technical information into clean, reusable infrastructure.

This role will appeal to someone who likes building order out of complexity, cares about getting details right, and wants their work to improve how an entire team operates. We’re looking for a high-output student with the academic horsepower, technical range, and work ethic to earn trust quickly in a demanding engineering environment.

This is a hybrid role with an expectation of roughly 70% in-office time in our Toronto studio.

Compensation: this role starts at $27/hour, aligned with the Toronto living wage.

What are WE Like?

We’re not your average medtech consultancy.

Cortex is an ISO 13485-certified, design-led firm that lives at the intersection of human-centred design and engineering excellence. We exist to create beautiful, life-changing experiences that substantially improve human quality of life.

We’ve helped clients develop award-winning, emotionally resonant products that span healthcare and consumer markets—and we’re just getting started. Our team is multidisciplinary, collaborative, fast-moving, and deeply invested in client success.

We’re also the only Canadian team ever to win an XPRIZE, and we take pride in blending artistry with technical rigour.

Clients come to us because they want products that are intuitive, beautiful, and engineered with discipline. Our downtown studio houses everything from early research and prototyping to precision assembly, giving us the ability to move fast and deliver with confidence.

If you want an inside look at how we think and work, check out our website, follow us on LinkedIn, or explore our blog.

What You’ll Do

You’ll own a high-leverage internal systems project that improves how we design and ship electronics.

You will:

  • Build and organize a scalable component library in Altium Designer, including schematic symbols, PCB footprints, parameter structures, and component metadata
  • Audit the existing library, remove obsolete or low-quality entries, and improve consistency across parts, naming, and documentation
  • Create and validate IPC-aligned footprints using datasheets, mechanical drawings, manufacturer recommendations, and engineering review
  • Populate and maintain component metadata such as lifecycle status, supplier information, datasheets, compliance details, and alternates
  • Source modern replacement components and add high-value new parts that expand the team’s usable library
  • Develop reusable design blocks from past projects such as power rails, MCU support circuits, interfaces, and protection architectures
  • Improve manufacturing output templates and library conventions so designs move to fabrication with fewer preventable errors
  • Support embedded quality documentation where needed, including templates, work instructions, and internal process material
  • Contribute to PCB design work when appropriate to your skill level, especially where library development can accelerate concept prototypes or internal hardware tools

Success in this role looks like a library the team trusts, documentation others can follow, and cleaner downstream execution in schematic design, layout, review, manufacturing prep, and bring-up.

What We’re Looking For

Foundational Requirements

  • Currently enrolled in Electrical Engineering, Computer Engineering, or a closely related post-secondary program, and eligible to complete a full 4-month internship
  • Strong academic performance in a rigorous engineering program
  • Coursework, design team experience, personal projects, or co-op work that shows hands-on exposure to schematic capture and PCB layout
  • Demonstrated familiarity or hands-on experience with Altium Designer is required
  • Ability to read datasheets, package drawings, and manufacturer application information carefully
  • Strong attention to detail and a habit of checking your work before handing it off
  • Clear written and verbal communication
  • Comfort working in a team where feedback is direct, expectations are high, and the pace is fast

Additional Strengths

  • Strong evidence of technical execution through projects, design teams, internships, research labs, or personal builds
  • Experience creating schematic symbols, PCB footprints, or organized CAD libraries
  • Exposure to component selection, distributor research, or comparing alternates
  • Familiarity with embedded hardware building blocks such as regulators, MCUs, interfaces, sensors, connectors, and protection circuits
  • Some programming experience in C, C++, or Python
  • Exposure to IPC standards, footprint best practices, design-for-manufacturing, BOMs, or engineering documentation
  • Evidence of self-directed technical learning and the ability to drive open-ended work forward

How We Work

You’ll do well here if you know how to work carefully without moving slowly.

We expect interns to understand the context behind the work, not just execute steps. In this role, that means understanding how a footprint choice affects layout quality, review effort, manufacturing risk, and downstream debugging. You should be comfortable making progress before every variable is perfectly defined, asking good questions when something is unclear, and improving the system as you learn.

You’ll collaborate with embedded engineers and adjacent disciplines, take reviews seriously, and follow through on details. We value people who notice gaps, propose better ways of working, and leave things in better condition than they found them.

Show us What You’ve Built.

For this role, student work counts. We want to see the strongest examples of how you think and execute, whether they came from coursework, a design team, a capstone, a co-op, a research lab, or a personal project.

That might include:

  • PCB designs or hardware projects you completed or contributed to
  • Schematic symbols, footprints, libraries, or reusable design blocks you created
  • Embedded projects that show sound technical decisions and attention to detail
  • Documentation, test plans, or design notes that make your thinking clear

You do not need a polished portfolio website to be considered. We care more about evidence of strong fundamentals, curiosity, technical judgment, and follow-through. If your best work lives in a PDF, Git repo, slide deck, lab report, or project folder, send that.
