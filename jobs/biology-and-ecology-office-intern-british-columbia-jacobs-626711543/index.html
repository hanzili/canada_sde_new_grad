About the Role

About Jacobs

Global engineering firm with multidisciplinary environment teams

Full Description

At Jacobs, we're challenging today to reinvent tomorrow by solving the world's most critical problems for thriving cities, resilient environments, mission-critical outcomes, operational advancement, scientific discovery and cutting-edge manufacturing, turning abstract ideas into realities that transform the world for good.

Your impact

At Jacobs, we’re transforming intangible ideas into innovative solutions designing the future - today. As an Environmental Science Field Intern based out of the Lower Mainland Region– you will too!

You'll impact the world around you by helping us solve real-life challenges. YOU are the future of our company. We’ll rely on you to provide support to our teams on critical projects while fostering a culture of continuous learning as you bring fresh ideas to the table. You’ll work with a knowledgeable and multi-disciplinary collaborative team as you support projects through field data collection efforts, data management, and assisting with a variety of technical deliverables for privately-owned infrastructure projects. This is a field-heavy position and is anticipated to be comprised of 80% travel (up to 18 days at a time) and 20% office work.

Pave the way for your career with a company that inspires and empowers you to deliver your best work so you can evolve, grow and succeed – today and into tomorrow.

Here's what you'll need

Must be in pursuit of a related college or university program (e.g. biology, environmental science, ecology, botany, planning, GIS)

  • Must be returning to school in the Fall
  • Willingness to work in the field in remote locations for extended periods of time as required.
  • Working knowledge of Microsoft Office programs
  • Must hold a valid driver’s license in Canada
  • Clean Driver’s abstract

Ideally, you’ll also have

  • Minimum 3.0 GPA
  • Interest and prior experience conducting fieldwork (e.g. through coursework or previous internships)
  • Field identification skills related to wildlife, wetlands, and/or aquatics.
  • Excellent oral and written communication skills.
  • Self-driven time management and organizational skills
  • Ability to effectively interface with all levels of personnel and management.
  • Ability to work and thrive in a team environment.
  • Dedication to safety.
  • Strong problem-solving skills.
  • Positive attitude and ability to self-motivate and work productively with minimal supervision.
