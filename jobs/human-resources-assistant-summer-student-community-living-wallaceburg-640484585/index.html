About Community Living Wallaceburg

Community living nonprofit supporting adults with disabilities

Full Description

POSITION SUMMARY

Reporting to the Director, People & Culture, the Human Resources Assistant will be responsible for providing comprehensive administrative support to the Human Resources department. This role includes handling day-to-day HR tasks, maintaining employee records, assisting with the recruitment processes, and ensuring compliance with company policies and procedures.

RESPONSIBILITIES

Scheduling Support:

  • Update HRIS for schedule changes including processing time-off requests (TOR) or any other matter that will alter the schedule after the schedule has been posted for union staff.
  • Schedule internal training and, supervisor/employee 1:1’s for new hires, Team meetings, and other meetings
  • Maintain HRIS including optimizing the HRIS capabilities
  • Perform other duties as required and assigned

Administrative Support:

  • Assist with the day-to-day operations of the HR functions and duties
  • Assist staff with IT support for Inclusion, Surge, Emails
  • Provide clerical and administrative support to Director, People & Culture
  • Maintain employee file organization
  • Update locations access (Teams, emails, Surge)
  • Participate on various committees and take minutes as required
  • Coverage for Administrative staff (including but not limited to reception duties)

Recruitment and Onboarding:

  • Assist in the recruitment process by posting job ads, organizing resumes, pre-screening and scheduling interviews
  • Conduct initial screenings, coordinate background checks, and reference checks
  • Compose offer letter for new hires
  • Assist in the onboarding process of new hires, including preparing new employee files, facilitate orientation, create ID badges

Compliance and Records Management:

  • Ensure compliance with the labour laws and regulations
  • Maintain accurate and up to date employee records and documentation
  • Perform fit tests on staff

Training and Development:

  • Schedule new hire and refresher training
  • Track employee training progress and maintain training records

QUALIFICATIONS

Experience:

  • While prior professional experience in HR is not required, candidates with any of the following experiences will be considered an asset:
  • Previous internship or volunteer roles in administrative, office support, or customer service position.
  • Involvement in student organizations, events, or clubs, particularly in leadership and/or community
  • Experience with data entry, document management, or handling confidential information
  • Any exposure to recruitment processes, onboarding, or employee relations

Education:

  • Human Resources Diploma, Business Administration, or a related field preferred

Knowledge/Skills:

  • Analytical and problem-solving skills
  • Exceptional time / priority management; ability to multi-task and change priorities quickly and with ease
  • Proficient in data entry and keyboarding
  • Professional, responsive and positive work attitude
  • Ability to communicate efficiently and professionally in writing and via the telephone
  • Well-developed professional ethics
  • Focus and attention to detail
  • A clear understanding of confidentiality
  • Understanding of Agency policies and how to respond when violations are noticed
  • Practical knowledge and application of Microsoft Office 365 and an aptitude to learn and utilize other software programs as required (i.e. AIMS, Inclusion)
