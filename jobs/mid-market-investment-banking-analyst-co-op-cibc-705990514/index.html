About the Role

Assist in M&A modeling and deal prep

About CIBC

Top Canadian bank with strong M&A presence

Full Description

We’re building a relationship-oriented bank for the modern world. We need talented, passionate professionals who are dedicated to doing what’s right for our clients.

At CIBC, we embrace your strengths and your ambitions, so you are empowered at work. Our team members have what they need to make a meaningful impact and are truly valued for who they are and what they contribute.

To learn more about CIBC, please visit CIBC.com

What You’ll Be Doing The Mid-Market Investment Banking group is a stand-alone team within Commercial Banking focused on serving the specific M&A advisory needs of companies with enterprise values ranging from $10 million to $150 million. With offices in Toronto, Montreal, Vancouver, and Calgary, Edmonton & Halifax, Mid-Market Investment Banking is one of the largest M&A groups dedicated to privately-owned companies of all Canadian banks, and is ranked among the top mid-market advisors in Canada for the last 10 years as measured by aggregate transaction volume of M&A deals up to $150 million. As a Co-op Analyst, you will assist Analysts, Associates and senior bankers in executing all facets of the group’s mandates. This will include researching industries, potential buyers and public valuation metrics; working on in-depth financial models of acquisitions and recapitalizations; assisting in the preparation of extensive written documents including business development proposals and confidential information memorandums. Co-op Analysts will get tremendous exposure to experienced senior bankers and 1 on 1 mentoring from their colleagues through their 4 month work term.

At CIBC we enable the work environment most optimal for you to thrive in your role. Details on your work arrangement (proportion of on-site and remote work) will be discussed at the time of your interview. Important information

  • You must be currently enrolled in post-secondary education and returning to full-time studies to be eligible. However, you do not need to be in a registered Co-op program to be considered for a role.
  • Recruitment timeline: Applications review – June/July; Interviews – June/July; Offers – July.
  • Please include your resume (no more than 2 pages), a cover letter (no more than 1 page), and your most recent unofficial transcript with your application. These should all be uploaded into the Resume section of the application as one document.
  • You have a strong knowledge of MS Office - Word, Excel, PowerPoint, Data Creation.
  • Work Location: 81 Bay St. Toronto, ON. All CIBC Co-op positions require you to be based in Canada for the duration of the term.
  • Duration of term: 4 months (September 2026 – December 2026).
  • Please note, we have multiple positions available under this posting and you may be considered by more than one hiring team.

How You’ll Succeed

  • Problem solving - you take initiative to develop creative solutions. You engage with team members and business partners to achieve common goals.
  • Strong Communication skills - You have well developed interpersonal skills. You are able to take complex concepts and present them in easy to understand written and graphical formats, utilizing spreadsheets, presentation software and web tools. You have strong verbal and presentation skills.
  • Adaptive - You are able to work independently with guidance from management and also in a dynamic team environment.
  • Cross Collaboration - You are able to work collaboratively with business stakeholders from various different teams to gather requirements and develop a deeper understanding of the bank’s processes.

Who You Are

  • You act like an owner. You thrive when you're empowered to take the lead, go above and beyond, and deliver results.
  • You put our clients first. You engage with purpose to find the right solutions. You go the extra mile, because it's the right thing to do.
  • You're driven by collective success. You know that collaboration can transform a good idea into a great one. You understand the power of an inclusive team that enjoys working together to bring a shared vision to life.
  • Values matter to you. You bring your real self to work and you live our values – trust, teamwork and accountability.

What CIBC Offers At CIBC, your goals are a priority. We start with your strengths and ambitions as an employee and strive to create opportunities to tap into your potential.

  • Subject to program terms and conditions
