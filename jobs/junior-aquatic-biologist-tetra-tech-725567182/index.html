About the Role

Perform aquatic sampling, analyze data, generate reports

About Tetra Tech

Global environmental consulting firm; field-based environmental work

Full Description

The Opportunity Tetra Tech is adding a Junior Aquatic Biologist to our Environment & Water – Biological Sciences team based in Nanaimo, BC. Why Tetra Tech At Tetra Tech, we are Leading with Science™ to solve the world’s most complex challenges. Our industry-leading experts in engineering and consulting are committed to driving positive change in communities around the world. For over 50 years, we have been at the forefront of innovation and sustainability. Today we stand as a market leader, offering cutting-edge solutions in water, environment, energy, and international development. Our work has improved more than 500 million lives around the world.

Your Impact Join Tetra Tech to make a real difference. Our work leverages cutting-edge technologies, advanced analytics, and the expertise of world-class scientists and engineers to create meaningful change around the world. Discover your full potential – join us to advance your career while leaving a lasting legacy.

The salary range for this position is $58,000 - $73,000 per year. Please note that Tetra Tech considers various factors in determining pay, including but not limited to: education, relevant and demonstrated work experience in the role, skills, geographic location, qualifications, certifications, and other competencies relevant to the needs of the business.

Your Role

  • Conducting construction Environmental Monitoring, including erosion and sediment control planning and monitoring, water quality sampling and data analysis.
  • Conducting biophysical assessments, focusing on fisheries and aquatic habitat, riparian habitat and vegetation, and/or wildlife and wildlife habitat, under the guidance of intermediate and senior biologists.
  • Complete post-construction monitoring for regulatory compliance.
  • Prepare field sampling permit applications for fish and wildlife salvages, and complete post-salvage reports.
  • Conduct water and/soil sampling and analysis according to provincial protocols.
  • Working closely with a multi-disciplinary team of professionals and technical experts in biology and environmental sciences.
  • Working with Project Managers/Discipline Teams to maintain quality control of data.
  • Data analysis and technical reporting (proficiency in Microsoft Word, Excel, Outlook).
  • Equipment calibration and maintenance.
  • Working outside in varied weather conditions.
  • Willingness to work away from home for extended periods.
  • Excellent attention to detail, and ability to work accurately and quickly to meet deadlines.
  • Be punctual and able to start work early in the morning.
  • Health and Safety for work at the project site.
  • Willingness to work independently and in a team environment.
  • Conduct activities in line with internal procedures, legislation, and industry standards.
  • Pursue continuous professional development and maintain a high degree of discipline knowledge and awareness.
  • Work in a safe manner at all times and report all health and safety incidents and concerns.
  • Additional duties as required.

Qualifications

  • Minimum of a Technical Diploma or a B.Sc. in biology or ecology, or related fields.
  • Eligibility for registration or currently registered with the College of Applied Biology as a Biologist or Technologist in Training.
  • Interest and aptitude for environmental monitoring and/or erosion and sediment control.
  • Successful applicant must have worked a minimum of 1 to 3 years in the environmental sciences including environmental monitoring, aquatic and riparian surveys, surface water quality sampling, fish and wildlife salvage.
  • Proficient technical writer.
  • A strong track record in Health and Safety management on projects.
  • Knowledge of BC environmental regulations.
  • Must possess a valid Class 5 Driver’s License and must provide a current drivers abstract.
  • Must be physically capable of working with heavy equipment and sensitive instruments in variable weather and river conditions.
  • Ability to exercise good judgment and discretion, practicing safe work methods.
  • Strong interpersonal skills, as well as strong written and verbal communication.

Life At Tetra Tech The perks of working at Tetra Tech include (if applicable):

  • Comprehensive and market-competitive benefits, available on day one of employment.
  • Overtime is calculated at 1.5 times.
  • We have embraced a hybrid culture. We will provide you the tools to work almost anywhere.
  • Flexibility and company-wide commitment to work/life balance.
  • Collaborative team atmosphere that values the contributions of all employees.
  • Learning and development opportunities for ongoing professional growth. Mentorship with world leading experts in your field.
