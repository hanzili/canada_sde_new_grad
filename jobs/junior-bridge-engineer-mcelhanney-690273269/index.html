Full Description

About McElhanney

Based in Western Canada for over 110 years, McElhanney offers innovative and practical engineering, surveying, mapping, planning, landscape architecture, and environmental services. As a 100% employee-owned and operated firm, with over 1,400 staff members in 31 locations, we are proud to offer qualified staff with shareholding options in our company. At McElhanney, we work in teams to build strong connections with our staff, clients, and local communities. Our employees enjoy working here because of our varied projects, high levels of autonomy, opportunities for career development, excellent total compensation and benefits package, and collaborating with a team of great professionals.

The Opportunity

McElhanney has an exciting opportunity for Junior Engineer to join our Bridge team in Edmonton. This will be an entry level bridge engineering role based out of Edmonton and working with our growing Alberta Bridge Group. Our project portfolio includes bridge culverts and standard bridges through to major interchange and river crossings and includes multiple award-winning projects in BC and Alberta. An example is the Stoney Trail Bridge Twinning involving bridge construction engineering support for a segmentally-launched, balanced, cantilevered 470 m-long bridge over the Bow River.

We foster a collaborative culture where mentorship, professional development, and leadership growth are prioritized. With access to industry experts and structured programs, McElhanney provides a positive and supportive environment where both careers and individuals thrive.

What You’ll Do

  • Assist with bridge inspections, assessments, and data collection.
  • Support the development of conceptual and structural designs for various bridge types and transportation structures.
  • Conduct hydrotechnical analyses of watercourse crossings and corresponding drainage basins using approved Alberta Transportation and Economic Corridors (TEC) methodologies.
  • Prepare design and contract cost estimates.
  • Coordinate with sub-consultants and stakeholders.
  • Assist in preparing and securing approval of tender and contract documents.
  • Support field reviews, construction submittals, and contract administration.
  • Collaborate with team members to meet project schedules, budgets, and quality standards.
  • Assist in preparing record drawings and final documentation packages.

What You’ll Bring

  • Degree in Civil Engineering and/or Structural Engineering from an accredited post-secondary institution. A postgraduate M.Eng. or M.Sc. with structural specialization would be considered an asset.
  • Registered or eligible to immediately register as an Engineer-in-Training (E.I.T.) with APEGA.
  • Knowledge and experience with bridge design related to CSA S6 and its application to bridge projects across highway, rural road, and resource projects would be considered an asset.
  • Experience in the application of MIDAS, SAP2000, CSiBridge, or comparable structural analysis software would be considered an asset.
  • Experience with bridge hydrotechnical analysis in accordance with TEC guidelines would be considered an asset.
