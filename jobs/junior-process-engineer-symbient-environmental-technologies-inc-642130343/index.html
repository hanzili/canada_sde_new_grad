Chemical / Process / Mining

Junior Process Engineer

at Symbient Environmental Technologies Inc.

📍 Location Mississauga, ON
📋 Details Full-time · Permanent · From $50,000/year · In-person
📅 Posted Mar 12, 2026

About the Role

Operate pilot reactors; support commissioning and maintenance

About Symbient Environmental Technologies Inc.

Pilot-scale process tech with R&D focus

Full Description

Purpose of Role

Symbient is designing, fabricating and deploying pilot scale research reactors for the purpose of process development, technology validation and techno-economic assessment of our technology in both simulated and operational environments. The successful candidate will join the Symbient Process Engineering Team working from the pilot plant R&D facility located in Mississauga, Ontario. The Junior Process Engineer will be hands-on, responsible for the operation of the pilot scale reactors and ancillary equipment, support the commissioning pilot plant systems and supporting the team through down time stages of preventive maintenance and reactor preparation. The successful candidate will become a process chemistry subject matter expert supporting the execution of pilot scale experiments.

Responsibilities

  • Responsible for the safe operation and maintenance of Symbient assets at pilot facilities. Complete and submit required operator and maintenance logs as well as situation reports in a timely manner.

  • Responsible for documentation of process knowledge, knowledge transfer and training of pilot plant operators including interns, contingent workers as well as academic and industrial partners.

  • Support the execution of pilot scale experiments for process development and technology validation. Compile and analyze process data. Prepare research reports, laboratory notes and prepare other records as required to document experimental activities and outcomes.

  • Work with technicians, engineers, stakeholders and vendors to bring assets online. Participate in pre-start health and safety reviews if required.

-Serve as shift supervisor in the pilot plant for operators and technicians for continuous pilot scale experiments as required.

  • Support and participate in pilot studies conducted at pilot sites in the field including commissioning of systems, perform catalyst change out and preventive maintenance and support operations as needed

Qualifications

-The ideal candidate is a chemical engineer (bachelor’s or master’s degree), chemical engineering technologist or chemical engineering technician with 0 to 3 years experience

  • Willing to work shift work (8 hour or 12 hour), including night shift if necessary to support pilot plant operations is required

-Eligible for IRAP funding under the Youth Employment Program (YEP) is highly desirable https://nrc.canada.ca/en/support-technology-innovation/nrc-irap-funding-hire-young-graduates

  • Demonstrated heavy lab experience or industrial process experience in a chemical manufacturing environment is an asset

-Knowledge and experience with process monitoring and data acquisition systems including SCADA, Labview, etc. is an asset. Experience with HMI such as Honeywell Experion HS R510 Process Knowledge System (PKS) or other is an asset

  • Strong working knowledge of chemical process control with hands on experience with controller hardware such as Honeywell ControlEdge/HC900 is an asset. Experience with manual controller tuning is an asset.

  • Previous experience with chemical reaction engineering and or catalytic reactors is an asset

  • Previous experience with distillation or vacuum distillation is an asset

  • Eligible and willing to obtain P. Eng. license is an asset

Pay: From $50,000.00 per year

Benefits:

  • Dental care
  • Disability insurance
  • Extended health care
  • Life insurance
  • On-site parking

Work Location: In person
