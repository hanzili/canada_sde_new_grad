About the Role

Design, test, and optimize FPGA hardware

About Avalon Holographics

Holographics startup with mentorship culture

Full Description

Looking for hands-on experience with a company that’s pushing the boundaries of 3D technology?

At Avalon Holographics, students are a vital part of our team and an important part of our long-term talent pipeline. We’re proud to provide meaningful opportunities that support both your learning and your career growth.

As a co-op student, you won’t just be watching from the sidelines, you’ll contribute to real projects, solve real challenges, and gain insight into how a fast-moving, tech company operates. You’ll be surrounded by supportive mentors, diverse teams, and a culture that values initiative, curiosity, and collaboration.

If you’re passionate, driven, and eager to grow, Avalon is an incredible place to launch your career!

As an FPGA co-op student, you’ll help build and optimize the microarchitecture at the heart of our 3D holographic display systems. You’ll work closely with our team to:

  • Design, test, and debug high-speed data processing systems on FPGAs

  • Support architecture development and contribute to detailed design specifications

  • Collaborate on system integration and functional validation

  • Work with advanced technologies to bring complex systems to life

What We’re Looking For:

  • Currently enrolled in Electrical Engineering, Computer Engineering, or Mechatronics

  • Familiarity with VHDL, Verilog, or other HDL languages

  • Enthusiasm for hardware, systems, and real-world problem-solving

  • Curious and open-minded approach with a drive to learn

  • Creative thinker who enjoys tackling challenges

  • Team player who values collaboration, diverse perspectives and shared goals

  • A passion for innovation and interest in contributing to disruptive technology

Why Join Avalon?

Get meaningful experience designing the technology of tomorrow with mentorship, collaboration, and real ownership of your work.

If you're ready to step beyond the 2D world and make your mark in the future of holography, apply today with your resume and transcripts! Please note that your application won’t be complete without both documents.

Deadline to Apply: January 16, 2026 for Spring Term (May to August 2026)
