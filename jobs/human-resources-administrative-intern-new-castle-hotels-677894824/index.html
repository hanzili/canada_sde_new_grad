About the Role

Support HR programs, SOPs, and systems rollout

About New Castle Hotels

Corporate HR team; UKG implementation exposure

Full Description

We are seeking a motivated and detail‑oriented Human Resources Administrative Intern to support our Corporate Human Resources team on a part‑time, temporary basis throughout the summer months. This role is ideal for a student or recent graduate interested in gaining hands‑on experience in corporate HR initiatives, governance, and systems implementation.

While the position is remote within Canada, candidates must be located in the Halifax or Dartmouth area and able to travel to local properties as required.

The intern will play an active role in supporting HR programs, maintaining policies and SOPs, and assisting with the rollout of HR system enhancements, including UKG modules. This position offers meaningful exposure to strategic HR work within a corporate environment.

What You’ll Do

  • Support corporate HR initiatives, projects, and annual HR calendar activities
  • Assist with reviewing, updating, and maintaining HR SOPs, policies, job descriptions, and governance documentation
  • Provide administrative and project support for HR programs and continuous improvement initiatives
  • Support the rollout and maintenance of UKG modules, including system support, training materials, and communications
  • Assist in developing HR tools, templates, and resources to promote consistency across the organization
  • Track project timelines, action items, and deliverables
  • Prepare reports, summaries, and documentation for HR leadership
  • Collaborate with HR team members on ad hoc projects and cross‑functional initiatives
  • Provide occasional support for HR meetings, training sessions, and data audits as needed

What We’re Looking For

  • Currently enrolled in or recently graduated from a post‑secondary program in Human Resources, Business Administration, or a related field
  • Strong organizational skills and attention to detail
  • Proficiency in Microsoft Excel, Word, Outlook, and PowerPoint
  • Interest in HR systems and technology (experience with or interest in UKG is an asset)
  • Ability to handle confidential information with professionalism and discretion
  • Strong written and verbal communication skills
  • Ability to work independently and collaboratively in a corporate setting

If you’re eager to learn, enjoy project‑based work, and want to build a strong foundation in corporate Human Resources, we’d love to hear from you.
