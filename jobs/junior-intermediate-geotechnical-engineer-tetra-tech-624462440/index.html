Full Description

The Opportunity Tetra Tech is adding an Junior to Intermediate Geotechnical Engineer to our Western Canada Engineering team based in Penticton, BC .

Why Tetra Tech At Tetra Tech, we are Leading with Science™ to solve the world’s most complex challenges. Our industry-leading experts in engineering and consulting are committed to driving positive change in communities around the world. For over 50 years, we have been at the forefront of innovation and sustainability. Today we stand as a market leader, offering cutting-edge solutions in water, environment, energy, and international development. Our work has improved more than 500 million lives around the world.

Your Impact Join Tetra Tech to make a real difference. Our work leverages cutting-edge technologies, advanced analytics, and the expertise of world-class scientists and engineers to create meaningful change around the world. Discover your full potential – join us to advance your career while leaving a lasting legacy.

The salary/hourly range for this position is $69,000 - $97,000 per annum/year. Please note that Tetra Tech considers various factors in determining pay, including but not limited to: education, relevant and demonstrated work experience in the role, skills, geographic location, qualifications, certifications, and other competencies relevant to the needs of the business.

Your Role

  • Planning, conducting, and supervising geotechnical investigations.
  • Evaluating geologic conditions and geotechnical characteristics for assessment and design of shallow and deep foundations, retaining structures and earth structures.
  • Conducting foundation investigations for commercial, industrial, and high-rise developments.
  • Conducting slope stability and embankment assessments and completing designs.
  • Conducting geohazard assessments and preparing mitigation designs.
  • Conducting emergency response assessments for rock fall, landslides and debris flows.
  • Conducting geologic structure mapping, kinematic stability analyses and design for rock slopes.
  • Conducting dam safety reviews.
  • Conducting construction field reviews.
  • Analyzing field data and preparing detailed engineering reports and recommendations.
  • Conducting pavement evaluations and completing designs.
  • Preparing proposals, budgeting and scheduling of technical field staff.
  • Mentoring and coaching junior staff members.
  • Communicating with clients, sub-consultants and stakeholders.
  • Conduct activities in line with internal procedures, legislation, and industry standards.
  • Pursue continuous professional development and maintain a high degree of discipline knowledge and awareness.
  • Work in a safe manner at all times and report all health and safety incidents and concerns.
  • Additional duties as required.

Qualifications

  • Minimum of an Undergraduate degree in Geotechnical, Geological or Civil Engineering.
  • Preference will be given to candidates that have obtained a Graduate Degree in the area of Geotechnical Engineering.
  • A minimum of 0 - 5 year of experience in a geotechnical engineering capacity.
  • Experience in a consulting engineering environment would be considered an asset.
  • Experience writing internal and external facing reports and proposals.
  • Strong people skills and an ability to work in a team environment.
  • Experience writing internal and external facing reports and proposals.
  • Strong people skills and an ability to work in a team environment. (these are duplicated)
  • Willingness to travel for work is essential to the position.
  • Previous experience using AutoCAD, GeoStudio 2007 Products and MS Office is essential.
  • Good time management skills, the ability to work with minimal supervision and the ability to contribute to the project by being a team player.
  • Must be or eligible to register as a professional engineer in British Columbia, be legally entitled to work in Canada, and possess a valid British Columbia Class 5 driver's license (or equivalent).

  • Comprehensive and market-competitive benefits, available on day one of employment.
  • Overtime is calculated at 1.5 times.
  • We have embraced a hybrid culture. We will provide you the tools to work almost anywhere.
  • Flexibility and company-wide commitment to work/life balance.
  • Collaborative team atmosphere that values the contributions of all employees.
  • Learning and development opportunities for ongoing professional growth.
  • Mentorship with world leading experts in your field.
