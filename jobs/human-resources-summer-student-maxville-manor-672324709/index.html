About the Role

Assist residents, support activities, ensure safety and care

About Maxville Manor

Long-term care facility with community service focus

Full Description

SUMMARY

The summer student program is an opportunity for students between ages 15 to 30 years, in partnership with Summer Jobs for Youth Program (SJFY), to work under the supervision of department staff to provide meaningful work. Departments who participate in this partnership include, but not limited to, the Day Program, Resident Recreation and Dietary services. Hiring 2 recreation students for 8 weeks (30 hours/week) plus 1 Dietary student for 8 weeks (35 hours/week).

Qualifications:

  • Mature individual with a desire to work with seniors and adults with disabilities.
  • Students are to be enrolled in school and return to school in the Fall.
  • Possess a sincere desire to help, be empathetic towards seniors, and able to motivate seniors.
  • Have effective communication skills, both written and oral.
  • Good computer skills.
  • Available to work daytime hours, the occasional evening, and weekends.
  • Understand and practice safe work habits.
  • Demonstrate organizational and time management skills.
  • Experience in Long-Term Care and/or community setting is favorable.

RESPONSIBILITIES AND DUTIES

Policy

  • Complies with SJFY program guidelines.
  • Complies with the code of ethics and standards of conduct as set out in the Maxville Manor Employee Handbook
  • Upholds the Organization’s Mission, Vision, and Values
  • Complies with all provincial and federal regulations and established department policies and procedures.
  • Follow workplace and department procedures.
  • Respectful of workplace dress code as identified within the department.

Quality Improvement

  • Supports the continuous quality improvement of the department by providing constructive ideas and solutions to identify problems and challenges.

Health and Safety

  • Complies with health and safety legislation and departmental safe job procedures.
  • Uses or wears protective equipment as required.
  • Reports malfunctioning equipment or other safety issues to the supervisor.
  • Adheres to IPAC strict hand washing and infection control practices.
  • Complies with safe food handling practices.

Training

  • Completes orientation, compulsory and in-service training as required to best meet the needs of residents/clients.
  • Attends staff meetings as required to be aware of changes and new practices.

Clinical Responsibilities

  • Adheres to the plan of care as determined by the supervisor.
  • Observes and reports on changes in resident/client condition to supervisor.
  • Demonstrates understanding and executes action plans determined for resident/client needs and abilities.
  • Maintains resident/client information confidentiality.
  • Refers resident/client or family concerns to the program supervisor.
  • Notifies supervisor of any concerns impacting resident/client safety
  • Assists in maintaining a safe and clean environment, including resident/client equipment and belongings.
  • Completes all other tasks as assigned.

JOB SPECIFICATIONS

Skills and Behaviors

  • Empathetic to residents and clients.
  • Safety minded.
  • Be a collaborator.
  • Motivated to learn.
  • Use creativity in programs and client approaches.
  • Cooperates and communicates effectively with co-workers and volunteers.
  • Dependable to work established schedule: Day Program – as assigned. Recreation – as assigned, including weekends and evenings. Dietary – as assigned.
  • Must familiarize self with program business, routines, and services e.g., mealtimes, transporting individuals to and from activities, safe client transfers, mobility, and behaviors.
  • Active participant in resident / client events, outings, and program delivery.

KEY WORKING RELATIONSHIPS

Department staff, volunteers and residents / clients will be the primary individuals with whom the student interacts daily. The Department Supervisor will provide support and direction for daily routine and work assignments. The Director of Outreach and Volunteer Services will oversee programs and reports as necessary.

Job Type: Full-time

Pay: $17.60 per hour

Application question(s):

  • Are you currently a student enrolled in secondary or post secondary school and returning in the fall?

Work Location: In person
