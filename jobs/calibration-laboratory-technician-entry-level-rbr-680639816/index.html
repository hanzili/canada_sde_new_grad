Full Description

About RBR: Making waves since 1973

At RBR, we’ve been designing cutting-edge instruments to measure our blue planet for over 50 years. Built to withstand the harshest environments on Earth, our instruments are deployed from the ocean’s deepest trenches to the polar ice caps, capturing essential data on water temperature, salinity, pH, and more. This data empowers researchers in the global fight against climate change, and our technology supports groundbreaking science with partners like Fisheries and Oceans Canada, Woods Hole, the Scripps Institution, and the British Antarctic Survey.

At RBR, we care deeply about our work, each other, the environment, and the global communities we serve. We value teamwork, continuous improvement, and having fun along the way. From weekly lunches to social events, we’re a crew that thrives on complex, exciting challenges that have a meaningful impact on the blue planet! Ready to make waves with us? Apply below!

About the Role

As an entry level Calibration Laboratory Technician at RBR, you will be a key member of our Calibration team, responsible for performing precise and consistent calibration of RBR instruments. Your work ensures that every instrument we ship meets our high standards for measurement accuracy, directly supporting the scientists and researchers who depend on our data.

This role is a great fit if you take pride in doing repetitive work with precision and care. For you, consistency is not about routine, it is about catching the one result that does not belong. You are disciplined, methodical, and sharp enough to notice when something has changed, even after hundreds of similar measurements.

You will work alongside technicians, metrologists, and engineers as part of a small, collaborative team. Clear communication, personal accountability, and a commitment to quality are what make this environment thrive, and what will make you successful in it.

What You'll Do:

  • Perform calibration of RBR products including temperature, pressure, conductivity, pH, Dissolved Oxygen and Turbidity
  • Perform calibration and test of RBR products and laboratory equipment, to issue calibration certificates
  • Operate, monitor and maintain test and calibration equipment and systems
  • Read and interpret operating instructions and test procedures accurately and efficiently
  • Follow prescribed test and calibration procedures or instructions, collect, documents and reporting findings
  • Document test or calibration failures through quality reporting system

What You Bring:

  • College diploma or bachelors degree
  • The ability to apply knowledge of mathematics and measurement science
  • Knowledge of measurement uncertainty analysis
  • The ability to work without direct supervision and effectively in a team environment
  • Strong organizational and problem-solving skills, detail oriented and customer focused
  • The ability to multi-task, effectively prioritize and execute tasks
  • Proficiency with computer technology
  • Good verbal and written communication skills
  • The ability to lift 50 lbs.

Salary range: $50,000–$60,000
