About Trench Group

Full Description

Scarborough, ON | 12 Month Contract | On-Site | Junior Level About Us In Trench Canada, we deliver state-of-the-art, safe, and reliable instrument transformers, coils, and bushings for seamless energy transmission. With a legacy of customized engineering and innovation, we enable society to harness the power of electricity with reliability and performance.

We are driven by a commitment to advancing the energy sector through innovative technology and sustainable practices. Our culture emphasizes continuous improvement, attention to detail, and adaptability, while fostering an inclusive and respectful environment where diverse perspectives are valued and everyone has the opportunity to contribute and succeed.

Our Values

  • Trust
  • Responsibility
  • Excellence
  • Navigating Change
  • Candor
  • Humility

A Snapshot of Your Day As a Jr. Project Manager for Industrial projects, you will play a vital role in ensuring the smooth operation of our production facility by managing various construction and maintenance projects. You will work closely with senior project managers, engineers, and cross-functional teams to deliver projects on time, within budget, and in accordance with safety and quality standards. This is an excellent opportunity to gain hands-on experience in project management within a dynamic factory setting.

Your Tasks:

  • Project Planning: Assist in the development of project plans, including scope, schedule, budget, and resource allocation.
  • Project Execution: Oversee the day-to-day activities of facility projects, ensuring they progress according to plan and are completed on time and within budget.
  • Vendor Management: Collaborate with external contractors and suppliers, managing relationships and ensuring they adhere to project requirements.
  • Documentation: Maintain accurate project documentation, including contracts, change orders, and progress reports.
  • Quality Control: Monitor and enforce quality standards and safety protocols throughout project execution.
  • Budget Management: Track project expenses and ensure adherence to budget constraints.
  • Stakeholder Communication: Maintain clear and effective communication with project stakeholders, including factory leadership and team members.
  • Problem Solving: Identify and address project issues and risks proactively, finding solutions to keep projects on track.
  • Reporting: Prepare regular project status reports and presentations for senior management.
  • Continuous Improvement: Contribute ideas and suggestions for process improvements and best practices in project management.

Requirements:

  • Bachelor's degree in engineering, projects management, or a related field preferred.
  • Mechanical/Mechatronics/Electrical Engineers are preferred.
  • 1-2 years of relevant experience in project management
  • Strong organizational and time-management skills.
  • Excellent communication and interpersonal skills.
  • Proficiency in project management software and Microsoft Office Suite; AutoCAD experience is an asset.
  • Ability to work independently and as part of a team.
  • Knowledge of safety regulations and industry best practices.
  • PMP or CAPM certification is a plus.

We offer:

  • The opportunity to gain practical experience in the field of optical current measurement technology and sustainable energy transmission in an innovative company
  • You will work in a motivated team on forward-looking projects and gain insights into the latest technologies
  • Support from experienced professionals in handling the latest measurement technology
  • An hourly rate of pay of $30 - $40
