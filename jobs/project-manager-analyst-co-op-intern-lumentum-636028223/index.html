Position Title: Project Manager Analyst – Co-op/Intern

Employment Type: Student – Co-op/Intern

Location: Ottawa ON, Onsite

About Welcome to Lumentum! We’re all about harnessing cutting-edge tech to create a brighter, more connected future. We are the leaders in optical and photonic products, working at the intersection of innovation and impact. Here’s what we’re up to:

Cloud & Networking

  • Powering high-speed data transmission for cloud data centers, AI/ML, enterprise systems, and communication services. Think streaming, gaming, and next-gen tech—we make it happen.

Industrial Tech

  • Innovating with state-of-the-art lasers used in industries like EVs, solar power, display technology, and semiconductors. We’re enabling the future of green tech and advanced manufacturing.

At Lumentum, we’re committed to collaboration, inclusivity, and empowering our team to shine. Together, we’re shaping the tech of tomorrow while supporting a culture where everyone thrives.

Lumentum is seeking an enthusiastic and innovative student to join the Optical Communication Transport Project Management team in Ottawa, Ontario.

The Opportunity

  • 16 month work term: May 2026 - August 2027

What You’ll Be Doing

  • Take ownership of hardware and software projects as the lead Project Manager, defining and documenting project scope, goals, budget, and deliverables with support from the Program Manager and stakeholders
  • Develop and create the project structure and maintain thorough documentation in Confluence
  • Support the development and implementation of a broad, coordinated set of plans and programs to meet the goals and priorities of Lumentum
  • Regularly collaborate with the Program Manager to monitor and control project schedules with engineering teams and communicate project status (technical and programmatic) to stakeholders and management
  • Effectively communicate project expectations to team members and stakeholders in a timely and clear fashion by leading regular occurring meetings
  • Support the development process by ordering and tracking the program’s material procurement through Oracle Enterprise Manager
  • Actively participate in hiring and onboarding the next cohort of co-ops, and develop training resources

What We’re Looking For

  • Working towards a Bachelor’s degree in engineering or business with an interest in Project or Program Management, returning to school starting September 2027
  • Excellent leadership skills, combining critical thinking with empathetic and diplomatic communication
  • Demonstrated ability to form collaborative and cooperative working relationships in all interactions
  • Ability to work in ambiguous situations and define a path to resolution
  • Excellent written and oral communication skills, motivated team player, self-starter and detail-oriented

Any of the following would be considered a strong asset:

  • Working knowledge of Miro, Confluence, JIRA, Oracle, Agile, or Tableau
  • Detail-oriented, organized, and highly motivated individual
  • Ability to operate effectively in highly technical and multi project environment
  • Structured Project Management certifications such as PMI (CAPM), PRINCE2, Scrum, Six Sigma

  • Prayer room
