About J-Squared Technologies Inc.

J-Squared has over 30 years of experience excelling in operationally demanding performance environments. Our ruggedized products and solutions are innovative, needs driven, and focused on quality and reliability. Our Octagon Systems line of products is a global leader in rugged computer systems built for use in extreme environments such as mining, military, transportation, and marine. We architect and manufacture systems that work no matter what.

J-Squared‚Äôs significant growth has resulted in the company expanding its teams in both Ottawa and Dartmouth to meet this increased demand.

J-Squared is looking to hire a Junior Embedded Software Developer to join our growing Dartmouth based team on a full-time basis. Reporting to Senior Engineers and VP of Engineering, you will be responsible for the design and implementation of software solutions for our evolving product line and to meet the needs of our customers.

Key Responsibilities

The role will include the following elements, balanced according to the evolving needs of the organization and our customers:

  • Develop and maintain embedded software using C/C++
  • Assist in implementing and testing communication protocols (e.g., UART, SPI, I2C, CAN, Ethernet)
  • Support debugging and troubleshooting of embedded systems using tools such as GDB, JTAG, logic analyzers, or oscilloscopes
  • Participate in code reviews and follow established coding standards
  • Use Git/GitLab for version control, issue tracking, and collaboration
  • Assist in documentation of software design and test results
  • Work closely with hardware engineers to bring up new boards

Key Qualifications

  • B.Sc. degree in Computer Engineering, Electrical Engineering, or related field
  • Basic knowledge of embedded systems and real-time concepts
  • Programming experience in C and/or C++
  • Familiarity with Git or GitLab
  • Basic understanding of embedded communication protocols
  • Basic understanding of microcontroller architecture and peripherals
  • Exposure to device drivers and low-level hardware interaction
  • Ability to modify and debug existing low-level code
  • Understanding of interrupts, registers, and memory concepts
  • Willingness to learn debugging techniques and tools
  • Good problem-solving and communication skills
  • Exposure to RTOS (FreeRTOS, VxWorks, etc.)
  • Experience with Linux-based embedded systems
  • Internship or academic project experience in embedded development
