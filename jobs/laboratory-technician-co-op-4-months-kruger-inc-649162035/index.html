Full Description

LABORATORY TECHNICIAN CO-OP (CHIP TESTING) Activity sector: Pulp and Paper

Site: Kamloops, B.C.

Wage: $25.00 - $27.00 per hour

Term: May 2026 – September 2026

Schedule: Monday to Friday 7am to 3pm or 2pm to 10pm (to be determined when started)

YOUR ROLE Reporting to the site Technical & Environmental manager, the Laboratory Chip Tester Co ‑ op will support daily chip sampling, testing, and quality monitoring within the site laboratory. This role is ideal for a 2nd year science student (or higher) or a technologist student interested in developing hands ‑ on experience in analytical testing, data collection, and process quality support.

Your Responsibilities

  • Perform chip sampling, preparation, and standardized chip testing procedures.
  • Conduct routine laboratory analyses and accurately record test results.
  • Support quality monitoring of wood chip characteristics used in the pulping process.
  • Maintain laboratory equipment and ensure testing areas are clean, safe, and organized.
  • Input data into laboratory systems and assist with basic data trend analysis.
  • Collaborate with lab personnel, process engineers, and operations staff as needed.
  • Follow all safety procedures and contribute to a safe work environment.
  • Assist with other laboratory tasks or small projects as assigned.

Your Skills And Abilities

  • Strong attention to detail and commitment to accurate data collection.
  • Excellent communication skills and ability to work effectively within a team.
  • Strong organizational skills with the ability to manage multiple tasks.
  • Self ‑ motivated, independent, and able to take initiative.
  • Comfortable working in a laboratory and industrial environment.

Your Qualifications Required:

  • Currently enrolled in post-secondary education → 2nd year or higher in a Science program (Biology, Chemistry, Natural Resource Science, Environmental Science, etc.) OR a Technologist program.
  • Good problem ‑ solving skills and willingness to learn technical testing procedures.
  • Strong commitment to safety and quality.
  • Eligibility to work in Canada.
  • Access to reliable transportation.

Preferred:

  • Previous laboratory experience (academic or work).
  • Familiarity with Microsoft Office (Excel, Word).
  • Interest in pulp & paper, biomass, or industrial processes.
