ASPIRE Commercial Banking - Indigenous Financial Services - Fall 2026 Co-op / Internship

at Scotiabank

📋 Details Co-op / Internship · Fall 2026 · Sep 2026–Dec 2026
📅 Posted Apr 21, 2026

About the Role

Provide admin and research support to client teams

About Scotiabank

Major Canadian bank with Indigenous services focus

Full Description

Requisition ID: 258915

Employee Referral Program – Potential Reward We are committed to investing in our employees and helping you continue your career at Scotiabank.

Join a purpose driven winning team, committed to results, in an inclusive and high-performing culture.

Location: Barrie, London, Ottawa, Toronto, Waterloo, Windsor, Halifax, Moncton, Winnipeg, Saskatoon, Calgary, Edmonton, Kelowna, Langley, Vancouver.

Start Date: September 2026

End Date: December 2026

Work Hours: 37.5 Hours per week

Application Deadline: 05/22/2026

Kickstart your career with Scotiabank’s Commercial Aspire Program! This is your opportunity to gain real-world experience, build your network, and explore what it’s like to work in Canadian Commercial Banking—all while contributing meaningfully to our teams. Aspire is designed for ambitious undergraduates like yourself who are curious, collaborative, and eager to learn. There’s a place for every type of student at Scotiabank, and all that we ask for is that you have passion and strong Attitude and Ownership of Opportunity (AOO) .

Scotiabank’s Commercial Banking professionals are committed to delivering advice. You’ll be a trusted advisor offering a full suite of customized lending, deposit, cash management, and trade finance solutions to mid and large-sized businesses. Team players with a client-centric approach and a strong emphasis on results will thrive in our flexible, growth-oriented environment, and our one-of-a-kind people-first culture. We are also committed to providing advancement opportunities for those individuals who have the ability, aspiration, and engagement to contribute at higher levels.

The Indigenous Financial Services team has a long-standing tradition of supporting Indigenous communities and organizations across Canada, adapting to changing needs and priorities over the years. From working with First Nations, Métis, and Inuit governments to partnering with Indigenous businesses and organizations, we remain dedicated to providing tailored financial solutions and meaningful support. What hasn’t changed is our unwavering commitment to championing those who are building strong, vibrant communities and shaping the future for generations to come.

Is this role right for you? With Aspire, You’ll Gain Early Exposure To Commercial Banking By Working On Meaningful Projects And Learning Directly From Experienced Professionals. You’ll Have Opportunities To

  • Deepen partner and stakeholder relationships by championing client needs and delivering actionable recommendations that support business goals and ongoing initiatives.
  • Provide administrative and research support to client relationship teams, including preparing presentations, project plans, and learning materials for various projects.
  • Build strong analytical and communication skills by gathering and synthesizing information to enable informed, collaborative decision-making.
  • Contribute timely, high-quality support on key projects by understanding business needs and driving process improvements.
  • Partner across teams to help drive business outcomes and process improvements.
  • Foster a client-focused culture by consistently demonstrating adaptability, energy, and a personable approach in all client interactions.
  • Ensure that the Bank’s risk appetite and risk culture are considered in day-to-day activities and decisions.

Who We’re Looking For You thrive in a structured environment with clear guidance and deadlines, and you bring a positive , flexible , and professional attitude .

Key Capabilities

  • You are client-centric and passionate about enhancing the client experience
  • You are collaborative and work effectively with others
  • You are organized and able to manage multiple priorities with strong attention to detail
  • You are comfortable operating in a fast-paced, team-oriented environment
  • You are analytical and use data to inform decisions and recommendations
  • You are adept at identifying problems and developing effective, strategic solutions

Key Requirements

  • You are enrolled in an undergraduate degree in Business, Commerce, Finance, Accounting, Economics, or a related field
  • You have strong Microsoft Office skills (Word, Excel, PowerPoint, Outlook)
  • You have excellent written and verbal communication skills
  • You have good presentation skills and consistently demonstrate professionalism
  • You have a natural curiosity and eagerness to learn about Commercial Banking
