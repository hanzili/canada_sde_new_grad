About Arcadis

Full Description

Arcadis is the world's leading company delivering sustainable design, engineering, and consultancy solutions for natural and built assets.

We are more than 34,000 people, in over 30 countries, dedicated to improving quality of life. Everyone has an important role to play. With the power of many curious minds, together we can solve the world’s most complex challenges and deliver more impact together.

Role description: Arcadis is growing! Arcadis Canada is seeking a Junior Environmental Field Technician, Scientist or Engineer in Training to join our Environmental Team in Canada. This position will be based out of Ottawa, Ontario. This role will include 85% travel to sites throughout region as well as an opportunity to travel across Canada.

This role sits within the Global Resilience Business Area. We work to protect our natural environment and water resources, while powering our world for future generations. Around the world, we’re feeling the effects climate change, rapid urbanization, loss of biodiversity. The rate at which we’re seeing large-scale, unforeseen events such as floods and wildfires, is becoming more frequent. We are here to protect our natural environment and water resources, while powering our world for future generations.

Role accountabilities: As an Environmental Field Technician/Scientist/Engineer in Training, you will coordinate and execute activities assigned by Project Managers pertaining to the execution of environmental projects including environmental site assessments, risk assessments, and remediation projects. This includes conducting site visits and inspections, monitoring and supervising drilling and/or remediation activities, preparing field notes, and collecting soil and groundwater samples, and writing environmental reports.

Responsibilities will include:

  • Strong understanding of field protocols including soil, groundwater, and vapour sampling, and overseeing drilling activities and subcontractor management
  • Maintain knowledge and familiarity with provincial and federal regulatory frameworks (O.Reg.153)
  • Diligent interpretation of environmental data
  • Comparison of sample results to applicable guidelines and standards
  • Preparation of reports documenting the results of investigation programs

Success in this role requires strong attention to detail and accuracy, excellent communication and interpersonal skills, clear and effective writing abilities, and solid proficiency with Microsoft Office tools including Word, Excel, and PowerPoint.

Qualifications & Experience:

  • Technical Diploma or bachelor's degree in environmental science, engineering, earth sciences, or related field

Preferred Qualifications

  • 1+ years' experience in soil, groundwater or vapour sampling, and overseeing drilling activities and subcontractor management preferred.
