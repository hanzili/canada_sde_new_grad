About the Role

Support recruitment, onboarding, and HR administration

About Positive Living Niagara

Community HR org focused on health inclusion

Full Description

Position: Full-time, contract for 16 weeks

Hours: Monday – Friday, 35 hours/week

Wage: $20.90 per hour

Positive Living Niagara is a community-focused organization composed of passionate staff and volunteers. We are committed to providing support, education, and advocacy for individuals affected by or at risk of HIV, Hepatitis C, and other sexually transmitted blood-borne infections (STBBIs). We are dedicated to fostering inclusivity, equity, and the meaningful involvement of those we serve.

We are looking for a Human Resources Summer Student to support our Admin team in maintaining an efficient, professional, and compliant workplace. This role includes assisting with recruitment, onboarding, general administrative duties and staff training coordination.

Key Responsibilities:

Administrative and Program Support

· Assist with organizing and maintaining HR documents and reports

· Provide general administrative support for organizational initiatives, including scheduling meetings, preparing materials and coordinating training sessions.

· Ensure accurate and secure data management practices are followed.

· Assist with gathering and analyzing organizational data, such as employee surveys and training feedback, to identify areas for continuous improvement.

Recruitment and Onboarding

· Support the admin team with drafting and posting job descriptions across various platforms.

· Assist with the recruitment process by screening resumes, scheduling interviews and preparing interview materials.

· Help coordinate the onboarding activities to ensure a welcoming, accessible and inclusive experience.

Internal Engagement and Communications

· Assist with the implementation and promotion of staff programs including events training sessions

· Help organize and facilitate workshops and training for staff.

· Coordinate communication related to initiatives, including newsletters, emails and social media updates

· Assist in promoting organizational events and activities within the organization, ensuring visibility and participation.

Qualifications:

· Be between 15 and 30 years of age at the start of the employment

· Experience gained through a college or university program in social services, human resources or business administration.

· Valid driver’s license, clear driving record, and access to a reliable vehicle.

· Be a Canadian Citizen, permanent resident or someone who has been officially given refugee protection in Canada

· Have a valid Social Insurance Number at the start of employment and be legally entitled to work in Canada

Required Skills:

· Proficiency in Microsoft Office 365

· Strong organizational and time management skills with attention to detail

· Effective verbal and written communication skills

· Knowledge of basic human resources practices

· Experience with data entry, record keeping and maintaining accurate HR documentation

· Ability to prioritize tasks and work both independently and as part of a team

To Apply:

Please submit your resume and cover letter to Vanessa Fyfe by April 26th, 2026 at vanessa@positivelivingniagara.com. Video submissions in place of cover letter are accepted.

Note: No telephone inquiries, please.

Equity and Inclusion Statement:

Positive Living Niagara is committed to employment equity and encourages applications from Black, Indigenous and racialized people, people of all gender identities and sexual orientations, and people with disabilities. We also recognize the importance of lived experience and encourage applications from individuals with lived or living experience of HIV, hepatitis C or substance use.

We are committed to providing accommodations throughout the hiring process for candidates who require them. Thank you to all who apply; however, only those selected for interviews will be contacted.
