If you’re looking for meaningful work that contributes to a safer, more secure world, you’ll find purpose here. Every day, your skills will directly impact programs that matter.

Job Description

The Tactical Communication and Information System (TacCIS) Solutions Product Line Management (PLM) team is looking for a Junior Hardware Engineer to support the development of new cutting edge MESHnet hardware products. The MESHnet product line includes Tactical Mobile Routers (TMR), Tactical Crew Stations (TCS) and Power Distribution Units (PDUs). The TacCIS PLM development team develops these products in-house, including the mechanical systems, power supplies, circuit boards, FPGAs and firmware.

As part of this dynamic team, you will be involved with the design, component selection, implementation, validation, debug and lifecycle management of various circuit cards. Circuit card complexities vary from simple debug cards to highly complex HDI PWBs with Intel Xeon processors, DDR4, Multi-Gigabit Ethernet, Artificial Intelligence accelerators and high speed FPGA SoCs. Expect to be working with smart, talented people like you. Every product for our customers presents new challenges to learn and grow from experienced designers.

Key Responsibilities

  • Design and develop electronic circuit card assemblies, harnesses, cables and test systems
  • Simulate and test circuits and electrical solutions.
  • Conducts, monitors, modifies and documents engineering test procedures
  • Execute and document various engineering investigations
  • Capture and maintain configuration control of product development
  • Create mechanical designs and drawings mechanical systems for use in rugged environments using Computer Aided Design (CAD) software.
  • Support the full design life cycle from requirements definition, concept through to successful production ramp-up, including support for prototype procurement, assembly, design verification and qualification testing, and on-going manufacturing support.
  • Review part and assembly drawings and parts lists in support of the release of design activities
  • Work with other mechanical designers and engineers, system engineers and human factor engineers in the development of installation designs of military vehicles.
  • Closely with other design professionals, systems engineers, Supply chain management personnel, and manufacturing engineers to ensure a smooth flow of information throughout a project.
  • Perform 3D scanning and process data in support of vehicle platforms.

Qualifications

  • Bachelor Degree in an application Engineering or Technical discipline
  • Knowledge of electronic circuit design
  • Strong understanding of computer systems, from low level hardware to OS systems
  • Innovative self-starter with excellent communication and team work skills
  • Ability to interpret and verify requirements
  • Strong technical documentation skills
  • Ability to work in a time-critical environment
  • Ability to multi-task and prioritize
  • Experience working with or familiarity with AI/ML models is preferred.

It is a requirement that General Dynamics Mission Systems-Canada be registered with the Canadian Controlled Goods program and that all of its workforce be security assessed. Successful applicants must meet all applicable security requirements, including but not limited to the ability to obtain and maintain a Canadian government security clearance. Applicants may be required to meet additional security requirements in order to gain access to technical data, classified areas or information that is subject to international regulations. You must be eligible to work in Canada.
