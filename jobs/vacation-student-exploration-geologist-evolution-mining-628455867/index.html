About Red Lake

Evolution’s star is rising in Red Lake.

At Red Lake, our people have many career and growth opportunities and a bright future. With three underground mines and two processing plants all within the one site, Red Lake is one of the highest-grade gold deposits in Canada. The operational diversity and complexity at Red Lake, along with outstanding exploration potential, opens the door to many career possibilities and highlights working with us.

Located in Northwestern Ontario, we encourage our people to live local in the stunning community of Red Lake, where you can enjoy the outdoor lifestyle and adventures the region is renowned for. It’s very special that our town and operations are all close by, so it really does feel like one big community.

About the role

The Vacation Student - Exploration role offers an exceptional opportunity to play an active role in field programs to start learning the essentials of collecting geologic data. Field programs will include soil sampling, regional surface mapping and underground mapping.

This position will be on an 5/2/4/3 -9.5hr shifts and will be based residentially in Red Lake, Ontario.

The compensation range for role is $29.00-$32.00 per hour.

Key result areas in this role include:
Assist with preparation of diamond drill core for geologic logging and sampling

Gain initial exposure to logging diamond drill core with an emphasis on lithology, structure, and alteration relating to gold mineralisation

Take part in some surface and underground drill rig inspections

Deliver a short report for a geological project/question relating to your summer work

Adhere to all relevant corporate policies and procedures

Perform other duties as assigned

About you

As a Vacation Student – Exploration, you will be enrolled in your 3rd or 4th year of a University program focused on Geology. It is also important that you have / can demonstrate:

Relevant experiences in core logging or geology related activities

Working knowledge of Microsoft Office

Some exposure to mining software (QGIS or Leapfrog) would be advantageous

Familiar with Occupational Health and Safety Act and Regulations for Mines and Mining Plants

Strong will to learn and develop.

Self-starter demonstrating motivation and initiative

The ability to deliver results according to agreed plans

Good writing and reporting skills

Excellent verbal and written communication skills
