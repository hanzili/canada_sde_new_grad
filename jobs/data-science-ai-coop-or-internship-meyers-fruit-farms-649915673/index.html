Data / ML Internship

Data Science / AI Co‑op or Internship

at Meyers Fruit Farms

📍 Location Niagara-on-the-Lake, ON
📋 Details Intern · 4mo · $17.60-$18.00/hr · Spring 2026
📅 Posted Mar 24, 2026

About the Role

Structure data, analyze pests, build visualizations

About Meyers Fruit Farms

Canadian fruit farm applying data-driven agriculture

Full Description

What You’ll Work On

  • Organizing and improving pest scouting and monitoring data (e.g., sticky card counts, scouting notes, greenhouse zones)
  • Connecting pest pressure with crop stage, variety, environment, and treatment actions
  • Analyzing pest trends and treatment outcomes over time
  • Supporting development of data‑informed pressure thresholds and forecasting tools
  • Creating clear visualizations or summaries that growers can use in real decisions
  • Emphasis on learning greenhouse context before heavy modeling

What You’ll Learn

  • How IPM operates in real commercial greenhouse conditions
  • How to work with incomplete, real‑world agricultural data
  • How biological, chemical, environmental, and cost factors interact
  • How data science is applied in an operational, sustainability‑focused setting

Required Qualifications

  • Enrolled in Data Science, Computer Science, AI, Engineering, Statistics, or a related program
  • Experience with Python or R
  • Understanding of basic statistics and data analysis
  • Comfortable working with real‑world, imperfect datasets
  • Interest in agriculture, sustainability, or applied analytics

Nice‑to‑Have Assets

  • Experience with Pandas, NumPy, scikit‑learn, TensorFlow, or PyTorch
  • Data visualization tools (Matplotlib, Power BI, Tableau, etc.)
  • Interest in plant science or biology

Work Environment

  • Combination of greenhouse floor exposure and analytical work
  • Warm, humid greenhouse conditions at times
  • Training and PPE provided as required

Project Background
Current IPM decisions are supported by sticky card counts, visual greenhouse maps, scouting notes, and grower experience. While effective, much of this information is not yet fully connected in structured datasets, limiting quantitative analysis, forecasting, and cost evaluation.
This internship supports the transition from experience‑driven tracking to experience + data‑supported IPM, while respecting operational realities.
Current State

  • Weekly yellow sticky card counts
  • Visual greenhouse maps by post/area showing crop, finish week, and notes
  • Qualitative pest pressure assessments (species, life stage, damage)
  • Biological control releases and spray programs based on experience, seasonality, crop stage, and product efficacy

Target Outcomes
By the end of the term, aim to have:

  • A structured dataset linking pest pressure, area, crop stage, environment, and treatments
  • Clear before/after comparisons for biological and chemical interventions
  • Exploratory pressure prediction or trend models (even if simple)
  • Draft numeric pressure thresholds to support decision timing
  • Basic cost and labour context tied to pest pressure and control actions
  • Documentation so work can be continued after the co‑op ends

Key Project Streams
1. Data Structuring
Convert existing counts, notes, and maps into consistent, analyzable formats.
2. Exploratory Analysis
Identify relationships between pest pressure, environment, crop stage, and treatments.
3. Modeling (Right‑Sized)
Develop simple statistical or ML models to support short‑term forecasting or early warning.
4. Decision Support
Produce visuals, summaries, or tools that growers can realistically use.

Success Criteria

  • Insights are understandable and actionable for IPM staff
  • Tools respect real greenhouse constraints (data gaps, time pressure)
  • Grower intuition is supported, not overridden
  • Work is documented and reusable

Term: April/ May- August/ September (Co‑op or Internship)
Hours: 35-42.5 hours/ week
Start Date: April/ May

Compensation: $17.60-$18.00

How to Apply:

If you enjoy variety and thrive in an upbeat, fast-moving environment, we’d love to hear from you! Please submit your resume online, we do not accept in person applications.

Accessibility & Inclusion:

Meyers Farms Inc. is committed to an inclusive, barrier‑free recruitment process and workplace. If you require accommodation at any stage of the hiring process, please contact our Human Resources team at careers@ meyersfruitfarms.com.

We appreciate all interest in the role. Only those selected for an interview will be contacted. AI is only used for posting purposes. All resumes are reviewed by a Meyers team member.
