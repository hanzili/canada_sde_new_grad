Job Description

AECOM is seeking a Entry-Level Aquatic Biologist to join our Environment team in Burnaby, BC. This new position is an excellent opportunity for an early-career biologist who is enthusiastic about fieldwork and eager to grow their technical skills in a consulting environment.

We are looking for candidates that are positive and team-oriented with a willingness to learn and take initiative. You will work alongside experienced biologists and environmental professionals on a wide variety of industrial, infrastructure, and government projects throughout British Columbia and Western Canada.

Key Responsibilities

  • Responsibilities include, but are not limited to:
  • Conducting background research on aquatic and terrestrial ecological resources
  • Participating in aquatic field programs (freshwater and/or marine), including biological sampling and ecological surveys such as:
  • Fish and fish habitat assessments
  • Intertidal surveys
  • Fish and amphibian salvages
  • Supporting terrestrial field programs, including vegetation surveys, wildlife habitat assessments, and nesting bird sweeps
  • Assisting with the preparation of environmental permits and approvals (federal, provincial, and municipal)
  • Supporting and participating in construction environmental monitoring and mitigation
  • Assisting with data compilation, analysis, and interpretation
  • Contributing to technical report writing and environmental assessments
  • Working collaboratively within interdisciplinary teams of biologists, planners, engineers, and other specialists
  • Preparing or contributing to health and safety plans and demonstrating personal safety leadership
  • Participating in out-of-town travel for fieldwork, including work in remote locations and flexible schedules

Qualifications

Minimum Qualifications

  • Bachelor’s degree in Biology, Environmental Science, Ecology, or a related discipline
  • Strong technical background, preferably in aquatic biology
  • Eligible for, or registered as, a Biology-in-Training (B.I.T.) or equivalent with the BC College of Applied Biology
  • Valid driver’s license
  • Willingness to conduct fieldwork in varying conditions and remote locations
  • Proficiency with Microsoft Excel and Word

Preferred Qualifications and Assets

  • Master’s degree in a related discipline
  • 1–2+ years of relevant environmental or consulting experience (experience in BC or Western Canada considered an asset)
  • Field experience with aquatic sampling methods and familiarity with BC Resources Information Standards Committee protocols
  • Working knowledge of relevant legislation such as the Fisheries Act, Species at Risk Act, and Water Sustainability Act
  • Strong written and verbal communication skills with excellent organizational and time-management abilities
  • Experience with ArcGIS, mapping, or statistical software an asset
  • Technical certifications such as electrofishing, CABIN, First Aid, or ATV/UTV training considered assets

Additional Information

  • This position will involve flexible hours and extended field days during field seasons.
  • Relocation is not available for this position.
  • Sponsorship for Canadian employment authorization is not available for this position.
