ENERGY Transportation Group is currently looking to add a Junior Helpdesk Technician to its LaSalle team. Please note this is an in-office position.

The Junior Helpdesk Technician provides first-level technical support to employees and supports daily IT operations. This role is designed for an early-career professional who is eager to learn, develop technical skills, and grow within the organization.

Key Responsibilities

  • Provide first-line support for day-to-day IT issues (hardware, software, and basic network support).
  • Monitor and manage support tickets under supervision.
  • Assist in troubleshooting common technical problems and escalate complex issues when required.
  • Support onboarding of new employees (equipment setup, basic system access, initial training).
  • Document solutions and update internal IT knowledge bases.
  • Assist in maintaining IT equipment inventory and storage.
  • Help prepare and update user guides and basic training materials.
  • Perform routine system checks and maintenance tasks with guidance.
  • Support software updates and basic system maintenance.
  • Participate in continuous learning and skills development.

Required Qualifications

  • Diploma or certificate in IT, Computer Science, or related field.
  • Internship or entry-level experience in IT support.
  • Familiarity with ticketing systems.
  • Basic knowledge of networking concepts.
  • Basic understanding of Windows operating systems and common computer hardware.
  • Introductory knowledge of Active Directory and Windows 10/11 (or willingness to learn).
  • Interest in IT support and technology.
  • Strong customer service mindset and communication skills.
  • Ability to follow procedures and work with supervision.
  • Comfortable learning new systems and tools.
  • Basic troubleshooting skills for laptops, desktops, and peripherals.
  • Good organizational skills and attention to detail.
  • Proficiency in both French and English, written and spoken, is required (as we conduct business with clients based in Canada and the United States, fluency in both languages is not only important but represents a significant asset for us).
