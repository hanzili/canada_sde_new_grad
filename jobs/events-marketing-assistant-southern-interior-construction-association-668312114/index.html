Full Description

Job Title: Events & Community Experience Assistant
Company: Southern Interior Construction Association (SICA)
Location: Kelowna, BC
Job Type: Full-time, Temporary (April – July 2026)
Salary: $18–$22 per hour

About Us

The Southern Interior Construction Association (SICA) represents over 400 companies across BC’s construction industry. We bring people together through events, training, and advocacy that strengthen our region and build the future workforce.

We run some of the most well-attended industry events in the Interior. Golf tournaments, networking nights, fundraisers, and community initiatives that directly support scholarships and youth entering the trades.

The Role

This is not a sit-at-your-desk job. You’ll be in the middle of everything. Planning, organizing, and executing events that people actually want to attend.

You’ll support our Events & Community Experience team to deliver high-quality, well-run events while building real skills in coordination, communication, and problem-solving.

What You’ll Do

  • Support planning and execution of events across Kelowna, Kamloops, and the Southern Interior
  • Coordinate event logistics: registration, signage, vendors, venues, and setup
  • Communicate with members, sponsors, and partners
  • Assist with event promotion, social media, and content creation
  • Help manage attendee lists, check-in, and on-site experience
  • Track event data and support post-event reporting
  • Assist with sponsorship fulfillment and community partnerships
  • Support general event prep, teardown, and day-of coordination

What You Bring

  • Highly organized and detail-focused
  • Strong communication skills. You’re comfortable talking to people
  • Able to juggle multiple tasks without dropping the ball
  • Positive, team-first attitude
  • Comfortable with basic tech: Microsoft Office, Google Workspace. Canva is a bonus
  • Willing to work occasional early mornings or evenings for events
  • Valid driver’s license is an asset

What You’ll Get

  • Real experience running large-scale events
  • Direct exposure to business leaders and the construction industry
  • Hands-on learning. Not busywork
  • A fast-paced, supportive team that will push you to grow
  • The chance to contribute to programs that fund scholarships and support youth entering the trades

Why This Role Matters

The work you do helps fund student scholarships and supports programs like Honour the Work, connecting young people to careers in construction. You’re not just running events. You’re helping build the workforce our region depends on.
