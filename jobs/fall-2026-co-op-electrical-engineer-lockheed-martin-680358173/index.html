About the role

Lockheed Martin Canada Inc. (Lockheed Martin) is Canada's most successful Combat System Integrator (CSI) specializing in large, sophisticated integration projects. We take advantage of advanced engineering practices and modern technologies to design, develop and integrate combat systems. We are seeking employees with exceptional technical skills to become part of our success story.

The candidate will participate in engineering activities including but not limited to:

  • Development of product and cable/harness design solutions to meet specific design requirements.
  • Conduct and minute discussion meetings and peer reviews of design artifacts.
  • Review and analysis of vendor or stakeholder design documentation and mechanical drawings.
  • Creation of power and system functional/block drawings and wiring diagrams.
  • Creation of electrical design artifacts such as schematics, interconnects, product specifications, Bill of Materials, etc. for system-level architecture connectivity.
  • Support of prototype development and testing along with definition of test procedures and needed resources.
  • Support of qualification planning and testing, including management of product test equipment and labour resources.
  • Support Production team during New Product Integration (NPI) stages.
  • Maintain ongoing research of new materials/suppliers to strengthen supply chain.
  • Document design information in Electronic Computer Aided Design (ECAD), Microsoft Visio and Office software products.

What you bring to the role

  • Current student in electrical engineering or an electrical technologist diploma from an accredited college, or equivalent.
  • Working knowledge of MS Suite including Word, Excel, PowerPoint, Access, Visio.
  • Resourceful/agreeable to enable strong vendor liaison/stakeholder communications.
  • Production and results-oriented self-starter.
  • Provides conceptual design, drafting and/or technical support to project groups.
  • Aptitude for working well in teams.
  • Ability to work independently.
  • Available for four months, starting September 2026
  • Eligible for North Atlantic Treaty Organization (NATO) Secret clearance in a timely manner.

Additional skills desired for the role

  • Experience with Electrical Computer Aided Design Software.
  • Basic understanding of the principles of shock and vibration, thermal management and electromagnetic interference and electromagnetic compliance management and how these environmental requirements are accommodated in product design.
  • Active security clearance will be considered an asset.
  • Results-Oriented Self-Starter Attitude.

About us

Headquartered in Ottawa, Lockheed Martin Canada is the Canadian unit of Lockheed Martin Corporation, a global defense technology company driving innovation and advancing scientific discovery. Our all-domain mission solutions and 21st Century Security® vision accelerate the delivery of transformative technologies to ensure those we serve always stay ahead of ready. We operate major facilities in Ottawa, Montreal, Halifax, Calgary and Victoria and work on a wide range of programs from advanced naval technology products, aircraft sustainment, and unmanned systems software. This position is part of the Rotary and Mission Systems business area, where employees across Canada support engineering, systems integration, training, sustainment and in-service support programs for Canadian and international military customers across land, air and sea.
