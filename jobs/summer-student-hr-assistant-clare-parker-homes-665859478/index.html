Full Description

Summer Student (Human Resource Assistant)

Anticipated Start Date: June 01, 2026

Number of Weeks: 09

Total Hours: 270

Schedule & Pay: 30 hours per week; $21.75 per hour

Because this role is dependent on Canada Summer Student Funding through Employment and Social Development, candidates will need to meet the following criteria:

· is between 16 and 30 years of age (inclusive) at the start of employment;

· was registered as a full-time student during the preceding academic year;

· intends to return to school on a full-time basis during the next academic year;

· is a student in a secondary, post-secondary, CEGEP (Quebec only), vocational or technical program;

· is a Canadian Citizen, permanent resident, or person on whom refugee protection has been conferred under the Immigration and Refugee Protection Act* and;

· is legally entitled to work according to the relevant provincial / territorial legislation and regulations

*Foreign students are not eligible.

GENERAL ACCOUNTABILITIY:

The CPH Summer Student (HR Assistant) is directly accountable to their HR Coordinator and indirectly to the Executive Director. He/she shall be directly accountable for providing administrative support to Human Resources department, working together with their Coordinator and the Payroll & Benefits Administrator to ensure that goals and objectives are met. Specifically, the Summer Student is responsible to:

1. Administrative Support:

  • General administrative support.
  • Maintain and update employee records and HR databases, including following up on criminal record checks and drivers abstracts.

2. Employee Surveys:

  • Assisting in the new design, distribution, and analysis of employee feedback surveys.

3. Employee Engagement Activities:

  • Organizing team building in agency or employee recognition event – could organize the years of service.
  • Supporting employee performance reviews.

4. Onboarding New Employees:

  • Helping to review & revise the orientation sessions for new recruits.
  • Schedule the orientation.

5. Recruitment:

  • Screen resumes and applications to identify suitable candidates for Willo.
  • Schedule structured interviews.
  • Post job advertisements on various platforms.

What skills will the participant develop during this job?

  • Adaptability
  • Communication
  • Creativity and Innovation
  • Digital skills
  • Problem solving
  • Technical skills

Good relations must be developed and maintained with the people we support, their network (family, friends, and personal networks), CPH management personnel and other co-supports at CPH.

The above statements reflect the general details considered necessary to describe the principal functions of the job and shall not be construed as a detailed description of all work assignments that may be inherent in the job.

PREFERRED QUALIFICATIONS:

· People wishing to enter the Human Resources field.

· Direct experience with or knowledge and belief in CPH philosophy and Gentle Teaching.

· Strong organizational and communication skills.

· Valid class 5 Driver’s License & access to a reliable vehicle.

* We highly recommend following candidates to apply:

  • Indigenous youth
  • Racialized youth
  • Black youth
  • 2SLGBTQI+ youth

Pay: $21.75 per hour
