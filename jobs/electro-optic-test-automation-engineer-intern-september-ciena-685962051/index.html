About the Role

Develop Matlab-based test automation for optical modems

About Ciena

Global leader in optical networking gear

Full Description

As the global leader in high-speed connectivity, Ciena is committed to a people-first approach. Our teams enjoy a culture focused on prioritizing a flexible work environment that empowers individual growth, well-being, and belonging. We’re a technology company that leads with our humanity—driving our business priorities alongside meaningful social, community, and societal impact.

The Opportunity 8 month work term: September 2026 – April 2027

12 month work term: September 2026 – August 2027 (preferred)

How You Will Contribute You will be joining the Coherent Modem Transmission Electro-Optic R&D group, a team responsible for developing the electro-optic transmission system for Ciena’s coherent optical modems.

In this role, you will be a key contributor in developing the robust code required to transition our cutting-edge designs from the lab into a high-volume manufacturing environment.

You Will

  • Develop and maintain Matlab-based software for the automated factory calibration and test of coherent optical modems.
  • Interface with a wide range of optical test and measurement equipment.
  • Work closely with hardware and manufacturing engineers to define test procedures, gather requirements, and validate your code.
  • Analyze test data to verify performance and support the deployment of your software into Ciena’s manufacturing facilities.

The Must Haves

  • Working towards a Bachelor's degree in Computer Engineering, Software Engineering, Electrical Engineering, Engineering Physics, Computer Science, or a related discipline.
  • Strong programming proficiency in Matlab or another language.
  • Demonstrated understanding of core software development principles, including version control (e.g., Git) and debugging.
  • Strong problem-solving skills and the ability to communicate technical concepts clearly.

Assets

  • Experience controlling hardware or test equipment programmatically.
  • Familiarity with optical communication systems or electro-optic devices.
