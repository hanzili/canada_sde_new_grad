  • Full-Time Assistant Financial Controller

Location: Surrey, BC, Canada

Employment: Full-time

Linus Media Group is a growing creative company looking for an Assistant Financial Controller who thrives in a fast-paced, collaborative environment. This role is ideal for someone who enjoys combining hands-on accounting with strategic financial support in a scaling organization.

The Assistant Financial Controller supports the leadership team by managing the daily operations of the accounting function and ensuring the integrity of financial reporting. You will help maintain strong internal controls, enhance accounting systems, and provide financial insight to support operational and strategic decision-making, while supporting an Accounting team of under 10.

As an Assistant Financial Controller, your responsibilities include, but are not limited to, the following:

Duties:

Accounting Operations

  • Oversee day-to-day accounting activities, ensuring compliance with established accounting methods, policies, and GAAP.
  • Supervise and review the work of accounting staff, providing guidance, training, and ongoing performance feedback.
  • Develop, implement, and improve systems and processes for collecting, analyzing, and reporting financial information.
  • Ensure accuracy and timeliness in month-end and year-end closings.

Financial Reporting and Analysis

  • Assist in the preparation of monthly, quarterly, and annual financial statements in accordance with applicable standards.
  • Partner with external accountants and auditors to support financial reviews and year-end audits.
  • Contribute to budgeting and forecasting activities on a monthly, quarterly and annual basis. Helping monitor and maintain alignment of financial plans with company goals and financial plan
  • Monitor and manage cash flow to maintain optimal liquidity and support business operations.

Compliance and Taxation

  • Maintain compliance with all tax filing and reporting requirements, including corporate income tax, sales tax, and tax incentive programs (e.g., SR&ED, film and digital media credits).
  • Support the finance team in preparing and submitting tax filings accurately and on time.
  • Coordinate with external advisors to manage film and digital media tax credits, SR&ED claims, and other incentive programs.

Cross-Functional Collaboration

  • Partner with production, operations, and management teams to ensure the accuracy and transparency of financial data.
  • Provide financial insights to help guide creative and operational decision-making.
  • Support internal discussions around corporate structure, efficiency improvements, and growth planning.

Continuous Improvement

  • Identify opportunities to streamline accounting and production finance processes using automation, AI and cloud tools.
  • Participate in special projects such as system implementations or new funding initiatives.
  • Champion best practices that enable scalability and transparency within the finance function.

Job Requirements:

  • Bachelor’s degree in Accounting, Finance, or a related field; BCIT Finance graduate or equivalent accepted.
  • Enrollment in the CPA program or recent CPA graduate preferred.
  • 3–5 years of progressive accounting experience in a small or mid-sized business environment.
  • Awareness of emerging workflow automation tools, or AI applications in accounting, including machine learning for financial analysis, document automation, and workflow optimization.
  • Previous experience in the film, media, or creative production industry is strongly preferred.
  • Advanced proficiency in QuickBooks, Microsoft Excel, and cloud-based productivity tools.
  • Strong analytical and problem-solving skills, with a proactive approach to continuous improvement.
  • Excellent interpersonal and communication skills; comfortable collaborating with both creative and technical teams.
  • Self-starter with the ability to manage multiple priorities and deadlines independently.
  • Positive, collaborative attitude and interest in contributing to the company’s long-term success.

Compensation:

For this position, the typical hiring salary base range falls between CAD $80,000 and $120,000 per year, based on market location and job-related qualifications such as knowledge, skills and experience. This role is also eligible for a discretionary bonus and additional benefits, which together make up the total compensation package, in line with our company’s philosophy to recognize and reward employee contributions.
