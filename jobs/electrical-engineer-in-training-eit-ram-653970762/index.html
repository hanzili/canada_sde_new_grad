Electrical Engineer-in-Training (EIT)

at RAM

About the Role

Assist electrical design and field implementation

About RAM

EGBC-accredited firm with mentorship for EITs

Full Description

RAM Consulting is a Canadian project delivery firm supporting some of North America’s most complex and impactful infrastructure projects. We specialize in engineering, project and construction management, and project support services across the marine, transportation, utilities, infrastructure, and energy sectors.

Our clients trust us to help deliver critical infrastructure that builds communities, supports economic growth, and improves everyday life. Everything we do is guided by a commitment to safety, integrity, and sustainability.

What sets RAM apart is our people. Our team brings diverse experience from owner, contractor, and consulting environments, giving us a practical, solutions-focused perspective at every stage of the project lifecycle. From early planning through to delivery, we work collaboratively with our clients and partners to get projects done right.

The Role

For this expression of interest (EOI), we are currently seeking an Electrical Engineer-in-Training (EIT) to support the design and delivery of overhead and underground electrical distribution infrastructure projects. This role provides strong exposure to engineering design, field work, and multidisciplinary collaboration early in your career.

RAM Consulting is proud to be an EGBC Accredited Employer. Through this program, EITs receive structured mentorship, clear competency development, and guidance from experienced Professional Engineers to support progression toward P.Eng. designation. Engineers at RAM gain exposure to real projects, practical design work, and construction support while building the experience required for professional registration.

This role is particularly well suited for a recent graduate or early-career engineer looking to develop strong technical foundations and learn directly from experienced engineers working on real infrastructure projects.

What You’ll Be Working On

  • Supporting the design of overhead and underground electrical distribution systems for infrastructure and utility projects
  • Preparing engineering calculations, cost estimates, and detailed design drawings
  • Applying relevant utility standards, CSA and IEEE codes, and regulatory requirements
  • Assisting with technical documentation including specifications, reports, and tender packages
  • Supporting construction activities by coordinating with contractors, engineers, and project teams to resolve field issues
  • Participating in site visits and field inspections to gather data and verify design feasibility
  • Assisting with as-built drawings, final calculations, and project close-out documentation
  • Collaborating with civil, structural, and environmental teams to integrate electrical systems into project designs

What We’re Looking For

  • Bachelor’s Degree in Electrical Engineering from an accredited program
  • EIT designation or eligibility for registration with Engineers and Geoscientists BC (EGBC) or APEGA
  • Strong understanding of electrical engineering fundamentals including power systems and control systems
  • Experience with CAD software for electrical design (AutoCAD or Revit; MicroStation considered an asset)
  • Familiarity with electrical analysis tools such as ETAP or SKM Power Tools is an asset
  • Strong analytical, communication, and teamwork skills with a proactive approach to learning

Compensation & Benefits

The estimated annual base salary for this position is $55,000 - $65,000 as of the posting date. Actual compensation within this range will be determined based on factors such as job-related skills, relevant experience, education, and training.

Please note that this range reflects base pay only and does not include the competitive bonus program or the comprehensive benefits package offered by RAM Consulting. RAM provides a strong total rewards package including extended health and dental coverage, paid vacation and sick time, statutory holidays, annual performance and salary reviews, tuition reimbursement, and career development opportunities.
