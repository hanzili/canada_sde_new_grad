Full Description

Junior Accountant

Panoptic Properties – Montréal, QC
Full-Time On-site · Commercial Real Estate

About Panoptic Properties
Panoptic Properties is a Montréal-based commercial real estate investment and management firm with a growing portfolio of office, retail, and industrial properties across Canada. We are committed to operational excellence, long-term value creation, and delivering exceptional service to our tenants and partners. As we continue to expand, we are seeking a motivated Junior Accountant to support the financial operations of our commercial portfolio.

Position Overview

The Junior Accountant plays a key role in supporting both the accounting and property management teams. This position includes day-to-day accounting tasks such as AP/AR, recoveries, reconciliations, and assisting with cash flow and budget preparation. The role also involves working closely with property managers to interpret lease terms, prepare rent rolls and lease masters, and support day‑to‑day operational and management needs.

Key ResponsibilitiesAccounting & Financial Operations

  • Process daily cash receipts and apply payments to tenant accounts accurately.
  • Handle full-cycle Accounts Payable: receive, code, and process commercial invoices (utilities, telecom, contractors, maintenance).
  • Manage Accounts Receivable, including issuing rent invoices, recoveries, and following up on outstanding balances.
  • Prepare monthly recoveries such as property taxes, utilities, insurance, operating costs (CAM), and other tenant chargebacks.
  • Conduct monthly bank reconciliations for multiple commercial properties.
  • Prepare and post journal entries, accruals, and adjustments during month-end close.
  • Assist with preparation of cash flow reports and support management in monitoring property-level liquidity.
  • Maintain accurate and organized financial records for each property.

Lease & Property Management Support

  • Collaborate with property managers to review commercial lease agreements and ensure billing aligns with lease terms.
  • Prepare and update rent rolls, lease abstracts, lease masters, and recovery masters.
  • Explain rent roll variances by referencing lease terms, amendments, and tenant changes.
  • Support the preparation of annual budgets, forecasts, and property operating statements.
  • Assist management with day-to-day tasks, reporting requirements, and ad‑hoc requests.
  • Maintain accuracy of tenant ledgers and ensure timely resolution of discrepancies.

Month-End & Reporting

  • Assist with month-end close for multiple entities.
  • Perform GL account reconciliations and prepare supporting schedules.
  • Support financial reporting for internal management and external stakeholders.

Compliance & Controls

  • Ensure compliance with internal controls, company accounting policies, and commercial lease provisions.
  • Assist in preparing documents for audits, reviews, and regulatory filings (GST/QST).

QualificationsEducation & Experience

  • Diploma or degree in Accounting, Finance, or a related field.
  • 0–3 years of experience in accounting or bookkeeping; experience in commercial real estate is an asset.

Technical Skills

  • Basic proficiency in Microsoft Excel (sorting, filtering, formulas, VLOOKUP preferred).
  • Familiarity with Sage accounting software or similar systems is an asset.
  • Understanding of GL, AP/AR, and reconciliation processes.

Professional Skills

  • Strong understanding of accounting fundamentals and attention to detail.
  • Ability to interpret and apply commercial lease terms to financial processes.
  • Strong organizational and time-management skills; ability to prioritize tasks.
  • Effective communication and collaboration skills when working with property managers, tenants, and vendors.
  • Ability to work independently while contributing to a team environment.
  • Flexibility during initial training to ensure smooth integration into the role.

Why Join Panoptic Properties?

  • Opportunity to gain hands-on experience in commercial real estate accounting.
  • Collaborative team environment with mentorship from experienced accountants and property managers.
  • Professional development and potential for internal growth.
  • Competitive compensation and benefits.

Pay: $30,000.00-$50,000.00 per year

  • Extended health care
  • Flexible schedule

  • Paid time off

Work Location: In person
