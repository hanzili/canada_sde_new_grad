About the Role

Perform electrical design tasks under supervision.

About McDermott

McDermott: global energy contractor with offshore focus

Full Description

Job Overview:

The Associate Electrical Engineer is responsible for utilizing existing Electrical procedures to solve routine or standard problems. They receive instruction, guidance, and direction from others within the team, and they will leverage their conceptual knowledge of theories, practices, and procedures related to Electrical Engineering.

Salary Range : $97,901 - $113,971. The range provided is for base salary contracted hours only. In addition, McDermott offers a competitive total rewards package, including project up-lift and bonuses.

Our ingenuity fuels daily life. Together, we’ve forged some of the most trusted partnerships across the energy value chain to make what was once just an idea a reality: laying subsea infrastructure thousands of feet below sea level, installing platforms hundreds of miles from shore, using our expertise to design and build offshore wind infrastructure, and reshaping the onshore landscape to deliver the energy products the world needs safely and sustainably.

For more than 100 years, we've been making the impossible possible. Today, we're driving the energy transition with more than 30,000 of the brightest minds across 54 countries.

Key Tasks and Responsibilities:

  • Develop an understanding of the primary responsibilities of Electrical Engineering across various project phases.
  • Carry out detailed engineering design, calculations, and analysis for basic technical tasks.
  • Understanding of industry standards IEEE, CSA CEC, NFPA, NEMA and related code.
  • Apply foundational knowledge and skills to complete routine work under close supervision and mentorship.
  • Prepare accurate and well-documented design calculations, analyses, reports, and procedures.
  • Manage and organize personal workload to meet deadlines established by others.
  • Communicate information clearly, ask questions when needed, and verify understanding.
  • Become familiar with all applicable quality processes and documentation systems.
  • Identify and specify basic Electrical equipment types based on process and project requirements, working under the guidance of the lead engineer.
  • Knowledge of Cable tray types and Electrical Cable including their proper installation and connection methods.
  • Demonstrate a solid understanding of Electrical systems and Equipment, including Switchgear, MCCs, Transformers, UPS and small power systems and related components.
  • Apply relevant procedures, industry practices, and international standards for the assigned Electrical tasks, ensuring alignment with project specifications.
  • Assist other engineering disciplines by providing necessary technical information as required.
  • Perform drafting-related tasks, including datasheet preparation and drawing updates or modifications.
  • Use effective communication skills to coordinate with site subcontractors and work toward resolving engineering issues under the guidance of the lead engineer.
  • Support the review of drawings, documents, and inspection and test procedures submitted by vendors and manufacturers.
  • Incorporate all applicable HSE requirements and criteria into engineering activities and decision-making

Essential Qualifications and Education:

  • Bachelor’s degree in Engineering ( Master’s Degree is preferred)
  • 0-3 years of experience in Oil and Gas
  • Preferably Registered Engineer in Training (EIT) or member of a professional engineering society
  • Good English in writing and speaking (B2 Level)
  • Quality-oriented and schedule conscious
  • Good communication skills and team player behavior
  • Show flexibility to assist colleagues and supervisors with increased workload
  • Learning the content and application of standards, codes, and guidelines as applicable
  • Learning design techniques and analysis methods
