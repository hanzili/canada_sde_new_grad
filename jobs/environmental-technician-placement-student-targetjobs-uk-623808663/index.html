Full Description

Are you passionate about the environment and technically minded with a practical approach to solving problems? Are you interested in pursuing a career in environmental science and data analysis? If so, this is an exciting opportunity for you to join our Environmental Investigations team as an Environmental Technician Placement Student.

What You'll Do As an Environmental Technician Placement Student, you'll become an integral part of our Environmental Investigations team. You will collaborate with Environmental Scientists and Project Managers to scope and deliver critical water quality investigations for the Water Industry National Environment Programme.

Whilst predominantly field-based, this role will also involve an element of office work, as you will help to review data from the samples you collect whilst assisting with quality control, mapping tasks, organising equipment, updating sample guides, and report writing.

You Will

  • assist in creating and implementing environmental monitoring plans, with a focus on water quality drivers such as bathing waters and eutrophication effects
  • engage and communicate with stakeholders, supporting the wider team in these efforts
  • conduct in-field monitoring activities, including water quality sampling, installing and maintaining monitoring instruments, river flow and groundwater level monitoring, ecological monitoring and sampling, and habitat and fish surveys
  • analyse and ensure the quality of existing and newly collected data, and prepare comprehensive reports for desk studies and final submissions to authorities like the Environment Agency
  • develop a personal development plan to enhance your industry-specific skills and overall employability throughout the year.

What You'll Need This role is an industrial placement to be completed as part of a university sandwich year. To be successful in this role, you will be studying towards a BSc in an environmental, science or math discipline. You will be well-organised, able to manage and prioritise your own workload, and comfortable working alone safely in both office and outdoor environments.

The Following Are Essential

  • a valid UK driving licence
  • the ability to swim
  • proficient use of Microsoft 365, e.g., PowerPoint, Outlook, Word, Excel
  • organisation and time management skills
  • the ability to effectively plan your own workload
  • effective communication and presentation skills
  • willingness to be outdoors in all weathers

The Following Are Beneficial

  • experience in data analysis and the use of R or Python as a method of statistical analysis and data visualisation
  • experience using ArcMap, ArcPro, or an equivalent GIS program
  • an understanding of environmental issues such as water abstraction, hydrology, pollution, conservation, and environmental legislation
  • a willingness to learn and develop on-the-job.

You do not need any prior knowledge of the water industry or water quality investigations for this role; you will be given the opportunity to complete a CIWEM-accredited ‚ÄòIntroduction to the water industry‚Äô course upon starting.
