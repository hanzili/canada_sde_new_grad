About the Role

Assist electrical design and power system analysis

About Stantec

Global design firm with strong EIT program

Full Description

As energy availability becomes increasingly critical, there’s no end to the scope and significance of your work — and to where it can take you at Stantec. Every day, we’re problem-solving and collaborating on projects that are as challenging as they are impactful.

Join the Stantec team to deliver innovation for the long term on some of the most important and interesting projects around the world. Our team works on a wide array of projects in areas including generation – thermal, hydropower and other renewable sources, substations, transmission and distribution, waste water, oil & gas, and mining. Seeing beyond the project at hand, you’ll work with us to advance the industry and build your career. We operate with the strength of a $8B company and have the agility of specialized groups to contribute to some of the most challenging and cutting-edge projects around the world. Whether helping to define microgrids, pushing the boundaries of clean energy or grid modernization, you’ll find yourself working with leaders in one of the world’s top 10 design firms, broadening your skills and developing your career.

Your Opportunity

The Fredericton Electrical team is looking to fill the role of an Electrical Engineer-in-Training. Reporting to the Electrical Manager, the Electrical EIT will work under the supervision of a Senior Engineer to develop skills and gain experience in performing engineering design and/or power system simulation and analysis.

The successful candidate will perform a variety of tasks which may include assisting with the design of transmission and distribution systems as well as the preparation of preliminary design and technical specifications for power cable installations. The EIT may also assist in the modelling, simulation, and analysis of power systems for planning and feasibility purposes. The candidate should have an understanding of power system protection concepts and common protection schemes as they relate to low voltage and medium voltage distribution systems.

Your Key Responsibilities

  • Perform calculations to analyze and design electrical system components using industry standard software and proprietary manufacturer software
  • Prepare reports that present technical and project information in a clear and concise manner and meet client requirements
  • Assist in the preparation of electrical design packages
  • Coordinate with designers and engineers of other disciplines to complete deliverables
  • Interpret and incorporate vendor information into design deliverables
  • Assist in the preparation of technical, commercial, and construction specifications
  • Work from schematics, sketches, and verbal instructions
  • Support the project team in development of design, documentation, plans and reports for relevant project design elements
  • Perform thorough quality control of construction documents and provide innovative solutions

Your Capabilities and Credentials

  • Preliminary knowledge of the NEC, CEC, and industry standards such as IEEE, ANSI, NFPA, and CSA
  • Proficient in Microsoft (MS) Office Suite, Bluebeam/Adobe Acrobat and AutoCAD
  • Experience with power system software such as ETAP is desired.
  • Some familiarity with designing low voltage systems such as AC and DC systems, cable and raceway systems, security and surveillance systems
  • Ability to create Single Line Diagrams and elevation & layout drawings
  • Ability to understand and interpret vendor information.
  • Recognize when technical problems are developing and initiate appropriate corrective actions.
  • Willing and able to travel occasionally when required.
  • Self-motivated, team-oriented, efficient, and a good communicator.
  • Strong organizational and time management skills.
  • Excellent communication abilities with a keen eye for detail. Ability to work with others in a collaborative team environment.
  • Able to work under and meet tight deadlines.
  • Help with continuous improvement and streamlining processes and activities.

Education and Experience

  • Bachelor’s degree in electrical engineering.
  • 0 - 2 years of experience.
  • Ability to manage multiple priorities and timelines.
  • Strong verbal and written communication skills.
  • Strong interpersonal skills to effectively work and collaborate with a range of internal and external customers.

Office Location and Schedule

  • Typical office environment, working with computers and drawings for long periods of time. Field work may include exposure to hazards including construction, weather, and industrial operations that may be experienced at the infrastructure facilities.
  • Location – Fredericton.
