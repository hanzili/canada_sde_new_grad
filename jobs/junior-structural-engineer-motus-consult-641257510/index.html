Junior Structural Engineer

About Motus Consult

Full Description

Location: Langley, BC Office;

About Us

Motus Consult Ltd. is a structural and building enclosure engineering firm with offices in BC, Alberta, and Ontario. We specialize in Building Enclosure, Restoration, Structural, and Construction Engineering across a wide range of sectors, including commercial, industrial, institutional, residential, and agricultural projects. Our work spans from restoring and maintaining existing buildings to designing new structures.

As a smaller firm, we pride ourselves on close collaboration, both within our team and with our clients, to deliver innovative and creative solutions. We partner with clients from project ideation to completion, ensuring exceptional results every step of the way.

‍Position Overview

Motus Consult is seeking a Junior Structural Engineer to join our BC office (Langley). This is an excellent opportunity for a recent engineering graduate or EIT with 1–3 years of experience to develop expertise in structural engineering design, analysis, and construction support. You will work alongside experienced engineers on diverse projects, gaining hands-on experience in structural design for new buildings, existing building assessments, field reviews, and construction administration while building a strong foundation in structural engineering principles.

Key Responsibilities
1. Structural Design and Analysis

  • Assist senior engineers with structural analysis including load calculations, stability assessments, and code compliance checks using industry-standard software (e.g., SAP2000, S-Frame, Revit).
  • Support the design of structural components and systems for building projects ranging from single-family residential to multi-storey commercial and institutional buildings.
  • Participate in the assessment and design of structural systems for existing buildings including renovations, upgrades, and structural repairs.
  • Prepare structural calculations, drawings, and technical documentation under the supervision of senior engineers.
  • Ensure designs comply with the Provincial Codes, the National Building Code of Canada (NBC), and relevant CSA standards (S16, A23.3, O86).
  • Learn and apply structural engineering principles for timber, steel, concrete, and masonry structural systems.

2. Construction Support and Field Review

  • Conduct site visits and field inspections to observe construction progress and verify compliance with structural design intent.
  • Document site conditions through written notes, photographs, and field sketches.
  • Assist with reviewing shop drawings, submittals, and contractor requests for information (RFIs).
  • Support senior engineers in addressing and resolving structural issues that arise during construction.
  • Prepare field inspection reports and observations.
  • Monitor construction quality and gain understanding of material installation methods and construction sequencing.
  • Work safely on active construction sites, including at heights using ladders and mechanical lifts as required.

3. Technical Documentation and Project Coordination

  • Prepare structural drawings using AutoCAD and/or Revit, coordinating with architectural and other engineering disciplines.
  • Develop technical specifications for structural components and systems.
  • Prepare engineering documents, structural assessment reports, technical memoranda, and feasibility studies under the supervision of senior engineers.
  • Assist with project coordination activities including attending project meetings and communicating with clients, architects, and contractors.
  • Support project documentation including tracking RFIs, change orders, and construction-phase correspondence.
  • Maintain organized project files and documentation in accordance with professional practice standards.

4. Professional Development and Learning

  • Develop technical knowledge in structural engineering design for various building types and structural systems.
  • Gain understanding of seismic design principles and lateral load resisting systems.
  • Learn building code requirements and standard of care for structural engineering practice
  • Work toward gaining engineering experience that meets PEO requirements for P.Eng. registration.
  • Participate in professional development opportunities, training sessions, and industry events.
  • Receive mentorship and technical guidance from senior engineers to advance your engineering skills.

Qualifications

  • Bachelor's degree in Civil or Structural Engineering from an accredited university.
  • Registered as an Engineer-in-Training (EIT) or eligible for registration.
  • 2–5 years of relevant experience in structural engineering for building design.
  • Basic understanding of structural engineering principles, load paths, and structural behavior.
  • Familiarity with structural systems including timber, steel, concrete, and masonry (training will be provided).
  • Proficiency in AutoCAD for drafting; experience with Revit and structural analysis software (SAP2000, S-Frame) is an asset.
  • Understanding of relevant building codes is essential.
  • Technical writing skills for preparing reports and documentation.

Personal Qualities and Competencies:

  • Strong desire to learn and develop expertise in structural engineering and building design.
  • Self-motivated with excellent attention to detail and organizational skills.
  • Effective written and verbal communication skills.
  • Ability to work both independently and collaboratively as part of a multidisciplinary team.
  • Positive attitude and willingness to take on new challenges and responsibilities.
  • Strong problem-solving abilities and analytical thinking.
  • Professional demeanor when interacting with clients, contractors, and team members.
  • Adaptable and able to manage multiple tasks and shifting priorities.
  • Commitment to safety and adherence to safety protocols on construction sites.

Why Work With Us?

  • Competitive salary commensurate with experience.
  • Comprehensive benefits package.
  • Mentorship from experienced structural engineers.
  • Hands-on learning opportunities across a diverse range of building types and project scopes.
  • Support for professional development and pursuit of P.Eng. designation.
  • Exposure to both new construction design and existing building assessment/restoration work.
  • Collaborative and supportive work environment in a growing firm.
  • Opportunities for career advancement as you develop your skills and expertise.
  • Involvement in meaningful projects that improve building performance and sustainability.
