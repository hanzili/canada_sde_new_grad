Full Description

Job Description
CMDC is seeking a Structural Engineer for our Saskatoon, SK office in the role of Masonry Design Engineer. This is a unique opportunity to work with Canada’s leading national technical authority in masonry construction, design, education, and codes and standards development.

As a contractor funded, contractor focused association with members in all 10 provinces and 8 offices across Canada, CMDC offers technically challenging, cutting edge, and field defining engineering work with direct and immediate impact on the masonry industry.

A role with CMDC offers a career path that is distinct from traditional engineering consulting. Our work combines high level technical engagement with strong industry relationships, meaningful influence, and excellent work life balance. We are looking for an individual with strong presentation skills who is outgoing, professional, and able to build lasting relationships with contractor members as well as with the local engineering and architectural communities.

Your Responsibilities

1. Industry outreach and technical support
Support and maintain strong relationships with members of SaskMasonry (SKM) by providing technical advice, delivering presentations, engaging in public speaking, and building connections within the local contractor, construction, and design communities. This will also include supporting research and educational initiatives at the University of Saskatchewan through the Saskatchewan Centre for Masonry Design (SCMD), advocating for the use of masonry construction with local public and private stakeholders, and delivering educational seminars for designers.

2. Local association support
Work in person within the SKM office to support the effective day to day operations of the association. This will include working with the SKM office administrator to help plan local activities such as golf tournaments, design awards, and association meetings, while also contributing to creative marketing strategies that promote the benefits of masonry construction.

3. National technical collaboration
Assist CMDC’s team of engineers across Canada in providing technical support to engineers, architects, and masonry contractors on the design and construction of masonry structures. This includes technical writing, explaining complex concepts clearly by phone and email, and helping to resolve issues and conflicts that arise on construction projects.

4. Broader CMDC initiatives
Contribute, both independently and collaboratively, to broader national CMDC initiatives as opportunities arise. This may include supporting codes and standards development, assisting with the development of MASS software, contributing to technical writing and review, supporting educational courses and seminars, attending meetings and conferences, and developing and delivering technical presentations.

The Skills and Experience We Are Seeking

  • Bachelor of Engineering degree in civil, structural, or a related field
  • Post graduate education and/or coursework related to masonry materials or masonry design is considered an asset
  • Experience working with masonry, or in construction and inspection, is considered an asset
  • Registration as an Engineer in Training (EIT), or designation as a Professional Engineer (P.Eng.) in the Province of Saskatchewan, is required
  • Ability to work effectively both independently and as part of a team
  • Demonstrated flexibility and adaptability in responding to changing priorities
  • Excellent oral and written communication skills in English
  • Proficiency with Microsoft Office
  • Experience with AutoCAD, structural analysis or finite element software such as RAM Suite, SAP2000, ETABS, STAAD, or S-Frame, and WordPress or website development is considered an asset
  • Hands on experience in construction, applied research, or material testing is considered an asset
  • A valid driver’s licence, along with the ability and eagerness to visit construction sites and meet with contractors, masons, site engineers, and other industry stakeholders

About the Job

  • In person work in a small office environment located at 701 Broadway Avenue, Saskatoon, SK
  • Periodic travel to Regina, approximately 10 times per year
  • Permanent full time position, 37.5 hours per week
  • Group health insurance benefits, group disability benefits, and an RRSP matching program

About CMDC
The Canada Masonry Design Centre exists to encourage the effective use of masonry through research, education, and technical support. CMDC is unique in its mandate, providing services not only to its contractor members, but also to the broader design and construction community.

CMDC is an association created and funded by for profit masonry contractor members to help bridge the gap between the design community and the masonry construction industry. Our support includes providing design advice, helping resolve job site issues, delivering educational seminars for both contractors and designers, and developing technical resources such as structural design software, textbooks for students and professionals, and instructional materials for university educators. CMDC is also an active participant in the development of building codes and CSA standards.

CMDC plays a unique role in information sharing and industry advancement across many areas important to its members. These include apprenticeship training, university and college research, codes and standards development, design aids, education and seminars, and direct technical support to designers and contractors.
