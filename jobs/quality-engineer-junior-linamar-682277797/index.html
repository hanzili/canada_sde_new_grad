Full Description

Job Title: Quality Engineer, Junior

Job Summary

The Junior Quality Engineer works with production and engineering teams to maintain high quality standards by designing inspection procedures, developing control plans, and guiding problem-solving. They also handle PPAP documentation, ensure compliance, and address quality concerns with customers and suppliers.

Responsibility

  • Assist with creating and managing essential quality documents, such as Process Control Plans, Work Instructions, Gauge Instructions, etc.
  • Support the training of production employees on new products, including the use of gauges, fixtures, and inspection procedures.
  • Help address and resolve customer concerns, which may involve preparing presentations or reports.
  • Contribute to the completion of Customer Production Part Approval Process (PPAP) packages.
  • Participate in meetings to help identify root cause and assist in implementing corrective actions for quality issues.
  • Help manage the corrective action process and maintain the Customer/Supplier Concern Log.
  • Assist with developing PPAP submissions from suppliers.
  • Collaborate with core team on ongoing continuous improvement initiatives.
  • Assist Quality Assurance team to ensure compliance with customer requirements and quality system.
  • Support the completion of dimensional reports, capability studies and measurement systems analysis.
  • Perform internal/external audits as per internal audit requirements. Audits are inclusive of LPA, system audits and manufacturing process audits.
  • Work with various departments to help resolve manufacturing issues and improve processes.
  • Ensure compliance with Customer Specific Requirements.

Academic/Educational Requirements

  • Post Secondary bachelor’s degree in engineering in relevant field (Manufacturing, Quality, Industrial).

Required Skills/Experience

  • Minimum 6 months of experience in the Quality Assurance department within the automotive industry in a similar role.
  • Working knowledge of APQP, PPAP, PCP, PFMEA,8D, MSA, GD&T, Gauge R&R and metrology.
  • Ability to interpret standards and customer requirements to ensure compliance.
  • Intermediate ability to read and interpret blueprint drawings and GD&T symbols.
  • Familiarity with Statistical Process Control (SPC) concepts and able to drive data driven process improvements.
  • Basic understanding of metallurgical principles.
  • Strong verbal and written communication skills, with the ability to create and deliver presentations.
  • Ability to prepare quality assurance reports detailing manufacturing issues, corrective actions, and preventative measures.
  • Proficiency in Microsoft Word, Excel, PowerPoint and Outlook.
  • Ability to learn new software applications.
