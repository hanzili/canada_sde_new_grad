Hardware & Test Engineering Co-op – High-Speed I/O Connectors (AI/ML) Location: Markham

Department: High-Speed IO Core Development

Position Overview Amphenol is seeking a hands-on Engineering Co-op student to support laboratory testing, hardware setup design, and fixture development for front-end I/O connectors used in next-generation AI and Machine Learning (ML) platforms.

This role focuses on practical engineering in a lab environment, including building and validating test setups, supporting high-speed measurements, and developing custom fixtures for reliable and repeatable testing.

Key Responsibilities Laboratory Testing & Validation

  • Execute electrical, mechanical, thermal and environmental testing of high-speed connector systems.
  • Operate lab equipment such as VNAs, oscilloscopes, TDR systems, and thermal equipment.
  • Collect and analyze test data to evaluate performance.
  • Troubleshoot test issues and support resolution.

Test Hardware & Setup Design

  • Design and assemble test setups for connector validation.
  • Design hardware and system test solutions.
  • Support development of test boards, breakout interfaces, and cable assemblies.
  • Optimize setups for signal integrity and measurement accuracy.
  • Translate test requirements into practical hardware solutions.

Fixture & Tooling Design

  • Design custom mechanical fixtures for testing.
  • Create CAD models and drawings for lab hardware.
  • Support fabrication and iteration of fixtures.
  • Ensure precision, durability, and usability.

Documentation & Lab Operations

  • Document test procedures, setups, and results.
  • Maintain organized lab records and reports.
  • Support improvements in lab workflows and processes.

Cross-Functional Collaboration

  • Work with electrical, mechanical, and product engineers.
  • Support validation tied to development programs.
  • Assist in design reviews and technical discussions.

Required Qualifications

  • Pursuing a Bachelor’s degree in Electrical, Mechanical, or Mechatronics Engineering.
  • Strong interest in hands-on lab work.
  • Basic understanding of circuits and mechanical design.
  • Familiarity with CAD tools is an asset.
  • Strong problem-solving and attention to detail.

Preferred Skills

  • Experience with lab instrumentation.
  • Exposure to high-speed or RF concepts.
  • Hands-on prototyping or soldering experience.
  • Familiarity with PCB or test board design.
  • Experience building fixtures or setups.

What You’ll Gain

  • Experience in engineering lab environments.
  • Exposure to high-speed interconnect validation.
  • Skills in fixture design and hardware debugging.
  • Mentorship on advanced hardware platforms.
