About the Role

About Ecoscape Environmental Consultants Ltd.

Environmental consulting firm focused on impact

Full Description

Ecoscape is a team of enthusiastic, highly talented individuals providing professional environmental

services from our Kelowna and Nelson, BC offices. We are respected industry leaders and set the

benchmark for excellence and innovation in environmental consulting. At the heart of our culture lies

integrity, innovation and hard work that empowers our team to tackle environmental challenges, deliver

practical results and drive meaningful change for our clients and communities. We provide a broad range

of environmental services for our clients which allows for numerous opportunities to learn and grow within

our team on a variety of projects. Potentially interested in working for a company that allows you to learn

and grow in a fast-paced, challenging work environment? If so, check out Ecoscape website at www.ecoscapeltd.com.

We are currently seeking applications for a Junior Biologist to work from our Kelowna office, where we offer

the following:

Why Join Ecoscape?

If you're looking for a role where you can grow, make an impact, and thrive in a dynamic, fast-paced

environment, Ecoscape is the place for you. Here’s what we offer:

  1. Modern Work Environment: A brand new, pet-friendly office conveniently situated just off the

Okanagan Rail Trail near the Kelowna International Airport.

  1. Flexibility: Work-life balance with schedules and opportunities to work from home.

  2. Competitive Wage and Benefits: Enjoy a comprehensive package, including competitive wages,

health and dental benefits, equipment allowance, and employer RRSP/TFSA matching.

Your role will involve:

• Preparing technical reports such as environmental assessments and developing strategies to mitigate potential impacts to the environment

• Interacting and engaging with clients

• Collecting environmental data in terrestrial and freshwater settings

• Championing health and safety requirements

• Writing environmental management and protection plans and environmental effects assessments

• Collaborating with industry, government, and First Nations to achieve beneficial environmental outcomes

• Contributing to proposals and business development activities

Think you have what it takes to be part of the Ecoscape team? Please make sure you have at least a few of the following skills:

• Working knowledge of fish, wildlife and ecosystems of the southern interior of BC, with preference given to those with a background in aquatic sciences • Some understanding of environmental permitting and associated environmental construction monitoring of small or large infrastructure projects

• Knowledge or experience with engineering and construction practices

• Strong written and verbal communication skills and excellent time management (examples may be requested)

• Highly motivated to self-train, improve your skillsets and quickly/effectively take on greater responsibility

• Preference given to those with some consulting or similar experience or who are eligible for grant programs like ECO Canada

• Candidates must possess or be eligible for a professional designation with the College of Applied Biology or the British Columbia Institute of Agrology

• Additional certifications such as backpack electrofishing, Riparian Areas Protection Regulation, Environmental Monitoring, Erosion and Sediment Control, Swift Water Rescue Technician, Small Vessel Operator Proficiency (SVOP) and/or Marine Emergency Duty (MEDA3)

• A strong aptitude for working outdoors in challenging environments is an asset

Compensation:

Salary: $58,000 - $68,000 dependent on applicant’s education, knowledge, skills, and experience.

Ready to Apply?

If this sounds like your ideal job, we’d love to hear from you! Submit your resume and cover letter detailing

your experience to ecoscape@ecoscapeltd.com or drop it off in person.

We thank all those interested in this opportunity; however, only those short-listed will be contacted. Short-

listed candidates can expect a virtual or in-person interview at our Kelowna office. We will consider

reasonable travel expenses following a pre-screening phone interview.

Be part of a team that’s making a difference – for the environment, for our communities, and for your

career. Join Ecoscape today!

Preference will be given to candidates who can start on or before April 1, 2026.
