Full Description

Job Summary

KMT Fuels is seeking a detail-oriented and motivated Accounting Intern to support our finance team in managing the day-to-day accounting operations of a fuel transportation and tanker business. This role provides hands-on experience in both accounting and bookkeeping functions, with a stronger emphasis on financial analysis, reporting, and industry-specific compliance.

The Accounting Intern will assist in maintaining accurate financial records, tracking fuel-related costs, supporting invoicing and receivables, and ensuring compliance with transportation and fuel tax regulations. This role is ideal for individuals looking to build a career in accounting within the logistics, transportation, and energy sectors.

Duties

  • Assist in preparing financial statements including income statements, balance sheets, and cash flow reports
  • Compile and analyze data during the month end reporting process, track information, and support the company or client.
  • Analyze operating costs including fuel, maintenance, and driver expenses
  • Track and reconcile fuel purchases and fuel card transactions
  • Assist with monthly financial statements and reporting
  • Assist with audit preparation and documentation
  • Maintain compliance with transportation regulations and financial reporting requirements
  • Work closely with dispatch, operations, and drivers to verify financial data

Qualifications

  • Currently enrolled in a Bachelor’s degree or diploma program in Accounting, Finance, Business Administration, or a related field
  • Completion of introductory accounting courses (financial and/or managerial accounting) is preferred
  • Foundational knowledge of accounting principles, including financial statement preparation and general ledger processes
  • Strong proficiency in Microsoft Excel (e.g., formulas, data organization, basic analysis)]
  • Good organizational and time management skills
  • Strong attention to detail with a high degree of accuracy in data entry and financial reporting
  • Ability to handle sensitive and confidential financial information with professionalism and discretion
  • Demonstrated willingness to develop knowledge in industry-specific areas, including fuel tax reporting (IFTA), cost tracking and analysis by vehicle, route, or customer, and fuel expense monitoring and overall operational cost analysis
