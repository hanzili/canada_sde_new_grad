Full Description

Overview Please note that this job posting is intended for you to express your interest in future opportunities within AtkinsRéalis

We are seeking engineers, scientists, and technologists to join our Site Assessment and Remediation (SAR) team. This role supports our local environmental group on a variety of projects within site assessment, remediation, and environmental monitoring. Reporting to a Project Manager or Coordinator, you will work closely with experienced environmental professionals and gain valuable hands‑on experience across diverse project environments early in your engineering or environmental career.

Your Role

  • To work directly with Project Managers and project team members to meet client deliverables on time and on budget
  • To conduct local and remote field work on diverse sites while maintaining a strong focus on health, safety, environment, and community
  • To execute field programs, with appropriate training, including drilling investigations, soil and sediment sampling, groundwater and soil vapour monitoring and sampling, remedial excavations, and environmental monitoring
  • To coordinate and oversee contractors during field activities
  • To calibrate, operate, and maintain field instruments and equipment
  • To collect field data and prepare detailed documentation such as monitoring data, field notes, site sketches, photographs, and GPS data
  • To submit field data and prepare summaries for project team members

About You

  • Ability to apply a bachelor's degree or technical diploma in Environmental acScience, Geoscience, Engineering, Environmental or Geological Engineering, or a related discipline
  • Aptitude for field‑based environmental or engineering work, with 1 to 2 years of experience or recent graduate status
  • Proficiency in Microsoft Office applications and working knowledge of ArcGIS
  • Ability to perform physically demanding field work, including lifting up to 40 lbs, working in varying climatic conditions, and traveling to remote locations
  • Ability to communicate effectively, manage time efficiently, and work collaboratively within multidisciplinary teams
  • Valid Class 5 BC driver's license.
