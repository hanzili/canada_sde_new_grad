Celestica is a global electronics manufacturing services company, headquartered in Toronto, Canada, with over 28,000 employees across 40 sites in North America, Europe, and Asia.

Working with the world’s leading technology companies across a broad range of industries, Celestica delivers a full suite of services – from design and engineering, to manufacturing and supply chain management – to help its customers develop and manufacture products that are transforming the global economy. These products span key areas such as data centre technology, green energy, healthtech, and autonomous vehicles.

Working at Celestica is more than a job. It’s about being part of something bigger and shaping the future with your work. You’ll take on exciting and challenging projects, get hands-on experience, collaborate with a global team, and learn from some of the greatest minds in the industry. And just as we aspire to imagine, develop and deliver a better future with our customers, we’re just as committed to delivering the best employee experience.

About This Opportunity The Process and Manufacturing Engineer Intern will solve technical process, product, or component problems affecting efficient manufacturing of electronic assemblies within a specific area of responsibility, from introduction of new products to existing processes. We are looking for individuals who are willing to enjoy a stimulating and challenging work environment with opportunities to develop skills, while working in a highly motivated team. Continuous improvement and implementation of world-class manufacturing techniques are important aspects of this responsibility. Knowledge and experience in lean manufacturing is an asset.

Preferred Skills

  • Team player who possesses good communication skills (written and verbal)
  • Basic manufacturing system knowledge
  • MS Office (Excel, PowerPoint)
  • Knowledge of web-based coding
  • Knowledge of CAD software such as SolidWorks, 3D printing is an asset
  • Ability to work within a fast-paced environment
  • Ability to learn quickly and adapt to situations
  • Takes initiative, create opportunities for themselves
  • Organized, ability to deliver on commitments
  • Excellent problem solving skills
  • Takes initiative and creates opportunities for themselves

Activities

  • Project coordination and customer interfacing
  • Design of experiments, tools, and fixtures
  • Support the development and updating of assembly work instructions
  • Improving quality and efficiency of the manufacturing process; process optimization
  • Engineering Support of Shop Floor Control Tool
  • Manufacturing technical support for new and existing products
  • Flexibility to work overtime and on weekends as required

Physical Demands, duties of this position are varied and will include:

  • Duties of this position are performed in a normal office environment.
  • Duties may require extended periods of sitting and sustained visual concentration on a computer monitor or on numbers and other detailed data.
  • Repetitive manual movements (e.g., data entry, using a computer mouse, using a calculator, etc.) are frequently required.
