About the Role

Develop electrical designs; model power systems; field support

About MAKA Power

Mentor-focused EIT program with practical projects

Full Description

Company Overview

Maka Engineering is a locally owned and operated firm and a subsidiary of Maka Power. Our unique structure allows our engineering team to work directly alongside electricians, technicians, and project managers. We offer a hands-on environment where you can work on diverse projects, from equipment maintenance and testing to arc-flash studies and design-build services. We are looking for an initiative-taking EIT, P.Eng., or Engineering Technologist (CET) who enjoys a mix of technical modelling and practical application.

  • Primary Work Location: 15 Ellen Louise Drive, Headingley, MB (Hybrid Eligible)
  • Hours: Full-time (40 hours per week)
  • Reports To: Principal Engineer

Key Responsibilities

  • Power Systems Analysis: Execute arc-flash, short-circuit, and coordination studies.
  • Technical Reporting: Process field data and identify equipment deficiencies or remediation actions.
  • Engineering Design: Develop electrical drawing packages for commercial and industrial clients.
  • Project Estimating: Support the creation of fee proposals and project scopes.
  • Field Services Technical Support: Support field service, commissioning, and maintenance activities through testing coordination, troubleshooting, and technical analysis.

Key Deliverables

  • Accurate Modelling: Develop reliable digital twins of electrical systems in EasyPower or ETAP.
  • Quality Documentation: Produce clear, actionable reports for arc-flash and maintenance testing.
  • Design Excellence: Ensure AutoCAD packages meet all provincial and national code requirements.
  • Timeline Management: Meet internal and client deadlines for project deliverables and proposals.

Detailed Responsibilities

Power Systems Modelling & Design

  • Perform arc-flash modelling using EasyPower or ETAP.
  • Develop engineering designs for industrial and commercial customers using AutoCAD.
  • Conduct protective device coordination and short-circuit analysis.
  • Identify electrical deficiencies and recommend specific remediation actions based on study findings.
  • Co-ordinate with on-site technicians to confirm data collection accuracy

Reporting & Data Analysis

  • Review and interpret field testing data from low and high-voltage apparatus.
  • Create comprehensive maintenance and acceptance testing reports using PowerDB.
  • Documenting and reporting testing results.

Field Services Technical Support

  • Support and perform site commissioning and maintenance testing activities on low and medium voltage apparatus
  • Define and coordinate testing scope, procedures, and requirements
  • Assist with troubleshooting, diagnostics, and root cause analysis of field issues
  • Interpret and analyze test results to ensure compliance with specifications and standards
  • Coordinate with operations, engineering, and contractors during field activities
  • Ensure all work is performed in accordance with safety standards and procedures (LOTO, PPE, etc.)
  • Prepare and maintain accurate field reports, test documentation, and commissioning records
  • Support equipment energization and post-commissioning verification

Proposals & Estimating

  • Scoping and quoting incoming engineering projects.
  • Review client requirements to estimate the engineering hours required for studies and designs.
  • Collaborate with the team to ensure technical proposals accurately reflect project needs.

Qualifications

  • Education: Degree in Electrical Engineering or a Diploma in Electrical Engineering Technology.
  • Registration: Eligible for registration as an EIT, P.Eng., or CET in Manitoba. Registration in Saskatchewan or Alberta is considered an asset.
  • Software Proficiency: Experience with EasyPower, ETAP, and AutoCAD.
  • Experience: Previous exposure to power systems, maintenance testing, or electrical design is preferred but not essential.

What Maka Engineering Offers

  • Direct Mentorship: Work directly alongside P.Eng. on day-to-day projects.
  • Team Environment: Collaborate closely with electricians and technicians on projects.
  • Variety of Work: Frequent opportunities to work on different types of projects and expand your skill set.
  • Professional Development: Full support for EITs and CETs seeking professional licensing and company-sponsored training.
  • Benefits: Group health and dental and RRSP matching.
  • Flexible hybrid schedule

Competitive Salary: Compensation that corresponds to your level of experience.
