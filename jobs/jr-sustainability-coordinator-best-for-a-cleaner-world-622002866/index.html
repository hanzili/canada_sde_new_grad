About BEST - For a Cleaner World

Full Description

ABOUT US:
Established in 1956, BEST is a Canadian janitorial company that aims to create a cleaner world by focusing on communities' social, environmental, and economic impact. We have been recognized by Deloitte as one of Canada’s Best Managed Companies and by Waterstone Human Capital as one of Canada’s 10 Most Admired Corporate Cultures.

When you join the BEST team, you’ll enjoy:

  • Flexible and hybrid work schedules for head office positions
  • Competitive wages and benefits packages
  • An excellent company culture, with access to educational grants and regular team events and celebrations
  • Employee recognition and appreciation programs
  • Opportunities for career advancement, on-the-job training, and upskilling within a quickly growing company

JOB OVERVIEW:
BEST is looking for a Junior Sustainability Coordinator to implement sustainability-friendly programs, and oversee environmental waste audits for the assigned site across Ontario. The ideal candidate is a self-motivated individual who works both independently and with a team to achieve environmental program initiatives and ensures compliance to related policies and procedures.

Job Location: London, ON
Shift Type: Full time
Shift Days: Monday- Friday
Shift Hours: 9:00am - 4:30pm
Salary: $40,000
VACANCY STATUS: This posting is for an existing vacant position

Duties and Responsibilities:

  • Monitor and collect data from campus waste, recycling, and other locations in order to improve waste reduction.
  • Collect, analyze and report on data related to client and internal sustainability initiatives.
  • Coordinate annual waste audit and report on findings.
  • Promote recycling, composting and waste education to internal and external stakeholders.
  • Participate at information booths and other education and awareness events.
  • Support the development of a culture focused on waste diversion and a commitment to sustainability.
  • Other duties as reasonably required from time to time.

Knowledge and Skills Required:

  • Environmental Science or related diploma or degree.
  • 1-2 years' experience in related experience is considered an asset
  • Strong communication skills (verbal and written).
  • Intermediate computer and smart phone knowledge with experience using MS Office programs.
  • Ability to effectively analyze, plan, organize, prioritize, and meet deadlines.
  • Multilingual ability considered an asset.
  • Valid driver’s license in Ontario.
  • All potential candidates must pass security and criminal record checks.
