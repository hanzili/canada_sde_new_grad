Full Description

Company Description UHN is Canada’s #1 hospital and the world’s #1 publicly funded hospital. With 10 sites and more than 44,000 TeamUHN members, UHN consists of Toronto General Hospital, Toronto Western Hospital, Princess Margaret Cancer Centre, Toronto Rehabilitation Institute, The Michener Institute of Education and West Park Healthcare Centre. As Canada's top research hospital, the scope of biomedical research and complexity of cases at UHN have made it a national and international source for discovery, education and patient care. UHN has the largest hospital-based research program in Canada, with major research in neurosciences, cardiology, transplantation, oncology, surgical innovation, infectious diseases, genomic medicine and rehabilitation medicine. UHN is a research hospital affiliated with the University of Toronto.

UHN’s vision is to build A Healthier World and it’s only because of the talented and dedicated people who work here that we are continually bringing that vision closer to reality.

www.uhn.ca

Role Summary We are seeking summer students to support a large‑scale environmental scan of UHN’s outpatient clinics. The students will help build a clear, standardized understanding of how UHN’s 320+ outpatient clinics operate, supporting future planning, analysis, and system improvement initiatives.

Key Responsibilities

  • Conduct comprehensive environmental and visual scans of outpatient clinic spaces
  • Observe and document physical layouts, wayfinding, patient flow, and use of space
  • Shadow clinic areas to understand workflows and day‑to‑day operations
  • Perform audits, assessments, and data collection activities
  • Review and analyze operational and access data (e.g., Epic, clinic volumes, phone line data)
  • Track and document clinic scheduling structures
  • Support current‑state process mapping and operational documentation
  • Interview and engage clinic staff to understand clinic operations and practices
  • Compile findings into standardized, clear, and digestible formats (e.g., templates, summaries, maps, and data sets)
  • Identify operational variations, themes, gaps

Qualifications

  • Applicants must be currently enrolled in an accredited high-school or post-secondary institution at the time of application.
  • Experience with Epic EMR (Electronic Medical Record) would be an asset.
