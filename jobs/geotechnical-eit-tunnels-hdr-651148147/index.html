We believe transportation is more than movement, it’s the foundation of connected, thriving communities. As part of HDR’s Transportation Business Group, you’ll help shape the systems that move people and goods safely, efficiently, and sustainably. From designing resilient highways and iconic bridges to advancing transit, passenger and freight rail, aviation, federal transportation ports and marine infrastructure, your work will directly support economic vitality, public safety, sustainable and resilient communities and quality of life. We bring together planners, engineers, architects, construction management staff, environmental, strategic communications, economists, management consultants and specialists across disciplines to solve complex mobility challenges with innovation, technical excellence, and a deep understanding of community needs. Whether you're modernizing aging infrastructure or pioneering next-generation transportation solutions, your contributions will help define the future of mobility. This isn’t just a job, it’s a chance to lead progress, drive meaningful impact, and leave a legacy of smarter, more connected transportation networks. We are all employee-owners at HDR, which is the foundation of our collaborative culture that connects employees around the world.

In the role of Geotechnical EIT, Tunnels, we'll count on you to:

  • Support implementation of Toronto’s Ontario Line Subway Project, a $27 billion 15 km mass transit railway with 9 km of twin tunnels, portals and complex underground interchange stations
  • Support design, engineering analyses, procurement and construction of tunnels, underground and surface infrastructure projects in Toronto and across North America
  • Conduct desk studies, geotechnical investigations, analysis and design assignments
  • Conduct field investigations, including borehole drilling, test pits, groundwater monitoring, and in-situ testing (e.g., SPT, vane shear, permeability tests)
  • Support hydrogeological investigations for infrastructure, land development, environmental assessment and dewatering
  • Assist with geotechnical analysis such as slope stability, settlement, bearing capacity, earth pressures, and pavement design
  • Prepare technical memos, data summaries, figures, and sections of hydrogeological reports
  • Prepare drawings, technical memos, management plans, geotechnical and design reports
  • Prepare compliance documents for Ontario regulations and environmental guidelines (MECP, O.Reg. 406/19, O.Reg. 387/04, Permit to Take Water)
  • Assist project managers and senior engineers to deliver infrastructure projects
  • Support delivery of HDR’s engineering consultancy services to budget and schedule
  • Develop professional profile through industry engagement and project engineering.

Preferred Qualifications

  • MSc degree from accredited university in geotechnical or geological engineering
  • Academic transcripts showing university courses completed with good grades
  • Minimum 3 years’ experience in field work or geotechnical engineering
  • Eligible for PEO registration as Professional Engineer (PEng)
  • Knowledge and experience in geotechnical site characterization and hydrogeology
  • Experience with geotechnical software (e.g., SLOPE/W, Settle3, Slide, PLAXIS, gINT).
  • Strong written and oral communication skills for meetings, design reports and technical memos
  • Available for travel to other HDR offices and remote locations for site work
  • Adaptable and willing to learn from senior engineering professionals

Required Qualifications

  • Bachelor's degree in Engineering
  • Engineer in Training (EIT) certificate.
  • Experience with MS Office, GIS, and CADD, as appropriate
  • An attitude and commitment to being an active participant of our employee-owned culture is a must
