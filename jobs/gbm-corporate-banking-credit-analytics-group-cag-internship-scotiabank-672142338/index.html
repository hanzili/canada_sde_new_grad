GBM - Corporate Banking, Credit Analytics Group (CAG) Internship/Co-op - Fall 2026

at Scotiabank

📍 Location Toronto, Ontario
📋 Details Co-op · 4mo · Fall 2026
📅 Posted Apr 27, 2026

About the Role

Perform analytics, financial statement analysis, risk assessment

About Scotiabank

Global bank with broad financial services

Full Description

Requisition ID: 258955

Join a purpose driven winning team, committed to results, in an inclusive and high-performing culture.

Term: September 2026 – December 2026

Work Hours/ Week: 37.5

Location: Toronto, ON

Application Deadline: 05/11/26 at 11:59pm EST

There's no better way to kickstart your career than to do a co-op or internship with Scotiabank! During your work term, you’ll have the opportunity to be part of a winning team, build your network, and discover what you love – all while getting paid to do it! There’s a place for every type of student at Scotiabank and all that we ask for is that you have passion and strong AOO (Attitude, Ownership and Opportunity).

Global Banking & Markets provides a full range of investment banking, credit and risk management products and services relevant to the financing and strategic development needs of our clients. Our products include debt and equity financing, mergers & acquisitions, corporate banking, institutional equity sales, trading and research, fixed income products, derivatives, energy, foreign exchange and precious & metals. We also cross-sell the full range of wholesale products and services offered by the Scotiabank Group.

Is this role right for you? In this role, you will:

  • Perform analytics functions – financial statement analysis and risk assessment, with extremely high degree of attention to details and accuracy, in accordance with guidelines, policies and established procedures.
  • Demonstrate straightforward and succinct identification and analysis of key risks of existing or proposed clients and/or transactions.
  • Analyze, prepare and process annual review or credit requests in accordance with Bank policies and guidelines.
  • Demonstrate a sense of urgency as appropriate to the situation.
  • Develop industry expertise for assigned industries.
  • Develop product knowledge and expertise
  • Complete CAG tasks in connection with quality control
  • Complete other tasks as assigned, i.e. research, assisting industries in special projects, etc.
  • Demonstrate professional approach in interactions with key stakeholders. Promote cooperation and strong profession working relationships with the working unit, Global Corporate Banking, GCM Banking, GBM Product Groups, and GRM.
  • Demonstrate flexibility and is seen as a valuable team resource
  • Ability to multi-task and work under pressure to meet deadlines, while maintaining high standards of work

Requirements Do you have the skills and requirements that will enable you to succeed in this role? – We’d love to work with you if:

  • You are currently enrolled in post-secondary education.
  • You love to learn and envision yourself working for an international organization that heavily invests in your future.
  • You enjoy being involved in extracurricular activities such as conferences, clubs and case competitions.
  • You embody Scotiabank Code of Conduct and contributes to an inclusive and performance-oriented culture.
  • You understand how the Bank’s risk appetite and risk culture should be considered in day-to-day activities and decisions.
  • You champion a high-performance environment and contributes to an inclusive work environment.
  • You have exceptional attention to detail.
  • Knowledge of Corporate Banking, loan products or loan operations an asset
  • Knowledge of internal systems (Credit360, CARDs, CRS, Salesforce) an asset
  • Knowledge of Capital Call facilities and requirements as asset
  • Solid understanding of financial statements and accounting
  • Team player with good interpersonal skills
  • Strong written and verbal communication skills
  • Proficiency in Spanish or French would be an asset.
  • Financial Modelling skills an asset.
  • Solid understanding of core credit products (loans, cash management, derivatives, FX, etc.) an asset

What’s in it for you?

  • You’ll be part of a diverse, collaborative, innovative, and high-performing team.
  • In-depth training to prepare you for the role, as well as ongoing coaching and feedback to help you succeed!
  • Exclusive student events such as Scotia Student Day, Lunch & Learns, leadership panels, and much more!
  • Bank-wide orientation to learn more about Scotiabank and gain exposure to senior leadership across the organization.

How do I apply? Apply to the role and upload your Resume , and your most recent Academic Transcript .
