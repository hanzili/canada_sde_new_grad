Your role

  • Participate in the planning, execution, and completion of audit, review, and compilation engagements.
  • Prepare financial statements, working papers, and supporting schedules in accordance with applicable professional standards.
  • Prepare and progressively begin to review personal and corporate income tax returns, increasing in complexity over time.
  • Identify issues, ask strong questions, and propose practical solutions throughout engagements.
  • Communicate effectively with team members and clients to support smooth engagement delivery and meet timelines.
  • Maintain accurate, organized documentation and files in line with firm standards and regulatory requirements.
  • Support and collaborate with junior team members, offering guidance and reviewing work where appropriate.
  • Contribute to process improvements by sharing ideas that enhance efficiency, quality, or the client experience.

What you bring to the table

  • A University or College degree in Business, Accounting, Finance, or a related field.
  • Currently enrolled in, or actively pursuing, the CPA designation.
  • Strong analytical, organizational, and communication skills.
  • Ability to manage multiple tasks, meet deadlines, and collaborate effectively.
  • A growth mindset, eagerness to learn, and a proactive approach to problem solving.
  • Bilingualism in French/English is considered an asset.

  • Client and employee referral incentives recognizing the value of your network.
  • Firm‑sponsored social events throughout the year that bring our teams together.
  • Paid time off for board service and volunteer activities, reflecting our commitment to community impact.
