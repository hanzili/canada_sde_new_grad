About the role

Support the Platform Delivery team of the River Class Destroyer (RCD) program by assessing the completeness of deliverables from the Warship Designer (BAE) in preparation for the Final Design Review (FDR).

The Platform delivery team is a multi-discipline team that includes, Naval Architects, Electrical, Mechanical and Marine Engineers working in cooperating product teams. Your role would be to support the Platform leadership team in managing the contracted deliverables to the Shipbuilder (Irving Shipbuilding Inc. (ISI)) and assess the overall completeness of the design, the technical gaps that still need to be completed and the risks associated with those gaps.

The candidate will participate in engineering activities including but not limited to:

  • Developing and owning engineering processes
  • Working with technical risk owners to develop handling plans
  • Coordinating the capture of evidence for Design Review Gates
  • Applying the fundamentals of systems engineering to requirement and change management processes.

What you bring to the role

  • Current student in an Science, Technology, Engineering and Mathematics (STEM) program
  • Working knowledge of Microsoft (MS) Suite including Word, Excel, PowerPoint, Outlook
  • Aptitude for working well in teams
  • Ability to work independently
  • Results-oriented self-starter attitude
  • Eligibility for North Atlantic Treaty Organization (NATO) Secret clearance. (i.e., 10 years in Canada or previous NATO Secret Clearance).

Additional skills desired for the role

  • 8+ Month availability desired
  • IBM Doors experience
  • Active NATO Secret Security Clearance
  • Agile process experience ideally using JIRA
  • Advanced knowledge of MS Excel
  • Experience with Visio
  • Experience in Agile development
  • Experience developing metrics using various software tools including Tableau, Python and Structured Query Language (SQL).

About us

Headquartered in Ottawa, Lockheed Martin Canada is the Canadian unit of Lockheed Martin Corporation, a global defense technology company driving innovation and advancing scientific discovery. Our all-domain mission solutions and 21st Century Security® vision accelerate the delivery of transformative technologies to ensure those we serve always stay ahead of ready. We operate major facilities in Ottawa, Montreal, Halifax, Calgary and Victoria and work on a wide range of programs from advanced naval technology products, aircraft sustainment, and unmanned systems software. This position is part of the Rotary and Mission Systems business area, where employees across Canada support engineering, systems integration, training, sustainment and in-service support programs for Canadian and international military customers across land, air and sea.
