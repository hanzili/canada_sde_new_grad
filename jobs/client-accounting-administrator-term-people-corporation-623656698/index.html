Full Description

The Client Accounting Administrator (Term) provides accounting and administrative services to Single and Multiple Employer Plans. This includes answering telephone inquiries and corresponding with the employers and members of various plans on such matters as coverage, admissions, terminations, and beneficiaries. It also includes producing financial reports and maintaining high-quality client services. We are seeking a motivated team player who thrives in a fast-paced, challenging environment, demonstrates strong problem-solving skills, works independently with minimal supervision, and is committed to long-term career growth.

The Client Accounting Administrator (Term) will:

  • Master the inhouse database systems called CAPS. Create, maintain, and update coverage in systems.
  • Verify premium rates for various benefits and process rate changes.
  • Collect, reconcile, and balance employer reports, member payment via cheques, EFT, or credit/debit.
  • Learn the online credit card system to collect payments from members and set up accounts and re-occurring billing.
  • Manage Accounts Receivable and Accounts Payable.
  • Track outstanding invoices, prepare deposits, and process EFT and wire transfers.
  • Perform bank and credit card reconciliations, post journal entries, and maintained accurate financial records.
  • Prepare monthly financial statements and monitor cash flow to support clients.
  • Complete year-end reconciliations and audit working papers, and assist with the preparation of T4A slips.
  • Execute administrative office support, document organization, email correspondence, and maintain accurate filing system.
  • Provide professional, friendly, and prompt client service.
  • Interact with consultants and collaborate with the pension and claims department.

To be successful as a Client Accounting Administrator (Term) with Coughlin & Associates, you will need:

  • Completion of post-secondary education in a related field such as Business Administration or Accounting.
  • 1 - 2 years of professional administrative experience in an office environment.
  • Proficiency using the MS Office Suite and accounting programs such as Sage or QuickBooks.
  • Strong organizational skills, the ability to prioritize and multi-task, and manage time effectively.
  • Excellent grammatical, spelling, and communication skills.
  • Strong attention to detail and the ability to meet strict deadlines.
  • The ability to work well in a team environment and independently.
  • To use tact and discretion to maintain information in the strictest of confidence.
  • To provide exceptional client service by promptly addressing client and member inquiries and executing requests.
  • To adhere to existing company policies and procedures.
  • Previous experience in the benefits or insurance industry would be considered as asset.
  • Fluency in both French and English would be considered an asset.

What’s in it for you:

  • Learn by working alongside our experts
  • Extended health care and dental benefits
  • A retirement savings plan with company contributions
  • Competitive compensation

At People Corporation we are committed to helping businesses succeed. We are a national provider of benefits, retirement, wealth, wellness, and human resource solutions. Our experts and solutions serve over 20,000 clients representing nearly 3 million Canadians. We offer customized solutions designed to fit the unique needs of businesses and their employees, members, and stakeholders.

We are committed to providing an inclusive, accessible environment, where all employees and clients feel valued, respected, and supported. We are dedicated to building a workforce that reflects the diversity of the communities in which we live, and to creating an environment where every employee has the opportunity to reach their potential.
