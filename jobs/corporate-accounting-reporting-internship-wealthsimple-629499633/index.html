About the Role

Assist month-end close; prepare journal entries

About Wealthsimple

Canadian fintech; robust internship and training program

Full Description

Who you are

  • Currently enrolled as a student at a Canadian post-secondary institution or technical bootcamp
  • New graduates - Within 6 months of your graduation date
  • Available to work full-time hours
  • Residing in Canada
  • Currently pursuing a Bachelor’s degree in Accounting, Finance, or a related field (or recently graduated within the last 6 months)
  • A foundational understanding of basic accounting principles (e.g., debits/credits, chart of accounts)
  • High proficiency in Microsoft Excel (e.g., Pivot Tables, VLOOKUPs) and ability to quickly learn new software systems
  • Excellent attention to detail, organizational skills, and a strong commitment to accuracy in all tasks
  • Strong verbal and written communication skills with a proactive and collaborative approach to teamwork

What the job involves

  • At Wealthsimple, we offer 4 to 8-month internships that are open to co-op and non-co-op students, and recent grads
  • During your internship, you will have the opportunity to contribute to projects that are changing the landscape of financial services for Canadians
  • You will be on a team that supports your growth, provides mentorship, and connects you to the broader Wealthsimple community!
  • The Accounting team is responsible for the financial backbone of Wealthsimple
  • We manage the company's financial records, ensure compliance with IFRS, and provide timely, accurate financial information to support strategic decision-making and operational efficiency across all business units
  • We are a collaborative, detail-oriented team that plays a crucial role in enabling the company's high-growth objectives
  • The Accounting Intern will be a crucial support member for the Finance and Accounting team, gaining hands-on, foundational experience in a fast-paced, high-growth financial technology environment
  • This role offers an unparalleled opportunity to apply classroom knowledge to real-world scenarios and direct
  • Assist with the month-end and quarter-end close processes, including preparing routine journal entries and balance sheet reconciliations
  • Support the Senior Accountant in day-to-day functions, as well as ad-hoc projects which may include bank reconciliations, accruals, and ad hoc tasks
  • Perform financial data entry and review supporting documentation to ensure the accuracy and integrity of the general ledger
  • Assisting in the preparation of schedules for external audits, tax filings, and regulatory compliance
  • Creating and updating documentation for accounting policies and procedures
  • Learn about regulatory compliance, internal controls, and the application of accounting principles (IFRS) in a dynamic, regulated fintech context
