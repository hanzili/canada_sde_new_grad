The Opportunity

Build the Future with Us – We are seeking a highly motivated Junior Geotechnical Engineer-in-Training to join our northern BC & Yukon – Ground Engineering team in the city of Terrace, BC. You’ll collaborate with and be mentored by junior and senior engineers while supporting field investigations, analysis, and design on a wide range of projects, helping deliver practical, resilient solutions for clients and communities.

Your Impact

  • Contribute to planning and executing geotechnical field investigations, including boreholes, test pits, and in-situ testing to support transportation, municipal, industrial, and environmental projects.
  • Assist in interpreting soil, rock, and groundwater conditions and help translate findings into clear, practical design recommendations.
  • Support the development of geotechnical design solutions for foundations, earthworks, slopes, retaining structures, and buried infrastructure that help keep people and communities safe.
  • Prepare concise, data-driven technical memoranda and reports that inform client decisions on scope, schedule, and investment.
  • Collaborate with multidisciplinary teams (environmental, structural, transportation, water, and more) to integrate ground engineering into larger project designs.
  • Help advance sustainable design practices by considering constructability, materials, and long-term performance in geotechnical solutions.
  • Participate in project meetings and contribute to problem-solving discussions, bringing an EIT perspective and curiosity to every challenge.

The Skills That Set You Apart

  • You have a degree in civil, geological, or geotechnical engineering and are registered, or eligible to register, as an Engineer-in-Training (EIT) with a Canadian engineering association.
  • You possess a valid Canadian driver’s license, have access to a vehicle, a clean Driver’s Abstract, and you know your way around the MS Office suite of products.
  • You’ve started building hands-on experience in geotechnical or ground engineering through co-op, internship, or early-career project work.
  • You are comfortable supporting work both in the field and in the office and are willing to travel to sites across the northern British Columbia and the Yukon, as project needs arise.
  • You enjoy working with data – from logs and lab results to monitoring records – and using it to support practical recommendations.
  • You are keen to grow your skills with geotechnical tools and software (for example, analysis, modeling, or drafting platforms) and to learn from senior mentors.
  • You care about doing work that matters – to clients, communities, and the environment – and you bring a collaborative, safety-focused mindset to every project.

Why Choose Terrace?

Terrace has a great deal to offer you both recreationally and professionally. Terrace is the undeniable heart of Northwest British Columbia, a thriving and resource rich region. Residents enjoy access to world-class hiking and mountain biking trails that ring the city. Nearby Shames Mountain offers alpine skiing and snowboarding enthusiasts one of the deepest snowpacks in Canada, while cross country skiers find their bliss at the Onion Lake Snow Valley Nordic Ski Club. Many rivers and lakes surround this vibrant community offering boundless fishing, swimming, and skating opportunities, everything being close and easily accessible, all without long lines or high costs. When it comes to work life balance, affordability, and opportunity, Terrace is your place to thrive.
