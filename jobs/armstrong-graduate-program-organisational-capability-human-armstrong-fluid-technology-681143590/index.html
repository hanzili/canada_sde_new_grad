Full Description

Kickstart Your Career in Organizational Capability with Armstrong!

Imagine working at the forefront of innovation in fluid-flow technology, with over 1,400 colleagues across the globe, and contributing to a legacy of excellence that spans eight manufacturing facilities on four continents. Armstrong Fluid Technology is more than just a leader in our industry; we are a community of the brightest and most creative minds, driven by a shared mission to engineer the future and safeguard our planet.

If you are a driven graduate with a passion for people, organizational development, and partnering with the business to drive performance, Armstrong’s Graduate Development Program – Organizational Capability Stream is your gateway to that mission. Through immersive rotations, mentorship, and hands-on experience, you’ll develop into a future-ready professional, enabling leaders and teams to deliver sustainable business outcomes.

Program Highlights

  • 18-Month Rotational Journey – Accelerated exposure across Talent Management, Learning & Development, Performance Management, Workforce Planning, and Business Partnering.
  • Business Partnering at the Core – Work directly with business leaders to understand priorities, diagnose people challenges, and co-create solutions that drive performance and engagement.
  • Organizational Impact – Contribute to real business challenges such as capability building, organizational design, and workforce planning aligned to strategic goals.
  • Talent & Culture Focus – Support initiatives that strengthen Armstrong’s leadership pipeline, employee experience, and culture.
  • Learning & Mentorship – Build strong foundations in HR, organizational capability, and consulting skills through structured training and mentorship from experienced HR leaders.
  • Cross-Functional Collaboration – Partner with Sales, Operations, Finance, and Engineering teams to align people strategies with business objectives.

What We’re Looking For

  • Education – A recent graduate with a Bachelor’s or Master’s degree in Human Resources, Organizational Psychology, Business Administration, or related fields.
  • Interest in Business Partnering – Strong inclination to work closely with business teams, understand commercial priorities, and translate them into people strategies.
  • Analytical & Problem-Solving Skills – Ability to interpret workforce data (engagement, performance, headcount) and generate actionable insights.
  • Business Acumen – Understanding of how organizations operate and how talent and culture impact business performance.
  • Communication & Influencing Skills – Ability to build relationships, communicate effectively, and influence stakeholders across levels.
  • Project Orientation – Experience through internships, academic projects, or extracurriculars in managing initiatives, programs, or change efforts.
  • Digital & Tools Orientation – Familiarity with MS Office; exposure to HR systems, analytics tools, or survey platforms is an advantage.
  • Collaboration & Teamwork – Proven ability to work across teams and functions in a matrixed environment.
  • Adaptability & Learning Agility – Comfortable navigating ambiguity in a fast-paced, global environment.
  • Values Alignment – Open, authentic, and passionate about contributing to Armstrong’s culture of innovation, sustainability, and service.

Why Join Armstrong?

Be part of a global leader in intelligent fluid-flow technology. Gain hands-on exposure to real business challenges and decision-making. Work in a culture that values innovation, integrity, and collaboration. Access career growth opportunities across our global network.
