About the Role

Coordinate campus events, create social media content

About Aramark Canada

Global campus dining provider with strong student focus

Full Description

Job Description In this role, you will support the planning, execution, and overall student experience related to meal plans, dining halls, retail food, and cultural events on campus. You will assist with creating pre- and post-event content for social media, and your passion for people will support regular in-person tabling events and engaging with the student population.

Job Responsibilities

  • Support the marketing team by coordinating in-person informational tabling and meal plan sales.
  • Assist in the planning and execution of dining hall events and promotions.
  • Assist with setting up events as required (decorating, setting up booths).
  • Be present at dining and retail events to take videos and photos with documented consent at events and collect feedback/testimonials from students.
  • Attend tabling events to promote and drive engagement in Campus Meal Plans and other Hospitality Services initiatives.
  • Assist executive social media planning and engagement tracking.
  • Record debriefings of all events.
  • Assist marketing coordinator in administrative tasks such as installing signage, swapping table toppers, laminating signage, picking up packages and mailings, and more.

Qualifications

  • Outgoing personality with strong communication skills (written and verbal)
  • Social media savvy
  • Comfortable being in front of the camera and speaking to peers
  • Ability to work independently with little to no supervision and as a team member
  • Strong ability to work under pressure in stressful situations with tight deadlines
  • Knowledge of Queen?s University building locations, facilities, etc.

Role will require 8 hours of work a week. Work shifts will vary, including evenings, weekends, and holidays. Successful candidates must be able to work flexible hours, with some shifts beginning early in the morning and others ending late in the evenings. This position is subject to a 4-week probationary period for the benefit of both employer and employee.
