Full Description

Job Description Number of Position(s): 7

Duration: 4 Months (possibly 8 Months)

Date: May 4 to Aug 28, 2026 (to Dec 18 if 8 Months)

Location: Onsite (Ottawa, Canada)

The team you will be a part of The IP Routing division leads the industry with the best Network Processor technology (FP5), QoS, parallel processing, Network Function Virtualization, and product quality. Products cover the spectrum from small CPE devices supporting a few low-speed Ethernet links to carrier-class and datacenter-class systems supporting terabits of routing capacity. Our world-leading technology enables us to offer customers 800GE and 1.6T links with unparalleled routing features.

How You Will Contribute And What You Will Learn As a student in the hardware group, you’ll work with an experienced team that designs, builds, and maintains hardware solutions for Nokia's routing platforms. You’ll gain hands-on lab experience working alongside hardware developers on the latest prototype circuit boards. In addition to technical guidance, you’ll be paired with a 1-to-1 mentor—your go-to person for questions, feedback, insights into Nokia/team culture, and career conversations. This is an on-site position, and presence in the office is required.

As part of the team, you will:

  • Verify and debug circuit designs using oscilloscopes and other test gear
  • Test hardware under thermal and environmental conditions
  • Complete the final assembly of new prototype units
  • Qualify new circuit components
  • Develop custom automation scripts (Perl, Python, TCL) to control test equipment

Key Skills And Experience

  • Currently a 2nd or 3rd-year candidate for a bachelor's degree or diploma in Computer Engineering or Electrical Engineering, or a related field with an accredited school in Canada. OR Currently an Electronic Engineering Technology Student in Canada who has completed 4 semesters of school and one co-op placement.
  • A solid foundation in hardware electronics concepts
  • Familiarity with electronic test equipment such as oscilloscopes and multimeters
  • Have completed at least one co-op work term, a 4-month paid internship, or have equivalent paid work experience
  • A proactive attitude, a willingness to learn quickly, and the ability to grasp technical concepts with ease

It would be nice if you also had:

  • Excellent communication skills, both written and oral
  • Good problem-solving skills
  • Ability to manage tasks with minimal supervision
