About the Role

About McElhanney

Full Description

About McElhanney Based in Western Canada for over 110 years, McElhanney offers innovative and practical engineering, surveying, mapping, planning, landscape architecture, and environmental services. As a 100% employee-owned and operated firm, with over 1,400 staff members in 31 locations, we are proud to offer qualified staff with shareholding options in our company. At McElhanney, we work in teams to build strong connections with our staff, clients, and local communities. Our employees enjoy working here because of our varied projects, high levels of autonomy, opportunities for career development, excellent total compensation and benefits package, and collaborating with a team of great professionals.

The Opportunity McElhanney has an opportunity for a Junior Civil Engineer (EIT) to join our Civil Communities Engineering team in Courtenay, BC. In this role, you will have the opportunity to provide innovative and integrated consulting services to our public and private sector clients. We work with local municipal, indigenous, provincial and land development clients in the Courtenay office. We value flexibility, giving you the opportunity to support a range of projects.

McElhanney has industry-leading professionals and mentors, and a culture that fosters growing future leaders. Our team has a reputation of taking great care in developing our people and fostering a positive, friendly, and rewarding work environment. Join McElhanney, a firm with big company opportunities, and a small company feel!  You’ll also have the benefit of working with a multidisciplinary team of professionals, including structural engineers, geotechnical engineers, environmental scientists, and survey professionals as well as access to the expertise and opportunities in other regional offices.

What You’ll Do

  • Provide design support for various infrastructure projects including municipal engineering, road works, utility, and facility upgrades.
  • Complete field inspections to monitor the quality and progress of the work being performed on various infrastructure projects including municipal engineering, road works, utility and facility upgrades.
  • Assist in the preparation of tender and contract documents and drawings.
  • Prepare and adhere to site-specific health and safety plans.
  • Provide support to other departments (geomatics, materials testing, and structural engineering) to learn about and develop a holistic approach to project execution.
  • Work with other team members to ensure schedules, budgets, and quality control standards are achieved and maintained.
  • You will be directly supervised by a professional (P.Eng.) registered in BC.

What You’ll Bring

  • Degree in Civil Engineering from an accredited post-secondary institution.
  • Prior experience working in the industry through internships, or Co-ops.
  • Registration in good standing as an Engineer-In-Training (EIT) in British Columbia, or eligibility to be registered.
  • Proficiency in the application of AutoCAD Civil 3D.
  • High productivity, attention to detail, effective verbal and written communication skills, and an ability to work well in a team environment.
  • Live in Courtenay, BC or a commutable distance from the area.
  • Be willing to work from the Courtenay office.
