Full Description

About Chick Pick

Chick Pick Solutions Inc. is a New Brunswick-based ag-tech startup developing automated chick-sorting systems for hatcheries. Our systems use high-speed cameras and conveyor systems to classify day-old chickens by gender in real time. We are scaling commercial deployments rapidly across Canada and abroad — and we need skilled hands to help build them. As a fast-growing startup, we move quickly and aren't without our growing pains, but if you thrive in a dynamic environment and want to be part of something being built from the ground up, you'll fit right in.

Role Overview

We are looking for a Junior Mechanical Engineer or CAD Designer to join our team in a hands on production and design role. This position blends mechanical design, CAD work, and real world system assembly.

You will be responsible for designing and refining conveyor based automation systems, including custom layouts and mechanical components. In addition to CAD and R&D work, you will be involved in building and testing the systems you help design, giving you full ownership from concept through to deployment.

This role is ideal for someone early in their career who enjoys both design and hands on work, and wants to contribute to the development of scalable automation systems.

Responsibilities

  • Design conveyor based automation systems, including custom layouts, frames, and mechanical components using CAD software
  • Create and maintain detailed CAD models, drawings, and assemblies for production
  • Participate in R&D activities including prototyping, testing new components, and refining system performance
  • Support development and iteration of automation systems and mechanical subsystems
  • Work closely with production to ensure designs are practical, buildable, and efficient
  • Assist in assembling conveyor systems and mechanical components as needed
  • Fit, align, and validate mechanical assemblies during builds
  • Collaborate with electrical and software teams to integrate full systems
  • Identify design improvements and contribute to continuous product development
  • Assist in developing standards for design, documentation, and system layout

Requirements

  • Degree or diploma in Mechanical Engineering, Mechatronics, or a related field, or equivalent practical experience
  • Experience with CAD software such as Fusion 360, or similar
  • Strong mechanical intuition and understanding of basic machine design principles
  • Ability to create and interpret technical drawings and assemblies
  • Hands on experience with mechanical systems, fabrication, or assembly is a strong asset
  • Attention to detail and focus on quality and usability in design
  • Ability to work independently and manage time across multiple tasks
