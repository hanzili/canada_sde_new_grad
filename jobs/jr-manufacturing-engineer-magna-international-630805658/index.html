Group Summary:

Job Responsibilities:

JOB REQUIREMENTS:

  • Engineering Degree/Diploma in Mechanical, Manufacturing, or Industrial Engineering, or Trades Certification with equivalent relevant experience in Assembly, Tooling, and Design.
  • 1 to 2 years of relevant experience (co-op, internship, or practical plant experience counts).
  • Working knowledge of Geometric Dimensioning and Tolerancing (GD&T) and ability to interpret engineering drawings.
  • Strong mechanical aptitude and comfort working in a manufacturing environment (hands-on mindset, practical problem solving).
  • Experience with CAD (AutoCAD preferred).
  • Strong organizational skills and ability to manage multiple priorities in a fast-paced environment.
  • Strong verbal and written communication skills, including the ability to collaborate across departments and support customer-facing communication when required.
  • Ability to uphold confidentiality and handle sensitive information appropriately.
  • Demonstrated ability to work effectively in a team environment.
  • Ability to work overtime to meet customer needs.

Assets / Nice to Have:

  • Exposure to project management fundamentals (timelines, action tracking, change control).
  • Programming or scripting exposure (Python preferred) to automate repeat tasks such as data cleaning, report generation, or simple analysis.
  • Basic database/query experience (SQL preferred).
  • Experience using AI tools to improve productivity (summarizing, drafting, data cleanup, idea generation) with sound judgment and respect for confidentiality.
  • Data analytics experience (Power BI, Tableau, or similar), including dashboarding and KPI reporting.
  • Familiarity with structured problem solving and continuous improvement tools.

JOB RESPONSIBILITIES:

  • Handles small scale projects from inception to completion (timing, concept, feasibility, design, construction, try-out, launch, final buy-off (PPAP and run at rate), prototype programs, and engineering changes) with continuous follow-up by leadership.
  • Conducts appropriate Manpower efficiency studies ‚Äì through time and motion studies or standard data (MOST is the Modatek standard). Line balancing manpower, machine charts and analysis
  • Prepares reports, charts and other documentation required for engineering records and projects
  • Works with all departments and customers to design solutions that improve production efficiency, ergonomics, safety and maintenance related issues
  • Supports continuous improvement initiatives
  • Assists in developing material handling logistics (i.e. Aisle layout, bin placement, dunnage assists, etc.)
  • Reviews, monitors and co-ordinates tooling design, build, approval process and capital budgets
  • Supervises equipment relocation and installation to Modatek
  • Assists in setting up preventative maintenance program for new equipment
  • Assists in resolving quality concerns
  • Reviews, co-ordinates and implements all customer approved engineering changes, including part level communication updates, physical part update, process sheet update, physical tooling change, etc
  • Knows and understands process documentation and customer specific requirements as they relate to this job function
  • Follows and comply with all Operational Procedures and Policies as outlined in the Employee Handbook, Quality and Environment programs (i.e. IATF 16949, ISO14001)
  • Understands, promotes and lives by Magna‚Äôs philosophies and Employee Charter
  • Adheres to Modatek‚Äôs core values; integrity, respect, trust, honesty, fairness and loyalty
  • Performs other duties as required
