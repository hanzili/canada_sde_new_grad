About Us!

The Region of Waterloo is a thriving, diverse community committed to fostering opportunities for current and future generations. Waterloo Region is part of the Dish with One Spoon wampum, a treaty guiding our commitment to:

  • Taking only what we need
  • Leaving some for others
  • Keeping the dish clean

This agreement underpins our vision, mission, and core value, and reinforces our dedication to meaningful reconciliation and equity.

Waterloo Region is home to over 674,000 residents and expected to grow to nearly one million by 2051. Guided by our 2023-2027 Strategic Plan, “Growing with Care,” we prioritize homes for all, climate aligned growth, equitable services and opportunities and a resilient and future ready organization. Our mission is to provide essential services that enhance the quality of life for all residents, while our core value emphasizes caring for people and the land we share. Join us and be part of a team dedicated to making a meaningful difference in our community.

Duties/Responsibilities

  • Monitors designated Accounts Payable (AP) inboxes/portals for supplier invoices and ensures invoices are complete and legible. Confirms invoices meet processing requirements (e.g., vendor remittance details, unique invoice number, clear description of goods/services).
  • Validates and verifies extracted data for electronic invoices. Ensures information is correct and approved before uploading for payment.
  • Enters data in template for manually keyed invoices. Scans and uploads into financial system. Investigates and reconciles discrepancies through follow-up checks on accounts. Consults with vendors and departmental staff as required.
  • Escalates issues for invoices that appear inconsistent or unusual (e.g., changed banking details, mismatched vendor names, unexpected increases) per internal fraud prevention procedures.
  • Reconciles supplier statements periodically. Identifies unpaid accounts that are in arrears and follows up as appropriate; initiates processing of the invoice by the departmental staff to resolve the issue of payment as required.
  • Mails and distributes cheques and inserts.
  • Maintains spreadsheets and generates reports.
  • Performs other basic accounting functions, such as journal entries and accounts receivable.
  • Files accounts payable invoices.
  • Supports the Treasury team with project work as assigned.
  • Uses a computer approximately 90% of the time. Retrieves files, involving bending and reaching. Occasionally lifts file boxes weighing up to 33 lbs/15 kg.
  • Performs other duties as assigned.

Knowledge, Skills, and Abilities

  • Knowledge and skills are acquired through a Grade 12 diploma and some general accounting experience, or current enrollment and completion of at least one year of a college or university program in a related field such as but not limited to Accounting or Business.
  • Knowledge of basic accounting principles and practices, general understanding of financial accounting, and skills in keyboarding and math.
  • Ability to work independently and adapt to changing priorities.
  • Computer skills with ability to use software such as Microsoft Office, financial systems, and database management systems.
  • Ability to participate as an effective team member.
  • Ability to support and demonstrate the Region’s values.
