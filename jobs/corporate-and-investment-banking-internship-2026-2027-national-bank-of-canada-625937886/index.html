About the Role

About National Bank of Canada

National Bank of Canada capital markets internship

Full Description

Attendance: On-Site

Employment Category: Professional

Type of Contract: Internship

Benefits for vendors:

National Bank Capital Markets is recruiting for Analyst positions within Corporate & Investment Banking for our offices in Montreal, Toronto, Calgary and Vancouver. Positions are available in various teams such as Corporate Banking, Loan Structuring & Syndication and Investment Banking. Internships will be offered during the Fall 2026, Winter 2027, and Summer 2027 sessions.

About Us:

National Bank Capital Markets offers a complete suite of products and services to corporations, institutional clients, and public-sector entities. We devote our energies to deliver comprehensive advisory services and research or capital markets solutions to support our clients in achieving their goals and growth.

Internship availabilities:

  • Information about the internship availabilities is available on the following page: https://emplois.bnc.ca/en_CA/careers/CIB_Internship

Our Program:

Our internship program offers dynamic hands-on exposure working alongside seasoned professionals and assisting members of the team in executing their responsibilities.

Responsibilities may include industry and company research utilizing financial modeling and valuation techniques, credit risk analysis, preparing client pitches and assisting in deal execution. As an internship program participant, you will have the opportunity to put your learnings into action and applying it in real time situations.

Criterias:

  • You are an undergraduate or a Master’s program student enrolled full-time at a Canadian university.
  • You have a strong interest in Capital markets and the financial industry.
  • You have a strong academic record with relevant extracurricular participation.
  • You are detail-oriented, organized, proactive, and possess strong interpersonal skills along with a high level of intellectual curiosity.
  • You demonstrate excellent verbal and written communication skills and can convey concepts and ideas clearly and concisely.
  • You have strong analytical skills and good understanding of basic accounting rules/concepts.
  • You have the ability to synthesize large amounts of information and develop innovative solutions.
  • You have advanced PowerPoint skills and Excel skills for modeling, valuation, etc.
  • You can manage projects independently and are ready to take on a high level of responsibility as an integral member of the team.
  • You are a team player and can work under pressure with tight deadlines.
  • You demonstrate outstanding ethics, integrity and show excellent judgement.
  • You have the ability to interact comfortably with clients in a professional and mature manner.
  • You have a strong academic record and relevant extracurricular participation.
  • You are legally eligible to work in Canada.
  • Bilingualism is an asset (French required for internships in Montreal).
