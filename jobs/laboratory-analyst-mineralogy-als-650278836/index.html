About the Role

Operate mineralogy instruments; analyze data; report

About ALS

Global mineralogy lab with strong data quality

Full Description

At ALS, we encourage you to dream big.

When you join us, you’ll be part of a global team harnessing the power of scientific testing and data-driven insights to build a healthier future.

About the Position: Perform technical jobs in Mineralogy Laboratory. Tasks may include sample mount preparation, operating scanning electron microscope (SEM), optical mineral assessment using microscope, data entry and analysis.

Specific Responsibilities:

  • Supervised training in preparation of samples for all kinds of analysis in the Mineralogy lab;
  • Supervised training in SEM Data and running samples for XRD analysis, and QEMSCAN setup and sample exchange;
  • Operate and perform maintenance on QEMSCAN, XRD, microscopes, sample preparation equipment;
  • Identify & correct QC related problems by ensuring the quality of the mineralogy data meets ALS standards;
  • Evaluate data and perform data entry using QEMSCAN, XRD and Clemex software;
  • Use Microsoft Excel and Word to analyze data from the test work;
  • Test and evaluate calibration standards;
  • Ensure samples for project work assigned by Manager, Lead Hand or Designate are accounted for in the lab;
  • Prioritize workloads according to daily stats and turnaround commitments;
  • Maintain and organize inventory of calibration standards, production logs and equipment records;
  • Maintain a clean and safe work environment by following all safety requirements within the Safety Manual and communicate all safety concerns to the Supervisor or Manager.
  • Other tasks as assigned by Supervisor or Manager.

Required Knowledge, Skills & Abilities:

  • Analytical and mechanical skills;
  • Quality focused individual;
  • Organized, highly motivated, detail oriented and able to work independently or as part of a team;
  • Ability to effectively handle a high volume of work and time critical issues;
  • Excellent English communication skills (written and verbal);
  • Basic MS Office skills, specifically MS Word, Excel and Outlook.

Required Qualifications:

  • Recognized Diploma or Degree in Sciences required;
  • Minimum of 6 months laboratory work experience in mineralogy, instrumentation or a related field preferred.

Physical Demands:

  • Stand for 8 hours per day. Manual dexterity to perform repetitive tasks;
  • Able to lift filled 20 litre containers (45 lb).

Our benefits include:

  • An estimated hourly pay of $24.25 CAD at the time of posting. Individual compensation is determined by factors such as job-related skills, relevant experience, education and/or training.
  • Comprehensive benefit package specific to your work status (including extended medical, dental, and vision coverage, access to company perks, life and disability insurance, retirement plan with company match, employee assistance and wellness programs)
  • Additional vacation days for years of service
  • Business support for education or training after 9 months with the company
  • Learning & development opportunities (unlimited access to e-learnings and more)

Please note: Benefits vary based on employee status.
