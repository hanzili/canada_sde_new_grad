Full Description

Become an Entry-Level Business Analyst

Who we are

ACF Associates specializes in the provision of quality program and product support solutions to government and civilian industry in aerospace maintenance and performance-based operations. We are an independent consulting firm that believes in working closely with clients throughout their projects, from conception to implementation.

Our team has a deep understanding of the Canadian Air Force and the broader aerospace defence sector, and we leverage this expertise to deliver timely, value-added, and result-oriented services. Simply stated, we provide our clients with solutions that work.

ACF Associates operates as a virtual workplace. We have associates currently located throughout Eastern Ontario, Quebec, Saskatchewan and Alberta. Our business model demands exacting standards, focus, and innovation. ACF has excelled at this model and translates these standards into outstanding results for our clients.

Position Description

This posting reflects an existing vacancy within the company. As an Entry-Level Associate with ACF Associates, you will:

  • Collaborate with stakeholders to understand customer needs, identify issues, and document technology requirements.
  • Capture and track product change requests as part of ongoing product enhancement efforts.
  • Participate in product roadmap discussions by providing consultative insights based on user needs and system behavior.
  • Support project teams in applying structured engineering problem‑solving methodologies (root cause analysis, troubleshooting, risk assessment, solution generation and implementation).
  • Review technical designs, test cases, and test results to help evaluate usability, performance considerations, and trade-offs.
  • Monitor production data to identify quality issues and collaborate with clients to resolve them using analytical and problem‑solving techniques.
  • Contribute to the design of performance metrics that measure aircraft maintenance effectiveness and operational outcomes.
  • Develop an understanding of preventive aircraft maintenance concepts and how they integrate across business functions.
  • Document detailed business requirements, including use cases, process maps, workflows, business rules, and data definitions.
  • Analyze information from multiple sources, reconcile discrepancies, and synthesize high-level concepts into structured, detailed requirements.
  • Assist with application training sessions and knowledge-transfer activities for clients.
  • Represent ACF on performance management projects, providing product and process-related support as required.
  • Support additional technical or managerial activities across projects as assigned.
  • Contribute to business development efforts such as proposal writing, research, data analysis, and information gathering.

Applicant Requirements

Required:

  • Post‑secondary education in Business, Engineering, Data Analytics, Computer Science, or a related field
  • Strong analytical and problem‑solving abilities
  • Excellent communication and documentation skills
  • Ability to collaborate with both technical and business stakeholders
  • High attention to detail and ability to manage competing priorities
  • Proficiency with Microsoft Office (Excel, PowerPoint, Word)

Nice to Have (Asset):

  • Exposure to business analysis methodologies (e.g., requirements gathering, process mapping)
  • Experience with data analysis or data quality concepts
  • Interest in aviation, maintenance operations, or performance management
  • Familiarity with Python, SQL, BI tools, Tableau or workflow mapping software (not required but beneficial)
