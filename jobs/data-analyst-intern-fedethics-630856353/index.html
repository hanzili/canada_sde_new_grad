About the Role

Analyze data, build dashboards, improve website UX

About FedEthics

Ethical AI consulting firm, data governance expert

Full Description

Company Description

FedEthics is a consulting and advisory firm specializing in Ethical AI, data governance, and data science delivery, helping organizations develop trustworthy, secure, and implementable decision systems. The company bridges policy and practice through services like responsible automation, data-sharing strategies, and capability-building. FedEthics is led by a recognized figure in the global AI community and actively contributes to senior practitioner networks. The organization is dedicated to creating solutions that promote transparency, responsibility, and measurable impact in data-driven fields.

Role Description

FedEthics is looking for a part-time Data Analyst Intern (≈ 10 hours/week) who enjoys turning messy information into clear insights—and who also has enough UX / web sensibility to help improve how those insights are presented on a website.

This is a hands-on, portfolio-building internship where you’ll work on real deliverables: datasets, dashboards, and practical website components (fedethics.ca/book).

What will you do?

  • Data analysis & research: frame questions, collect/structure data, analyze patterns, and write short insight briefs.
  • Dataset building: clean and maintain datasets with strong documentation (data dictionary, assumptions, refresh notes).
  • Dashboards: build executive-ready dashboards (Power BI Desktop preferred).
  • Website enablement (UX-informed): help improve user journeys and content layout; support forms, downloadable assets, and basic QA/testing.
  • Operational analytics: define simple KPIs and tracking for launch and sales activities (e.g., outreach pipelines, conversion touchpoints).

Tools & Skills

  • Power BI Desktop (preferred)
  • Excel (Power Query/Power Pivot)
  • Python and/or R (pandas/tidyverse basics)
  • Optional: SQL (SQLite/Postgres/DuckDB)
  • Bonus: comfort with basic website tools (forms, page edits, CMS workflows)

Who is this for?

Students or recent grads (Bachelor’s) in:

Political Science / Public Policy, Data Science, Computer Engineering / Computer Science, Information Studies, Business Analytics, or related programs.

You’re a strong match if you are

  • Structured and detail-oriented (you care about data quality)
  • Able to communicate insights clearly in writing
  • Curious about how UX affects the adoption of analytics
  • Reliable with weekly delivery and check-ins

Time commitment

✅ 10 hours/week

✅ Flexible scheduling (evenings/weekends welcome)

What you’ll gain

  • Real-world, end-to-end experience: question → dataset → analysis → dashboard → website delivery
  • Portfolio-ready assets (dashboards + briefs + web-facing components)
  • Mentorship and exposure to executive-level analytics expectations
  • A credible experience you can add to LinkedIn and your portfolio
