About Matcor Metal Fabrication

Full Description

Who We Are:

Matcor Metal Fabrication is a North American Leader in the Fabrication industry with operations in Canada, and the USA. We are a fully-integrated supplier of manufacturing and engineering services providing steel components and complex, high value-added assemblies for the agricultural, construction, mining, energy, heavy truck and other industrial sectors.

Position Summary:

The Junior Buyer supports the purchasing function at the facility level, assisting with procurement activities for specific commodities such as fabrication materials and consumables. This role helps ensure timely availability of materials and services while supporting cost‑effective purchasing and inventory management practices.

This position may be structured as a multi-site role and depending on business needs may provide support to our other locations across the organization.

The Junior Buyer reports directly to the Materials Manager.

Recruitment Status:

This role is for a current vacancy.

Essential Duties and Responsibilities:

Purchasing & Supplier Support

  • Assist with sourcing and purchasing materials and supplies under the department direction
  • Collect and compare supplier quotations for pricing, delivery, and basic specification alignment.
  • Support supplier evaluations and help maintain positive supplier relationships.
  • Communicate order details, follow up on delivery commitments, and escalate issues when needed.

Procurement Strategy & Cost Improvement

  • Support the implementation of procurement tools and strategies (JIT, consignment, VMI, e‑commerce).
  • Participate in cost‑saving initiatives by gathering data, preparing reports, and assisting cross‑functional teams.
  • Help identify opportunities for process improvements within purchasing and materials management.

Inventory & Data Management

  • Prepare and analyze basic inventory data to support purchasing decisions.
  • Assist with maintaining accurate ERP data, including lead times, order quantities, pricing, and supplier information.
  • Coordinate and follow up on the open orders with steel Suppliers
  • Manage third party Suppliers that support when capacity issue arises.
  • Participate in cycle counts, physical inventory activities, and related reporting.

Purchase Order Administration

  • Create and process Purchase Orders accurately and in a timely manner based on requestion
  • Track order status, follow up on delivery dates, and assist with expediting when required.
  • Support resolution of invoice discrepancies and non‑conformance issues in collaboration with Quality, Accounting, and suppliers.

Documentation & Compliance

  • Assist with documentation and activities related to ISO9001, CTPAT, ISO14001, and other materials management audits.
  • Maintain organized records and ensure compliance with internal procedures and external standards.

Cross‑Functional Collaboration

  • Work closely with Manufacturing, Finance, Quality, Engineering, and other departments to support operational needs.
  • Participate in continuous improvement initiatives and operational excellence activities.
  • Provide general support to the Materials Manager and assist with special projects as assigned.

Characteristics

  • Hands‑on, proactive, and eager to learn
  • Strong sense of ownership and accountability
  • Able to work in a fast‑paced, resource‑conscious environment
  • Effective problem‑solver with a willingness to ask questions and seek guidance
  • Strong team player with a collaborative mindset
  • Organized, detail‑oriented, and capable of managing multiple tasks

Education, Knowledge, Skills, and Abilities

  • Bachelor’s degree in business, supply chain, economics, engineering, or related field; equivalent experience will be considered.
  • 1–2 years of experience in procurement, materials planning, logistics, or supply chain (co‑op or internship experience is an asset).
  • Interest in fabrication, MRO consumables, and manufacturing supply chain environments.
  • Familiarity with inventory control concepts and basic procurement processes.
  • Strong analytical and mathematical skills.
  • ERP/MRP experience preferred; willingness to learn required. Prior exposure to CMS/Epicor is an asset.
  • Ability to read and interpret basic technical information (drawings, specifications) is an asset.
  • Strong interpersonal and communication skills, both written and verbal.
  • Proficiency in Outlook, Excel, PowerPoint, and Word.
  • Strong organizational skills and attention to detail.
  • Willingness to grow into more advanced procurement responsibilities over time.
