Full Description

This is a co-op position that will begin May 4, 2026 and run through the end of August, 2026.

The Actuarial Benefits Analyst (co-op) will provide group benefits consulting support, including design and financial management for existing medium and large clients and prospects. In addition, the Actuarial Benefits Analyst (co-op) will perform actuarial valuations, accounting disclosures of post-retirement and post-employment benefits, reserve calculations, simulation modelling, flex plan pricing and modelling and other actuarial and analytic projects. The Actuarial Benefits Analyst (co-op) will work closely with internal consulting teams, interface with vendors, support client deliverables, and ensure quality standards are met.

The Actuarial Benefits Analyst (Co-op Student) Will

  • Contribute to the overall design and management of group medical, dental, life, disability and special risk benefits for clients.
  • Prepare actuarial valuations of post-retirement and post-employment benefits plan, in accordance with accounting standards appliable to the plan;
  • Review all benefits documentation (contracts, booklets, collective agreement, amendments) for accuracy;
  • Annual renewal analysis and negotiations with carriers; prepare client reports and presentations;
  • Experience reporting, budget projections and reviewing plan financial performance;
  • Review financial statements for accuracy and adherence to underwriting agreements;
  • Conduct plan design benchmarking studies;
  • Assist and build plan design tools and financial database;
  • Conduct marketings, including preparing and submitting specifications, analysis, cost comparisons and recommendations on marketing responses, and preparing marketing report;
  • Gather data and determine cost impact for plan design changes;
  • Monitor carrier/provider services standards;
  • Support the consulting team in preparing documents for client presentations and meetings

To be successful as an Actuarial Benefits Analyst (Co-op Student) with People Corporation, you will need:

  • University student in Mathematics, Statistics, Actuarial Science, or related discipline
  • Advanced competency with Microsoft Excel
  • Proficiency with Word, PowerPoint and Outlook
  • Strong analytical skills
  • Excellent organizational skills with strong attention to detail and high level of accuracy
  • Ability to manage and prioritize competing demands in order to meet client and company needs
  • Ability to work independently and in a team environment
  • Strong written and verbal communication skills
  • Sensitivity to confidential information
  • Actively writing Society of Actuaries (SOA) or Canadian Institute of Actuaries (CIA) exams
  • Knowledge of actuarial valuation software (e.g., ProVal) is an asset
