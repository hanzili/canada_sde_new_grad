About the Role

Shift Ground Axis is hiring an Agri-STEM & Food Systems Research Intern to support our Groundwork Pathways initiative. THIS IS A PAID INTERNSHIP

This role is ideal for a young person interested in food systems, agriculture, hydroponics, controlled-environment growing, greenhouses, community food security, Agri-STEM, research, and youth employment pathways.

The intern will help us learn from people and organizations already doing food systems, agriculture, growing, training, and community food work in Manitoba. This may include research, field visits, partner conversations, site visits, documentation, and helping us understand what is already working locally.

This is not a traditional farm labour role. It is a research, learning, documentation, and partnership-support role focused on food systems and Agri-STEM pathways.

Key Responsibilities

The intern may support the following activities:

  • Research food systems, agriculture, hydroponics, greenhouses, controlled-environment agriculture, and modular growing models.
  • Learn about Manitoba organizations, farms, growers, community food projects, training programs, and technical partners already working in this space.
  • Support possible field visits, site visits, partner meetings, and learning conversations.
  • Take notes and prepare clear summaries from research, meetings, and visits.
  • Help prepare simple briefs, learning materials, and internal planning documents.
  • Explore what youth need to learn to enter Agri-STEM, food systems, growing, and green economy pathways.
  • Help document practical requirements for future food systems projects, such as training, maintenance, growing knowledge, partnerships, equipment, and site-readiness.
  • Support planning for future youth learning opportunities connected to food systems, hydroponics, community growing, and Agri-STEM.
  • Work with Shift Ground Axis staff to turn research and field learning into practical next steps.

Who We’re Looking For

We are looking for someone who is curious, reliable, organized, and interested in learning.

You do not need to be an expert in agriculture or hydroponics. This is an internship, so we are looking for someone with strong interest, willingness to learn, and the ability to follow through.

This role may be a good fit for someone who enjoys asking questions, visiting places, learning from others, organizing information, and turning ideas into practical next steps.

Helpful Backgrounds or Interests

A background or interest in any of the following would be helpful:

  • Agriculture
  • Food systems
  • Biology
  • Environmental studies
  • Nutrition
  • Sustainability
  • Community development
  • Youth work
  • Research
  • Hands-on growing
  • Hydroponics or greenhouse growing
  • Controlled-environment agriculture
  • Community food security

Skills We Value

  • Strong curiosity and willingness to learn
  • Clear writing and note-taking
  • Good organization
  • Comfort doing online research
  • Ability to summarize information clearly
  • Respectful communication
  • Interest in food systems, youth employment, and community impact
  • Comfort participating in field visits, site visits, partner meetings, or learning activities

About Shift Ground Axis

Shift Ground Axis is a Manitoba-based organization focused on youth economic mobility, green jobs, food systems, community innovation, and future-facing skills.

Our work is organized through three connected pathways:

Groundwork focuses on Agri-STEM, food systems, practical implementation, and youth employment pathways.

Futurework focuses on technology, digital tools, AI literacy, environmental data, and the future of work.

Lifework focuses on food sovereignty, public health, community wellbeing, and youth agency.

This internship sits mainly within Groundwork, while connecting to Lifework through food security, community wellbeing, and public health.

Eligibility and Availability

Applicants should be:

  • Legally entitled to work in Canada
  • Available to work 30 hours per week
  • Available from July 6, 2026 to December 31, 2026
  • Able to work in a hybrid role based in Winnipeg, with some remote work and some in-person meetings, field visits, or learning activities
  • Within the eligible youth age range required by the funding program

Why This Role Matters

This internship will help Shift Ground Axis better understand Manitoba’s food systems and Agri-STEM landscape.

The work will support future planning for youth employment pathways, food systems learning, community growing projects, controlled-environment agriculture, and practical partnerships across Manitoba.
