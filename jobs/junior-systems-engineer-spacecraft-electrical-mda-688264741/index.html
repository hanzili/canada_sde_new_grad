Role:

MDA is seeking a Junior Systems Member of Technical Staff (MTS), Spacecraft Electrical to support system-level engineering activities across earth observation and remote sensing satellite programs. Working as part of a cross-functional, multi-disciplinary team, you will contribute to the specification, analysis, integration, and verification of spacecraft electrical systems, including power systems, communications/RF systems, payload electrical interfaces, and EMC/EMI considerations. You will work under the guidance of intermediate and senior engineers, gaining experience in system-level engineering while contributing to the successful delivery of end-to-end satellite missions.

Responsibilities:

As a Junior Systems MTS, Spacecraft Electrical you may have a wide range of responsibilities that may include:

  • Supporting the development and maintenance of electrical requirements from mission level down to subsystem level (bus, payload, ground segment, launch, etc.)
  • Assisting with mission analyses including power budgets, link budgets, and EMC/EMI analyses
  • Supporting development and maintenance of interface control documents (ICDs) across mission segments
  • Contributing to verification planning including development of test matrices and requirements
  • Reviewing subcontractor design documentation for compliance to requirements with guidance from senior engineers
  • Assisting in development of Assembly, Integration and Test (AIT) procedures and supporting execution activities
  • Participating in test campaigns including data review, anomaly tracking, and troubleshooting support
  • Participating in spacecraft validation, commissioning, and early operations activities
  • Supporting engineering documentation, planning, and cost estimation activities

Required Experience:

  • Bachelor’s degree in Electrical Engineering, Engineering Physics, or a related discipline
  • Familiarity with spacecraft electrical systems including power systems, communications/RF systems, and payload electronics
  • Exposure to RF systems, radar systems, or antenna concepts is an asset
  • Familiarity with engineering analysis tools such as Matlab, Python, or similar
  • Experience using MS Excel for engineering analysis
  • Familiarity with lab/test equipment such as oscilloscopes or spectrum analyzers is an asset
  • Strong problem-solving skills and willingness to learn
  • Effective verbal and written communication skills
  • Ability to work in a team environment under guidance from experienced engineers

Comments/Special Considerations:

Successful candidates must obtain and hold security clearance at the reliability status level, and pass security assessment for the Controlled Goods Program (CGP).
