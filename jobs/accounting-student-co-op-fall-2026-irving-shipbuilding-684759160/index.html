About the Role

About Irving Shipbuilding

Canada's national shipbuilder; defense programs

Full Description

Launch your career with an Accounting Co-op at Irving Shipbuilding, where you’ll gain hands-on experience in accounting and reporting while supporting impactful shipbuilding programs for the Royal Canadian Navy. You’ll work alongside experienced professionals, build strong connections, and explore future career opportunities. This role is perfect for proactive, team-oriented Finance, Accounting, or Business students eager to learn and grow.

As a bonus, Irving Shipbuilding covers up to $1000 in co-op fees!

  • Execute standard journal entries with precision and accuracy, contributing to the financial heartbeat of our operations.
  • Reconcile general ledger accounts and ensure everything adds up—your attention to detail will make a real impact.
  • Compile and prepare financial reports that help leaders make informed decisions.
  • Use cutting-edge tools and technologies to analyze financial data and create compelling visualizations.

  • Take advantage of professional development sessions and mentorship from experienced finance professionals who want to see you succeed.

  • Currently enrolled in a 4-year Commerce or Business Administration degree program.

  • In good academic standing with a strong desire to learn and grow.
  • 0–2 years of experience – we’re here to help you build it!
  • Comfortable using the Microsoft Office Suite, especially Excel for data analysis.
  • A strong communicator with excellent interpersonal and problem-solving skills.
  • Organized, adaptable, and able to juggle multiple priorities with ease.
  • A quick learner who’s curious and always looking for ways to improve processes.
  • Passionate about the shipbuilding, defense, or marine industry and excited to be part of a fast-paced organization.

Due to the nature of work Irving Shipbuilding does, all successful applicants must meet requirements for the Canadian Controlled Goods Program (CGP), Canadian Government Security clearance, and U.S. International Traffic in Arms Regulations (ITAR).

Please note this position requires a full-time, in-office presence.

Irving Shipbuilding is proud to be Canada's National Shipbuilder. Our team is constructing more than 20 modern patrol ships and destroyers for the Royal Canadian Navy and Canadian Coast Guard as part of the largest defence procurement project in Canadian history.

By joining our team of more than 2,400 shipbuilders, you can enjoy a dynamic and exciting career with a company dedicated to your success, development, and safety.

At Irving Shipbuilding, we're making history - why not make it with us? Learn more about careers at Irving Shipbuilding: shipsforcanada.ca/opportunities.

We invite applications from all qualified candidates, including racially visible persons, women in underrepresented roles, persons with disabilities, Indigenous persons, and members of the 2SLGBTQIA+ community, and encourage applicants to self-identify.

Irving, Limited, you are part of a dynamic team of professionals who lead with integrity and are passionate about delivering results.
