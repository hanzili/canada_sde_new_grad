About the Role

Conduct field surveys, analyze data, deliver reports

About SYSTRA

Environmental consultancy with field and GIS focus

Full Description

:

SYSTRA Canada is part of the SYSTRA group, an international consulting and engineering group, a world leader in the design of transport infrastructures. SYSTRA Canada is an engineering and consulting firm whose primary focus is to offer transportation solutions, whether for passengers or goods: feasibility studies of building a new railway line, increasing the capacity of the existing infrastructure, privatization of a railway.

Context:

Within an engineering consulting firm, the junior biologist/technician contributes to environmental studies, secures regulatory approvals, and integrates ecological considerations into development and infrastructure projects. The role combines fieldwork, data analysis, and the preparation of deliverables, under the supervision of senior biologists and engineers.

Missions/Main Duties:

  • Fieldwork and Inventories: Plan and conduct field campaigns per protocols; survey wildlife, vegetation, and habitats (wetlands, watercourses, sensitive areas); assess water quality; map vegetation; collect GPS/metadata; ensure data quality, site safety, and accurate records.
  • Analysis, Mapping, and Data Management: Process field data, run basic analyses, and produce thematic maps (QGIS/ArcGIS); support habitat classification, ecological assessments, and preparation of figures and appendices.
  • Environmental Monitoring and Mitigation: Support construction and environmental monitoring; verify controls (sediment, wildlife, vegetation, noise); contribute to avoidance, reduction, and offset measures; adjust plans based on field conditions.

Profile/Skills:

Education : A college or university degree in biology, ecology, natural sciences, or a related field (DEC, BSc).
Years of experience : Prior experience (internships or seasonal roles) in biological surveys or environmental monitoring is an asset.
Technical competencies :

  • Proficiency with fish inventory methods and fish habitat assessment.
  • Proficiency with several basic survey methods (ichthyofauna, wetlands, flora, herpetofauna, avifauna) and associated field equipment (an asset).
  • Knowledge of Quebec and Canadian environmental regulations, as well as methodological guidelines.
  • Experience with geospatial databases and GIS, such as ArcGIS and QGIS (an asset).
  • Clear verbal and written communication skills, both in French and in English*
  • Reasons for requiring bilingualism: You will have the opportunity to work with a varied clientele, including both French- and English-speaking companies. Bilingualism is therefore fundamental to building strong relationships with our customers, understanding their specific needs, communicating effectively in their preferred language and facilitating strategic meetings.

Personal and soft skills :

  • Methodological and scientific rigor.
  • Strong organization and ability to manage unforeseen situations.
  • Proactivity and curiosity for technological innovation.

Other requirement:

  • Valid driver’s license.
