The Junior Embedded Software Designer will support the development of embedded software for an upcoming automotive project. This role is ideal for an new grad engineer with a strong foundation in embedded C, microcontroller-based systems, and a desire to grow within an automotive environment. The engineer will work under the guidance of senior engineers and contribute to software design, implementation, testing, and debugging activities across the embedded software lifecycle.

What You'll Do

  • Contribute to the full embedded software development lifecycle, including requirements analysis, software architecture and detailed design, embedded software implementation, unit and integration test development, and software verification and validation.
  • Collaborate closely with cross-functional teams, including Hardware, Mechanical, Product, Software Project Engineering, and Systems Engineering.
  • Support the development and implementation of new features on automotive embedded platforms, such as bring-up and integration of cameras, SoCs, sensors, chipsets, and low-level drivers.
  • Participate in code reviews, effort estimation, and project planning activities.
  • Actively contribute to process improvement initiatives and ensure adherence to software development and compliance standards.
  • Identify, analyze, triage, and help resolve complex hardware and software issues during development and integration.

How You'll Do It

  • Demonstrate strong attention to detail, ensuring work is complete, accurate, and aligned with technical and process requirements, while following up on assigned actions and commitments.
  • Show an action‑oriented mindset, approaching new tasks and technical challenges with enthusiasm, ownership, and a willingness to learn.
  • Apply structured problem‑solving skills by analyzing issues, identifying root causes, evaluating solution alternatives, and implementing effective fixes.
  • Communicate clearly and professionally with team members and stakeholders, adapting communication style to suit technical and non‑technical audiences.
  • Contribute positively to a collaborative team environment, building strong working relationships to achieve shared project goals.
  • Maintain a high standard of integrity, professionalism, and confidentiality in all aspects of work.
  • Adhere to all company policies, procedures, and safety requirements, and actively support a safe and compliant workplace.

What You'll Need To Get The Job Done

  • Bachelor’s degree in Electrical Engineering, Computer Engineering, Computer Science, or a related discipline.
  • Solid foundational knowledge of embedded systems and electronic components.
  • Proficiency in C programming for embedded applications; exposure to assembly language and real-time operating systems is an asset.
  • Understanding of embedded software development concepts, including low-level drivers, hardware interaction, and constrained systems.
  • Strong motivation, a proactive attitude, and effective interpersonal and communication skills.
  • Ability to manage multiple tasks, prioritize effectively, and adapt in a fast‑paced development environment.

What Will Make Us REALLY Love You

  • 2+ years of experience (including internships or academic projects) across phases of the software development lifecycle.
  • Working experience or strong familiarity with Linux-based development environments.
  • Exposure to multimedia systems, such as video, audio, signal processing, or compression technologies.
  • Experience or academic exposure to camera systems and Image Signal Processing (ISP).
  • Hands-on experience with development and collaboration tools (e.g., Git, Bitbucket, Jenkins, JIRA, Azure DevOps).
  • Understanding of Agile development methodologies.
  • Awareness of static code analysis tools, code coverage metrics, and unit test development techniques.
