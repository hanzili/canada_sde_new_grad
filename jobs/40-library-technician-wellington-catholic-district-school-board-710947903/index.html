About the Role

Library technician supports cataloging and reference

About Wellington Catholic District School Board

Ontario Catholic school board serving local students

Full Description

The Wellington Catholic DSB is committed to equity for all students and staff, and to delivering the highest quality education through a qualified workforce that reflects the diversity of the communities we serve. Upon request, we will provide reasonable accommodation in the hiring process to support applicants with disabilities. Please see the job opportunities page on our website for more details.

Job Posting 26-099 40% Library Technician – 10 Month PERMANENT Location: St John Catholic Elementary School, Arthur Expected Start Date: TBA – approximately August 31, 2026 Schedule: 14 hours per week, 7 hours/full day between 8:00 a.m. and 4:30 p.m. Scheduled Days: TBD Band 3: $30.29- $34.64 per hour The Library Technician supports the operation of the library by performing duties in acquisitions, cataloging and classification, circulation, reference, audio visual, and as appropriate, technical and administrative services under the direction of the Principal. Library technicians are responsible for organizing and maintaining learning resources using a manual or automated library management system. The Library Technician will also be responsible for assisting library patrons in locating information.

Responsibilities And Duties

  • Instruct patrons in the use of the Library Catalog and Digital Resources
  • Instruct patrons in research methodologies and academic honesty
  • Provide Digital Resource training for patrons and staff
  • Assist in collection development and maintenance
  • Gather Digital resources (such as indexes of web sites, manuals and tutorials) to support Reference functions
  • Prepare displays, booklists and programs to promote Library goals
  • Descriptive cataloging, classification, coding and processing of print and audio-visual material
  • Provide assistance with interlibrary loans
  • Assist with reference services
  • Produce instructional materials and maintain files
  • Work within the library budget; ordering all materials for the library
  • Incidental supervision of students as required
  • Assist in the preparation of document control procedures; implement and manage project document control and document management processes and systems
  • Ensure that all received electronic media are filed appropriately within the document management system
  • Develop filing error tracking system; index and maintain hardcopy information
  • Operate audio-visual equipment and assist in simple maintenance
  • Other administration duties as required

Required Qualifications And Experience

  • Must have a Secondary School Diploma and a Community College 2 (two) year Diploma program in Library & Information Technician
  • Must have at least two (2) years of previous related experience
  • Experience in library automation software, Microsoft Office, electronic technological devices and standard AV equipment
  • Previous experience in a school library setting would be an asset
  • Effective communication skills, both verbal and written, with individuals at all levels of the Board
  • Excellent interpersonal and customer service skills
  • Strong organizational, time management and prioritization skills
  • Ability to create and develop promotional materials
  • Demonstrated ability to work well as part of a professional team
  • Demonstrated accuracy and attention to detail
  • Excellent research skills
  • Strong problem identification and problem resolution skills
  • Critical and logical thinking, analysis and/or reasoning

Interested and qualified applicants are invited to respond.
