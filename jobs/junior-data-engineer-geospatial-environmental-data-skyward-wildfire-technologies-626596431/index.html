About the Role

Build data pipelines for geospatial datasets

About Skyward Wildfire Technologies

Canadian startup with impactful wildfire analytics

Full Description

Company Overview

Skyward is a dynamic Canadian startup on a mission to make the world safer. Our technology can prevent lightning and out-of-control wildfires, protect communities, and avoid up to 5% of global GHG emissions. We combine science, big data, and machine learning to tackle one of nature's most unpredictable and devastating forces; lightning. If you’re passionate about making a real-world impact, we want you on our team!

Role Overview

We are seeking a Junior Data Engineer to join our data science team and work closely with a Senior Data Scientist on projects involving large-scale geospatial and environmental datasets. This role is ideal for someone who enjoys working with real-world data, building reliable data pipelines, and operating in a Unix-based, remote computing environment.

Key Responsibilities

  • Support analysis and modeling efforts using geospatial and environmental datasets
  • Clean, validate, and preprocess large, complex datasets
  • Design, implement, and maintain data pipelines and ETL workflows
  • Work efficiently on remote machines and high-performance computing environments
  • Collaborate closely with the supervising Senior Data Scientist to translate research needs into reproducible data workflows
  • Document data processes and ensure reproducibility and data quality

Required Skills and Qualifications

  • Bachelor's degree in Meteorology, Atmospheric Science, or a related field
  • Strong programming skills in Python, including experience with libraries such as pandas, numpy, xarray, geopandas, rasterio, and/or netCDF-related tools
  • Experience working in Unix/Linux environments (shell scripting, file systems, remote access)
  • Familiarity with geospatial and environmental data formats (e.g., GeoTIFF, NetCDF, HDF, shapefiles)
  • Experience building and maintaining data pipelines and performing robust data cleaning
  • Comfortable working with large datasets on remote servers or cloud/HPC systems
  • Solid understanding of version control (e.g., Git)

Nice-to-Have Skills and Qualifications

  • Master’s degree in Meteorology, Atmospheric Science, or a related field
  • Familiarity with some earth observation, climate model, or other geospatial datasets
  • Experience working in one or more cloud platforms (AWS, GCP, Azure)
  • Basic data visualization and mapping skills (e.g., matplotlib, seaborn, folium, cartopy)
  • Background in environmental science, climate science, remote sensing, or related domains
  • Experience with wildfire behaviour and modeling

What We Offer:

  • An Exciting Mission:

Contribute to groundbreaking solutions aimed at reducing wildfires and protecting the planet. - Growth Opportunities:

As an early member of the team, you’ll have significant impact and ample opportunities for career advancement. - A Collaborative, Fast-Paced Culture:

Work alongside passionate individuals who are dedicated to innovating for good. - Competitive Compensation & Benefits:
