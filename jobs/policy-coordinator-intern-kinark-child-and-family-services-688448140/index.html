About the Role

Support policy development and policy coordination.

About Kinark Child and Family Services

Kinark: respected Canadian child and family services nonprofit

Full Description

Job Description The Policy Coordinator supports the effective development, implementation, and maintenance of organizational policies. The primary purpose of the role is to ensure policies are well ‑ researched, clearly written, accurately tracked, and kept current, supporting compliance, clarity, and alignment with organizational objectives. Key outcomes include timely coordination of policy updates, high ‑ quality policy documentation, and reliable administrative and research support to policy leads and stakeholders.

The hourly rate is $23.00 CAD The Policy Coordinator will be responsible for the following duties and responsibilities:

  • Support the development, review, approval, and update of policies and related documents.
  • Conduct background research, summarize findings, and support evidence ‑ based policy work.
  • Assist with drafting, formatting, and proofreading policies, briefings, and supporting materials.
  • Maintain policy inventories, version control, records, and tracking tools to ensure accuracy and currency.
  • Coordinate input from internal stakeholders, track feedback, and support consultations or review cycles.
  • Help ensure policies align with applicable legislation, standards, and organizational requirements.
  • Schedule meetings, prepare agendas and notes, and support reporting and workflow processes.
  • Assist with publishing policies and responding to internal inquiries related to policy content or processes.
  • Other duties may be assigned within the scope of this position

Key Relationships:

  • Policy leads – receive direction, support policy development, and coordinate reviews and approvals.
  • Communications team – assist with formatting, publishing, and sharing finalized policies.
  • Administrative or project support teams – coordinate schedules, meetings, documentation, and workflow processes.

Qualifications:

  • Post ‑ secondary education in public policy, political science, public administration, communications, law, social sciences, or a related field
  • Strong written communication skills, with the ability to draft clear, accurate, and well ‑ structured documents
  • Strong organizational and time ‑ management skills, with attention to detail
  • Proficiency in standard office software (e.g., Microsoft Word, Excel, SharePoint, or similar document ‑ management tools)
  • Ability to work collaboratively with diverse stakeholders and follow established processes
  • Some experience in policy, research, administration, governance, or a related area
  • Experience conducting research and summarizing information is an asset
  • Experience supporting document development, editing, or records management is an asset
  • Familiarity with policy approval processes, compliance, or legislative frameworks is an asset
  • Demonstrated ability to manage multiple tasks and meet deadlines in a structured environment
  • Strong interpersonal skills
  • Health care experience would be an asset
  • Bilingualism is an asset
