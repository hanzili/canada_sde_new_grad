Overview

Reporting to both the Director of Finance and Intermediate Accountant, the Junior Accountant plays a key role in supporting accounting operations, ensuring accurate financial records, and assisting with day-to-day financial processes.

Responsibilities

  • Post and process journal entries to ensure accurate recording of business transactions.
  • Maintain and update financial data in the accounting system (QuickBooks Online) to ensure accuracy and timeliness.
  • Manage and monitor accounts payable, including invoice processing, payment coordination and vendor follow-ups.
  • Assist with accounts receivable, including invoice preparation, billing coordination, and follow-up on outstanding balances.
  • Assist with payroll remittances and statutory filings as required.
  • Assist in the preparation of balance sheets, income statements, and other financial reports.
  • Reconcile bank accounts, credit cards, and other balance sheet accounts, and prepare related journal entries.
  • Assist in month-end close processes, including accruals, fixed asset depreciation, and other adjusting entries.
  • Track and monitor purchase orders for vendors.
  • Assist in audit preparation, including gathering and organizing required financial information and supporting documentation
  • Communicate with vendors and contractors regarding routine accounting queries.
  • Perform other duties as requested by the Director of Finance and Accountant.

Requirements

  • Diploma or Bachelor’s degree in Accounting, Finance, or related field.
  • 1-2 years of accounting experience preferred
  • Proficiency in QuickBooks Online or similar accounting software.
  • Strong knowledge of Microsoft Excel (formulas, pivot tables, basic functions).
  • Understanding of accounting principles (GAAP or IFRS).
  • Attention to detail and high level of accuracy in data entry and financial reporting.
  • Strong organizational and time management skills to handle multiple tasks and meet deadlines.
  • Analytical and problem-solving skills to identify discrepancies and assist in reconciliation.
  • Good communication skills, both written and verbal, for interacting with vendors, customers, and internal teams.
  • Ability to work independently and as part of a team.
  • Discretion and integrity in handling confidential financial information.
