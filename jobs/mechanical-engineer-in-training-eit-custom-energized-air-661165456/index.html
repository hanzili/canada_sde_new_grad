About the Role

About Custom Energized Air

Edmonton-based industrial group with strong EIT progression

Full Description

Carmela Industries Inc., established in 1997 and headquartered in Edmonton, Alberta, is the parent company of several specialized industrial divisions. We provide strategic guidance and corporate services—including accounting, human resources, marketing, and IT—to support our subsidiaries' success.

Our Divisions & Expertise

  • Summit Valve and Controls (est. 1992) – A trusted supplier of world-class valve, automation, and instrumentation products, with locations across Western Canada.
  • Special Alloy Fabricators (SAF) (est. 1989) – A leader in custom-engineered, high-performance valves and fittings, serving clients globally with patented technologies.
  • Custom Energized Air (CEA) (est. 1999 – acquired by Carmela in 2023) – A premier provider of compressed air solutions, offering in-house engineering and state-of-the-art manufacturing capabilities.

Through these divisions, we drive innovation, deliver exceptional service, and build long-term customer relationships.

Start Your Engineering Career Where Design Meets Real‑World Execution

Custom Energized Air (CEA) is seeking a Mechanical Engineer‑in‑Training (EIT) to join our growing Engineering team in Edmonton. This opportunity is ideal for a recent graduate or early‑career engineer looking to gain hands‑on experience across the full lifecycle of engineered industrial systems—from RFQ and design through fabrication, testing, and commissioning.

At CEA, engineers don’t just design on paper, you’ll work closely with experienced engineers, operations, service teams, and suppliers to deliver hydraulic, pneumatic, and packaged mechanical systems that support real customer applications.

What You’ll Do

Engineering & Technical Support

  • Assist in the application and design of hydraulic and pneumatic systems, including packaged equipment, HPUs, and air compressors
  • Support system sizing, component selection, and basic engineering calculations under supervision
  • Assist with preparing:
  • P&IDs and schematics
  • General arrangement and basic fabrication drawings
  • Technical documentation and data sheets
  • Build foundational knowledge of:
  • Fluid power systems (hydraulic and pneumatic)
  • Mechanical design and fabrication practices
  • Industrial equipment and system integration

RFQ & Project Support

  • Support RFQ reviews and quoting activities by assisting with technical scope definition
  • Review customer specifications and help identify technical requirements
  • Participate in project handovers and support execution activities
  • Assist with documentation, reporting, and tracking for engineered project orders

Execution & Field Exposure

  • Work with Operations and Service teams to support fabrication, assembly, and testing
  • Assist with factory acceptance testing (FAT) and related documentation
  • Support commissioning preparation and troubleshooting under supervision
  • Provide on‑site or field support as required (occasional travel)

Supply Chain & Vendor Exposure

  • Assist with technical evaluation of components and suppliers
  • Support sourcing activities from an engineering perspective
  • Communicate with vendors under senior engineer guidance
  • Participate in cost reviews and technical comparisons

What We’re Looking For:

Required Qualifications

  • Bachelor’s degree in Mechanical Engineering from an accredited institution
  • Registered or eligible for registration as an Engineer‑in‑Training (EIT) with APEGA
  • 0–2 years of related experience (co‑op or internship experience considered an asset)
  • Strong interest in industrial equipment, mechanical systems, or fluid power applications

Skills & Attributes

  • Strong analytical and problem‑solving skills
  • High attention to detail and quality
  • Proactive, organized, and adaptable approach
  • Willingness to learn and take on increasing responsibility
  • Strong written and verbal communication skills
  • Ability to work collaboratively across engineering, operations, and service teams
  • Customer‑focused mindset

Tools You’ll Use

  • SolidWorks or equivalent 3D CAD
  • AutoCAD (2D layouts and schematics)
  • Microsoft Office 365 (Outlook, Word, Excel, SharePoint)
  • ERP and CRM systems (exposure provided)

Why Join Custom Energized Air?

  • Hands‑on experience with real engineered systems, not just theory
  • Strong mentorship and support toward P.Eng. progression
  • Broad exposure across engineering, operations, and supply chain
  • Opportunity to grow into Project Engineer and Design Engineer roles
  • Collaborative, practical, and team‑focused work environment
