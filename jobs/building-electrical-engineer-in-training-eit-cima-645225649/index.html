Job Description

CIMA+'s Buildings team is renowned for its expertise in designing high-quality buildings.  We are committed to providing the most cost-effective solutions to engineering challenges and offer a diverse range of projects from the initial planning stages to design and construction. CIMA+ cultivates career growth and provides opportunities as unique as you are. With a constant desire to offer our clients the best possible service, we have set up a team of specialists dedicated to high-performance projects to support our clients and design teams across Canada. Join us and immerse yourself in a dynamic, innovative, and collaborative environment where you’ll make a real impact. Together, we’ll push boundaries and go above what’s expected of us to meet the challenges of tomorrow and engineer a better world!

We are looking for a Building Electrical Engineer in Training (EIT) in the Atlantic area. As a team member in building electrical projects, the EIT will assist to the design of plans and models, perform surveys, and assist the engineering team in the realization of projects in the commercial, industrial, institutional and hospital sectors.

You will work in a dynamic environment, as part of a team of engineers, technicians and drafters, on challenging projects. You will evolve within a passionate team in a context where initiative and trust are strongly encouraged.

Main responsibilities

  • Assist with proposal preparation and responding to RFPs
  • Assist the team in the electrical design of the building, such as electrical distribution, lighting including AGI32 calculation, fire alarm, etc.
  • Liaise with mechanical team on HVAC system design, etc.
  • Assist with selection of equipment
  • Assist with preparing technical reports
  • Assist with preparation of specifications
  • Assist in preparing construction cost estimates
  • Project planning including design schedule, and proposed construction schedules
  • Ensure compliance to the quality assurance and control program
  • Assist with building assessments for electrical systems.

Qualifications

  • Bachelor's degree in electrical engineering or equivalent
  • Needs to be Engineer in Training (EIT) from a Canadian regulatory body (ex: Engineers Nova Scotia, PEO, etc.);
  • Need to have demonstrable experience in building electrical systems (electrical distribution, lighting, fire alarm, hvac) etc.
  • Knowledge of building electrical codes and regulations
  • Minimum 2 years of relevant experience
  • Knowledge of Revit and AutoCAD software
  • Knowledge of AGI 32 lighting calculation software (an asset)
  • ASP Construction card (an asset)
  • Valid Driver's license .
  • Proficiency in French (an asset).
