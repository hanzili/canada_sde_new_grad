The Opportunity Your Impact Starts Here – Join our Mine Surface Water Team and contribute to hydrology and hydrotechnical engineering services for high-profile projects across Canada and globally. Your work will help future-proof communities and support sustainable mining practices.

Your Impact

  • Participate in civil engineering design of hydraulic structures.
  • Conduct statistical analysis of climate and hydrology data.
  • Perform hydrological modeling, climate change assessments, and 1D, 2D, or 3D hydraulic modeling for flood studies, dam breach studies, and tailings dam breach studies.
  • Design baseline hydrological studies, instrumentation, and field data collection.
  • Lead or assist with field studies, including bathymetric/topographic surveys and flow measurements.
  • Conduct water balance and drainage basin analysis.
  • Assist with preparation of technical reports and proposals.

The Skills That Set You Apart

  • Bachelor’s or Master’s degree in Civil or Water Resources Engineering.
  • Eligible for registration as an Engineer-in-Training (EIT) in Alberta (APEGA) or British Columbia (EGBC).
  • Class 5 driver’s license with a clean abstract.
  • Experience with hydrological and hydraulic models is an asset.
  • Comfort with remote fieldwork and working around water (training available).
  • Awareness or experience in environmental science and/or engineering is an asset.
  • Commitment to technical excellence and ability to troubleshoot on the fly.
