About the Role

Assist wildlife field programs and ecological assessments

About Environmental Resources Management

Global sustainability consultancy focused on wildlife and environment

Full Description

Are you a junior wildlife biologist ready to make a measurable impact on Canada’s most significant mining and energy projects?

Join ERM’s Capital Project Delivery Team in one of our Canadian offices (Vancouver, Smithers, Calgary, or Saskatoon) and help drive sustainable solutions that balance development with biodiversity protection.

Why This Role Matters

At ERM, we partner with leading organizations to solve complex environmental challenges. As a Consulting Associate, Junior Wildlife Biologist, you’ll play a critical role in safeguarding wildlife and habitats while enabling responsible resource development. Your expertise will influence decisions that shape landscapes and communities for generations.

What Your Impact Is

  • Assist with wildlife field programs across Western and Northern Canada
  • Contribute to high-quality ecological assessments and mitigation strategies for major mining and energy projects.
  • Support collaboration with Indigenous communities, regulators, and industry leaders to ensure compliance and conservation.
  • Contribute to the challenging and impactful programs which help ERM’s clients shape a sustainable future.

What You'll Bring

Required

  • B.Sc. or diploma in Biological Sciences or related field (or equivalent experience).
  • 0-2 years in wildlife biology, environmental consulting or related sectors (government, mining, energy).
  • Previous experience participating in wildlife field surveys.
  • Some knowledge of federal, provincial, and territorial environmental regulations.
  • Strong communication skills and ability to build meaningful relationships with team members and clients.
  • Willingness to ask questions and learn from senior members of our project teams.
  • Eligibility for professional registration (e.g., R.P.Bio., P.Biol., or equivalent).
  • Driver's License Required: This position requires a valid driver's license and/or the ability to operate a company vehicle due to the nature of job duties, which include frequent travel to various client locations across a large geographical area.
  • This position is not eligible for immigration sponsorship.

Preferred

  • M.Sc. in Biological Sciences or related discipline.
  • 2+ years of consulting experience.
  • Experience deploying and maintaining autonomous recording units (ARUs) and/or wildlife trail cameras
  • Experience using Field Maps, Survey 123, and handheld GPS units
  • Strong knowledge of the regulatory regulations in British Columbia, Nunavut, and/or Northwest Territories

Key Responsibilities

  • Assist with wildlife field programs (birds, amphibians, large mammals).
  • Contribute to wildlife assessments for Environmental Impact Statements and management plans under the direction of a project team.
  • Conduct literature reviews and identify recommendations for wildlife mitigations.
  • Support tasks for technical data reporting and compliance reporting.
  • Build positive working relationships with clients, Indigenous assistants, and ERM colleagues worldwide.

WhoWe Are:

As the largest global pure play sustainability consultancy, we partner with the world’s leading organizations, creating innovative solutions to sustainability challenges and unlocking commercial opportunities that meet the needs of today while preserving opportunity for future generations.

At ERM we know that creating a diverse, equitable and inclusive work environment is an essential part of making our company a great place to build a career. We also see our diversity as a strength that helps us create better solutions for our clients. Our diverse team of world-class experts supports clients across the breadth of their organizations to operationalize sustainability, underpinned by our deep technical expertise in addressing their environmental, health, safety, risk and social issues. We call this capability our “boots to boardroom” approach for its comprehensive service model that allows ERM to develop strategic and technical solutions that advance objectives on the ground or at the executive level.
