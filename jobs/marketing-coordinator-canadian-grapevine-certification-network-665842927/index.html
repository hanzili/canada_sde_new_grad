Full Description

Job Overview

We are seeking an enthusiastic, driven, creative, and organized individual to join our team and help us reach a wider audience through engaging and professional-grade digital content. This is a temporary role that is to be funded in part by the Canada Summer Jobs Grant. The position would start early May and end late August (exact dates to be determined). The selected candidate must meet all requirements of the aforementioned grant program.

The ideal candidate will assist in all areas of marketing content creation, management, planning and other supporting responsibilities. They will be comfortable in maintaining various social media channels, developing new creative content, and have a excellent time management skills which enables them to juggle multiple priorities.

To qualify for this role, the candidate must be a Canadian citizen (international students on visa status are NOT eligible), and enrolled in a post-secondary program in marketing, communications, information technology, media, graphic design, or a related field of study, or be between the ages of 18 to 30 with relevant experience in any of the previously listed fields.

Responsibilities

  • Analysis and improvement of marketing strategy in digital channels: social media, website, Google Analytics, and others as appropriate;
  • Organizing and leading “content days” with industry members such as vineyard managers, researchers, winemakers, board members, etc.
  • Planning and implementation of social media strategy, marketing emails, and assistance with organizing webinars;
  • Webpage updates and design;
  • Attendance at regular virtual Knowledge and Technology Transfer (KTT) Committee meetings and occasional virtual Board meetings;
  • Marketing content development for KTT-related events and/or conferences (e.g., brochures, information packets, business cards, posters, etc.);
  • Other responsibilities as required to support the Project Manager and Executive Director regarding marketing, communications, and/or digital media activities.
  • Developing content day plans, themes, and schedules.
  • Digital content development, including, but not limited to: short videos, photos, graphics, blog posts, marketing emails, and social media;

Skills

  • Experience in digital content creation such as graphic design, social media (X, Facebook, Instagram, LinkedIn, YouTube), videos, photos, webpages, etc.;
  • Knowledge and experience working with the Canva, Adobe Creative Suite, or other creative software;
  • Knowledge of website platforms such as Tymbrel, Wordpress, Wix, etc;
  • Consistently takes initiative and brings new ideas to the table;
  • Adept user of Microsoft Office applications;
  • Strong organizational skills;
  • Ability to multitask and effectively prioritize projects, with the ability to consistently meet deadlines;
  • Strong communication skills;
  • Self-motivation and proactivity.

Other items that make a candidate stand out may include:

  • Fluency in both English and French;
  • Ability to provide examples of previous work as a frame of reference for the assessment of quality of work and fit to CGCN-RCCV;
  • Experience or knowledge about agriculture, specifically the grape and wine industry.

This is a completely remote role, with the organization’s head office located at 1634 South Service Road, St. Catharines, ON L2R 6P9. The occasional in-person workday may be arranged ahead of time as needed or desired. Locations of content days will be arranged well in advance.

Anticipated Term: May 4th through August 21st, 2026 OR May 11th through August 28th, 2026.

Hours: 30 hours per week. Regular working hours are 8:30 to 4:30 pm EST Monday through Friday. Flexible working hours may be available and can be negotiated with the candidate.

Pay: $20.60 per hour

Benefits:

  • Flexible schedule
  • Work from home

Work Location: Hybrid remote in St. Catharines, ON
