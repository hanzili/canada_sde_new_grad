2026 Fall, Commercial Banking - Commercial Banking Advisor Student Intern

About the Role

Assist clients with banking solutions and needs

About RBC

Top Canadian bank with strong internship programs

Full Description

Job Description

This four-month Co-op in a Commercial Banking Centre will prepare the intern to provide excellent reactive and proactive service to Commercial clients in a range of specialized areas. Comprehensive training will combine observation, self-study and interactive courses, guaranteeing an exceptional internship experience.

What is the opportunity?

This role is a stepping stone to a rewarding career that will give you every opportunity to reach your full potential in a supportive, high-performance environment.

During this contractual co-op, you will learn about an exciting career in commercial banking. This is your opportunity to become a critical member of a results-oriented team, a key partner to the Relationship Manager, Commercial Financial Services and their clients. You will work with colleagues who have a passion in delivering a superior experience to business clients.

What will you do?

  • Learn about needs discovery and solution development to provide commercial clients with advice for their day-to-day banking operations
  • Partner with Account Managers to identify, plan & deliver the best solutions to clients and prospects based on strong knowledge of client’s operational requirements
  • Participate in ongoing contact programs to forge strong relationships with clients by staying in touch, providing value-added service and meaningful advice

What do you need to succeed?

Must-have

  • Currently enrolled in a post-secondary (university/college) in Business, Commerce, Economics, or a related discipline.
  • Strong communication, organizational, and analytical skills
  • Financial acumen; ability to complete financial studies and risk analysis; experience in assembling financing solutions for the needs of clients with different levels of complexity
  • Ability to cultivate solid relationships with partners.
  • Experience with emerging communications and technology (i.e., web based meetings, social media, digital banking, and mobile applications)

Nice-to-have

  • Bilingualism (English/French) written and spoken.

What’s in it for you?

We thrive on the challenge to be our best, to think progressively so we keep growing, and to work together to deliver trusted advice so our clients can succeed and communities can prosper. We care about each other and are determined to reach our full potential, make a difference in our communities, and help everyone achieve success.

  • A dynamic, innovative and collaborative team
  • Flexible options for work–life balance
  • Opportunity to make an important contribution and have a lasting impact

Please note:

In order to be eligible for these student positions, you must either:

  • Be returning back to school after the work term end-date; or
  • If you are not returning back to school (i.e. are graduating immediately after the work term), you must require the full work term as a mandatory component in order to graduate successfully
