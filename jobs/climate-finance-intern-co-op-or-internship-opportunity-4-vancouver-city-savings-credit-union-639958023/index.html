About the Role

Assist credit underwriting, support deal origination and reporting

About Vancouver City Savings Credit Union

Vancity: Canada's values-driven credit union

Full Description

This position is based at Vancity Community Investment Bank (VCIB), an Ontario-based subsidiary of Vancouver City Savings Credit Union (Vancity). As Canada’s first values-driven bank, VCIB provides banking, investing and financing solutions to help purpose-driven businesses and organizations create, grow and foster change, including specialized financing for social purpose real estate projects as well as clean energy and energy efficiency projects.

Vancity is a member-owned credit union built on the principles of inclusion, social justice, and community impact. Since 1946, we’ve worked to challenge the status quo, break down barriers, and build a more equitable future. With bold commitments—including becoming net-zero by 2040 across all mortgages and loans—we are championing financial resilience, climate action, and belonging for our members and communities.

As the largest private sector Living Wage Employer in Canada and home to a team of 2,300 passionate employees, Vancity offers competitive rewards and benefits, diverse career pathways, and the opportunity to be part of something bigger.

The Opportunity:

We’re looking for an intern to join our Vancity Community Investment Bank’s (VCIB) Climate Finance team for a 4-month opportunity. VCIB’s Climate Finance team originates, evaluates, and structures financing solutions for environmentally positive and impactful infrastructure projects across a range of asset classes in Canada. This role reports into the Manager, Business Development – Climate Finance.

A typical day would involve:

  • Assisting with ongoing portfolio management by coordinating quarterly and annual reviews, information requests, tracking receipt of financial statements and compliance certificates, and serving as the point person to ensure timely submission of required reporting from clients.
  • Managing the collection and tracking of climate impact data, including estimated GHG emissions reductions (tCO₂e), clean energy generation metrics, and other sustainability indicators used for internal reporting and external disclosures.
  • Supporting the deal team on active mandates by contributing to credit memos and amendment documents among other working files.
  • Supporting with deal origination and pipeline development by conducting market research on renewable energy, energy efficiency, storage, and clean infrastructure opportunities across Canada.
  • Assisting in organizing and maintaining internal documentation systems, Q&A logs, and diligence trackers by improving file structures and enhancing data accessibility across the Climate Finance team.

You are:

  • A Critical Thinker - You seek to facilitate data-driven decision making, including "rolling up your sleeves" to elicit requirements, research, analyze data
  • Curious & innovative - You always do your research and analyze it to assess and recommend a course of action considering industry best practices
  • A team player - you can engage with members of your team as well as cross-functional teams and are willing to collaborate across projects and initiatives.

You have:

  • Current enrollment in an undergraduate program within an applicable field and eligible for co-op/internship opportunities
  • An interest in Climate and sustainability, impact-oriented credit investing
  • A willingness to work in a highly flexible environment with multiple competing priorities.
  • A high level of attention to detail

Bonus points, if you have:

  • Coursework or demonstrated interest in corporate finance, project finance, infrastructure, or private credit, with an understanding of basic credit and cash‑flow concepts.
  • Familiarity with climate, sustainability, or impact investing themes, including energy transition, decarbonization, infrastructure, or ESG/impact frameworks.
  • Strong analytical and organizational skills, with experience working with financial data, diligence materials, or credit underwriting.
  • Foundational Excel modeling skills, with familiarity with core credit metrics such as leverage, debt service coverage, cash‑flow analysis, and downside scenarios.
