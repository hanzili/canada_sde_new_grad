Accounting / FP&A Internship

Co-op, Finance & Accounting - Summer 2026

at Global Relay

📍 Location Vancouver, British Columbia Hybrid
📋 Details Co-op internship · Summer 2026 (4 months) · $42,000–$56,400 CAD · Summer 2026
📅 Posted Feb 27, 2026

About the Role

Process AP/AR, reconciliations, reporting; support tax

About Global Relay

Global Relay: established, regulator-focused archiving leader

Full Description

Who we are: For over 25 years, Global Relay has set the standard in enterprise information archiving with industry-leading cloud archiving, surveillance, eDiscovery, and analytics solutions. We securely capture and preserve the communications data of the world’s most highly regulated firms, giving them greater visibility and control over their information and ensuring compliance with stringent regulations.

Though we offer competitive compensation and benefits and all the other perks one would expect from an established company, we are not your typical technology company. Global Relay is a career-building company. A place for big ideas. New challenges. Groundbreaking innovation. It’s a place where you can genuinely make an impact – and be recognized for it.

We believe great businesses thrive on diversity, inclusion, and the contributions of all employees. To that end, we recruit candidates from different backgrounds and foster a work environment that encourages employees to collaborate and learn from each other, completely free of barriers.

Your role: The Finance & Accounting Co-op reports to the Controller, supporting the Finance & Accounting team with diverse tasks across the accounting department. This internship offers a unique opportunity for a university student to gain hands-on experience on the day-to-day operations of an accounting department. The role focuses on key areas, such as procurement, accounts payable, accounts receivable, financial reporting, and taxation. Additionally, the intern will contribute to other areas within the department, as needed, allowing them to broaden their understanding of full-cycle accounting principles and develop a comprehensive skill set.

Your responsibilities:

  • Verify and process vendor invoices in accordance with established financial policies and procedures, ensuring that all necessary approvals and department allocations are obtained and that invoices are properly matched to supporting documentation, such as purchase orders, contracts, and pre-approved budgets, to ensure accuracy and compliance.
  • Maintain accurate and up-to-date records of accounts payable correspondence.
  • Provide support for daily purchasing operations, including stakeholder engagement, purchase order management, vendor quote solicitation, and pricing analysis.
  • Manage and maintain accurate and up-to-date records in the purchasing portal and expenditure tracking databases.
  • Ensure the accuracy and integrity of vendor records and provide support for the onboarding process of new vendors, including verification of account information and electronic payment details.
  • Assist Travel team with organizing travel request forms and ensuring required approvals are obtained.
  • Support submission and tracking of travel-related receipts and reimbursements in accordance with company policy.
  • Prepare monthly bank reconciliations and assist in other areas of month-end close.
  • Prepare GST/HST, PST, value-added tax, and various other sales and use tax returns on a timely basis.
  • Identify opportunities for process improvement and support automation or documentation initiatives.
  • Act as a key organizing member of the Finance & Accounting team’s social functions, including organizing our summer rooftop BBQ and other events during the summer months.
  • Assist the Controller with ad-hoc projects and tasks within the Finance & Accounting team as needed.

About you:

  • Current post-secondary student pursuing a business degree, preferably in Accounting or Finance.
  • High attention to detail and accuracy.
  • Strong organization and time management skills, with an ability to learn quickly and respond to changing priorities.
  • Strong interpersonal and communication skills.
  • Ability to exercise professionalism and confidentiality in financial matters.
  • Familiarity with the Microsoft Office suite (Outlook, Word, Excel).
  • Familiarity with cloud-based accounting software (e.g. NetSuite, Salesforce, etc.) is an asset but not required.

To Apply: Think you’re a great fit for this job? Apply on our Global Relay Careers page with your cover letter, resume, and university transcripts in one PDF document. No emails or phone calls please; candidates must be legally authorized to work in Canada.

Compensation:Global Relay advertises the pay range for this role in accordance with provincial law. Salaries are displayed in the form of annual base salary but are paid out semi-monthly for the duration of employment.

Base salary range: $42,000 CAD - $56,400 CAD

And you’ll be working alongside smart, talented individuals from diverse backgrounds, with complementary knowledge and skills.
