Job description

Software Engineering Co-op | ML/AI Focus

Photon-IV Inc. | Cambridge, Ontario (Hybrid) | Summer 2026 | $22-25/hr CAD

About Us

Photon-IV is a Canadian defence-tech startup building AI-driven satellite-terrestrial network orchestration software. We work with DND IDEaS, ESA, CSA, and commercial partners across the global space and connectivity ecosystem.

This is a small team. You will own real work that ships.

The Role

We are hiring a co-op student to work directly on our ML inference pipeline. You will support the team that built our Temporal Fusion Transformer for predictive handover (currently at 2.34 dBm RSRP MAE, 20.9ms P50 inference latency) and our LightGBM models for IoT fleet prediction.

You will write production code, train models, and your work will land in deliverables for federal contracts and commercial pilots.

What You Will Do

  • Build and maintain inference pipelines, with a focus on latency optimization and edge deployment
  • Run experiments comparing TFT, LightGBM, CatBoost, and other architectures across handover prediction, RSRP forecasting, and battery optimization tasks
  • Contribute to our agentic AI work: LangGraph orchestration wrapping our inference models, scoped for IDEaS Phase 2
  • Generate clean documentation and reproducible experiment notebooks

What You Bring

Required:

  • Currently enrolled in an undergraduate or graduate program in Computer Science, Engineering, Math, Physics, or a related field
  • Solid Python (NumPy, Pandas, scikit-learn, PyTorch or TensorFlow)
  • Hands-on experience training at least one non-trivial ML model end to end (course project counts if it was real)
  • Comfortable with Git, command line, and reading code you did not write
  • Eligibility to work in Canada with PR or citizenhip

Nice to have (any one of these is a plus):

  • Experience with LightGBM, XGBoost, or CatBoost on tabular data
  • Exposure to time-series forecasting (Prophet, ARIMA, Temporal Fusion Transformer, N-BEATS)
  • Familiarity with wireless or networking concepts (5G, O-RAN, satellite communications)
  • ONNX, TensorRT, or model quantization for edge deployment
  • Reinforcement learning or agentic AI (LangGraph, LangChain)
  • Prior co-op or research experience

What You Get

  • Direct mentorship from the CEO and Chief AI Architect
  • Real ownership of work that goes into federal contract deliverables and commercial deployments
  • Hybrid setup (Cambridge office plus remote flex)

How to Apply

Send your resume, GitHub or portfolio link, and a short note (3-5 sentences is fine) on the most interesting ML problem you have worked on to info@photon-iv.com. No cover letter required.

Applications reviewed on a rolling basis. Start date flexible around your academic calendar.

Photon-IV is committed to building a team that reflects diverse perspectives. We encourage applications from candidates of all backgrounds.
