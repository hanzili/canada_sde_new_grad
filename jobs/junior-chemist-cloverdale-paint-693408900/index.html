About the Role

Test materials, update formulations, document lab results

About Cloverdale Paint

Largest family-owned paint company in North America

Full Description

Cloverdale Paint is the largest family owned and operated paint company in North America. With over 75 Company stores and 150 Authorized Dealers across Canada, Cloverdale Paint has had a special relationship with its customers ever since the company was founded in 1933.

Cloverdale Paint has an immediate opening for a Junior Chemist at our lab in Winnipeg, MB.

The successful candidate will be available to work full-time shifts from Monday to Friday .

This position collaborates with other chemists in preparing experimental paint samples for improving existing formulation and monitoring production batches.

Salary is negotiable based on experience.

WHAT WE OFFER

  • Company paid benefits – Medical, Dental and Vision
  • Annual cost of living salary increases
  • Profit-sharing Program
  • Family Assistance Program
  • Staff Discounts
  • Comprehensive training
  • A supportive and growth-oriented management team
  • A great work environment with collaborative and caring colleagues

RESPONSIBILITIES

  • Evaluate and test new raw materials under supervision
  • Update existing formulations and manufacturing processes to ensure best technological and manufacturing efficiency and current regulatory compliance
  • Provide assistance to Production Department by ensuring that all formulations issued are accurate and process friendly
  • Coordinate with suppliers to keep abreast of new raw materials and technology available to maintain the highest quality formulations and processes
  • Update the knowledge and competency by attending relevant training available related to the paint industry
  • Ensure good time management to provide timely results when managing multiple priorities
  • Maintain comprehensive records of all project work carried out and provide weekly progress reports
  • Ensure that laboratory equipment is properly maintained and constantly improve the standard of housekeeping of the laboratory
  • Perform other duties as assigned

REQUIREMENTS

  • Bachelor’s degree in Chemistry or equivalent scientific field of study with 1-2 years lab experience
  • Major in Chemistry is preferred
  • Previous work experience working in a paint manufacturing lab environment will be an advantage
  • Ability to communicate effectively in cross-functional teams as well as with internal and external customers
  • Willingness to learn and respect group members
  • Committed, dedicated and a team player
  • Must have excellent listening and communication skills
  • Capable of working as part of a team, and individually with minimal supervision
  • Computer literate with good knowledge of MS Office suites
  • Quick learner
  • Details oriented

Our employees enjoy excellent work environments, respectful workplaces with credible leadership and receive fair remuneration and annual profit sharing. Be part of a Canadian family-owned business and be valued, appreciated and respected!
