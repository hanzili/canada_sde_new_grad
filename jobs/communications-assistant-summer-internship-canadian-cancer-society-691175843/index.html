About Canadian Cancer Society

Full Description

Job Title: Communications Assistant (Summer Internship)

Vacancies: This posting is for (1) new role.

Location: Toronto, ON (Detailed office location information can be found by visiting this link: https://cancer.ca/en/contact-us)

Work Model: Hybrid Work Model

Salary Band: $20-$22 CAD per Hour

(This is a Summer Internship Opportunity for 12-16 weeks) HELP SHAPE THE FUTURE OF CANCER CARE IN CANADA The Canadian Cancer Society works tirelessly to save lives, improve lives and drive collective action against cancer. Together with patients, volunteers, donors and communities across the country, we raise funds to invest in transformative cancer research, we provide a caring support system for everyone affected by cancer and we advocate to governments to create a healthier future for all. It takes a society to take on cancer – and the Canadian Cancer Society is leading the way

MAKING AN IMPACT The Communications Assistant will support national campaigns and storytelling initiatives at the Canadian Cancer Society, contributing to programs like CIBC Run for the Cure and Relay For Life. This role provides hands-on experience in media relations, content creation, and campaign support, while helping bring impactful stories to life and supporting day-to-day communications operations.

What You’ll Be Doing Story Centre support

  • Shadow and participate in full onboarding process for new storytellers
  • Use story writing template to highlight people who have benefited from CCS for our in-house storytelling program, including interviewing and writing
  • Coordinating internal story requests and content creation opportunities

Communications support

  • Support communications efforts for major nationwide programs, events, and virtual experiences such as the CIBC Run for the Cure (35 th anniversary), Cops for Cancer, Relay For Life, corporate partnership programs and more with ideation and execution
  • Conduct audits of competitor fundraising communication campaigns
  • Conduct research and collect data that supports key message development
  • Assist with proactive and reactive media relations for fundraising campaigns including developing media lists, drafting and sending pitches, coordinating interviews and shadowing media training
  • Learn how to analyze campaign performance and develop media reports
  • Participate in cross-functional meetings with PR agencies and integrated marketing team

Administrative support

  • Participate in weekly team meetings
  • Ad hoc support for Communications team such as notetaking and resource gathering
  • Data entry and auditing story tags to keep the SharePoint database up to date
  • Media monitoring using Cision
  • Contribute to our culture of diversity, inclusion, belonging and equity (DIBE) by ensuring that all staff feel represented, valued, and heard across all aspects of their identity, including gender, age, religion, ethnicity, nationality, race, and sexuality.
  • Other duties as assigned

Qualifications

  • In process of obtaining a post-secondary degree, diploma or certifications in communications, public relations, journalism or a related field
  • Excellent interpersonal skills, tact and diplomacy
  • Working knowledge of MS Office suite and SharePoint (asset)
  • Excellent attention to detail and organizational skills
  • Excellent communication skills including the ability to write clearly and succinctly in a variety of communication settings and styles
  • Ability to maintain the confidentiality of sensitive information
  • Experience working in a team-oriented environment; ability to take direction and collaborate with others
  • English is required, French/Bilingualism (French /English) is an asset due to the organization’s nationwide operations.
  • Others may apply
