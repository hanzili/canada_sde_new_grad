Full Description

Job Title: Constituency and Communications Assistant - Summer Job/Internship

Nature of the Work:

Our team is looking for someone who is highly committed to public service and is ready to join a dynamic, fast-paced office this summer! Our Calgary office supports the MP and collaborates with her Parliamentary team in Ottawa to support her legislative responsibilities. Are you ready to be a part of a hard-working and outcomes-focused team that makes a difference?

As a Constituency Affairs and Communications Assistant, you will help manage stakeholder relationships, help create engaging social media content, and assist with the day-to-day administrative duties for both the Member, the Parliamentary office, and the Constituency office.

Working in a federal office is often high-pressure, and you will regularly be expected to meet tight deadlines. Duties will vary over the course of the parliamentary calendar year. At times, you will be serving as the primary point of contact for individuals and organizations in the constituency. Being able to professionally communicate and interact with various individuals and groups is critical.

Primary duties include:

  • Assist with stakeholder management, which includes:
  • Meeting with community stakeholders
  • Conducting monthly reports
  • Attending events/meetings with/on behalf of the Member
  • Stakeholder database management
  • Drafting and distributing proactive and reactive communications products
  • Social Media and Communications
  • Assisting with the creation and distribution of social media products
  • Assisting with administrative office responsibilities

Qualifications

  • Demonstrated experience with editing video and producing social media products
  • Strong interpersonal skills
  • Strong writing and research skills
  • Ability to take direction and complete tasks independently
  • Able to work under pressure with changing priorities
  • Strong multi-tasking and time management capabilities
  • Ability to set goals and meet deadlines
  • Ability to work and communicate well with others
  • Highly organized
  • Must be able to work in the office (not remotely)
  • Evenings and weekends may occasionally be required
  • Must be already eligible to work in Canada.

Interested applicants can send a copy of their resume and cover letter (including salary expectations) to: sean.schnell.709@parl.gc.ca

Open to full-time or part-time considerations. Discussion around a longer term role is also available for successful candidates.

Job Types: Full-time, Part-time

Work Location: In person
