Full Description

Summer Student– Geology – 2026-041

Foran Mining Corporation (TSX:FOM) is a forward-thinking mining company redefining the industry. We are seeking a motivated and detail-oriented Geology Summer Student to support our Exploration team. This is a full-time position which will work between our Saskatoon office working Monday – Friday 8:00 am – 4:30 pm, and our McIlvenna Bay site on a rotational schedule.

Foran Mining is a modern mining development and exploration company advancing Canada's only greenfield copper project currently under construction: the McIlvenna Bay deposit in Saskatchewan. With commercial production expected in mid-2026, this world-class deposit contains copper, zinc, gold, and silver – key metals for the global transition to green energy. At Foran, we are committed to sustainable development, environmental protection, and building strong communities. We believe in empowering our people, fostering a culture of growth, and creating opportunities for meaningful careers as we build the mines of the future.

The Geology Summer Student will support the Exploration team by assisting with organizing and digitizing geological data and records in our Saskatoon office. Through periodic work at our site, the student will also support exploration and site-based activities by supporting field programs, sampling, and core logging activities.

Job Responsibilities:

  • Provide general support to office-based geologists and technical staff
  • Ensure documents are properly scanned, catalogued, and digitally stored
  • Identify gaps in documentation and support completion of digital records
  • Assist with data entry, compilation, and basic analysis
  • Support preparation of technical materials, reports, and presentations as needed
  • Participate in field programs at the McIlvenna Bay site
  • Assist with sampling programs and geological data collection
  • Support core logging and core handling activities
  • Follow all safety protocols and site procedures

Job Qualifications and Skills:

  • Must be 18 years of age or older and currently enrolled full-time in a Geology, Earth Sciences, or related post-secondary program

  • Valid Class 5 driver’s license with a clean driving record.

  • Standard First Aid certification, or willingness to obtain.
  • Ability to work outdoors in an industrial environment.

  • Ability to demonstrate safe and efficient work practices.

  • Strong attention to detail and organizational skills

  • Comfortable working with large volumes of data and documentation
  • Proficient in Microsoft Office (Excel, Word, Outlook)
  • Strong communication and teamwork skills
  • Willingness to travel to site and work in a field environment as required

Compensation: $22.40/hr

Foran is committed to building a skilled and diverse workforce as we move into our next phase of growth. Indigenous applicants are encouraged to self-declare when submitting applications and resumes. All qualified candidates are encouraged to apply; however, you must be legally able to work in Canada.

The McIlvenna Bay Project site is a drug and alcohol-free workplace. Due to the safety-sensitive nature of this position and the remote work environment, candidates must successfully complete a pre-employment medical assessment, which includes drug and alcohol testing, as a condition of employment.

Deadline for applications to be submitted is: April 8, 2026
