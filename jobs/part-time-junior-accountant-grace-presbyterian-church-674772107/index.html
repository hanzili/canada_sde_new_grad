Role Summary

The Part-time Junior Accountant supports the church’s mission by maintaining accurate financial records, processing weekly contributions, and assisting with day-to-day, monthly, and annual financial reporting.

The Junior Accountant blends standard accounting duties with unique nonprofit requirements, including fund-based accounting, tracking offerings, and maintaining strict confidentiality regarding member donations.

Reports to: Accountant

General Accounting:

  • Contribution Management: accurately record weekly offerings, and special fund donations (e.g., building fund, missions) into the church management software.
  • Accounts Payable: process invoices for church operations and prepare cheques or electronic payments.
  • Donor Statements: generate annual charitable giving statements for income tax purposes.
  • Bank Reconciliations: perform regular reconciliations of bank and investment accounts to ensure all transaction data is correctly documented.
  • Fund Accounting: assist in maintaining the General Ledger using fund-based accounting to track restricted vs. unrestricted assets.
  • Reporting: assist with the preparation monthly financial statements (balance sheets, income statements) for the Finance Committee.
  • External Review Support: Organize documentation for annual internal reviews or external audits.

Reporting:

· Assist in the preparation of monthly reports for Session committees.

· Assist in the preparation of quarterly reports and other interim reports as required.

· Assist in the preparation of year-end financial statements for the annual congregational meeting.

  • Assist in the analysis and preparation of reports of financial results to identify factors and trends which may be of help in understanding the reasons for past and current outcomes and which may be useful in developing plans and projections for the future.

· Ensure all accounting work is executed on a timely basis, and accounts are up to date within established procedures.

· Process all invoices and expense claims by coding and posting in the accounting system, ensuring that appropriate approval for payment has been obtained, generating cheques and / or EFT file, submitting to signing officers for signature.

  • Assist with compliance with statutory filing requirements for charities, e.g. T3010.

· Assist in preparing financial section of PCIC annual statistical report.

· Assist with the processing of invoices and expense claims by coding and posting in the accounting system, ensuring that appropriate approval for payment has been obtained, generating cheques and / or EFT file, submitting to signing officers for signature.

· Assist the accountant and the Finance Committee in preparing the annual church budget.

Required Qualifications

· Education two-year diploma in accounting from a community college, or equivalent.

  • Technical Skills: proficiency in Microsoft Excel and experience with accounting software QuickBooks.
  • Confidentiality: demonstrated ability to handle sensitive financial information and donor records with absolute discretion.
  • Integrity: high personal integrity and alignment with the church’s values or mission.
  • Background Check: successful criminal background and credit check.

Key Soft Skills

  • Attention to Detail: precision in recording small, frequent donations and identifying discrepancies.
  • Communication: ability to professionally interact with staff, vendors, and congregation members.
  • Servant Leadership: commitment to supporting the church’s various ministries and team environment.

· Must be able to communicate effectively with work colleagues, ministers and Finance Committee and Session.
