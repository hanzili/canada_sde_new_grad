Full Description

Junior Biologist

Job Title: Junior Biologist

  • Position Start Date/End Date – March/April (8 month contract with potential to become full time permanent)
  • Location – Sylvan Lake, Alberta

Job Information

  • Duration – 8 month contract (with potential to become full time permanent)
  • Salary – $27-$29/hr plus OT
  • Health, dental, and wellness benefits for full time employees
  • Paid flex time off
  • We are recruiting for a Junior Biologist position to join our team based out of Sylvan Lake, Alberta. This position is ideal for someone who desires ongoing skills development in fish and wildlife assessment through both field work and technical report writing, within the consulting industry.

Job Description working in both a field and office environment and who desires to continually develop their skills in environmental assessment within the consulting industry. We have the ability to cross-train in multiple fields if desired by the successful applicant.

The position is based in Sylvan Lake, Alberta and requires travel and field work throughout Western and Northern Canada. Job duties include, but are not limited to, the following:

  • Bird nest sweeps, wildlife assessments, and monitoring
  • Fish and fish habitat assessments and construction monitoring
  • Data management, Technical report writing
  • Assisting other disciplines (e.g. vegetation/wetlands) as required

Requirements:

We are seeking a hardworking, mature, career-orientated individual with an interestin fisheries, wildlife biology, and/or vegetation/wetland ecology. The successful candidate will report to the intermediate and senior fisheries and wildlife biologists. Both field and writing aptitude are important skills required for this position.

  • Possession of a Bachelor’s degree in biological, conservation sciences, natural resource, or equivalent degree
  • Proficiency in Microsoft Word and Excel
  • Availability to work flexible hours as project requirements dictate
  • Willing and able to travel and work in remote locations in potentially harsh weather conditions for extended periods of time (2 to 3 weeks), & good physical condition
  • Valid class 5 driver’s license
  • Excellent verbal and written communication skills in English
  • Canadian citizen

Experience Assets

  • Fisheries, wildlife, or vegetation experience in Canada, preferably in the consulting industry.
  • Master’s Degree in biology
  • Field experience in Western or Northern Canada, consulting experience
  • GPS, compass navigation, orienteering
  • Report writing, data analysis, statistical analysis, GIS (QGIS or Arc GIS)
  • Field equipment use and maintenance (e.g. 4x4 trucks, trailers, UTV, Argo, ATV, boats, snowmobile)

RC BioSolutions Ltd. is a professional environmental consulting services firm based in central Alberta that provides all aspects of environmental impact assessment including baseline studies and environmental monitoring in the aquatic biology and terrestrial ecology disciplines. RC Bio will provide a comprehensive health care program, paid professional development opportunities, performance bonuses, and strong mentorship from senior staff. Check out RC Bio at https://www.rcbio.ca/ or our social media pages (LinkedIn, Facebook, and Instagram)

Contact: Please send your resume, cover letter, and the names/contact information for three references to Richard Carson – richard.carson@rcbio.ca
