Full Description

About Kinaxis Are you looking to join an innovative, market-leading company where you can truly elevate your career? At Kinaxis we are serious about culture, we are serious about technology, we are serious about customers, and we are serious about not taking ourselves too seriously. If you are looking to be part of an incredible growth story, then we might just be the place for you!

In 1984, we started out as a team of three engineers. Today, we have grown to become a global organization with over 2000 employees around the world, 6 global office and a best-in-class HQ in Ottawa, Canada. As winners of several Top Employer awards globally, we are proud to work with our customers and employees towards solving some of the biggest challenges facing supply chains today.

Kinaxis is a global leader in modern supply chain orchestration, powering complex global supply chains, and supporting the people who manage them. Our powerful, AI infused platform provides full transparency and visibility across end-to-end supply chains, enabling our customers to make faster, better decisions. We are trusted by renowned global brands to provide the agility and predictability needed to navigate today’s volatility and disruption. With more than 40,000 users in over 100 countries, we are expanding our team as we continue to innovate and revolutionize how we support our customers.

About The Team Are you curious about the world of tax and eager to gain hands-on experience in a dynamic, global environment? Join our Tax team and contribute to meaningful work that supports both our day-to-day operations and long-term strategy. This is a great opportunity to learn from experienced professionals, build your technical skills, and see how tax plays a critical role in business decision-making.

Location

You must be in the Ottawa, Canada office at least three days a week.

Term Duration

This is a full-time 4-month position, starting September 2026.

Co-op / Intern Eligibility

This position is open to co-ops and interns. To be eligible, you must be currently enrolled in full-time education or be a recent/upcoming graduate within 12 months of the placement end date.

Compensation range

$19.46-$28.55 hourly rate. The final offer within this range will reflect the candidate’s skills, year of education, and experience.

Vacancy Status

This is an existing job vacancy.

What you will do

As a Tax Co-Op, you’ll gain hands-on experience across a wide range of tax functions, including:

  • Supporting global income tax accounting and compliance processes
  • Preparing working papers and tax provisions for quarterly financial reporting
  • Assisting with transfer pricing documentation and related-party charge calculations
  • Preparing indirect tax filings and reconciling related financial statement accounts
  • Contributing to global corporate tax compliance across multiple jurisdictions
  • Conducting research, analyzing tax issues, summarizing findings, and presenting insights to the tax team and broader finance organization
  • Performing monthly account reconciliations to support financial reporting
  • Assisting with audit-related documentation and support
  • Supporting month-end close activities
  • Participating in ad hoc projects aligned with business initiatives

What we are looking for

  • Working toward a degree in business, accounting, finance, or a related field
  • A proactive problem-solver with strong analytical skills
  • Comfortable working independently and collaboratively in a team environment
  • Organized and able to manage multiple priorities effectively
  • A clear and confident communicator, both written and verbal
  • Proficient in Microsoft Office (Excel, Word, PowerPoint)
  • Demonstrates leadership potential through academic, extracurricular, or work experiences
  • Thinks creatively and brings fresh ideas to the table

Things that would definitely help

While not required, the following experience and knowledge would be a strong asset:

  • Familiarity with accounting systems—especially NetSuite
  • Previous work experience in a related field (e.g., accounting, finance, tax)
  • A basic understanding of intercompany accounting and foreign currency transactions
  • Experience conducting research and analysis to support decision-making
  • Completion of introductory tax courses or relevant academic projects
