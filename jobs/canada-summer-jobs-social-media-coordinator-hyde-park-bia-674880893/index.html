Full Description

About the HPBIA

The Hyde Park Business Improvement Association represents more than 450 businesses in the Hyde Park area of Uptown London. Our mission is to create a vibrant, beautiful, and community-focused commercial district. Through placemaking, events, beautification, and business support, the HPBIA fosters growth and enhances the local economy while connecting with the broader London community.

Position Overview

The Social Media Coordinator will support the HPBIA's digital presence by creating engaging content, managing day-to-day social media activity, and helping tell the story of Hyde Park's businesses and community. This is an 8-week summer position ideal for someone creative, communicative, and passionate about local community. The successful candidate will report to the Programs, Marketing & Events Lead and collaborate closely with member businesses, staff, and volunteers.

Key Responsibilities

Content Creation & Social Media Management

  • Plan, create, and schedule content for the HPBIA's social media channels (Instagram, Facebook) to promote member businesses, events, and BIA initiatives.
  • Develop engaging graphics, reels, stories, and written copy that reflect the HPBIA's brand voice.
  • Work directly with member businesses to gather content, highlight promotions, and identify compelling stories.
  • Serve as an HPBIA ambassador at community events, capturing real-time content for social channels.

Analytics & Reporting

  • Monitor social media performance and document analytics to assess the effectiveness of each channel.
  • Conduct digital audits to evaluate the HPBIA's online presence across platforms.
  • Support the creation of monthly reporting that communicates digital marketing results to the Marketing team and Board of Directors.
  • Help define measurement strategies to inform ongoing content and channel optimization.

Communications Support

  • Assist in producing the BIA Member & community newsletters and email marketing content.
  • Make updates to the HPBIA website, including event listings and business promotions.
  • Support general communications with members and partners regarding BIA initiatives and upcoming events.
  • Distribute event materials and promotional information to local businesses.

Qualifications

  • Strong written communication skills with a flair for engaging, on-brand storytelling.
  • Active familiarity with Instagram and Facebook, including Meta Business Suite.
  • Experience with Canva or similar design tools; basic photography or video skills are an asset.
  • Detail-oriented with strong time management and the ability to manage a content calendar independently.
  • Personable, professional, and community-minded.
  • Currently enrolled in, or a recent graduate of, a program in communications, marketing, journalism, or a related field.
  • Familiarity with Hyde Park and/or the London community is a strong asset.

Eligibility (Canada Summer Jobs Program)

Be a Canadian citizen, permanent resident, or have refugee protection status

Be legally entitled to work in Canada
