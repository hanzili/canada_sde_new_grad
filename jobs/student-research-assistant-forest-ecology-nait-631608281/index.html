We are looking for self-motivated and science-minded individuals to join our research team this summer within the Forest Reclamation and Reforestation Team at the Centre for Boreal Research (CBR) (led by Dr. Amanda Schoonmaker). This position will assist with the execution of applied research projects across a range of topics including forest reclamation, post-harvest reforestation, and forest management research. These positions are largely field-based (90% or more) though activities will include a mix of field work, lab/greenhouse work, and desk-based work, requiring a variety of skills, independent thinking, strong organizational skills, and ability to work in a fast-paced and busy team setting. Fieldwork involves extensive walking through diverse and uneven terrain, with crews typically covering 5‚Äì10 km per day. Field days can last up to 10 hours and occasionally longer, following a condensed schedule of 8 days on and 6 days off. Positions may also support routine lab and greenhouse activities as well as desk-based work including data entry and organization.

Key Responsibilities:

  • Assist researchers in the establishment and monitoring of research sites. This will include activities such as plot layout, seedling planting, soil sampling, vegetation identification, seedling growth and physiological measurements as well as larger tree measurements in mature forest stands.
  • Routine plant care in greenhouse setting; preparing fertilizer mixes, watering plants, clean-up of greenhouse space.
  • Laboratory work such as root washing, weighing biomass, and processing soil samples.
  • Data entry and assistance with technical reporting.
