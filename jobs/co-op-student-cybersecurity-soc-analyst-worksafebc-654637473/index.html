Overview

The IT Security and End User Technology department of WorkSafeBC is looking for one highly motivated, dynamic, innovative Co-op student, Cybersecurity SOC Analyst to join our team to help us strive towards our vision.

The department provides an exceptional technology experience for our organization by meeting end-user expectations for technology education and support, offering best-in-class Cybersecurity, and ensuring transparent and accountable decision-making through governance practices and policies.

This is an eight-month temporary opportunity starting in May 2026.

How you’ll make a difference: You’ll support innovations that benefit workers and employers across B.C.

Where you’ll work

At WorkSafeBC, we offer a hybrid work model that combines working remotely, and in our offices based on the operational needs of the position.

What you'll do

On Day 1 in this role, you will get a chance to meet your manager and get acquainted with your team. You will spend time setting up administrative and technological essentials, and completing onboarding tasks, including security and payroll setup.

During your first week you will engage in a comprehensive onboarding program designed to equip you for success. You will be partnering and meeting with your manager to discuss your goals and align them with the team’s vision. Additionally, you will learn how our department contributes to the overall business strategy and supports the organization’s mandate. You will also work through the onboarding program alongside your supervisor for added guidance and support.

This role is ideal for an individual who is eager to step beyond their comfort zone. We are looking for someone with a detective mindset, strong motivation, and a passion for innovation. The right candidate will possess analytical and critical thinking skills, enabling them to propose ideas that enhance various processes and initiatives, as well as to explore new approaches to work within the department.

Your daily responsibilities include:

  • Enhance and maintain threat detection queries in Azure Sentinel and Splunk to improve visibility and response across the environment.
  • Support incident investigations and response efforts by documenting findings, coordinating remediation, and contributing to response playbooks.
  • Assist in planning and executing tabletop exercises to strengthen the incident response plan and align technical and business continuity strategies.
  • Monitor security events, analyze external threats, and help develop best practices and documentation to support security operations maturity.

Is this a good fit for you?

We're looking for someone who can:

  • Monitor security tools and systems for potential threats, investigate incidents, and support remediation efforts across domains.
  • Analyze and improve detection logic in Azure Sentinel and Splunk and contribute to incident response planning and documentation.
  • Participate in research and evaluation of security tools and help align technical response with business continuity needs.
  • Collaborate effectively with the team, think critically, and bring curiosity and enthusiasm to solving complex cybersecurity challenges.
  • Participate in awareness programs to heighten security consciousness among users; makes information presentations regarding security issues as required
  • Contribute to a positive work environment by collaborating and working effectively as a member of the team
  • Think critically while also having fun working through complex problems

Learn more: Hear from past co-op students on what it’s like to work here.

Your background and experience

  • Currently a 3rd or 4th year student enrolled in a cybersecurity, computer science or related program
  • Previous co-op or relevant work experience in an office environment is preferred
  • Microsoft 365 applications and Microsoft Azure experience is an asset

Important to know

Before we can finalize any offer of employment, you must:

  • Confirm you’re legally entitled to work in Canada
  • Confirm you’re a registered student at a post-secondary institution and is participating in a work integrated learning program
  • Consent to criminal records check

Who are we?

At WorkSafeBC, we promote safe and healthy workplaces across British Columbia. We partner with workers and employers to save lives and prevent injury, disease, and disability. When work-related injuries and diseases occur, we provide compensation and support injured workers in their recovery, rehabilitation, and safe return to work. We’re honoured to serve the 2.49 million workers and 263,000 registered employers in our province.

What’s it like to work at WorkSafeBC?

It’s challenging, stimulating, and rewarding. Our positions offer diversity and opportunities for professional growth. Every day, the work we do impacts people and changes lives. What we do is important, and so are the people we do it for.
Our ability to make a difference relies on building a team with a rich variety of skills, knowledge, backgrounds, abilities, and experiences that reflects the diversity of the people we serve. We are committed to fostering a welcoming, inclusive, and supportive work culture where everyone can contribute as their best, authentic self.
