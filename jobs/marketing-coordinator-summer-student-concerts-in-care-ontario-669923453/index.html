Full Description

Overview
Concerts in Care Ontario presents professional musical programming that brings joy, relief, and beauty to a range of seniors' care communities across Ontario. Our concerts provide a focal point for building bridges between elders, caregivers and loved ones, while engaging powerful emotions which can activate memory and improve mood for seniors in care who may be isolated and withdrawn.

We curate concert series and special event concerts in seniors' facilities, performed exclusively by top-calibre professional musicians. Managers know that our turnkey service is as excellent as our musicians' performances. We serve long-term and memory care, assisted and independent living, and palliative care facilities, as well as seniors living at home through our partnerships with Seniors' Active Living Centres (SALCs) across the province.

Our concerts are 45-60 minutes in length, including narrative and storytelling about the music, the musicians, and the instruments.

Since 2011, CiC Ontario has presented more than 2,300 concerts, reaching 112,000 seniors and caregivers live, with almost 200,000 virtual concert views, and providing more than 3,900 performer engagements.

Tasks and Responsibilities

  • The Marketing Coordinator will work with both the Executive Director and General Manager/Bookkeeper to develope and improve skills in this entry-level experience that could lead to employment in a more senior role.
  • Candidate must have some experience or knowledge of professional musicians and music.
  • Candidate must be comfortable and have empathy with seniors in care.
  • As the Marketing Coordinator, they will perform a range of tasks focussed on two main streams of activity: 1. raising the profile and creating publicity for Concerts in Care Ontario (CiCO), and marketing the impact of our work to potential donors.
  • Concerts in Care ONTARIO does not require basic sales marketing, as we turn down live concert requests every year even as we grow. We do need to promote ourselves in a more effective way to prospective donors and funders in order to bring the joy, energy, and connection of live music to more seniors.

Skills

  • Communication and Stewardship (communication): writing copy for social media, regional publications/seniors' facility newsletters, and donor/foundation stewardship updates.
  • Research & Organization: generate leads for corporate donors and compile them into an online database (existing)
  • Tech (digital skills): investigate incorporating AI into online database for improved lead management; facilitate digital zoom concerts
  • Interpersonal Relationships (communication): cold calling corporations for details on development opportunities; acting as ambassador at live concerts
  • Project Management (collaboration & adaptability) : Support with the planning andexecution of two annual fundraising events, learning to balance priorities effectively, responding to changes as they arise.
  • The Marketing Coordinator's primary responsibility will be to bring the skills they hold to the table, work hard to learn and improve them, and confidently offer creative ideas to adapt to the small, smart, nimble organization who is constantly thinking on its feet, leaping at opportunities once we establish fiscal diligence, and maintain high standards of both musical excellence and turnkey service to the seniors' facilities and programs.
  • CiCO welcomes fresh thinking, new perspectives, and hard working persons with energy and drive.
  • Excellent research skills to identify market opportunities and industry trends.
  • Ability to analyze data effectively to inform strategic decisions using analytics tools.
  • Strong organizational skills with the ability to manage multiple projects simultaneously within budget constraints.
  • Effective communication skills for collaborating with team members.
