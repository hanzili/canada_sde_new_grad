Human Resources Intern - Seasonal

at Westside Regional Recreation Society

📍 Location Calgary, AB T3H 4V7
📋 Details Internship · 8 weeks · May–June 2026
📅 Posted Apr 20, 2026

About the Role

Assist recruiting, onboarding, and HR administration

About Westside Regional Recreation Society

Community org offering youth HR internship program

Full Description

Human Resources Intern Responsibilities:

  • Assist in recruiting for summer staff and volunteer positions including interviews and reference checks
  • Assist with onboarding and orientations for new staff and volunteers
  • Assist with training of summer staff and volunteers
  • Manage staff certifications and upload into UKG Software
  • Evaluation of onboarding process

Administrative:

  • Assist with the creation of training sessions for day camp staff and volunteers
  • Upload staff certifications into UKG for document management to keep accurate personnel files
  • Track staff and volunteer progress in training software
  • Assist in promotion of Westside's Mission, Vision, Values
  • General office administrative duties as required
  • Attend meetings as required

Risk Management:

  • Complete First Aid/CPR and related documentation as required
  • Report unsafe hazards for staff, volunteers and patrons
  • Maintain and promote Westside Healthy and Safety Standards
  • Keep customers informed of policies and procedures to ensure safety and respect standards are met

Skills Required:

  • Strong attention to detail skills ensuring accuracy and quality in tasks
  • Strong organizational and planning skills
  • Strong verbal and written communication skills
  • Ability to pivot between tasks, learn new tools and adapt to changing environments
  • Ability to work within systems including UKG, When to Work and Bridge
  • Ability to work independently

Physical/Psychological Demands:

  • Ability to maintain discretion with confidential information

Employment Term:

  • May - June (8 week position) 35 hours/week
  • Hours vary based on recruiting timelines and schedules

Requirements:

  • Must be available for mandatory training in June (Times vary- daytime, evening & weekends required for training dates)
  • Must have current Alberta Approved First Aid training agency - CSA Standard Z1210-17 Intermediate First Aid and must be valid through to June 30, 2026
  • Successful applicant must have a clear Vulnerable Sector Search completed at their own cost
  • The successful candidate must hold high ethical standards as they are working with vulnerable populations and has access to confidential information.

Experience and Qualifications:

  • Post-secondary education (or currently enrolled in); Human Resources, Business Administration, Recreation and Leisure and/or other related fields
  • Experience in presenting content to 100 people
  • Experience in an office setting is an asset

This position may be funded by the Government of Canada- Summer Job Grant program. Grant eligibility requires the successful candidate to be between the ages of 15Y – 30Y and legally entitled to work in Canada. International students are not eligible.

CLOSING DATE: April 29, 2026

Interviews will begin prior to closing date.
