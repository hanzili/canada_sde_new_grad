Financial Analyst Intern | Job Description

This internship opportunity is a unique financial analyst experience to learn about the real estate industry in a property development company, PCI Developments.

About Us

PCI (www.pci-group.com) is a Vancouver-based real estate developer and investor. Since our inception in 1982, PCI has forged strong business relationships and created a culture of innovation and integrity. PCI is a developer with an entrepreneurial focus. They have assembled an experienced team with comprehensive real estate expertise. This approach enables the organization to successfully undertake a variety of real estate projects, including large, transit-oriented, mixed-use urban developments, residential rental buildings, built-to-suit commercial buildings, shopping centers, and industrial development. PCI is not confined by formula, size or product type. Developments include the iconic Marine Gateway, King George Hub, 601 West Hastings, 565 Great Northern Way, and Crossroads - all of which are neighbourhood-changing mixed-use developments. The company also manages an increasing portfolio of real estate assets of their own and on behalf of external clients.

Primary Purpose & Specific Responsibilities

The purpose of this Financial Analyst Assistant Summer Internship will assist on ad-hoc projects and support the financing team. You will work closely with Senior Financial Analysts and the Director of Real Estate Finance.

Specific Responsibilities

The responsibilities of this position may include, but not limited to, the following:

  • Assist with decision making for development programs, capital structuring, acquisitions, dispositions, and strategy.
  • Creating, analyzing, and updating pro forma models, cashflows, and capital profiles for residential, retail, and office development projects.
  • Cross collaboration projects between our different divisions including development, construction, sustainability, accounting, asset management, marketing, leasing, and property management.
  • Support and assist with administration for underwriting, due diligence, and placement of land and pre-development loans, construction facilities, term loans, and equity partnerships.
  • Other duties and special projects as assigned.

Education and Experience

  • Working towards a degree in a related field, with a preference for commerce or finance-related major.
  • Experience with excel modeling an asset.
  • Intermediate computer skills, particularly in Microsoft Office applications (Microsoft Outlook, Excel and Word).
  • Experience and working knowledge of Yardi and Argus an asset.

Competencies

  • Practical judgement and decisive manner.
  • Excellent communication skills, both written and verbal in the English language.
  • Ability to work efficiently under pressure and maintain a calm focus.
  • Detail oriented with exceptional analytical and problem-solving abilities.
  • Positive self-starter, self-motivated, hands-on mentality with the ability to work independently and in a team environment.
  • A dedication to providing exceptional customer service.
  • Excellent organization and time management skills with the ability to complete work in a timely manner with accuracy and attention to detail.
  • Ability to cope with changes including changes in business and client needs.
  • Ability to work in a fast-paced environment.
  • Ability to manage multiple deadlines and priorities.
