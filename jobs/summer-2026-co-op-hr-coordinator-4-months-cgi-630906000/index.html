About CGI

Full Description

We are Canada's largest independent information technology services firm, and after almost 50 years, we're still growing. In this HR Coordinator Co-op role, you will provide high-level support within the CHRO’s pillar, reporting directly to the HR Manager. Moving beyond basic administrative tasks, you will be a key contributor to the planning of executive-level meetings, from weekly cabinet sessions to the Annual Business Plan deck. By leveraging AI tools for data synthesis and maintaining advanced proficiency in Microsoft Office, you will ensure that global HR initiatives and quarterly knowledge exchanges are organized, professional, and data-driven.

This position is ideal for a proactive student who excels at multitasking and maintains the highest standards of discretion while supporting senior leadership.

Perks of being a CGI Co-op:

The co-op program was designed to help students improve their skills and explore their professional interests through hands-on experience and professional guidance, while also helping them develop core competencies. As an intern, you have access to:

  • Professional development sessions
  • Professional development platform
  • Networking events with colleagues and leaders
  • Mentorship from leaders and colleagues aimed at setting and achieving your career goals

Your future duties and responsibilities

As the HR Corporate Coordinator, you will provide high-level strategic and operational support within the Chief Human Resources Officer’s (CHRO) pillar. Reporting directly to the HR Manager, you will play a key role in ensuring the seamless execution of executive-level meetings and global HR initiatives.
This is not your average "filing and scanning" internship. You will be at the heart of HR operations, helping craft the narrative of our global people strategy and ensuring our leadership team is prepared for high-stakes decision-making.

Key Responsibilities

  • Executive Meeting Support: Assist in the planning and content creation for Quarterly presentations and Weekly Cabinet Meetings. This includes drafting agendas, tracking action items, and ensuring all materials are ready for leadership review.
  • Play a hands-on role in developing the Annual Business Plan deck, integrating data and departmental goals.
  • Global Knowledge Exchange: Help coordinate the Quarterly Global HR Knowledge Exchange, collaborating with international HR Partners to facilitate sharing best practices across regions.
  • Data & Documentation: Leverage advanced Microsoft Office skills to manage spreadsheets, draft formal internal communications, and build high-impact presentations.
  • Special Projects: Support the HR Manager and CHRO on ad-hoc initiatives.

Required qualifications to be successful in this role

  • To thrive in this role, you should be a "power user" of productivity tools and possess a keen eye for detail.
  • AI literacy: Ability to use basic AI tools (e.g., ChatGPT, Gemini, or Copilot) for content drafting, data synthesis, and research.
  • Technical Proficiency: Advanced mastery of Excel (v-lookups, pivot tables), PowerPoint (slide master, animations, layout), and Word.
  • Communication: Strong professional writing skills and the ability to communicate effectively with senior executives.
  • Organizational Skills: A knack for project management; you can juggle multiple deadlines without breaking a sweat.
  • Discretion: Ability to handle highly sensitive and confidential organizational data with the utmost integrity.
  • Proactive Mindset: You don't just wait for instructions—you anticipate what the HR Manager needs before they ask.

Bilingualism (French and English) is required for this position due to the nature of the role, which involves interactions with both national and international clients.
