Full Description

Junior Data Analyst Co-op (4 to 8-Month Term)

Location: Victoria, BC (On-site)
Start Date: May 4, 2026
Compensation: $52,000 - $58,000 (Pro-rated)

About Waterworth
Waterworth is a fast-growing, trailblazing SaaS company here in Victoria, BC. We empower more than 350 municipalities across North America to take control of their financial futures through proprietary financial modeling and analysis software.

As a mission-driven company, we value innovation, collaboration, and making a real impact in the communities we serve. Join us as we continue to grow and deliver valuable financial tools to the public sector.

The Role
We’re looking for a motivated Junior Data Analyst Co-op to support the data onboarding, analysis, and forecasting needs of our Client Success team.

This is a position for a student who has completed at least one prior co-op term and is studying accounting, economics, finance, or mathematics. You’ll play a key role in ensuring the accuracy and usability of client data, while helping improve processes that drive client retention and success. In addition to your data-focused responsibilities, you'll contribute to system-building efforts that support our overall operations.

Key Responsibilities:

Forecasting & Analysis:
Conduct data analysis to track client engagement, churn, and meeting outcomes, focusing on identifying patterns that drive client success.

Collaborate with the Client Success team to analyze client behavior and provide insights that help improve retention and satisfaction.

Data Onboarding & Transformation:
Import and validate client financial statements, capital project data, and utility billing information.

Transform and standardize data for seamless integration into the Waterworth platform.

Product Collaboration:
Work closely with the Implementation and Product teams to improve data handling and reporting processes.

Communicate technical findings and insights clearly to both technical and non-technical stakeholders.

Documentation:
Create clear, reusable documentation for data transformation workflows and onboarding processes.

Ensure documentation is easily accessible for team members and clients.

Requirements

Educational Background

Currently pursuing a degree in Accounting, Economics, Finance, Mathematics, or a related field.

Only candidates that have already completed at least one co-op will be considered.

Please list relevant coursework in your application (e.g., managerial accounting)

Analytical & Technical Skills

Proficient in Excel: You must be able to demonstrate experience using spreadsheets to analyze data and build models. Candidates will be tested on their Excel skills in the interview process.

Excel-based projects: You will be asked to describe a previous project you have carried out that demonstrated attention to detail and technical proficiency.

Leadership & Involvement

Experience in student societies, clubs, or extracurriculars, especially where you’ve taken on leadership or organizational roles, is strongly preferred.

Communication & Presentation

Proven ability to explain technical concepts clearly and collaborate effectively within a team.

Experience with case competitions and research projects is an advantage.

Benefits

Downtown Office & Friendly Atmosphere

Join our you, growing team in a 100% in-person office that promotes innovation and fun. Enjoy regular socials, BBQs, office pets for morale, a ping pong table, and free snacks, creating an enjoyable work environment.

Career Development

Develop your skills with exceptional training, regular check-ins, and ongoing opportunities. Gain real-world experience in a high-performing tech SaaS company, ideal for entering the lucrative tech sector in Victoria.

Impactful Work

Our skilled team empowers communities across North America to take control of their futures, making a genuine difference in hundreds of lives.

How to Apply
Ready to make a real impact while gaining valuable experience? We want to hear from you! Please submit your resume and get in touch!
