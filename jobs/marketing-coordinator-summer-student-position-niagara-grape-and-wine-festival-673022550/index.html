About Niagara Grape and Wine Festival

Iconic Niagara wine festival; hands-on marketing

Full Description

The Niagara Grape & Wine Festival is seeking a creative, detail-oriented Marketing Coordinator to join our team for the summer. This is an exciting opportunity for a student or recent graduate to gain hands-on experience supporting one of Niagara’s most iconic events—helping not only build the brand, but turn audience interest into ticket sales and event attendance.

As Marketing Coordinator, you’ll play a key role in planning and executing marketing initiatives that support both our seasonal programming and year-round brand presence. You’ll contribute to the development of marketing strategies, create engaging content, and help ensure our digital channels drive awareness, engagement, and measurable results.

This is a 16-week contract position, 30-40 hours per week, with the potential to continue in a part-time capacity through the school year.

Work Environment

  • 4 days per week in the Festival office in Downtown St. Catharines
  • 1 day per week working remotely
  • Occasional evening and weekend work required to support events

Key Responsibilities

  • Assist in the development of the Festival’s seasonal and year-long marketing plan, grounded in research, insights, and audience trends, with a focus on driving ticket sales and event participation
  • Support the creation and execution of content calendars for social media and e-newsletter campaigns that move audiences from awareness to action
  • Write, edit, and schedule compelling content across digital platforms, with clear calls-to-action and conversion in mind
  • Maintain and update website content using SquareSpace, optimizing for user experience and ticket purchase pathways
  • Assist in planning, launching, and optimizing Meta (Facebook/Instagram) advertising campaigns focused on conversions and return on investment
  • Collaborate on visual content creation using Canva, ensuring consistency in brand look and feel while supporting campaign performance
  • Track, analyze, and report on campaign performance (e.g., engagement, click-through rates, conversions), providing recommendations to improve results
  • Support promotional efforts tied directly to ticketed events, including launches, limited-time offers, and key sales periods

Qualifications

  • Currently enrolled in or a recent graduate of a post-secondary Business program with a focus or strong interest in Marketing
  • Strong written communication skills with attention to tone, clarity, and persuasive messaging
  • A keen eye for graphic design, layout, and visual storytelling
  • Proficiency in Microsoft Office Suite, Canva, and SquareSpace
  • Familiarity with social media platforms and Meta Ads Manager; exposure to performance marketing or analytics tools is an asset
  • Highly organized, proactive, and able to manage multiple projects in a fast-paced environment
  • Comfortable working both independently and collaboratively

Terms and Conditions:

  • This position is created through the Canada Summer Jobs Program, and therefore must align with those candidate requirements listed below.
  • Candidate is required to work at the workstation provided by the Niagara Grape & Wine Festival at 8 Church Street, St. Catharines, Ontario Monday through Thursday (the office is open from 9:30am – 5:00pm) and may work from home on Fridays, unless advised otherwise.
  • Weekend and evening work may occasionally be required for this position. These dates will be shared at least 2 weeks in advance and will be included in the 40-hour work week.
  • If required, the candidate will be reimbursed for mileage at $0.60/km and any pre-approved expenses.
  • A cellular phone allowance of $25/month will be provided.
  • The Niagara Grape & Wine Festival is responsible for all payroll government remittances

Canada Summer Jobs Candidate Requirements:

To be eligible, youth must:

  • be between 15 and 30 years of age at the beginning of the employment period
  • be a Canadian citizen, permanent resident, or person to whom refugee protection has been conferred under the Immigration and Refugee Protection Act for the duration of the employment and
  • have a valid Social Insurance Number at the start of employment and be legally entitled to work in Canada in accordance with relevant provincial or territorial legislation and regulations
  • Note: International students are not eligible participants. As the objective of the CSJ program is to support youth entering the Canadian labour market, the temporary nature of an international student's time in Canada does not allow for a long-term connection to the labour market.
  • International students include anyone who is temporarily in Canada for studies and who is not a Canadian citizen, permanent resident, or person who has been granted refugee status in Canada.
  • Youth awaiting a refugee status ruling, as well as those who hold a temporary visitor visa, youth visa or work visa are ineligible.
