Position Title: Calibration and Test Software Engineer (Co-op Student)

Employment Type: Student – Co-op, existing vacancy

Location: Ottawa ON, Onsite

About Lumentum

At Lumentum, we’re building the tech behind the world’s fastest networks and most advanced systems. Our optical and photonic solutions power everything from AI and cloud computing to data centers, telecom, and advanced manufacturing.

We’re a global team of innovators working where light meets technology, solving big challenges that keep the world connected and moving forward. If shaping the future of connectivity excites you, you’ll fit right in.

Overview

As a co-op student you will be part of the Modules Calibration and Test Automation Team. The team designs and develops test and calibration automation infrastructure (software and hardware solution) for Lumentum WSS / ROADM Modules. The team also supports the exiting software solutions that are deployed to Manufacturing site for bug fixes and feature additions.

The Opportunity

  • Duration: 8-month term (September - April 2026)

  • Pay Range: $24-35/hr

What You'll Be Doing

  • Work closely with R&D teams to develop calibration and test plans aimed at optimizing product performance and ensuring compliance to specifications.
  • Develop automation software and work with manufacturing facilities to introduce and support calibration and test implementation.
  • Through teamwork and technical approaches, solve problems related to product failures or test system issues.
  • Enhance reliability, yield, and throughput of test processes in manufacturing while maintaining low cost.
  • Manage test data and production trends to enable continuous improvement.
  • Trial and deploy developed improvements in remotely located manufacturing plants.

What We're Looking For

Education:

  • 3rd or 4th year Physics, Engineering Physics, Software/Computer Engineering or Computer Science. Software-inclined candidates from other Engineering/Science disciplines would also be considered
  • Candidates must be currently enrolled in a recognized Canadian post-secondary institution

Skills & Qualifications:

  • Programming experience with Object-oriented languages (VB, C#, Python)
  • Experience with optical systems, fiber optics, optical measurement instruments.
  • Strong troubleshooting and debugging skills.
  • Experience developing/working with embedded systems.
  • Familiarity with version control systems (Git/Subversion)
  • Excellent verbal and written communication skills.
