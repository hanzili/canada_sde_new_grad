Junior Process Engineer / Metallurgist (Contract)

About Green Manganese

Full Description

About Green Manganese

Green Manganese is a clean technology company focused on developing low-emission processes for manganese recovery and refining, supporting the transition to next-generation battery materials and sustainable steel production. Our work includes processing low-grade ores, mining waste, and industrial by-products using innovative hydrometallurgical techniques.

About the Role

We are looking for a Junior Process Engineer or Metallurgist to support ongoing R&D and pilot-scale development of manganese recovery processes, including chloride-based leaching and purification systems.

This role is ideal for a recent graduate who wants hands-on experience in process development, extractive metallurgy, and scale-up within a fast-moving startup environment.

Tasks

Key Responsibilities

  • Support laboratory and pilot-scale test work, including leaching, precipitation, and solid-liquid separation
  • Assist in development and optimization of chloride-based hydrometallurgical processes
  • Collect, analyze, and interpret experimental and process data
  • Contribute to process flow diagrams (PFDs) and mass balance calculations
  • Prepare technical reports, summaries, and internal documentation
  • Work closely with senior team members on process scale-up and pilot plant design
  • Ensure safe laboratory practices and maintain organized workspaces

Requirements

Qualifications

  • Bachelor’s degree in Chemical Engineering, Metallurgical Engineering, or Materials Engineering from a Canadian university (recent graduate preferred)
  • Strong interest in hydrometallurgy, extractive metallurgy, or chemical process development
  • Solid understanding of mass balances, thermodynamics, and reaction kinetics
  • Comfortable working in a hands-on lab or pilot environment
  • Strong analytical and problem-solving skills
  • Good written and verbal communication skills

Preferred (Nice to Have)

  • Internship or co-op experience in mining, metallurgy, or chemical processing
  • Exposure to leaching systems, solvent extraction, or crystallization
  • Familiarity with Excel, Python, or process simulation tools
  • Experience working with analytical methods (ICP, XRF, etc.)
  • Russian language proficiency is an asset, particularly for effective collaboration with senior technical team members

Eligibility

  • Must be legally eligible to work in Canada
  • Preference may be given to candidates eligible for youth employment or innovation support programs

Benefits

What We Offer

  • Direct involvement in cutting-edge battery materials and critical minerals processing
  • Hands-on experience with pilot plant development and scale-up
  • Opportunity to work alongside experienced metallurgists and process engineers
  • Flexible, part-time schedule (30 hours/week)
  • Exposure to a high-impact climate-tech startup environment
