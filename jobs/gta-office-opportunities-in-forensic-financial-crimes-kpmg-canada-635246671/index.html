GTA Office – Opportunities in Forensic, Financial Crimes Analytics – Co-op – Winter 2027 or Summer 2027 Start

at KPMG Canada

📍 Location Toronto, Ontario
📋 Details Co-op · Winter 2027
📅 Posted Mar 02, 2026

About the Role

Analyze data, build dashboards, validate models

About KPMG Canada

Global professional services firm; financial crimes focus.

Full Description

Overview Please note that you are limited to ONE APPLICATION during the Spring Recruitment period as we ask you to think ahead to what office and service line you are interested in. At KPMG in Canada, you’ll join a team of diverse and dedicated problem solvers, connected by a common cause turning insight into opportunity for clients and communities around the world.

Do work that matters, supported by a community that values difference and cares about you. Gain learning that’ll last you a lifetime and be recognized for the impact you make. With a focus on encouraging employees to come as they are, KPMG provides its employees with a variety of unique tools and support systems that ensure they thrive and make their mark in their career and in their communities.

What You Will Do Our Toronto office is seeking undergraduate students to join the team as a Co-op in Winter or Summer 2027 as a part of our Risk Services practice.

Our Forensic Services Team

Within our National Financial Crimes Centre of Excellence (FC COE), we help organizations manage financial crime risk by combining regulatory expertise, data analytics, and innovative technology solutions. Our team supports financial institutions and other regulated entities in navigating anti-money laundering (AML), sanctions, anti-corruption, and broader financial crime requirements.

As a Co-op student in Financial Crimes, you will gain hands-on exposure to client engagements while working alongside experienced professionals in a collaborative and purpose-driven environment.

You will support the delivery of financial crime engagements and contribute to client projects focused on strengthening compliance programs, systems, and analytics capabilities.

Your responsibilities may include

  • Supporting client engagements related to AML, sanctions, anti-corruption, and broader financial crime risk management initiatives.
  • Assisting with data analysis activities, including reviewing datasets, performing validation checks, identifying trends, and summarizing findings using tools such as SQL, Python, or other analytical platforms.
  • Supporting transaction monitoring reviews, model validation, and system testing activities by documenting observations and testing results.
  • Contributing to data mapping, cleansing, and remediation efforts to improve data quality and reporting processes.
  • Assisting in the preparation of client deliverables, including reports, presentations, dashboards, and working papers.
  • Conducting research on emerging financial crime trends and regulatory developments (e.g., FINTRAC, OSFI).
  • Collaborating with cross-functional teams including compliance, technology, data, and risk specialists.

What’s In It For You Interns & Co-op Students Our Intern and Co-op program aims to develop the next generation of leaders through challenging and interesting work. During your term, you will gain authentic engagement experience working with client projects across our teams. Along the way, you will broaden your skill set through KPMG’s vast learning and development opportunities that you can take on to your future career.

The national Power Up programming will also provide you with opportunities to learn from our leaders, build a network with other student hires through social events, collaborate with professionals from across KPMG and learn how KPMG supports you in your career journey.

Our Intern and Co-op program is designed to help you build meaningful skills through real client experiences.

During your term, you will

  • Gain exposure to financial crime engagements across AML, sanctions, and regulatory compliance initiatives.
  • Develop technical and analytical skills through hands-on experience with data-driven tools and methodologies.
  • Strengthen your problem-solving, communication, and teamwork skills in a fast-paced environment.
  • Learn how regulatory expectations translate into practical risk management solutions for clients.

You will also benefit from KPMG’s learning and development offerings, including technical training, professional skills development, and mentorship opportunities that support your long-term career growth.

What You Bring To The Role You strive to make a meaningful and positive difference alongside your peers, to work collaboratively and with innovation at the forefront, and are curious about how financial systems and regulatory frameworks operate. You’re passionate about being involved in your local community and post-secondary institution and have a proven ability to achieve personal and professional success while being curious as you learn emerging practices and technologies.

You demonstrate

  • A strong academic record in engineering, mathematics, data science, computer science, business, finance, accounting, economics, criminology, or a related field.
  • An interest in financial crime, including AML, sanctions, fraud, anti-corruption, or regulatory compliance.
  • Curiosity about how data and technology can support risk management and compliance efforts.
  • Foundational analytical skills and experience working with data (e.g., SQL, Python, R, or similar tools) through coursework or projects (considered an asset).
  • An interest in financial services and regulated industries (considered an asset).
  • Strong written and verbal communication skills.
  • Attention to detail, initiative, and a willingness to learn.
  • The ability to work effectively in a team environment.

How you will succeed

  • You have a strong academic record and demonstrate intellectual curiosity through coursework, projects, or extracurricular involvement.
  • You build collaborative relationships You establish strong relationships internally and externally and contribute positively to team-based environments. You respect diverse perspectives and work effectively across functions.
  • You develop and motivate others You engage peers with enthusiasm, contribute ideas confidently, and create a positive team dynamic that supports shared success.
  • You take opportunities to learn and adapt You actively seek feedback, reflect on experiences, and embrace new tools, technologies, and regulatory developments as part of your professional growth.
  • You drive quality You deliver accurate, thoughtful, and well-documented work, demonstrating attention to detail and a commitment to excellence in all tasks.
  • You foster innovation You are open to experimentation and continuous improvement, showing curiosity about how analytics, automation, and emerging technologies can enhance financial crime risk management.
  • Familiarity with AI‑enabled tools or analytics platforms is considered an asset, along with comfort in leveraging AI to enhance efficiency, generate insights, or support problem‑solving, and a demonstrated interest in how these technologies can be applied in professional settings.
