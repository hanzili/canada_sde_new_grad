About IKO North America

Full Description

IKO Industries Ltd. is a market leader in the manufacturing of roofing and building materials. IKO is a Canadian owned and operated business with production facilities worldwide and has many years of unparalleled success in the roofing materials industry. Quality, integrity, and trustworthiness are the values that underlie this success, and we have built this company by hiring people who hold these values. People like you!

Job Description The Junior Laboratory Technician supports laboratory operations by performing routine tests, maintaining equipment, and ensuring compliance with safety and quality standards. This entry-level role is ideal for individuals looking to gain hands-on experience in a scientific laboratory environment. The technician will contribute to the accuracy and efficiency of laboratory processes.

Location & Salary This position is based out of our corporate office in Brampton, ON. Salary Range: $50,000-55,000

Job Responsibilities

  • Safely conduct testing, analysis and reporting of asphalt roofing shingle products and raw materials.
  • Prepare samples, reagents, and solutions for analysis according to standard procedures.
  • Perform routine laboratory tests and experiments following standard operating procedures (SOPs) and relevant standards.
  • Accurately record and report test results in a timely manner.
  • Assist in inventory management of laboratory supplies and reagents.
  • Follow all laboratory health and safety protocols and best practices and guidelines.
  • Ensure proper labeling, handling, and storage of chemicals and samples in accordance with Material Safety Data Sheets (MSDS).
  • Adhere to internal standards, policies, and procedures.
  • Clean, calibrate, and maintain laboratory instruments and equipment according to procedures.
  • Document repairs, maintenance, calibration, and verification of laboratory instruments.
  • Coordinate work to maintain efficiency and ensure timely completion of laboratory tasks.
  • Coordinate with other technicians to complete tasks according to the planned schedule.
  • Participate in team meetings, training sessions, and knowledge-sharing activities.
  • Support senior technicians and scientists in ongoing projects.

Qualifications

  • Diploma or Bachelor’s degree in Chemistry, Environmental Science, Engineering Technology, or related field.
  • 0-2 years of experience in a laboratory setting (internships or co-op placements considered).
  • Certified Technician (C.Tech) or Certified Engineering Technologist (C.E.T.) is considered an asset (or eligibility).
  • Experience in a laboratory environment is considered an asset.
  • Familiar with laboratory techniques, instrumentation, and processes including the safe operation of testing equipment and adherence to lab procedures.
  • Knowledge of the Occupational Health and Safety Act and related regulations.
  • Detail oriented and maintains organizational skills to support team tasks.
  • Demonstrates problem-solving and analytical abilities in routine tasks.
  • Excellent oral and written communication skills to interact effectively and efficiently with the team.
  • Ability to occasionally lift materials up to 25 lbs.
