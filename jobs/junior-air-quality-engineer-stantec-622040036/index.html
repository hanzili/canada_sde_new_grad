About Stantec

Full Description

Grounded in safety, quality, and ethics, our experts lead their fields and guide our work with rigor, a creative spirit, and vision for growth. We draw from more than 20 technical specialties around the globe and are committed to fostering an inclusive community of diverse talents, backgrounds, and expertise. We are a place to apply your passion and collaborate with top environmental talent on work that’s critical to our clients and the communities they support.

Projects require environmental review and licensing, and this step is becoming increasingly critical for development across Canda and around the world. We are seeking a motivated team player that works collaboratively and is inspired to make a difference for the environment. Organizational skills and the ability to support multidisciplinary teams in a fast-paced company are essential qualities. This is a permanent full-time position in our Prairies and Territories Assessment and Permitting Team based in the Yellowknife office. There’s no end to the scope and significance of your work, and to where it can take you at Stantec.

Your Opportunity

Our Environmental Services Group is dedicated to managing environmental issues professionally and proactively. Our staff of professionals includes specialists in atmospheric sciences, acoustics and vibration, marine biology, wetland science, wildlife biology, soil science, fisheries biology, botany, hydrogeology, hydrology, forestry, habitat and ecosystem restoration, permitting, GIS, and Information Management. We help our clients identify and respond to opportunities and threats within constantly changing regulatory and corporate environments, through active engagement with the public, stakeholder groups, and governmental regulators. Our environmental services practitioners offer a full suite of services including permitting, monitoring, modeling, planning, policy studies, design, and control.

Stantec’s Environmental Services Practice is currently seeking a full-time position for a Junior Air Quality Engineer in our Calgary office location. We are seeking an exceptionally talented and enthusiastic individual with a keen interest in air quality (AQ) assessment to join our team and advance their career. Our Air Quality Specialists provide specialized services to a wide variety of sectors including industry, government, architecture, transportation, and buildings. In addition to air quality related work, our Atmospheric Science group provides services such as greenhouse gas (GHG) verification, hazard and risk assessment, air monitoring network design, and noise assessment.

Your Key Responsibilities

  • Conducting air quality assessments for a wide range of facilities/processes and for various jurisdictions, with a primary focus in western North America.
  • Completing air quality assessments, including air emissions inventory development, air dispersion modelling, regulatory compliance support and recommendations, etc..
  • Assisting other team members in the preparation of reports, proposals, marketing materials, and/or technical papers.

Your Capabilities and Experience

  • Working knowledge in industrial process and environmental impact assessments
  • Proficient understanding of provincial and federal regulatory requirements and guidelines
  • Experience in a consulting work environment
  • Strong analytical skills and experience with air dispersion models (i.e., AERMOD, CALPUFF) is an asset
  • Training and applicable experience in the ISO 14064 greenhouse standards is an asset

Education and Experience

  • Post-secondary education in mechanical, chemical or environmental engineering
  • 1-2 years consulting experience in environmental services, air quality assessment services, ambient data monitoring, or GHG verification services
  • Demonstrate strength in verbal and written communication
  • Strong team player and ability to manage multiple projects
  • Strong programming skills is an asset

A typical office environment includes working with computers and remaining sedentary for long periods of time.
