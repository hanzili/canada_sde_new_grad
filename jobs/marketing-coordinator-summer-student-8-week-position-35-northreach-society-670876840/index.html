About Northreach Society

Northreach Society is a non‑profit organization based in Grande Prairie, Alberta, dedicated to improving health and well‑being across Northern Alberta. Our mission is to build a respectful, comprehensive, and dignified network of support for all populations through collaboration, education, health promotion, and injury prevention.

We provide a broad range of community‑based services, including overdose prevention, STBBI testing and support, harm‑reduction and injury‑prevention supply distribution, and additional health‑promotion initiatives. These include programs addressing food insecurity through our Helping Hands Division.

Guided by our vision of a safe and inclusive world free from discrimination, Northreach Society works to empower individuals and strengthen communities through innovation, compassion, and respect. Our organization is supported by a dedicated team of staff, collaborative partners, and a committed board of directors who work together to deliver essential programs that promote health, dignity, and community well‑being across the region.

Northreach Society strives to create a welcoming and inclusive workplace. We encourage applications from Indigenous Peoples, Black and other racialized individuals, people with lived or living experience, and others who bring diverse perspectives. We are committed to equity in hiring and ensure that all candidates are fairly considered based on their strengths and experience.

Position Summary

The Marketing Coordinator is a vital member of Northreach Society’s team, responsible for driving marketing and communication efforts that strengthen our brand and advance our mission to improve health and well-being across Northern Alberta. This role focuses on creating compelling, audience-centered content and implementing strategies that increase awareness, engagement, and support for our programs and initiatives. The Marketing Coordinator will collaborate with internal teams and community partners to craft impactful stories that highlight our work and foster meaningful connections.

The ideal candidate is a creative and strategic thinker with strong organizational skills, attention to detail, and a passion for using digital media to inspire positive change. This position offers an opportunity to combine marketing expertise with purpose-driven work in a dynamic nonprofit environment.

Key Responsibilities include:

Content Creation & Digital Media

  • Develop and manage engaging content for social media, website, newsletters, and other digital platforms.
  • Write, edit, and proofread content to ensure clarity, accuracy, and alignment with Northreach Society’s brand voice.
  • Create or coordinate visually engaging graphics and multimedia content to support storytelling and audience engagement.

Digital Marketing & Analytics

  • Manage social media accounts, including scheduling posts, monitoring interactions, and responding as appropriate.
  • Track and analyze social media, website, and campaign analytics to inform content optimization and audience engagement.
  • Support planning and execution of email marketing campaigns and assist with content scheduling and delivery.
  • Assist with website content updates to ensure information remains current, accurate, and accessible.

Branding & Strategy

  • Ensure consistent branding, messaging, and tone across all communications and platforms.
  • Contribute to the implementation of communications and marketing strategies aligned with organizational goals.
  • Monitor digital marketing trends and best practices to inform outreach and engagement efforts.

Community & Stakeholder Engagement

  • Collaborate with internal teams to promote programs, services, and events effectively.
  • Support relationship‑building with external partners and stakeholders to enhance visibility and community reach.
  • Capture and share stories, testimonials, and impact narratives that reflect the organization’s mission and values.

Administrative & Project Support

  • Maintain and update communications and marketing calendars to support timely execution of initiatives.
  • Track campaign performance metrics and assist with reporting and evaluation.
  • Provide general administrative support for communications and marketing activities, including file organization and resource management.

Education & Certification

  • Current Post-secondary Student in Marketing, Communications, Public Relations, or a related field.

Additional Requirements:

  • Criminal and Vulnerable Sector Check.

Additional Qualifications:

  • Proficiency in social media platforms, content management systems, and email marketing tools.
  • Strong writing, editing, and storytelling skills.
  • Basic graphic design and video editing skills (Canva, Adobe Suite, or similar tools preferred).
  • Knowledge of analytics tools (Google Analytics, social media insights) is an asset.

Core Competencies:

  • Creativity & Innovation: Ability to generate fresh ideas and deliver engaging content.
  • Attention to Detail: Ensures accuracy and consistency in all communications.
  • Organization & Time Management: Manages multiple projects and deadlines effectively.
  • Collaboration: Works well with diverse teams and community partners.
  • Adaptability: Responds positively to changing priorities and emerging trends.
  • Mission-Driven: Passionate about advancing health and well-being through nonprofit work

Work Schedule and Conditions

This position is full-time, typically scheduled Monday to Friday with a 30-minute lunch break. Flexibility in hours and days may be required to meet community needs, including occasional pre-scheduled evening or weekend tasks. Work involves a combination of physical and administrative duties:

  • Ability to lift and carry objects, bend, and stand or walk for extended periods.
  • Significant time performing desk-based tasks such as administrative work and digital content management.
  • Daily interactions include individuals experiencing food insecurity and other vulnerabilities, requiring professionalism, empathy, and cultural sensitivity.
  • Emotional resilience and a strong commitment to client-centered care are essential.
  • Due to the nature of living and working in Northern Alberta, candidates must be able to drive in all weather conditions and may occasionally travel within the region.
