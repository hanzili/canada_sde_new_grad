About the Role

The Youth Recreational & Leisure Activities Program Coordinator is a dynamic, youth-focused leadership position designed to empower young people to lead, design, and deliver impactful community programming.

Hosted by the EL Shama Cares Foundation, this role supports the delivery of our summer youth youth initiative, combining recreation, leadership development, digital engagement, and community building.

This is not a traditional desk job, it is a hands-on leadership and mentorship experience where youth help shape programming, facilitate activities, and contribute to a city-wide anti-hate movement.

Program Purpose

This position exists to:

  • Build youth leadership and employability skills
  • Provide structured recreational and leisure programming for youth
  • Support anti-hate education through sport, dialogue, and creative expression
  • Engage communities through both in-person and digital initiatives

Key Responsibilities

As a Youth Program Coordinator, you will:

  • Plan, organize, and deliver youth recreational and leisure activities
  • Support and facilitate youth-led workshops and engagement sessions
  • Assist in delivering a youth anti-hate digital campaign
  • Help coordinate two in-person community engagement sessions
  • Support delivery of a Sports Against Hate drop-in program
  • Contribute to a youth-led debate and storytelling series
  • Assist in planning a final community showcase event
  • Support outreach, participant engagement, and program logistics
  • Work collaboratively with a team of youth staff and mentors

Eligibility Requirements

Applicants must:

  • Be between 15–30 years of age
  • Be legally eligible to work in Canada
  • Have a valid Social Insurance Number (SIN)
  • Be able to commit to the full 9-week summer program

Priority Hiring

In alignment with the Canada Summer Jobs mandate, priority will be given to:

  • Marginalized and equity-deserving youth
  • Racialized youth
  • Newcomer youth
  • Youth facing barriers to employment

Ideal Candidate

You are a great fit if you:

  • Enjoy working with youth and community programs
  • Are interested in leadership, recreation, or social impact
  • Are willing to learn and grow in a team environment
  • Have strong communication skills (or want to build them)
  • Care about inclusion, equity, and community safety

No previous experience required — training will be provided.

Program Details

  • 9-week paid summer employment program
  • Full-time hours (schedule provided upon hiring)
  • Combination of training, recreation programming, and community engagement
  • Final capstone presentation and community showcase

How to Apply

Please submit your resume and short cover letter outlining your interest in youth leadership and community engagement.
