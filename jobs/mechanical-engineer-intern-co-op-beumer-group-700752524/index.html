About the Role

Create CAD drawings and 3D models; support design

About BEUMER Group

Global intralogistics leader; supportive engineering internship program

Full Description

Company Description Conveying, loading, palletising, packing, sortation and distribution – BEUMER Group is a leader within the development and manufacture of high-technological intralogistics systems for global markets. Our employees stand out from others thanks to their ability to supply innovative solutions to our customers. They can do this because they know the industry and are passionate about continuously developing and expanding their knowledge. We support these high standards through teamwork, mutual respect and a working culture based on trust that fosters stability and security for all of our employees. Our common goal is to implement outstanding and innovative projects worldwide.

Job Description As a Mechanical Engineering Intern, you will join our Calgary-based Engineering team, contributing to Mineral and Mining projects while gaining valuable hands-on experience in mechanical design, engineering analysis, and project execution. This role is designed to bridge academic theory with practical application, giving you exposure to industrial equipment design, and real-world problem-solving. You will collaborate with experienced engineers and project teams to support the development of innovative, efficient, and reliable mechanical solutions.

  • Term length: 4 months
  • Start date: September 2026
  • Location: Calgary, AB

Key Responsibilities

  • Support the Engineering team in solving mechanical design and engineering challenges.
  • Assist with daily engineering tasks as required, gaining real-world experience across different areas of mechanical engineering.
  • Participate in the design and development of mechanical components.
  • Create and develop AutoCAD drawings and 3D models.
  • Contribute to the preparation of engineering documentation, including drawings, layouts, and technical specifications.
  • Other administrative or engineering duties assigned as necessary by senior engineering staff.

Qualifications

  • Currently pursuing a Bachelor’s degree in Mechanical Engineering or a related field.
  • Strong technical knowledge and understanding of basic engineering principles.
  • Knowledge of AutoCAD and Inventor or similar.
  • Ability to read drawings and to create sketches.
  • Exceptional technical and problem-solving skills and reasoning ability.
  • Willingness to learn and grow in a fast-paced environment.
  • Excellent time management, attention to detail, and ability to quickly absorb new concepts.
  • Strong verbal and written communication skills.
  • Professional, positive attitude and a strong team player.
  • Must be legally able to work in Canada at time of application.
