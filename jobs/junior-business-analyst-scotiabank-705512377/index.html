About the Role

Gather requirements, map processes, support QA

About Scotiabank

Large Canadian bank; liquidity risk modernization projects

Full Description

Requisition ID: 262651

Join a purpose driven winning team, committed to results, in an inclusive and high-performing culture.

Role

  • Business group: Group Treasury – Liquidity Risk
  • Project: Supporting multiple liquidity regulatory and modernization initiatives across in‑house and vendor platforms, including requirements gathering, business analysis, testing, and cross‑team coordination.

Candidate Value Proposition

  • Opportunity to work on high-impact liquidity regulatory projects (LCR, NSFR, NCCF, FR 2052a) and modernization efforts across core Treasury platforms.
  • Exposure to senior stakeholders, cross-functional teams, and complex Treasury systems.

In This Role You Will

  • Participate in development, testing, and implementation of liquidity applications.
  • Gather, analyze, and document requirements (BRDs, functional specs, data definitions).
  • Collaborate with Treasury business lines, Operations, Development, Data teams, and Application Support.
  • Support BAU and regulatory initiatives with project managers, architects, and developers.
  • Interpret regulatory materials and translate them into business/functional requirements.
  • Define test plans, functional test cases, and support full QA validation.
  • Contribute to process mapping and business architecture documentation.
  • Support production processes and participate in UAT.
  • Work independently on multiple concurrent assignments.

Do you have the skills that will enable you to succeed in this role?

  • 2+ years IT/business experience within banking or financial institutions.
  • 2+ years in Risk Management, Regulatory Domains, Liquidity Risk.
  • Strong knowledge of FR 2052a, LCR, NSFR, NCCF and OSFI regulatory expectations.
  • 2+ years writing SQL queries and performing data validation.
  • 2+ years QA/testing experience, including defining functional test scenarios.
  • Experience preparing BRDs, functional specs, business architecture documents, and data requirements.
  • Ability to interpret regulatory documentation and translate into system requirements.
  • Experience with Jira and Confluence.

Nice to Have

  • Strong communication, analytical skills, and ability to work independently.
  • Familiarity with all phases of the SDLC.
  • Programming experience (Python preferred).
  • Scripting experience.
  • Experience with Treasury or vendor liquidity platforms.
  • Prior Scotiabank experience.

Soft Skills

  • Strong analytical thinking and problem-solving ability.
  • Self-driven, proactive, and able to work with minimal supervision.
  • Able to manage multiple concurrent assignments.
  • Excellent written and verbal communication.
  • Strong attention to detail and disciplined documentation practices.

What's in it for you?

  • Diversity, Equity, Inclusion & Allyship - We strive to create an inclusive culture where every employee is empowered to reach their fullest potential, respected for who they are, and are embraced through bias-free practices and inclusive values across Scotiabank. We embrace diversity and provide opportunities for all employee to learn, grow & participate through our various Employee Resource Groups (ERGs) that span across diverse gender identities, ethnicity, race, age, ability & veterans.
  • Accessibility - We value the unique skills and experiences each individual brings to the Bank and are committed to creating and maintaining an inclusive and accessible environment for everyone.
  • Upskilling through online courses, cross-functional development opportunities, and tuition assistance.
  • Competitive Rewards program including bonus, flexible vacation, personal, sick days, and benefits will start on day one.
  • Community Engagement - no matter where you choose to work from; we offer opportunities for community engagement & belonging with our various programs

Location(s): Canada : Ontario : Toronto

Scotiabank is a leading bank in the Americas. Guided by our purpose: "for every future", we help our customers, their families and their communities achieve success through a broad range of advice, products and services, including personal and commercial banking, wealth management and private banking, corporate and investment banking, and capital markets.
