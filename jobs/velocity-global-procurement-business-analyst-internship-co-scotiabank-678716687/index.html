Full Description

Requisition ID: 260565

Join a purpose driven winning team, committed to results, in an inclusive and high-performing culture.

Velocity – Global Procurement, Business Analyst Internship/Co-Op – Fall 2026

Term: September – April 2027 (8 months)

Work Hours: 37.5

Application Deadline: 05/22/2026

There's no better way to kickstart your career than to do a co-op or internship with Scotiabank! During your work term, you’ll have the opportunity to be part of a winning team, build your network, and discover what you love – all while getting paid to do it! There’s a place for every type of student at Scotiabank and all that we ask for is that you have passion and strong AOO (Attitude, Ownership and Opportunity).

The Velocity Program is not your average internship or co-op! This is a customized experience that focuses on providing you with the development and tools you need to grow your career. After successfully completing a co-op or internship with top performance, we will work with you to establish your future placements. As a result, you will get to learn, grow, and experiment within different areas of both Scotiabank and Tangerine!

Is this role right for you? In this role, you will:

  • Conduct internal spend analysis by developing cost/pricing models by consolidating data from various sourcing categories

  • Creating and maintaining various presentations and reports using Excel, PowerPoint, SharePoint and PowerBI

  • Conduct market, industry and product research using internal and external resources

  • Support the development of documentation, presentations, supplier contracts and implementation plans

  • Assist in spend validation and data cleansing to improve the quality of data sources

  • Assist in the creation of RF(x) proposals, including evaluation criteria and pricing models

  • Create and configure RF(x) strategies and competitive bidding events using the Bank’s technology platforms

  • Support the team in the execution of initiatives, project management, or other ad-hoc analyses

  • Support with developing, coordinating, and improving the internal and external capabilities of Global Procurement (e.g. tools, processes, templates, technologies)

  • Support with bank-wide enablement of procurement standards and tools

  • Applies risk management and compliance checks when researching and analyzing supply markets and contract performance to identify potential risks and trigger risk mitigation and management responses

  • Support dependent projects needed to reach the overall strategic objectives, including monitoring progress to ensure that milestones are being met

Do you have the skills and requirements that will enable you to succeed in this role? – We’d love to work with you if:

  • You are currently enrolled in post-secondary education.

  • You love to learn and envision yourself working for an international organization that heavily invests in your future.

  • You enjoy being involved in extracurricular activities such as conferences, clubs, and competitions and/or hackathons.

  • You are resourceful and creative in gathering & mining data from various sources and applications (includes both quantitative and qualitative data).

  • You have excellent analytical skills: able to understand & manipulate complex and large datasets; being comfortable with unstructured data is important.

  • A collaborative team-player who can build relationships easily across various stakeholder groups.

  • You can learn and navigate through new applications quickly and efficiently.

  • You have basic knowledge in data collection, manipulation, cleansing and analysis experience with work or project experience with spreadsheet modeling and database management.

  • You are proficient in MS Office applications (i.e. Excel, PowerPoint, Word).

  • Procurement, sourcing, vendor management and contract analysis experience are good-to-have’s (but not required)

  • Knowledge of project and program management best practices and methodologies are good-to-have’s (but not required)

  • Data visualization and reporting experience are an asset (ex: Power BI).

What’s in it for you?

  • You’ll be part of a diverse, collaborative, innovative, and high-performing team.

  • In-depth training to prepare you for the role, as well as ongoing coaching and feedback to help you succeed!

  • Exclusive student events such as Scotia Student Day, Lunch & Learns, leadership panels, and much more!

  • Bank-wide orientation to learn more about Scotiabank and gain exposure to senior leadership across the organization.

How do I apply?

For students applying to our internship/co-op or new graduate programs, we do not require resumes! We believe in evaluating you through relationships over resumes and are focused on learning about who you are and what you’re interested in to uncover your true potential. Instead, we ask that as part of your application you complete the below:

Step 1: Complete your PLUM Profile here save as a screenshot.

Step 2: Complete a short one-way video interview here.

Step 3: Apply to the role and when the application asks for your resume, instead upload the screenshot of your PLUM Profile.

  • To be considered for student opportunities at Scotiabank, Tangerine, and MD Financial you must complete all steps above.
