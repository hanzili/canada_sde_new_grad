The 12-Month Contract Compliance Analyst will support the TMX Datalinx Business Operations team by ensuring rigorous adherence to reporting policies, oversight standards, and internal controls across the Datalinx Xpress & Audit Programs. The role is responsible for monitoring client compliance, conducting reviews and True Ups, interpreting data for compliance-related insights, and enhancing governance processes across Datalinx sales, product, and operations teams. This position requires strong analytical capabilities, excellent stakeholder communication, and a commitment to maintaining high levels of accuracy, transparency, and regulatory alignment.

Key Accountabilities: Compliance Oversight & Client Governance:

  • Partner with the TMX Datalinx Business Operations team to strengthen compliance controls within the Datalinx Xpress & Audit Program.
  • Conduct compliance True Ups with clients to verify adherence to TMX Datalinx reporting policies and contractual obligations.
  • Develop and maintain discovery cases based on client feedback, ensuring potential compliance gaps or risk areas are identified early.
  • Communicate policy requirements, product roadmaps, and regulatory-aligned offerings to strategic clients to support transparency and compliance readiness.

Data Monitoring & Compliance Analytics:

  • Identify, analyze, and interpret patterns in complex datasets to detect compliance risks, inconsistencies, or anomalies in sales, pricing, operational activity, and client behavior.
  • Generate actionable insights that inform compliance decisions, risk mitigation planning, and operational improvements.
  • Prepare compliance reports and metrics for internal stakeholders, supporting audit readiness and internal review cycles.

Audit Support & Controls Enhancement:

  • Collaborate with cross-functional teams—technical delivery, sales, product, and operations—to enhance processes and ensure alignment with compliance best practices.
  • Support the development and refinement of compliance monitoring workflows, documentation standards, and audit procedures.
  • Assist in process improvement initiatives to reduce risk exposure, streamline compliance checks, and enhance end-to-end governance across Datalinx teams.

Must Have(s):

  • Recent graduate with a Bachelor’s degree in Business, Commerce, Economics, or Finance
  • Proven quantitative analysis skills, preferably in financial products or asset classes
  • Strong attention to detail and adherence to high compliance accuracy standards
  • Collaborative, proactive, and results-oriented mindset
  • Strong client-facing communication and relationship management capabilities
  • Demonstrated learning agility and ability to adapt to evolving compliance requirements
  • Advanced proficiency in Microsoft Excel for compliance tracking and reporting

Nice to Have(s):

  • Experience in the securities industry, including knowledge of trading workflows and how broker/dealers, buy-side firms, traders, analysts, and advisors leverage TMX products—especially in contexts requiring compliance, reporting accuracy, or audit support.
