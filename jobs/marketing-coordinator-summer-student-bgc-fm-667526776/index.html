Position Summary

The Marketing Coordinator (Summer Student) will support the marketing, communications, and outreach initiatives of The Fort McMurray Boys and Girls Club. A key focus of this role will be supporting the promotion of a new daycare expansion, including marketing material creation, community outreach, and engagement activities. The position offers hands‑on experience in nonprofit marketing, sales‑oriented outreach, and community feedback collection.

Key Responsibilities

Marketing & Communications

  • Create marketing materials to promote programs, services, and the new daycare expansion (digital and print)
  • Assist with the development of promotional campaigns for summer programs, special events, and new services
  • Support website updates, newsletters, posters, flyers, and other communications
  • Ensure brand consistency across all materials

Digital & Social Media

  • Draft, schedule, and post content on social media platforms
  • Capture photos and short videos for promotional use, following consent and privacy policies
  • Monitor and track basic engagement metrics and assist with reporting

Outreach, Promotion & Engagement

  • Conduct in‑person, phone, and email outreach to:
  • Promote programs and the new daycare expansion
  • Share information with families, community members, and partners
  • Support enrollment and awareness efforts
  • Represent the organization at community events, outreach activities, and information sessions as required

Survey & Feedback Collection

  • Assist in distributing surveys related to programs and daycare expansion
  • Collect feedback from families, community members, and partners
  • Organize and summarize survey responses for staff review

Events & Administrative Support

  • Assist with planning and promoting community events and fundraising activities
  • Support event setup, breakdown, and documentation
  • Maintain organized digital files and contact lists
  • Perform other marketing‑related duties as assigned

Qualifications

Required

  • Eligible under the Canada Summer Jobs program (18-30 years old)
  • Strong written and verbal communication skills
  • Comfortable speaking with community members in person and by phone
  • Basic computer literacy and familiarity with email and social media
  • Organized, reliable, and professional
  • Ability to work independently and as part of a team

Assets

  • Interest in marketing, communications, sales, or nonprofit work
  • Experience or interest in community outreach or customer engagement
  • Familiarity with tools such as Canva or survey platforms
  • Experience working with children, youth, or family‑serving organizations

Work Environment

  • Mix of office‑based work and community/outreach activities
  • Supervised by a designated staff member
  • Training and mentorship provided throughout the placement
