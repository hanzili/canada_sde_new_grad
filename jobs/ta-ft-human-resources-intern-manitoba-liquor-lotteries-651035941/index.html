TA FT Human Resources Intern

at Manitoba Liquor & Lotteries

📍 Location Winnipeg, MB Hybrid
📋 Details Type: Internship · Duration: Not specified · Pay: Not specified · Term: Not specified
📅 Posted Mar 26, 2026

About the Role

Support HR admin, data entry, documentation, scheduling

About Manitoba Liquor & Lotteries

Public sector employer; strong training and benefits.

Full Description

All Manitoba Liquor & Lotteries employees may apply. Manitoba Liquor and Lotteries is committed to Diversity, Equity and Inclusion. We strive to hire a workforce that reflects the community we serve. Employment equity will be considered therefore applicants who identify as women, Indigenous people, members of racialized groups, and persons with a disability are encouraged to apply.

If you require an accommodation at any time during the recruitment process, please let us know how we can meet your needs.

Job Purpose

Reporting to the Manager, Organizational Development, the Human Resources Intern is responsible for providing a diverse range of administrative functions and providing support to the Human Resources departments. The incumbent will act in a highly confidential manner as it pertains to sensitive employee and applicant information.

Job Responsibilities

  • Audit, monitor or verify eligibility of internal programming requirements.
  • Provide required documents/forms for processing and distribute as required.
  • Collect, utilize and enter information into databases or spreadsheets.
  • May compile data, statistics and other information and prepare summary reports.

  • Produce graphs from statistical tables/spreadsheets for various reports.

  • Assist Consultants with trend identification based on graphical reports.

  • Coordinate, process and monitor clearances or eligibility lists.
  • Create and maintain required documents and files.
  • Assist with program documentation, development, review and revision.
  • Assist with scanning documents
  • Maintain filing systems, including confidential Human Resources business documentation and employee records.
  • Liaise with Records Management staff to perform department records transfer as needed and ensure compliance with the Records Management policy and associated procedures.
  • Assist with identifying and preparing obsolete records for archiving.
  • Monitor and maintain HR Mailbox and route to appropriate department contact. Retrieve and distribute faxes and mail.
  • Update iNet (i.e. job postings, Safety and Health Resources, Workplace Safety and Health Committee Minutes) and/or update communications through the Communications Department.
  • Assist with communication, including mailing and distribution of documents.
  • Assist/attend related events (i.e. career fair, North American Occupational Safety and Health Week).
  • Process internal and external invoices.
  • Ensure P-card maintenance and monthly reconciliation.
  • Respond to general employee and public inquiries.
  • Schedule and organize meetings. Attend meetings, take minutes and distribute minutes.
  • Register employees for training, as required.
  • Administer testing/assessments and ensure files are updated.
  • Order and maintain inventories (i.e. first aid kits, defibrillators, office supplies)
  • Initiate and track work orders.
  • Prepare presentation materials/resources (e.g., PowerPoint slides), as required.
  • Liaise with internal departments and external carriers, vendors, LGCA, WCB, etc. on programming activities or requirements.
  • Perform other related duties as required.

Primary Qualifications

  • Completion of a Grade 12 High School diploma.
  • Current enrollment in, or recent completion of, a diploma/certificate program in Human Resource Management.
  • Experience and proficiency in Microsoft Office (Word, Excel, PowerPoint and Outlook).
  • A minimum of two years’ experience in an administrative support role – recording, compiling and reviewing statistical data and entering data containing a variety of detailed and highly confidential information.
  • Working knowledge of HRIS systems, COGNOS, Workers Compensation Board database and time and attendance systems is an asset.
  • Ability to apply time management skills, being able to organize heavy workloads and multiple supervisors.
  • Excellent written and verbal communication skills.
  • Must maintain the strictest confidence in dealing with highly sensitive employee and applicant information.
  • Knowledge of Manitoba Liquor & Lotteries collective agreements, benefit plan provisions, safety and health/workers compensation board legislation for reporting, submissions and follow up and policies & procedures is an asset.
  • The core competencies for this position include achieving quality results, adaptability/managing change, communication, customer service, decision making and problem solving, integrity and building trust, teamwork and cooperation and valuing diversity. Leadership competencies include building strategic performance, coaching and developing, influencing and leading. These competencies are deemed important for the success of the position and organization.

Secondary Qualifications

  • Bilingual (French/English) language skills.
