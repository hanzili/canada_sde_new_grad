About the Role

Assist HR operations; support recruitment and records

About Radiance Family Society

Community-focused charity; growing HR operations team.

Full Description

Purpose:

The Human Resources Summer Student will provide valuable support to the Human Resources manager by assisting with key projects and day-to-day activities that enhance organizational effectiveness and compliance. The successful candidate will contribute to updating and finalizing the draft Employee Handbook, auditing and organizing employee files to meet accreditation standards, supporting recruitment processes, and developing practical tools and resources to strengthen HR operations.

Responsibilities...

  • Assist in reviewing, formatting, and finalizing the draft Employee Handbook.
  • Audit and clean up employee files to ensure accuracy, completeness, and compliance with accreditation and legislative requirements.
  • Support the recruitment process by screening resumes and shortlisting candidates.
  • Assist with uploading training documents and ensure tracking is up to date.
  • Help conduct reference checks as needed.
  • Help build reports in HRIS as needed.
  • Develop easy-to-follow “How-To” tools and guides for HRIS processes to support employee and supervisor use.
  • Participate in additional HR projects and initiatives as time allows, contributing ideas and solutions to improve HR processes and employee experience.

Qualifications...

  • Currently enrolled in a post-secondary program in Human Resources, Business Administration, or a related field.
  • Well organized, methodical, and proficient in administration practices.
  • Strong organizational and attention-to-detail skills.
  • Must have the ability to handle confidential information with professionalism and discretion.
  • Proficiency in Microsoft Office (Word, Excel, Outlook, PowerPoint).
  • Familiarity with HRIS systems considered an asset.
  • Team player with strong interpersonal, organizational and communication skills.
  • Must have a cheerful outlook, be amiable and a team player.
  • Effective time and task management and organizational skills

Work Ethic Expectations...

  • Must be able to work 35 hours per week, Monday through Friday.
  • Ability to effectively establish and maintain cooperative working relationships within a diverse multicultural workplace.
  • Ability to follow instructions and is task oriented.
  • Must be self-motivated with a strong work ethic.
  • Ability to work independently and within a team environment and to collaborate in a professional manner with colleagues, clients, and members of the public.

Appreciation of the needs and challenges of families who have experienced domestic violence.

CANADA SUMMER JOBS REQUIREMENTS:

  • Be between 18 and 30 years of age at the start of the employment.
  • Be a Canadian citizen, permanent resident, or person to whom refugee protection has been conferred under the Immigration and Refugee Protection Act for the duration of the employment.
  • Have a valid Social Insurance Number at the start of employment and be legally entitled to work in Canada in accordance with relevant provincial or territorial legislation.
  • International students are not eligible participants. As the objective of the Canada Summer Jobs program is to support youth entering the Canadian labour market, the temporary nature of an international student’s time in Canada does not allow for a long-term connection to the labour market.
