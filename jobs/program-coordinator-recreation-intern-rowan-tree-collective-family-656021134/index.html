Full Description

Overview

The Rowan Tree Collective is a local not for profit programming hub dedicated to promoting lifelong learning while offering social and recreational opportunities to young adults with Autism and other developmental disabilities in an inclusive and welcoming space that fosters friendship, belonging, and a sense of community.

We are seeking a dedicated and organized individual to develop and lead inclusive programs aligned with our main branches of programming – Life Skills, Health and Wellness, Recreation and Entertainment, Active Citizenship and Employment and Volunteerism.

Responsibilities

· Adapt existing and create new activities to meet the diverse needs, interests, and abilities of individuals with autism and other exceptionalities.

· Encourage participation, build rapport with members, and create a welcoming, supportive environment.

· Work collaboratively with the Rowan Tree team to align programming with the goals and preferences of participants.

· Support the planning and promotion of programs, including the creation of supporting materials.

· Track attendance and collect feedback from members to inform continuous improvement.

· Assist in developing partnerships with local organizations or professionals that may expand program offerings.

· As a small grass roots organization, all team members are expected to participate in all aspects of operating a small not-for-profit. This may include tasks related to broader operational aspects such as shopping, food preparation and cleaning. As a certified food production establishment, district safe food handling standards are upheld.

Qualifications

· Background in Developmental Services, Recreation Therapy, Education or other social sciences.

· Experience working or volunteering with individuals with autism or other intellectual disabilities.

· Strong interpersonal skills with the ability to communicate effectively with diverse audiences.

· Excellent organizational skills with attention to detail.

· Ability to manage multiple tasks simultaneously in a fast-paced environment.

· Positive attitude, professional demeanor, and a commitment to upholding the core values of our organization.

· Ability to work collaboratively within a team while also demonstrating initiative and independence.

· Ability to manage large groups with a strong relational approach to behaviour.

· First Aid

· Class G Drivers License

This opportunity is proudly supported by Northern Ontario Heritage Fund Corporation and is funded through the Workforce Development Program. Eligibility requirements of the program can be found here: https://nohfc.ca/en/pages/programs/people-talent-program/workforce-development-stream
