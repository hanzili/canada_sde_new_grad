Choose Collingwood General & Marine Hospital:

Explore a meaningful career in a place where lifestyle and purpose align. Collingwood General & Marine Hospital (CGMH) is located on the beautiful shores of Georgian Bay and surrounded by the Blue Mountains. With access to skiing, hiking, paddling, and a vibrant arts and culture scene, Collingwood offers a four-season recreational lifestyle and a strong sense of community.

CGMH is an 84-bed acute care hospital serving over 74,000 permanent residents and welcoming 3.5 million annual visitors across Collingwood, Clearview, Wasaga Beach, and the Blue Mountains. Our team of over 1,200 staff, physicians, midwives, and volunteers is dedicated to delivering high-quality care in areas including emergency, surgery, orthopaedics, obstetrics, dialysis, diagnostics, and mental health.

At CGMH, we’re not just caring for today, we’re building the healthcare system of tomorrow. Through our bold strategic plan, Tomorrow is Built Today, we are transforming care in South Georgian Bay.

To learn more about our development and capital campaign, visit: https://cgmh.on.ca/who-we-are/strategic-plan

The Opportunity:

The Junior Accountant supports the finance team by assisting with accurate financial recording,

reconciliation, and reporting in compliance with hospital policies and healthcare regulations. This role performs full-cycle accounting, financial analysis, regulatory reporting, and collaborates with accounting clerks to ensure transactional accuracy and completeness, contributing to the hospital’s financial integrity and operational efficiency.

Qualifications:

  • Post secondary education in Accounting, Finance, Business Administration, or related field required.

  • Actively pursuing CPA designation is considered an asset.

  • Minimum 5 years of accounting or finance experience, preferably within healthcare or public sector environments.

  • Familiarity with accounting software and hospital financial systems (e.g., Meditech) preferred.

  • Ability to maintain confidentiality and manage sensitive financial information responsibly.

  • Strong attention to detail and accuracy in financial data handling.

  • Proficiency in Microsoft Excel and other Microsoft Office applications.

  • Must strive to continuously provide a safe and supportive environment for patients, staff, physicians, and volunteers.

  • Must adhere to the duties of workers, as stipulated in the Occupational Health and Safety Act.

  • Must have an ability to effectively communicate (written and verbal) in the English language in order to provide excellent communication with team members, patients and families. Ability to communicate in additional language(s) is considered an asset.

  • Must have basic computer skills.

  • Must be dedicated to professional growth & development.

  • Must be able to perform all bona fide and essential duties of the position, which may include physical demands such as moving/transferring patients, pushing, pulling, bending, etc.

  • Must have demonstrated excellent attendance/performance record.

  • Excellent organization and prioritization/time-management skills.

  • Ability to work independently and effectively communicate with other members of the health care team.

Duties and Responsibilities:

Accounting & Financial Operations:

  • Assist in preparing journal entries and account adjustments, including those related to month-end and year-end closing.

  • Maintain the capital assets module, including tracking asset acquisitions, disposals, and depreciation, and prepare related journal entries accurately and timely.

  • Perform detailed reconciliations and account analyses to support financial reporting and decision-making

  • Analyse trends and variances in financial data and provide recommendations to support informed decision-making.

  • Liaise with external partners and the leadership team to facilitate accurate and timely processing of accounts payable transactions.

  • Collaborate with accounting clerks to review and validate accounts receivable and accounts payable transactions, ensuring accuracy and completeness.

  • Perform full-cycle accounting functions – including month-end, quarter-end, and year-end closing, Ministry reporting, and audit support – for external partners.

  • Perform detailed analysis on payroll data, ensuring accuracy and maintaining confidentiality in accordance with hospital policies.

  • Serve as the primary contact for physicians regarding remuneration, ensuring timely and accurate payments in compliance with Ministry of Health guidelines and hospital policies.

  • Other duties as assigned.

Reporting & Compliance

  • Support the preparation of internal and external financial reports, including submissions to the Ministry of Health, Ontario Health, and Canada Revenue Agency.

  • Maintain compliance with Public Sector Accounting Standards (PSAS), hospital policies, and applicable healthcare financial regulations.

  • Organize and maintain documentation required for audits, financial reviews, and regulatory compliance

Financial Systems & Process Support

  • Utilize hospital financial systems (e.g., Meditech and other platforms) to ensure data and accuracy and completeness

  • Contribute to process involvement initiatives aimed at enhancing operational efficiency and strengthening internal controls

  • Participate in special projects and other assignments as directed by the finance leadership team.
