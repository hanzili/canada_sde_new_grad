About the Role

About Motor Coach Industries

Bus coach maker; structured co-op program

Full Description

MCI is North America’s public and private market motor coach leader. Products include the luxury J-Series (an industry best-seller for over a decade), the workhorse D-Series, and the brand new zero-emission luxury and commuter coaches: the battery-electric J4500 CHARGE™ and MCI D45 CRT LE CHARGE™. MCI also provides maintenance, repair, 24-hour roadside assistance, parts, and technician training through the industry’s only Automotive Service Excellence (ASE) accredited MCI Academy. Further information is available at www.mcicoach.com.

Position Summary

The Manufacturing Engineering Co-op Student will support day-to-day production activities by assisting with key entry-level engineering tasks. This role provides hands-on experience in a dynamic manufacturing environment and contributes to continuous improvement initiatives. The student will work closely with the Manufacturing Engineering team to ensure production efficiency, quality, and process reliability.

Key Responsibilities

  • Resource Model Support: Assist in rebuilding and updating the production resource model to reflect current operations.
  • Installation Audits: Conduct audits to verify proper installation of components and systems on the production line.
  • Station Tool Audits: Perform audits of tools at workstations and update the tool database to ensure accuracy and availability.
  • DC Torque Tool Implementation: Support the rollout of DC torque tools and participate in process audits to ensure proper usage and calibration.
  • Time Studies: Collect and analyze data on new builds to identify process improvements and validate labor standards.

  • Quality at Source (QAS) Projects: Contribute to various QAS initiatives such as:

  • Coach Protection Project

  • Paint Quality Improvement
  • Refurbishing Component Shipping Containers
  • Updating Preventive Maintenance (PM) and Total Productive Maintenance (TPM) tasks for long-term sustainment

Learning Outcomes

  • Gain practical experience in manufacturing engineering processes
  • Develop communication and collaboration skills in a production environment
  • Learn how to conduct audits, analyze data, and support continuous improvement initiatives
  • Understand the importance of quality assurance and process reliability in manufacturing

Qualifications

  • Currently enrolled in a Mechanical, Electrical, Manufacturing, or Industrial Engineering program
  • Strong attention to detail and organizational skills
  • Basic understanding of manufacturing principles and production environments
  • Proficient in Microsoft Office (Excel, Word, Outlook)
  • Good communication and teamwork skills
  • Familiarity with CAD software is an asset but not required
