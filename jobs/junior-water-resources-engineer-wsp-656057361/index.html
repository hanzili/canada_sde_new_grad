The Opportunity

Build the Future with Us — As part of WSP’s Southern Ontario Mine Water Team , this role offers the opportunity to support water‑related engineering solutions for mining and environmental projects locally, nationally, and internationally.

Based in our Mississauga, ON office, this position is well suited for a Junior Water Resources Engineer who is eager to learn, contribute to meaningful projects, and grow under the guidance of experienced consultants. You’ll support intermediate and senior engineers while gaining hands‑on exposure to hydrologic and hydraulic analysis, field programs, and multidisciplinary collaboration.

If it involves water — you’ll be part of it.

Please note: A reduced work week may be considered for this role if it better supports the balance you’re seeking.

Your Impact

  • Support intermediate and senior engineers on mine water and water resources engineering projects.
  • Contribute to hydrologic and hydraulic analyses including water balances, streamflow assessments, flood studies, and hydraulic evaluations.
  • Assist with assessments and designs related to water diversions, control structures, flood mitigation, and hydraulic infrastructure.
  • Help plan and participate in hydrological field programs at local and remote sites.
  • Compile, organize, and analyze field data to support technical assessments and design.
  • Develop inputs for hydrologic and hydraulic models with guidance from senior team members.
  • Collaborate with multidisciplinary teams across Canada and globally.
  • Participate in technical development opportunities supported by structured mentorship and training.

The Skills That Set You Apart

  • Bachelor’s degree in Civil or Environmental Engineering (or equivalent).
  • Academic or early‑career experience with hydrologic and hydraulic analysis and field techniques (e.g., streamflow measurements).
  • Commitment to working toward professional designation in Ontario when eligible.
  • Valid Ontario Class G Driver’s License , clean driver’s abstract, and access to a vehicle.
  • Proficiency with MS Office applications.
  • Comfort working in field environments, including active mine sites, with safety as a top priority.
  • Willingness to travel locally and remotely, including comfort with off‑road vehicles and small aircraft.
  • Strong interest in learning new systems, tools, and technical skills.
  • Experience with hydrologic/hydraulic modelling, AutoCAD , GIS , or mine water management is an asset.
  • Fluency in additional languages (e.g., French or Spanish) is an asset.
