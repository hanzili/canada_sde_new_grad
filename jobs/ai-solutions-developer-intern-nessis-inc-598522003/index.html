About the Role

Develop AI features and integrate LLM APIs

About Nessis Inc

Nessis Inc, AI software company

Full Description

The Opportunity

As an AI Solutions Developer Intern, you'll work directly with our development and strategy team to design, prototype, and implement AI-powered features that enhance our quality management and manufacturing execution systems. This is a hands-on role where you'll create real features that impact how manufacturers work.

You'll be working on projects like, but not specific to:

  • Building AI-assisted document analysis for quality records and supplier documentation.
  • Developing intelligent defect classification and root cause suggestion tools.
  • Creating natural language interfaces for querying manufacturing data.
  • Prototyping predictive quality features using production data.
  • Integrating large language model APIs into existing .NET/web applications.

Required Qualifications

  • Post-secondary degree or diploma in Computer Science, Software Engineering, Data Science, or a related field.

  • Proficiency in Python.

  • Knowledge of fine tuning LLM’s, use of trainable models (Llama, Phi, Qwen), setting up open-source models.

  • Familiarity with REST APIs and web services.

  • Understanding of AI/ML concepts, particularly large language models.

  • Strong critical thinking skills and ability to work independently.

  • Excellent written and verbal communication skills.

  • Clear ability to speak English, the position requires verbal communication with clients.

Required Skills

  • Experience with LLM APIs (OpenAI, Anthropic, Azure OpenAI).

  • Proficiency in RAG & fine tuning LLMs.

  • Knowledge of .NET, C#, or JavaScript/TypeScript.

  • Experience with SQL databases.

What matters most: We value curiosity, initiative, and the ability to turn ideas into working software. Show us projects you've built—GitHub repos, hackathon entries, personal apps—anything that demonstrates you can ship.

Program Eligibility

This position is funded through the NRC Industrial Research Assistance Program (IRAP) Youth Employment Program. To be eligible, you must be:

  • Between 15 and 30 years of age at the start of the internship

  • A Canadian citizen, permanent resident, or person granted refugee status in Canada

  • A post-secondary graduate

  • Legally entitled to work in Ontario

  • Not currently receiving Employment Insurance (EI) benefits

What We Offer

  • Competitive salary funded through NRC-IRAP

  • Mentorship from experienced software and business professionals

  • Real-world experience building AI solutions for enterprise manufacturing clients

  • Flexible hybrid/remote work arrangements

  • Opportunity to see your work deployed to major automotive suppliers

  • Potential for continued employment based on performance and business needs
