Full Description

Reporting to: Head of Finance, Tate ASP Canada

Location: Burlington, Ontario (Canada)

Salary Range: $20–25/hour

Duration: 10 weeks (May to July)

About the role

We are seeking a highly motivated third- or fourth-year accounting student to join our Finance team for a 10-week Kingspan Internship Program. This role provides hands-on exposure to transactional accounting, inventory controls, and finance process improvement within a growing construction business.

Tate Access Floors is part of the Data Solutions division of Kingspan Group PLC, an Irish publicly traded company founded in 1965. Kingspan reported revenues of €8.6bn and EBITDA of €1.14bn in 2024.

Tate ASP Canada serves as the Canadian headquarters for Tate Access Floors. In addition to supplying Tate’s manufactured products across both the Commercial Office and Data Centre sectors, Tate ASP self-performs installations throughout Ontario.

What You’ll Do

  • Support transactional accounting activities, including validation and review of AP transactions and inventory movements
  • Assist with the mid-year inventory count, including preparation, participation, and post-count reconciliation and variance analysis
  • Support AP process review initiatives by identifying recurring exceptions, documenting workflows, and assisting with process improvements
  • Assist during month-end activities, including reconciliations and variance investigation.
  • Contribute to documentation and standardization of finance processes to support operational consistency and audit readiness
  • Provide structured analysis and reporting support for ad hoc finance initiatives

Additional Expectations

  • Remain compliant with the Code of Conduct and Policies, including the Kingspan Group Compliance Policy.
  • Ensure all duties related to product compliance are followed in accordance with the Product
  • Compliance Policy, relevant laws, regulations, and market requirements.
  • Be responsible for completing tasks to achieve compliance goals and meet the demands of the Compliance Management System.
  • Raise concerns related to the Compliance Management System with a supervisor, manager, any member of the Leadership Team, or through the confidential whistleblower service.

Internship Project

In addition to day-to-day responsibilities, the intern will complete a structured project focused on AP process improvements.

This project will include:

  • Reviewing AP transaction flows to identify recurring exceptions and manual workarounds
  • Assessing how AP processes operate in practice versus documented procedures
  • Supporting planned process improvements and strengthening consistency
  • Standardizing templates and documenting improved workflows

At the end of the internship, the intern will present their findings, key learnings, and recommendations as part of a formal showcase.

What You’ll Bring

  • Currently enrolled in a 3rd or 4th year Accounting or Finance program at an accredited post-secondary institution
  • Basic understanding of accounting principles (AP, inventory, accruals, revenue recognition basics)
  • Proficiency in Excel, including Pivot Tables and XLOOKUP/VLOOKUP
  • Strong analytical mindset with the ability to investigate discrepancies and identify root causes.
  • Detail-oriented with a high degree of accuracy
  • Strong written and verbal communication skills

Eligibility Requirement

  • Must be enrolled in a recognized post-secondary program and eligible to participate in a registered internship or co-op program that qualifies for applicable government student grant funding (where applicable)

What You’ll Learn

  • Real exposure to operational finance in a project-based construction environment
  • Experience supporting inventory controls and process improvement initiatives
  • Direct mentorship from senior finance leadership
  • Opportunity to present findings and recommendations to internal stakeholders.
