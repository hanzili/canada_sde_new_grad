Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need a CPA to get an accounting job in Canada?

Not for entry-level roles, but most employers expect you to be enrolled in or planning to pursue the CPA program. Many firms sponsor CPA fees and provide study time. The CPA designation significantly increases your earning potential.

What is the salary range for entry-level accounting roles in Canada?

Junior accountants and financial analysts typically earn $50,000-$65,000 CAD. Big Four firms start around $55,000-$60,000. Corporate FP&A roles at large companies can start at $60,000-$75,000.

What is the difference between public accounting and corporate finance?

Public accounting (Big Four) involves auditing or advising multiple clients with busy seasons. Corporate finance (FP&A, controllership) focuses on one company's internal financials with more predictable hours. Both are excellent career paths.
